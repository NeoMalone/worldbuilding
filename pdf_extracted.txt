The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF) escalates its control over South America by
deploying operatives and military assets to Argentina, Brazil, and Boombayah, aiming to
neutralize large portions of their populations. This action is part of a broader plan to
consolidate LETF dominance in the Americas.
Campaign Details
1. Argentina:
Operatives: LETF assassins infiltrate major cities such as Buenos Aires, Córdoba,
and Rosario, targeting resistance leaders, military personnel, and prominent
civilians.
Military Assets: Bombing campaigns focus on urban centers and industrial hubs,
with widespread use of chemical agents.
2. Brazil:
Tactics:
Ground forces sweep through key regions, including São Paulo and
the Amazon, targeting villages and resistance strongholds.
Airstrikes on Brasília aim to cripple the central government.
Outcome: Many regions are depopulated, and the remaining population is forced
into labor camps under LETF rule.

Territorial Control: The LETF tightens its grip over South America but faces growing

2. LETF Creates a United Nations Hit List
The LETF compiles a hit list of United Nations countries opposing its actions, marking
them as potential future targets for annexation or neutralization.
Notable Countries on the Hit List:
1. United States Allies:
Austria, Sweden, and others suspected of covertly undermining LETF efforts.
2. Neutral Nations:
Nations like Switzerland and Boombayah, accused of harboring resistance
operatives.
3. Major Powers:
China, India, and France, seen as threats to LETF expansion.

As LETF aggression grows, the Golden Lemons Alliance (GLA)—
comprising Austria, Sweden, and the United States—meets secretly to discuss strategies

1963: Golden Lemons Alliance Launches Operation Iron Dawn
Objective
The Golden Lemons Alliance (GLA)—comprising Austria, Sweden, and the United States—
launches Operation Iron Dawn, a classified mission aimed at dismantling LETF control over
former USSR territories and restoring an independent Soviet Union as a bulwark against
LETF domination.
Plan and Execution
1. Intelligence Gathering
U.S. deploys E-12 Sentinel aircraft for surveillance over LETF-controlled USSR regions,
identifying weak points in LETF supply chains and military installations.
Austrian operatives infiltrate LETF-administered cities like Moscow and St. Petersburg,
establishing contact with underground resistance networks.
Sweden provides advanced encryption technology to coordinate communication
among resistance cells.
2. Covert Operations
Sabotage Teams:
Austrian Jagdkommando operatives sabotage LETF infrastructure, including
railways and ammunition depots.
Resistance Armament:
Swedish forces smuggle arms to local resistance fighters, including Soviet-era
weapons and modern equipment.
Propaganda Campaign:
GLA broadcasts pro-USSR propaganda, urging citizens to rise against LETF rule
and promising support for an independent Soviet Union.
3. Military Engagement
Phase 1: Air Superiority

The U.S. deploys stealth bombers to disable LETF air defenses in major cities
like Moscow, Volgograd, and Kiev.
Swedish fighters provide support, engaging LETF aircraft in contested zones.
Phase 2: Ground Assaults
Resistance forces, armed and trained by the GLA, begin coordinated assaults on
LETF outposts.
U.S. special forces lead strikes on key LETF command centers, focusing on
dismantling leadership in the region.
Phase 3: Naval Operations
Swedish naval forces blockade LETF supply lines in the Black Sea, while U.S.
submarines disrupt LETF operations in the Arctic Ocean.
Key Locations Targeted
1. Moscow:
Heavy fighting erupts as resistance fighters and GLA operatives push to liberate
the capital.
LETF command structures are destroyed in a combined air and ground assault.
2. Kiev:
Resistance forces reclaim the city after LETF troops withdraw under pressure from
coordinated attacks.
3. Volgograd:
Strategic for supply lines, the city is taken after weeks of intense fighting.
Outcome
USSR Reinstated
After months of sustained efforts, LETF forces retreat from most USSR territories.
An independent Soviet Union is declared, with leadership composed of former
resistance leaders and surviving Soviet officials.
Territorial Reclamation
Key regions, including Russia, Ukraine, Belarus, and the Baltic States, are restored to
Soviet control.

Casualties
LETF: Sustains heavy losses, with thousands of troops killed or captured.
