In the 1961–1964 country game, **Osama bin Laden** is assigned the fictional role of **Hero of the Air Force** within the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]. This is a surreal alternate-history casting within the game's absurdist political mythology — the LETF deifies him as a divine pilot rather than as any historical figure. The role is antagonist-coded and functions purely within the game's fictional narrative.

## Role in LETF Mythology

Within [[Morganism]], bin Laden is deified as the supreme pilot — a figure of *unmatched aerial skill* whose divine precision in combat makes him a god of air warfare. LETF propaganda films depict him flying fighter jets with superhuman accuracy, and he is invoked in pre-battle prayers by LETF air force personnel.

He is classified alongside [[Mr. Clean]], [[Gorg Morgan]], [[Vexbolts]], and [[Skibidi Toilet]] as a divine figure of the Morganist pantheon, though his specific designation as "Hero of the Air Force" places him in a more military than theological role — a bridge figure between the divine pantheon and the operational command structure.

## Campaign Activities

Bin Laden leads LETF air raid operations throughout the campaign:
- **Japan Campaign** ([[Operation Golden Dawn]]): Leads air raids, achieving multiple key victories against Japanese air forces.
- **Middle East Campaigns**: Oversees air superiority missions during operations in Israel, Lebanon, Syria, and Jordan.
- **USSR campaigns**: Participates in air operations during [[Operation Crimson Fade]] and [[Operation Soviet Eclipse]].

## Death

During [[Operation Silent Fade]] (1963), bin Laden is located in an **underground bunker in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia**. **U.S. Delta Force operatives** conduct a night raid, neutralize him, and destroy the bunker to conceal evidence of the operation. His death removes the LETF's most celebrated air-combat figure.
