In the 1961–1964 country game, **Jeffrey Dahmer's Dad** is assigned the fictional role of **Psychological Warfare and Moral Realignment Director** within the rebuilt [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]] leadership following [[Operation Silent Fade]]. He is tasked with *"moral realignment"* — a vague but ominous function in the LETF's post-collapse governance strategy.

## Context: Post-Silent Fade Resurgence

During [[Operation Eternal Fade]], the LETF assembles a new leadership roster to replace those killed during [[Operation Silent Fade]]. The roster is deliberately eclectic, mixing historical figures ([[Alexander the Great (Fictional LETF Role)]], [[King Darius III]]), pop culture figures ([[Shrek (LETF Symbolic Figure)]]), and deliberately unsettling choices — of which this character is the clearest example.

## Role in the LETF

His assigned portfolio — *"moral realignment" and psychological warfare* — covers two related dimensions of LETF control:

1. **Moral Realignment**: Enforcing behavioral and ideological conformity across LETF-occupied populations, with special emphasis on territories that have shown resistance. This likely involves propaganda, re-education, and coercive compliance programs.
2. **Psychological Warfare**: Using fear, confusion, and psychological pressure against both resistance movements and potentially rival LETF factions.

The specific choice of this character for this role is characteristic of the LETF's tendency to assign figures whose very identity carries a psychological charge — his presence in the leadership communicates something about the LETF's values and methods that explicit propaganda cannot.

## Significance

Like [[Shrek (LETF Symbolic Figure)]] but in a darker register, this appointment signals the LETF's post-collapse state: an empire that has lost its original divine leadership structure and is reassembling itself with whatever symbolic material is available, regardless of coherence.
