**King Darius III** is a fictionalized historical figure in the 1961–1964 country game, assigned the role of **Head of Historical Strategy and Propaganda** within the rebuilt [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]] following [[Operation Silent Fade]]. He is recruited for his historical association with the Persian Achaemenid Empire, which the LETF uses as a propaganda touchstone — particularly given the LETF's own origins in Egypt and the Middle East.

## Historical Context (In-Game Use)

Darius III (historically the last ruler of the Persian Empire, defeated by Alexander the Great at Gaugamela in 331 BC) is recruited by the LETF in part as an ironic counterpoint to [[Alexander the Great (Fictional LETF Role)]] — the LETF now employs both the conqueror (Alexander) and the conquered (Darius III), framing itself as the synthesis and heir of all great empires.

## Role in the LETF

King Darius III is appointed to **head historical strategy and propaganda**, with a specific focus on:
- Invoking ancient Persian glory in LETF cultural and military messaging
- Framing the LETF's Middle Eastern conquests as a restoration of Persian imperial destiny
- Providing historical strategic precedents (from the Persian Wars, the Achaemenid administrative system) for governing large multi-ethnic empires
- Connecting the LETF's Egyptian origins to the broader ancient Near Eastern imperial tradition

Within the [[Order of Morganism]]'s restructured leadership, Darius III represents the historical-legitimacy portfolio — the argument that the LETF is not merely a modern empire but the natural continuation of a 2,500-year imperial tradition.

## Significance

King Darius III's inclusion alongside [[Alexander the Great (Fictional LETF Role)]] creates a remarkable historical dynamic within the LETF's rebuilt leadership: the conqueror and the conquered, united under Morganist ideology. This reflects the LETF's characteristic tendency to incorporate contradictions into its mythology rather than resolve them.
