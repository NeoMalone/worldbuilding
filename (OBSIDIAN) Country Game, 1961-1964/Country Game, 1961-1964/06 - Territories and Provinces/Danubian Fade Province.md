The **Danubian Fade Province** is the LETF's administrative designation for occupied [[Hungary]] following the 1962 [[Operation Total Fade]] annexation and the nuclear strike on Budapest during the [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]]. The province occupies a strategically significant Central European position at the crossroads of LETF-controlled territory and Swedish-administered Germanic Province. Its eventual liberation — including the retaking of Budapest with Swedish-backed resistance — is one of the campaign's notable Central European endgame events.

## Geography and Administration

The province covers Hungarian national territory under Morganist institutional governance. Following Budapest's nuclear devastation, provincial administration is distributed across surviving secondary cities (Debrecen, Miskolc, Pécs, Győr). The Danube corridor provides the province's main logistical artery.

## Strategic Value

The Danubian Province serves several LETF strategic functions:
- **Central European positioning**: Connects LETF Eastern European holdings to its Carpathian and Romanian provinces
- **Danube corridor**: Provides riverine logistics across LETF Central Europe
- **Buffer with Swedish territory**: Adjacent to the Germanic Province under Swedish administration, creating a contested LETF-Sweden frontier zone

## Resistance and Swedish Support

Hungarian resistance benefits from Sweden's geographic proximity once the Swedish anti-LETF pivot is underway:
- Swedish intelligence networks extend into the Danubian Province
- Swedish SOG special forces provide tactical support to Hungarian resistance fighters
- Coordination between Hungarian resistance and Swedish counter-LETF operations becomes one of the Central European resistance success stories

## Liberation: Budapest Retaken

The retaking of Budapest is among the campaign's significant endgame events. Swedish-backed Hungarian resistance forces, exploiting LETF garrison reductions during the overextension collapse, launch coordinated operations that reclaim the capital. The Budapest liberation symbolically validates Swedish resistance support strategy and contributes to the broader collapse-phase territorial liberation narrative.
