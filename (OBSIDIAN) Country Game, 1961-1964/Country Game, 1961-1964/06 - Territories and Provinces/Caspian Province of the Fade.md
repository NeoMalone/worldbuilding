The **Caspian Province of the Fade** is the LETF's administrative designation for southern Russian and Caucasus territories acquired in the 1961 conditional surrender following [[Operation Crimson Fade]]. The province covers regions including Rostov-on-Don, Astrakhan, Stalingrad (Volgograd), and the northern Caucasus, giving the LETF Caspian Sea coastal access, significant oil reserves, and a continental corridor connecting LETF Middle Eastern holdings to former Soviet zones.

## Geography and Administration

The province encompasses:
- **Northern Caucasus**: Mountain corridor connecting Russian plains to LETF Levantine and Iranian holdings
- **Lower Volga region**: Stalingrad/Volgograd corridor with significant industrial infrastructure
- **Caspian coast**: Astrakhan and surrounding Caspian Sea access
- **Don River basin**: Rostov-on-Don administrative center

Provincial governance is administered from Rostov-on-Don under Morganist institutional frameworks, replacing Soviet administrative structures.

## Strategic Value

The province's strategic assets are substantial:
- **Caspian Sea access**: Enables LETF naval and logistics operations in the Caspian basin, complementing Persian Gulf and Mediterranean access
- **Oil reserves**: Caucasus and lower Volga oil production feeds LETF energy supply chains
- **Land bridge**: Connects LETF Middle Eastern holdings (via Iran and Iraq) to former Soviet territories
- **Industrial infrastructure**: Stalingrad/Volgograd industrial base supports LETF military production

## Resistance

Resistance in the Caspian Province is sustained throughout the occupation:
- [[Restored Soviet Resistance]] cells operate across the territory, particularly in industrial cities
- Mountain terrain in the Caucasus provides sanctuary zones for partisan operations
- Resistance networks coordinate with broader Soviet liberation movement structures supported by the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]]

## Liberation via Operation Iron Dawn

[[Operation Iron Dawn]] (1963) liberates the Caspian Province as part of the broader restoration of an independent Soviet Union. Volgograd is among the cities specifically named in Iron Dawn's liberation operations. Following the operation, the territory is integrated into the restored Soviet state.
