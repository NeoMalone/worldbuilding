The **Central Steppe Province** is the LETF's administrative designation for the former Kazakh SSR territories absorbed during [[Operation Soviet Eclipse]] (1962). The province covers the vast Central Asian steppes from Almaty through Novosibirsk, providing the LETF with the continental corridor connecting its [[Mongolian Province of the Fade]] holdings to its European territorial cluster and serving as the staging ground for [[Operation Jade Fade]]'s northern advance into China.

## Geography and Administration

The province spans an enormous Central Asian territory:
- **Almaty zone**: Southern administrative center near the Chinese border
- **Northern steppe**: Novosibirsk-centered region linking to former Russian territory
- **Central Asian interior**: Vast lightly populated steppe terrain difficult for LETF garrison forces to fully patrol

LETF provincial governance under Morganist frameworks replaces Soviet Kazakh administrative structures. The province's vast geographic scale relative to garrison capacity creates persistent administrative challenges.

## Strategic Value

The province serves multiple critical strategic functions:
- **Sino-Soviet corridor**: Connects LETF holdings in the former USSR to Chinese conquest zones via [[Operation Jade Fade]]
- **Mongolia link**: Provides continuous territorial connection between LETF Central Asian holdings and the Mongolian Province
- **Resource base**: Steppe agriculture and Central Asian mineral resources feed LETF supply chains
- **Strategic depth**: The territory's geographic scale provides defensive depth for LETF central administrative positions

## Resistance

Steppe resistance leverages the province's geographic characteristics:
- Vast steppe terrain provides operational depth for partisan movements
- Central Asian populations with distinctive cultural identities resist Morganist imposition
- [[Restored Soviet Resistance]] networks integrate Central Asian operations with broader Soviet liberation efforts

## Liberation via Operation Iron Dawn

[[Operation Iron Dawn]] (1963) restores Central Asian territories to the independent Soviet Union. The province's eastern reaches near the Chinese border become contested zones during the rollback phase, with both Chinese and Central Asian resistance networks operating across the former LETF administrative frontier.
