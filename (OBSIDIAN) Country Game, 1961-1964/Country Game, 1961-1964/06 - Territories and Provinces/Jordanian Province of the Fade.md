The **Jordanian Province of the Fade** is the LETF's administrative designation for annexed [[Jordan]] following [[Operation Fades Crossing]] (1961). Centered on Amman with key holdings at Irbid (north), Aqaba (south), and Zarqa (military hub), the province is one of the smaller but strategically essential 1961 LETF acquisitions — its function is connective rather than valuable in its own right, providing the territorial bridge that unifies the LETF's Levantine and Mesopotamian holdings and opening Red Sea access via the Aqaba port.

## Geography and Administration

The province covers Jordanian national territory under Morganist institutional governance. Provincial administration operates from Amman as the capital, with regional administrative nodes:
- **Amman**: Provincial capital and primary administrative center
- **Irbid**: Northern Jordan administrative node, near the Syrian border
- **Aqaba**: Red Sea port; major LETF maritime logistics hub
- **Zarqa**: Military-administrative center; absorbed into LETF regional garrison network

## Strategic Value

The province's strategic value to the LETF is primarily connective:

- **Land bridge function**: Connects LETF Levantine holdings (Lebanon, Syria, Israel) to LETF Mesopotamian Province (Iraq), creating unbroken territorial continuity across the Middle East
- **Red Sea access**: Aqaba port provides Red Sea naval access, complementing LETF's Persian Gulf access via Basra; gives the empire maritime presence in the Red Sea-Indian Ocean corridor
- **Saudi approach corridor**: The southern Jordanian-Saudi border serves as the primary LETF approach vector for future operations against Saudi Arabia ([[Operation Total Fade]])
- **Resistance isolation**: The province eliminates a potential resistance sanctuary between Israel and Iraq

## Stability

The Jordanian Province is one of the more stable LETF Middle Eastern provinces during the empire's height. Its compact geography and integration into broader LETF regional administrative networks make it manageable for available garrison capacity. Unlike larger occupied territories, Jordan's geographic scale does not exceed LETF administrative reach.
