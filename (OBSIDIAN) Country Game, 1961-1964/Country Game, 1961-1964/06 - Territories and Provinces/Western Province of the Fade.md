The **Western Province of the Fade** is the LETF's administrative designation for the former Ukraine SSR following the 1961 conditional surrender that concluded [[Operation Crimson Fade]]. Centered on Kiev and Kharkiv, the province provides the LETF with significant agricultural and industrial capacity, a continental land bridge connecting LETF European territories to former Soviet zones, and a forward position for subsequent Soviet operations. The province's liberation via [[Operation Iron Dawn]] (1963) is a central element of the campaign's restoration of an independent Soviet Union.

## Geography and Administration

The province covers former Ukrainian territory under Morganist institutional governance:
- **Kiev**: Provincial capital; major Ukrainian historical and administrative center
- **Kharkiv**: Major eastern Ukrainian industrial city
- **Black Sea coast**: Odessa and Crimean coastal access (where LETF presence extends)
- **Western Ukraine**: Border zones with Polish remnant territory and Austrian Brest Province

The Ukrainian agricultural plains feed substantial production into LETF supply chains; the industrial infrastructure of eastern Ukraine supports LETF military production.

## Strategic Value

The province provides multiple critical strategic assets:
- **Agricultural production**: Ukrainian grain and agricultural production provides significant LETF food supply
- **Industrial capacity**: Eastern Ukrainian industrial infrastructure feeds military production
- **Land bridge**: Connects LETF European territories to former Soviet zones
- **Black Sea access**: Coastal positioning supports LETF Black Sea maritime operations
- **Soviet operations staging**: Western Province serves as the staging ground for [[Operation Soviet Eclipse]]'s western front advance toward Moscow in 1962

## Resistance

Ukrainian resistance under LETF occupation contributes to the broader [[Restored Soviet Resistance]] movement. The province's resistance is shaped by Ukrainian historical memory of multiple foreign occupations and the deep agricultural-rural population dispersion that provides sanctuary zones LETF forces cannot effectively patrol.

## Liberation via Operation Iron Dawn

[[Operation Iron Dawn]] (1963) liberates the Western Province as part of the broader Soviet restoration:
- U.S. stealth bombers neutralize LETF air defenses in Kiev
- Armed Ukrainian resistance, equipped through GLA channels, leads the Kiev liberation
- Sweden's naval operations in the Black Sea cut LETF maritime supply lines
- Following liberation, Ukrainian territory is incorporated into the restored Soviet Union

## Post-Restoration

The Western Province ceases to exist as an LETF administrative entity following Iron Dawn's liberation. Ukrainian governance is restored as part of the reconstituted Soviet Union led by former resistance leaders and surviving Soviet officials.
