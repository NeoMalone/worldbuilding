The **Arabian Fade Province** is the LETF's administrative designation for occupied [[Saudi Arabia]] following the 1962 [[Operation Total Fade]] annexation and the nuclear strike on Riyadh during the [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]]. The province covers the Arabian Peninsula's Saudi territory under [[Morganism]] doctrinal imposition — a particularly fraught arrangement given the territory contains Islam's holiest sites at Mecca and Medina. The LETF's claim to religious authority over these sites generates intense, sustained religious resistance throughout the occupation.

## Geography and Administration

LETF provincial governance operates from Jeddah following Riyadh's nuclear devastation. The territory is divided into:
- **Coastal administrative zones**: LETF authority concentrated in Red Sea and Persian Gulf coastal cities
- **Najd interior**: LETF presence is intermittent; tribal authority structures persist parallel to formal LETF governance
- **Hejaz religious zone**: Mecca and Medina are nominally under LETF religious-political authority but face the deepest cultural resistance

## Strategic Value

The province's primary strategic value is economic — Saudi oil reserves are integrated into LETF energy supply chains. Persian Gulf and Red Sea coastal access supports LETF naval operations. However, the religious imposition of Morganism on Mecca and Medina creates ideological costs that exceed any economic benefit.

## Resistance

Saudi resistance is structured around tribal networks rather than conventional military organization:
- Pre-state tribal authority systems operate parallel to LETF administration
- Desert geography provides natural sanctuary zones LETF cannot patrol
- Religious motivation around Mecca and Medina generates broad community-level resistance
- Resistance coordination uses kinship and alliance structures invisible to LETF surveillance

## Liberation

During the collapse phase, Saudi tribal resistance — supported externally through GLA counter-coalition channels — drives sovereignty restoration. The combination of indigenous tribal capability and external GLA backing produces one of the more effectively coordinated regional liberation efforts.
