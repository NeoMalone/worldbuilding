**North Japan** is the northern Japanese territorial zone annexed by [[Austria]] following the GLA conquest of Japan in [[Operation Golden Dawn]] (1961). Renamed by Austria as the **Austrian Northern Prefecture**, the territory provides Austria with its primary Pacific holding — a forward intelligence and naval position on the Pacific Rim that complements Austria's continental European territorial profile. North Japan is administered under Austrian governance frameworks, distinctly different from the Morganist administration imposed on [[South Japan]] under LETF control.

## Geography and Administration

The territory covers the northern portion of the Japanese archipelago:
- **Hokkaido**: Austria's primary administrative and military base
- **Sendai**: Major Tohoku regional center
- **Aomori**: Northern Honshu administrative point
- **Niigata**: Sea of Japan coastal hub
- **Northern Tokyo districts**: Border zones with LETF-controlled South Japan

Austrian governance preserves significant existing Japanese institutional frameworks rather than imposing wholesale replacement, contrasting with LETF Morganist imposition in adjacent South Japan.

## Strategic Value

The territory provides Austria with several strategic assets:
- **Pacific signals intelligence hub**: Austrian intelligence facilities in Hokkaido feed [[Golden Lemons Intelligence Network]] capabilities; the [[E-12 Sentinel]] reconnaissance aircraft operate from North Japan bases
- **Forward naval base**: Pacific maritime positioning enables Austrian power projection beyond its continental European base
- **Counterbalance to LETF South Japan**: Austrian presence in northern Japan provides geographic counterweight to LETF Pacific operations from Pearl Harbor and South Japan
- **Pacific surveillance**: Pacific surveillance network coordinated with U.S. assets

## Stability

North Japan's stability under Austrian administration is significantly higher than LETF Pacific holdings. Austrian governance avoids the ideological imposition that drives resistance in LETF Fade Provinces. The Austrian Northern Prefecture remains a stable Austrian holding through the campaign timeline.

## Role in Late-Period Operations

During the 1962-1963 anti-LETF pivot, Austrian assets in North Japan support broader counter-LETF operations:
- E-12 Sentinel reconnaissance contributes to GLA intelligence on LETF Pacific operations
- Pacific positioning supports coordination with U.S. anti-LETF operations
- Austrian Northern Prefecture serves as one of several Austrian platforms for the broader GLA intelligence apparatus
