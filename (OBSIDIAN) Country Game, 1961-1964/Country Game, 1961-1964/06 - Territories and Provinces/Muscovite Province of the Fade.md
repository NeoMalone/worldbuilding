The **Muscovite Province of the Fade** is the LETF's administrative designation for the Moscow region and surrounding western Russian territory following [[Operation Soviet Eclipse]] (1962). As the province containing the Soviet Union's former capital, it carries the highest symbolic significance among LETF former-Soviet provincial holdings — the conquest of Moscow represents the LETF's claim to have ended Russian sovereignty entirely. The province's liberation via [[Operation Iron Dawn]] (1963) and the restoration of Soviet sovereignty over Moscow is one of the campaign's most consequential reversals.

## Geography and Administration

The province covers western Russian territory centered on Moscow, including:
- **Moscow**: Provincial capital and former Soviet capital; LETF symbolic center of post-Soviet administrative authority
- **Surrounding oblasts**: Major western Russian cities and the Moscow region's rural hinterland

LETF provincial governance under Morganist frameworks replaces Soviet governmental structures within the province. The Kremlin and Soviet symbolic infrastructure are converted to LETF administrative use.

## Strategic Value

The province's strategic value is primarily symbolic and administrative:
- **Symbolic dominance**: Control of Moscow represents the LETF's claim to have superseded the Soviet political order
- **Administrative core**: Moscow's institutional infrastructure provides LETF administrative reach across former Soviet zones
- **Russian resistance focal point**: Moscow's symbolic significance makes it the natural focus of [[Restored Soviet Resistance]] activity

## Resistance and Liberation

The Muscovite Province is the central focus of [[Restored Soviet Resistance]] operations:
- Urban resistance networks operate within Moscow itself
- Surrounding rural and forest sanctuary zones support partisan activity
- Resistance coordination feeds into broader liberation planning supported by [[Golden Lemons Intelligence Network]]

[[Operation Iron Dawn]] (1963) liberates Moscow through coordinated GLA stealth bombing of LETF air defenses combined with armed resistance assault on the city. Moscow's recovery is the centerpiece of the Soviet restoration narrative — the symbolic restoration of Russian sovereignty in its historical capital.

## Post-Restoration

Following Iron Dawn, Moscow returns to its position as capital of the restored Soviet Union. The Muscovite Province as an LETF administrative entity ceases to exist; Russian governmental institutions are progressively rebuilt under post-restoration leadership.
