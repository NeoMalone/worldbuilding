The **Pacific Province of the Fade** is the LETF's designation for Hawaii following its transfer from the [[United States of America]] in 1961. As one of two U.S. territorial cessions to the LETF (alongside [[LETF-US Disputed Territory (Newport County)]]), the Hawaii transfer is the more strategically significant of the two — converting Pearl Harbor into a LETF naval base gives the empire its primary mid-Pacific power projection platform. The transfer's domestic political controversy in the United States contributes substantially to growing American dissent against LETF alliance during 1962.

## Geography and Administration

The province covers the Hawaiian Islands under LETF administrative governance:
- **Honolulu**: Administrative capital and primary urban center
- **Pearl Harbor**: Major LETF naval base; converted from U.S. naval infrastructure
- **Outer islands**: LETF presence varies; smaller islands receive limited garrison attention

Morganist institutional frameworks are imposed on Hawaiian governance, replacing U.S. state-level institutions.

## Strategic Value

The province is one of the LETF's most strategically valuable Pacific holdings:
- **Pearl Harbor**: Major naval base enabling LETF mid-Pacific presence; supports operations between the Asian theater and the Americas
- **Pacific power projection**: Hawaiian positioning enables LETF naval reach across the entire Pacific Ocean
- **Transit hub**: Mid-ocean position makes Hawaii a critical logistics node for LETF Pacific operations
- **Symbolic value**: Demonstrating U.S. willingness to transfer historically significant U.S. territory validates LETF claims to ascendant global power

## U.S. Domestic Controversy

The Hawaii transfer generates significant U.S. domestic political controversy. American public opinion increasingly questions the cession of a U.S. state to a foreign theocratic empire, particularly as LETF actions escalate during 1962. The transfer becomes one of several factors that contribute to U.S. political pivot against the LETF, leading to U.S. covert operations against the alliance partner ([[Operation Silent Halo]]) and ultimately the U.S. role in [[Operation Silent Fade]].

## Post-Pivot Status

Following the U.S. anti-LETF pivot and LETF leadership elimination via Operation Silent Fade, the Pacific Province's status presumably reverts toward U.S. control as LETF administrative claims become unenforceable. The territory's specific status during the LETF collapse period and the formal mechanics of any reversion are not detailed in canon.
