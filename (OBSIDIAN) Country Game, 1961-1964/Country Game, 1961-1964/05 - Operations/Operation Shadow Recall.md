**Operation Shadow Recall** is the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]'s 1963 covert mission to locate, infiltrate, and neutralize [[Operation Silent Fade]]'s survivors — specifically the two LETF leaders captured alive and held in classified [[Golden Lemons Alliance]] facilities: [[Magnus Maximus]] (held at a U.S. black site) and [[King Darius Morgan]] (detained at an undisclosed location by Swedish SOG). The operation is led by [[John Wilkes Booth (Fictional LETF Role)]] and succeeds in eliminating both captives. A deliberate disinformation component frames Switzerland and Boombayah as responsible, creating diplomatic crises that destabilize neutral-nation diplomacy.

## Background

After [[Operation Silent Fade]], the LETF's new leadership under [[Operation Eternal Fade]] faces an urgent intelligence problem: Magnus Maximus and King Darius Morgan are alive, in custody, and being interrogated. The knowledge they possess — LETF command structures, hidden weapons caches, operational protocols, the identities of double agents — poses a catastrophic intelligence risk. John Wilkes Booth, as Covert Operations Director, is tasked with eliminating this risk.

## Plan and Execution

### Locating Collaborators

LETF intelligence agencies deploy across annexed territories and neutral nations, using advanced surveillance technology and covert networks to track operatives linked to the Silent Fade assassination campaign. The goal is both to find resistance collaborators who enabled the operation and to identify where captured LETF leaders are held.

### Infiltrating GLA Facilities

LETF special forces led by John Wilkes Booth infiltrate high-security U.S., Austrian, and Swedish facilities. The operation requires falsified credentials, compromised insiders, and multi-stage infiltration protocols developed specifically for this mission.

### Assassinating Captured Leaders

- **[[Magnus Maximus]]** — Assassinated post-interrogation at a classified U.S. black site; the killing is staged to appear as a security failure
- **[[King Darius Morgan]]** — Eliminated at an undisclosed Swedish-held location; his death ends the LETF's formal religious leadership line

### Cover-up

LETF agents plant evidence implicating neutral nations — specifically **Switzerland** and **Boombayah** — in the assassination of the detained leaders, framing them as having collaborated with the GLA's infiltration teams. The planted evidence creates international confusion and diplomatic crises for both neutral states.

## Outcome

**All captured LETF leaders eliminated:** The intelligence risk is neutralized. Magnus Maximus and King Darius Morgan cannot be further interrogated.

**Diplomatic destabilization:** The framing of Switzerland and Boombayah creates immediate international crises. Both nations vehemently deny involvement. The LETF uses the planted evidence to justify future aggressive action against Switzerland ([[Operation Alpine Fade]]).

**Resistance movements:** Take heavy losses as LETF forces simultaneously pursue resistance network members across occupied territories in [[Operation Shadow Purge]]; however, underground cells remain active.

## Global Reactions

- **United Nations**: Calls for international investigation into the assassinations of LETF detainees in GLA custody; efforts blocked by LETF-aligned factions
- **GLA (Austria, Sweden, U.S.)**: Increase covert funding and support for resistance movements in response; ramp up anti-LETF operational planning
- **Switzerland**: Denies all LETF accusations; the diplomatic crisis contributes to LETF justification for [[Operation Alpine Fade]]
- **Boombayah**: Strongly condemns the framing; offers expanded sanctuary for resistance leaders

## Legacy

Operation Shadow Recall is the LETF's last successful intelligence operation of the era. By eliminating Magnus Maximus and King Darius Morgan, it prevents further GLA exploitation of LETF internal knowledge — but it also permanently closes the door on any negotiated resolution. The framing of Switzerland triggers the Alpine Fade campaign, pulling even more resources into an already overextended empire. And the assassination of the two most legitimizing figures of the original LETF leadership structure (the supreme military commander and the divine religious authority) accelerates the symbolic collapse of the empire's ideological foundations.
