**Operation Shadow Purge** is the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]'s internal security campaign launched in 1963 as part of [[Operation Eternal Fade]], following [[Operation Silent Fade]]'s elimination of all LETF leadership. Led by [[John Wilkes Booth (Fictional LETF Role)]] — the newly appointed Covert Operations Director — the campaign aims to dismantle resistance networks across LETF-occupied territories and remove anyone suspected of involvement in or sympathy with the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]]'s covert operations.

## Background

After [[Operation Silent Fade]] decapitates the LETF's entire leadership, the LETF's new leadership roster correctly identifies that resistance networks worldwide were emboldened by the event and are now operating with greater coordination. John Wilkes Booth is specifically assigned the counter-resistance portfolio, deploying the LETF Security Apparatus across all occupied territories.

## Actions

### Surveillance and Intelligence

- Advanced drones and LETF operatives deployed to track resistance networks
- Focus on uncovering connections in major occupied territories: India, Brazil, Canada, and the former Soviet resistance zones
- [[Golden Lemons Intelligence Network]] assets identified and targeted

### Neutralization Teams

Specialized LETF units led by John Wilkes Booth conduct targeted strikes against suspected resistance members and collaborators across occupied territories.

### Propaganda Blitz

A LETF media campaign frames resistance members as enemies of global unity, rallying occupied populations around the narrative that resistance destabilizes peace and prosperity. Citizens are encouraged to report suspected resistance activity.

### Mass Arrests and Purges

Cities in annexed territories experience widespread purges. Suspected collaborators are sent to LETF-controlled labor facilities. Resistance hubs in major cities — London, Berlin, New Delhi — are systematically dismantled.

## Outcome

**Resistance networks are severely weakened in the short term** — key cells in major urban centers are disrupted and multiple resistance leaders are captured or killed.

**However, repression backfires strategically:** the mass arrests and purges increase public resentment across occupied territories, particularly in cities already suffering under LETF administration. Resistance recruitment accelerates in rural and suburban areas as displaced urbanites join underground networks.

**Neutral nation reaction**: Countries like Switzerland and Boombayah offer refuge to defectors and resistance leaders, creating sanctuary zones outside LETF reach.

## Global Reactions

- **Austria, Sweden, U.S.**: Remain covertly involved in supporting resistance movements despite LETF countermeasures; Operation Shadow Purge intensifies rather than reduces their commitment to the counter-LETF pivot
- **United Nations remnants**: Debates sanctions or military intervention; internal divisions prevent decisive action
- **Resistance Movements**: Adopt guerrilla tactics focused on disrupting LETF supply chains and spreading anti-LETF propaganda; the purge makes urban operations riskier but pushes resistance into harder-to-disrupt rural and remote networks

## Legacy

Operation Shadow Purge partially succeeds in its immediate objectives — resistance is disrupted and its urban infrastructure damaged. But it fails at its strategic objective: eliminating the resistance movement. The purge actually accelerates the decentralization of resistance networks, making them harder to target and more geographically distributed. By the time [[Operation Iron Dawn]] launches, resistance movements across former Soviet territories are arguably more capable than before the purge, having adapted their operational security in response to LETF surveillance methods.
