**Operation Silent Halo** is a [[United States of America|U.S.]] covert operation in 1962 to secretly fund and arm Chinese resistance forces in the aftermath of [[Operation Jade Suppression]]. The operation has two simultaneous objectives: strengthening Chinese civilian defenders (particularly around hospital complexes) and framing [[Iran]] as the source of weapons reaching Chinese resistance, thereby deflecting scrutiny from U.S. involvement and creating a diplomatic crisis for a nation the LETF has been eyeing as a target.

## Background

The U.S. is formally allied with the LETF through the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]] but has been increasingly alarmed by the LETF's conduct — the China genocide, the Romania nuclear strikes, and the Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign all represent escalations that strain U.S. political will to remain openly supportive. Operation Silent Halo allows the U.S. to support resistance against the LETF's most visible atrocity without formally breaking GLA commitments.

The operation is based out of U.S. positions in Taiwan and South Korea, which serve as logistics hubs for arms and training delivery into mainland China.

## Plan and Execution

### Funding and Arming Resistance

U.S. intelligence agents provide Chinese resistance forces with:
- Weapons and ammunition
- Communications gear and encrypted radio equipment
- Medical supplies for hospital defense operations

Resistance fighters receive covert training from U.S. operatives stationed in Taiwan and South Korea.

### Framing Iran

U.S. propaganda machinery attributes the weapons reaching Chinese resistance to Iranian sources, citing evidence of Iranian-made military equipment (planted by U.S. operatives) in resistance hands. The disinformation is carefully seeded through international intelligence channels to reach LETF analysts.

## Outcome

**Resistance strengthened:** Chinese forces defending hospitals manage to hold key locations against LETF attacks, creating functional humanitarian enclaves within the occupied Sino Fade Provinces.

**Iran under scrutiny:** Tehran faces global suspicion, vehemently denying U.S. accusations and calling for an international investigation. The LETF accuses Iran of supporting resistance, which contributes to Iran eventually becoming a target in the [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]].

**GLA tensions elevated:** The LETF accuses the U.S. of indirect involvement; while U.S. deniability holds, Sweden and Austria note the operation as evidence of the alliance's internal fracture.

## Global Reactions

- **Iran**: Outrage; demands international investigation; denies all involvement
- **LETF**: Accuses the U.S. of hypocrisy; strains the alliance relationship
- **GLA** (Austria, Sweden): Quietly acknowledge the U.S.'s increasing discomfort with LETF conduct; coordinate their own growing disengagement
- **China (Resistance)**: Resistance cells in hospital zones survive longer than expected, providing a morale boost and a model for the broader resistance network

## Legacy

Operation Silent Halo is the U.S.'s first covert action directly aimed at constraining LETF operations rather than supporting them. It is a precursor to the full break that comes with [[Operation Silent Fade]] the following year. The framing of Iran also contributes to the LETF's decision to include Iran in the Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign target list — an unintended consequence of U.S. disinformation that further strains the GLA.
