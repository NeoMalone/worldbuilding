**Operation Northern Fury** is [[Sweden]]'s invasion of France in 1961, conducted simultaneously with the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]'s campaign against Jordan ([[Operation Fade's Crossing]]). The [[United States of America]] provides substantially heavier military support than it offered in the Syrian campaign — deploying navy, bombers, and amphibious landing craft at greater strength — while Sweden leads the ground campaign. The operation is the defining offensive action of Sweden's early-game strategic posture and results in the [[Treaty of Paris]], which formally divides France and establishes Swedish territorial holdings in Europe.

## Background

Sweden had chosen domestic stability in an earlier decision point — focusing on cancer vaccine production and infrastructure. With that foundation secured, Sweden turns to military expansion in Europe. France is identified as a target for Swedish continental leverage, and the United States — which has consistently backed GLA members' individual campaigns — agrees to provide substantial air and naval support.

## Plan and Execution

### Phase 1: Air Superiority

- **Paris** — Bombed by U.S. B-52 Stratofortresses, crippling French infrastructure and communications networks
- **Marseille** — Targeted by U.S. naval forces to destroy coastal defenses
- **Lyon** — Swedish air force neutralizes French air defenses in the southeast

### Phase 2: Amphibious Assaults

**Normandy:**
U.S. Marines land on the northern coast of France and secure the landing zones, establishing a staging ground for Swedish ground forces to move south toward Paris.

**Marseille:**
U.S. naval forces provide cover for Swedish troops landing in southern France, establishing a second front from the Mediterranean coast.

### Phase 3: Ground Invasion

**Northern Push (Swedish Forces):**
Swedish mechanized infantry advance from Normandy toward Paris, engaging in heavy urban combat as they push through the French heartland.

**Southern Push (Joint U.S.-Swedish Forces):**
Troops advance from Marseille northward, capturing **Lyon** and cutting French military coordination in the southeast. Lyon becomes a key logistical hub for the campaign.

**Paris:**
The city falls after intense urban fighting. Swedish forces raise their flag over the Eiffel Tower — the most visible single symbolic act of the operation and a major propaganda event for the Golden Lemons Alliance.

## Outcome

| **Territory** | **Controlling Power** | **Status** |
|---|---|---|
| Paris and northern France | Sweden | Occupied; later formalized through Treaty of Paris |
| Marseille and Mediterranean coast | U.S. (transferred to Sweden) | Strategic coastal zone |
| Lyon | Sweden | Logistical hub |

France is fully occupied. Scattered French resistance movements form, but their impact is minimal against the combined Swedish-U.S. military presence. The occupation is later formalized through the [[Treaty of Paris]], which divides French territory and establishes the exact terms of Swedish governance.

## Global Reactions

- **United Nations**: Condemns the invasion and occupation but its authority continues to erode under GLA dominance.
- **USSR**: Mobilizes forces along its borders; the combination of Swedish conquest of France and the LETF's sweep through the Middle East convinces Soviet leadership that the GLA represents an existential threat.
- **NATO**: Destabilized. The occupation of a NATO member by GLA forces without any collective NATO response accelerates the dissolution of the alliance.
- **China**: Issues protests against U.S. involvement but focuses on internal stability.

## Legacy

Operation Northern Fury is the single action that most firmly establishes Sweden as a major military power within the Golden Lemons Alliance rather than merely a medical and humanitarian contributor. The fall of Paris becomes the most iconic image of GLA expansion in 1961. It also directly precedes the [[Treaty of Paris]], which formalizes Swedish European holdings and creates a model for GLA territorial administration of conquered states.

Sweden later uses Corsica — acquired through [[Operation Nordic Dawn]] following the French campaign — as a Mediterranean naval outpost, demonstrating that the strategic gains of Northern Fury extend beyond France itself.
