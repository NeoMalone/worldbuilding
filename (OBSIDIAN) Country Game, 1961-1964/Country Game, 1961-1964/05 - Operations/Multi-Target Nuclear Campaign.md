The **Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign** is the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]'s 1962 coordinated nuclear strike against eleven nations simultaneously, executed as a precursor softening action for [[Operation Total Fade]]. Each targeted nation receives one nuclear strike on a major city, followed by conventional air raids and bomber campaigns. It is the LETF's largest single act of nuclear aggression and the event that most directly causes Sweden and Austria to begin the process of building the counter-alliance that eventually executes [[Operation Silent Fade]].

## Background

Following [[Operation Jade Suppression]], the LETF compiles a list of nations providing backlash or harboring resistance: the United Kingdom, Canada, Iran, Australia, Brazil, India, West Germany, South Korea, Indonesia, Saudi Arabia, and Hungary. Rather than invade each nation separately, the LETF opts for simultaneous nuclear strikes to demoralize all targets at once, followed by conventional campaigns.

## Consequences

**Immediate:** Massive civilian and military casualties across all targeted nations. Infrastructure, cultural landmarks, and government facilities destroyed. Nuclear fallout creates long-term environmental and health crises in each affected region.

**Resistance:** Nations with surviving leadership begin coordinating counterattacks. Global coalitions begin forming among surviving neutral states.

## Global Reactions

- **United Nations**: Declares the campaign "the greatest violation of international law in history"; cannot take direct action
- **Austria and Sweden**: Distance themselves politically from LETF nuclear actions but remain formally allied for strategic reasons — this moment marks the point of no return for GLA member alienation
- **United States**: Condemns the use of nuclear weapons despite being an ally; raises serious internal tensions within the GLA

## Legacy

The Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign is the most extreme single LETF action of the 1961–1964 era. It softens eleven nations for [[Operation Total Fade]]'s simultaneous annexation campaign, but at the cost of permanently alienating three of the four GLA members. The political fallout from this campaign is the direct background to Austria, Sweden, and the U.S. beginning classified planning for what eventually becomes [[Operation Silent Fade]] — the operation that eliminates LETF leadership and initiates the empire's collapse.
