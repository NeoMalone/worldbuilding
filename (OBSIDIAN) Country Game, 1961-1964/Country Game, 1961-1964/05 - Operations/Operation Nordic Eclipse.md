**Operation Nordic Eclipse** is [[Sweden]]'s full-scale annexation of East Germany in 1962, conducted simultaneously with the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]'s invasion of China ([[Operation Jade Fade]]). Launched at the same time Austria imposes a 90% tariff on LETF imports — the first major sign of Golden Lemons Alliance internal tension — the operation demonstrates Sweden's independent military ambitions in Europe, separate from the LETF's global expansion arc.

## Background

By 1962, Sweden controls mainland France (via the [[Treaty of Paris]]) and Corsica ([[Operation Nordic Dawn]]). East Germany, already isolated by the collapse of NATO and the Soviet Union, is identified as the next logical target for Swedish Central European consolidation. It borders Poland (partially under LETF occupation), Austria's territories, and the remnants of West Germany — making its annexation a natural extension of Sweden's European holdings.

## Plan and Execution

### Phase 1: Air and Ground Assaults

The Swedish Air Force secures air superiority, bombing military bases in **Leipzig** and **Dresden**. Swedish mechanized infantry advance through northern and southern entry corridors simultaneously:

- Northern corridor: forces advance toward **Berlin**
- Southern corridor: forces target **Erfurt** and the Thuringian basin

### Phase 2: Urban Combat

- **Berlin** — Falls after prolonged fighting; the former divided capital sustains significant infrastructure damage during the Swedish assault
- **Leipzig** — Captured quickly under heavy Swedish bombardment
- **Dresden** — Secured after combined air and ground pressure
- **Erfurt** — Taken with moderate resistance

### Phase 3: Occupation and Control

Swedish forces establish control over all major cities. Minor resistance in rural areas is suppressed. Former East German military personnel are disarmed and the state apparatus is dissolved.

## Outcome

East Germany is fully annexed and declared the **Germanic Province of Sweden**. Swedish dominance in Central Europe grows substantially — Sweden now holds an unbroken territorial arc from Scandinavia through France, Corsica, and now central Germany.

## Global Reactions

- **NATO**: Condemns the invasion but lacks the power to intervene; NATO's dissolution process accelerates.
- **West Germany**: Deeply alarmed; West Germany's position becomes increasingly precarious with Sweden now bordering it from the east.
- **LETF**: Notes Sweden's growing territorial holdings with mixed feelings — Sweden is becoming a more powerful partner but also a more independent one, as evidenced by Austria's simultaneous tariff imposition.

## Legacy

Operation Nordic Eclipse completes Sweden's European land empire. Combined with France, Corsica, and Nordic territories, Sweden by 1962 is the dominant power in Western and Central Europe — a status unprecedented in modern Scandinavian history. The operation also signals the maturing of the [[S-61 Arctic Phantom]] as an operational platform, with Swedish stealth bombers providing precision air support throughout the campaign.

The annexation of East Germany creates immediate friction with West Germany, which later becomes one of the contested front states as GLA internal tensions evolve.
