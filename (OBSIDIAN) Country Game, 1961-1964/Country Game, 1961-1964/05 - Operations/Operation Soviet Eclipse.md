**Operation Soviet Eclipse** is the decisive 1962 campaign in which the entire [[Golden Lemons Alliance]] — the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]], [[Austria]], [[Sweden]], and the [[United States of America]] — mobilizes for a total war against the [[USSR]], completing its conquest and ending the Soviet Union's existence as a state. It follows the partial conquest and conditional surrender reached in [[Operation Crimson Fade]] (1961), which the LETF terminates when it determines the USSR is too weakened to resist total annexation. The fall of the USSR marks the final collapse of both NATO and the United Nations as functioning international bodies.

## Background

The 1961 Conditional Surrender between the LETF and USSR gave the Soviets a decade of nominal non-aggression. The LETF breaks this agreement in 1962, citing the USSR's remaining military capacity as an unacceptable threat. The full Golden Lemons Alliance mobilizes — Sweden's newly operational [[S-61 Arctic Phantom]] stealth bombers provide a new strategic air capability, while U.S. Pacific forces prepare an eastern amphibious assault.

## Plan and Execution

### Phase 1: Air Superiority

**LETF Air Force (Su-24s and MiG-21s):**
Target major Soviet airbases across Moscow, Novosibirsk, and Vladivostok. Tactical nuclear weapons held in reserve.

**U.S. Air Force (B-52 Stratofortresses and F-100 Super Sabres):**
Bomb strategic industrial hubs in Yekaterinburg and Omsk.

**Swedish [[S-61 Arctic Phantom]] Stealth Bombers:**
Deployed for stealth raids on radar installations and Soviet military headquarters near Moscow and St. Petersburg.

### Phase 2: Four-Front Ground Invasion

**Western Push (LETF from Ukraine):**
LETF mechanized divisions push from the Western Province of the Fade toward Moscow, engaging Soviet defenses at Kursk and Smolensk.

**Northern Push (Sweden and Austria):**
Swedish and Austrian forces invade from Finland and Poland, targeting **St. Petersburg** and **Murmansk**.

**Central Push (LETF and U.S. from Kazakhstan):**
Combined forces advance from Kazakhstan and southern LETF territories into **Novosibirsk** and **Almaty**, securing the five remaining Kazakhstani provinces.

**Eastern Push (U.S. Pacific Forces):**
U.S. Marines and naval forces launch amphibious assaults near **Vladivostok**, securing the Soviet Far East.

### Phase 3: Tactical Strikes and Urban Combat

- **Moscow** — Encircled by LETF and Austrian forces; prolonged urban combat; falls to combined pressure
- **St. Petersburg** — Falls to Swedish mechanized infantry after heavy resistance
- **Kazakhstan SSR** — All five provinces secured by LETF forces

## Key Territorial Gains

| **Region** | **Territory** | **Fate** |
|---|---|---|
| Western USSR | Moscow, St. Petersburg | LETF — Muscovite Province of the Fade |
| Central USSR | Yekaterinburg, Omsk, Novosibirsk | LETF industrial absorption |
| Eastern USSR | Vladivostok | U.S. strategic military bases |
| Kazakhstan SSR | All 5 remaining provinces | LETF — Central Steppe Province |
| Byelorussian SSR (Brest) | Brest Province | Austria — bilateral agreement |
| Rest of Byelorussian SSR | Minsk and surroundings | LETF — Belarusian Province of the Fade |

**Territorial allocation (post-campaign bilateral agreement):**
Austria accepts only the Brest Province of the Byelorussian SSR. Sweden secures strategic northern territories including Murmansk. The United States establishes military bases in eastern Russia and former Soviet satellite states without annexing territory.

## Outcome

The USSR formally surrenders after the fall of Moscow, ending its existence as a global superpower. Soviet territories are reorganized by the LETF into Fade Provinces for administrative purposes.

**Casualties:** LETF and allies suffer moderate losses, particularly in Moscow urban combat. The USSR suffers devastating losses with the majority of military and civilian infrastructure destroyed.

## Global Reactions

- **United Nations**: Disbands entirely — the fall of the USSR marks the final blow to the U.N.'s ability to function as a global mediator.
- **NATO**: Collapses. Member states either fall to the Golden Lemons or abandon the organization.
- **China**: Becomes the last significant independent power; adopts a policy of fortified neutrality.

## Legacy

Operation Soviet Eclipse is the most consequential single campaign of the 1961–1964 era. It transforms the LETF from a dominant regional power into an unchallenged global hegemon, controlling the vast majority of Eurasia. The simultaneous collapse of NATO and the UN creates an international vacuum the LETF exploits through [[Operation Total Fade]] — an immediate multi-continent annexation wave that stretches LETF logistics to breaking point and plants the seeds of the 1963 rollback. Sweden's first combat deployment of the [[S-61 Arctic Phantom]] in this operation validates the investment in Project Arctic Shadow.
