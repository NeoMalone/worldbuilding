**Operation Anatolian Fade** is the joint [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]] and [[United States of America]] campaign to conquer Turkey in 1961. It is one of the most strategically significant operations of the year: the U.S. expels Turkey from NATO before the invasion, politically isolating the country and preventing any collective defense response. The operation is also notable for its scale — a simultaneous four-axis campaign involving eastern, central, western, and coastal approaches — making it the largest LETF military operation to date. The conquest of Turkey causes shockwaves through NATO, accelerating the alliance's dissolution.

## Background

After securing Iraq, the LETF turns immediately north to Turkey. Turkey's location at the strategic junction of Europe and Asia, its control of the Bosporus strait, and its status as a NATO member make it both a prized objective and the most politically sensitive target the LETF has yet attacked. The United States resolves the political problem by expelling Turkey from NATO before the campaign, stripping it of treaty protection.

## Plan and Execution

### Phase 1: Air Superiority and Bombardments

**LETF Air Force (Su-24s):**
- **Ankara** — Government buildings and military bases destroyed
- **Istanbul** — Communications hubs and defenses neutralized
- **Izmir** — Coastal defenses weakened

**U.S. Air Force (B-52 Stratofortresses and F-105 Thunderchiefs):**
Provides additional air support, crippling Turkish air defenses across the country.

### Phase 2: Naval Blockade and Amphibious Assaults

U.S. carrier strike groups establish a naval blockade of both the Aegean Sea and the Mediterranean, cutting Turkey off from external aid.

**Amphibious landings:**
- **Southern Coast (Mersin and Antalya)** — LETF forces supported by U.S. Marines land and secure southern coastal cities
- **Western Coast (Izmir)** — U.S. Navy lands mechanized divisions, establishing a western foothold

### Phase 3: Ground Invasion

**Eastern Push (LETF from Iraq):**
LETF mechanized divisions cross the Iraqi border, capturing **Van** and advancing westward across eastern Anatolia.

**Central Push (Joint Forces from Syria):**
Combined forces drive through **Gaziantep** and **Diyarbakir**, advancing toward Ankara.

**Western Push (U.S. Focus):**
U.S.-led forces from the Izmir beachhead secure **Bursa**, closing in on Istanbul from the west.

### Phase 4: Urban Combat

**Ankara** — Intense street battles as LETF forces from east and central converge on the Turkish capital. Significant casualties on both sides before the city falls.

**Istanbul** — Heavy fighting as U.S. and LETF forces cross the Bosporus strait and secure both the European and Asian sides of the city.

## Outcome

| **Territory** | **Outcome** |
|---|---|
| Eastern Turkey (Van, Diyarbakir) | First to fall; LETF eastern flank secured |
| Central Turkey (Gaziantep, Ankara, Konya) | Captured after prolonged fighting |
| Western Turkey (Izmir, Bursa, Istanbul) | Taken by combined U.S.-LETF forces |
| Bosporus Strait | Secured by LETF; strategic waterway now under LETF control |

Turkey is declared the **Turkish Province of the Fade**. Casualties are the heaviest of any 1961 LETF campaign, reflecting the scale and urban intensity of the operation.

## Global Reactions

- **United Nations**: Outrage; the conquest of Turkey — and its prior expulsion from NATO by one of NATO's own members — is condemned as a fundamental violation of international law. No effective action taken.
- **NATO**: Panic and collapse pressure. The fall of Turkey after the U.S. itself stripped it of NATO protection destroys the credibility of the collective defense guarantee. Remaining NATO members begin fortifying independently.
- **USSR**: The Soviet Union amasses troops near its southern borders; the LETF now holds a land frontier extending from Egypt through the entire Middle East and Anatolia to the Caucasus.

## Legacy

Operation Anatolian Fade is the watershed moment of the 1961 campaign season. The LETF's control of Istanbul and the Bosporus gives it authority over the strategic waterway connecting the Black Sea to the Mediterranean — transforming it from a Middle Eastern empire into a transcontinental one. The U.S. decision to expel Turkey from NATO before the invasion becomes the first significant crack in public perception of the Golden Lemons Alliance as a stabilizing force.
