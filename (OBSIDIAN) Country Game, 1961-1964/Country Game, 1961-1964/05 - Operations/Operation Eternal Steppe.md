**Operation Eternal Steppe** is the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]'s annexation of Mongolia in 1962. Launched as part of the LETF's broader 1962 Asian expansion arc, the operation aims to close the gap between the LETF's Central Steppe Province (Kazakhstan) and its Pacific-facing territories, establishing a continuous land empire across Central and East Asia. The operation is notable for a parallel [[Austria|Austrian]] special forces mission — codenamed **Operation Eternal Legacy** — to protect the tomb of Genghis Khan from the conflict.

## Background

Following [[Operation Soviet Eclipse]], the LETF controls vast swaths of Central Asia but lacks the Asian land bridge needed to connect its Kazakhstan territories with its Mongolian-border approaches to China. Mongolia, with a sparse population and limited military capacity, is identified as an ideal corridor conquest. [[Magnus Maximus]] commands the operation with direct oversight from [[King Darius Morgan]] (who blesses troops before deployment) and [[Adolf Hitler (Fictional LETF Role)|Adolf Hitler]] (who manages logistics).

Austria, aware of the operation's planned routes through the Khentii Mountains, negotiates permission to deploy a special forces unit to protect the Genghis Khan tomb complex — a site Austria regards as a historical treasure. The LETF respects the agreement, avoiding the tomb's immediate vicinity.

## Plan and Execution

### Phase 1: Airstrikes and Bombardments

LETF Su-24 bombers target Mongolian military bases in:
- **Ulaanbaatar** — capital city; command and control targeted
- **Erdenet** — northern industrial center
- **Choibalsan** — eastern military hub

Rocket artillery stationed near the Central Steppe Province border delivers heavy bombardments, neutralizing key infrastructure.

### Phase 2: Ground Assault

**Northern Push:**
LETF mechanized infantry cross into Mongolia from the Central Steppe Province, targeting **Erdenet** and **Darkhan** in the north.

**Southern Push:**
Armored divisions advance toward **Ulaanbaatar**, engaging Mongolian forces in urban combat. LETF's superior armor and air support overwhelms Mongolian defenders.

### Phase 3: Strategic Encirclement

LETF special forces secure mountain passes and supply routes in the Khentii Mountains and central highlands, cutting off Mongolian resistance forces in remote regions. Austria's special forces simultaneously operate in the Khentii Mountains to secure the Genghis Khan tomb complex, with minor skirmishes occurring at the perimeter before LETF forces honor the agreed exclusion zone.

## Outcome

| **Territory** | **Outcome** |
|---|---|
| Ulaanbaatar | Captured after fierce urban fighting; LETF administrative capital |
| Erdenet | Secured; key mining and industrial center |
| Darkhan | Secured; northern anchor of occupation |
| Western Mongolia | Falls quickly; sparse population, minimal resistance |

Mongolia is declared the **Mongolian Province of the Fade**. LETF casualties are light due to overwhelming military superiority; Mongolia suffers heavy losses and significant infrastructure damage.

Austria's special forces successfully secure the Genghis Khan tomb, earning international recognition for its commitment to preserving cultural heritage — a rare humanitarian note amid the broader LETF expansion campaign.

## Global Reactions

- **United Nations**: Commends Austria for preserving the tomb; condemns the LETF's invasion of Mongolia.
- **China**: Deeply alarmed — the LETF now borders China from the north (Mongolia), the west (Kazakhstan), and potentially the south (Vietnam). China begins fortifying its borders against LETF approach vectors.
- **Austria**: Receives global acclaim for the tomb protection mission, bolstering its diplomatic reputation while remaining embedded in the LETF-led alliance.

## Legacy

Operation Eternal Steppe completes the LETF's Central Asian arc. With Mongolia annexed, the LETF has an unbroken land front along China's entire northern and western border — making [[Operation Jade Fade]] (the China invasion) a logical and inevitable next step. The Austrian tomb protection mission establishes a diplomatic precedent for GLA members pursuing cultural-preservation initiatives independent of LETF military objectives, foreshadowing the growing autonomy within the alliance.
