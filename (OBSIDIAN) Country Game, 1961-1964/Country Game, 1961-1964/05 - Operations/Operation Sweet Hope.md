**Operation Sweet Hope** is [[Austria]]'s 1962 humanitarian mission to bake and personally deliver 10,000 oatmeal raisin cookies to the world's largest orphanage, located in **Mumbai, India**. Conducted while the LETF is prosecuting the invasion of Mongolia ([[Operation Eternal Steppe]]) and the People's Republic of La Plata is receiving LETF nuclear weapons, Operation Sweet Hope is Austria's deliberate counterpoint to the dominant military tone of the era — an act of goodwill that earns Austria rare positive international recognition at a moment when Golden Lemons Alliance members are universally condemned for territorial expansion.

## Background

Austria, though embedded within the Golden Lemons Alliance, has consistently sought to maintain a humanitarian dimension to its international activities. Following the Genghis Khan tomb protection mission during Operation Eternal Steppe, Austria's government decides to conduct a second high-profile humanitarian gesture — this time entirely civilian in nature. The target is the world's largest orphanage in Mumbai, identified by Austrian diplomatic staff as a location where a gesture of this kind would have maximum goodwill impact.

The operation is approved at the highest levels of the Austrian government and coordinated with military logistics to ensure the cookies arrive fresh and in good condition.

## Execution

### Production

10,000 oatmeal raisin cookies are baked in Austrian military kitchens — repurposed from their normal function for the duration of the project. Austrian bakers produce batches continuously to meet the volume requirement.

### Delivery

The cookies are transported via humanitarian aid flights to Mumbai. Austrian diplomats and soldiers personally deliver the cookies to the orphanage, distributing them directly to children and staff. The mission is conducted with full media access, allowing international press coverage.

## Outcome

Austria receives **widespread global acclaim** for the humanitarian effort, improving its reputation at a time when GLA-associated nations are broadly condemned. The gesture is described internationally as a demonstration of Austria's commitment to goodwill amidst global tensions and earns Austria positive diplomatic capital that proves useful in subsequent negotiations.

The children and staff of the Mumbai orphanage receive the cookies as a gesture of international care at a moment when the world has experienced years of war, nuclear escalation, and territorial conquest.

## Significance

Operation Sweet Hope is one of the most tonally distinct entries in the 1961–1964 campaign record — a named operation conducted not with bombers or special forces, but with oatmeal raisin cookies and diplomatic flights. It establishes Austria as the GLA member most committed to maintaining moral distinctions in a period when those distinctions are increasingly difficult to draw. The operation foreshadows Austria's eventual pivot against the LETF in 1963.
