The **Council of the Divine Fade** is the governing-symbolic body within the [[Order of Morganism]] — the religious-political institution that translates the Morganist pantheon's mythology into operational guidance for [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]] state administration. The Council formally includes the divine and mythic figures of the Morganist pantheon: [[Vexbolts]] (Goddess of Chaos), [[Gorg Morgan]] (Patron Goddess of Purity), [[Mr. Clean]] (Goddess of Happiness and Lightning), and [[Skibidi Toilet]] (God of War and Fools). Functionally, the Council serves as the institutional mechanism by which the LETF's surreal pantheon lore is converted into actual governance directives — a bureaucratic theological body that gives the empire's absurdist mythology administrative form.

## Composition

The Council includes both living and mythologized figures within the Morganist religious framework:

The membership reflects [[Morganism]]'s distinctive blend of historical figures, mythological deities, and pop-cultural symbols treated as theologically equivalent.

## Function

The Council operates as the bridge between Morganist mythology and state bureaucracy:

- **Doctrinal interpretation**: The Council issues authoritative interpretations of Morganist doctrine on questions where pantheon precedent is unclear or contested
- **Policy translation**: Mythological narratives are translated into practical administrative directives — for example, [[Mr. Clean]]'s role as "Goddess of Happiness and Lightning" produces specific enforcement protocols around the sacred Low Taper Fade hairstyle
- **Ritual authority**: Major LETF ceremonies, including pre-campaign blessings and military parades, derive their religious authority from Council sanction
- **Internal religious order**: The Council adjudicates internal religious disputes within the [[Order of Morganism]] and oversees the broader Morganist priesthood

## Strategic Importance

The Council's existence reflects a defining feature of the LETF's institutional design: the intentional fusion of absurdist religious mythology with totalitarian bureaucratic governance. The Council is the mechanism that allows the empire to take its surreal pantheon seriously as a basis for state administration. Without the Council, Morganism's mythology would remain disconnected from practical governance; with it, every pantheon decision has institutional consequences.

This fusion is also one of Morganism's structural vulnerabilities. The Council depends on the legitimacy of its divine members — and when [[Operation Silent Fade]] (1963) eliminates [[Magnus Maximus]], [[King Darius Morgan]], and the other living members of the pantheon, the Council loses its central authority figures simultaneously.

## Decline After Silent Fade

Following the assassinations of LETF leadership in [[Operation Silent Fade]], the Council attempts to function with replacement figures — including the eclectic new leadership roster from [[Operation Eternal Fade]] ([[King Darius III]], [[Shrek (LETF Symbolic Figure)]], [[Jeffrey Dahmer's Dad (Fictional LETF Role)]], etc.). However, the substituted membership cannot replicate the original pantheon's symbolic authority. The Council continues nominally but loses operational legitimacy as the LETF's broader collapse accelerates.

## Legacy

The Council of the Divine Fade is the institutional embodiment of Morganism's distinctive theological-bureaucratic synthesis. Its existence makes the LETF's surreal religion administratively actionable, and its decline after Silent Fade contributes to the broader collapse of Morganist legitimacy across the empire's occupied territories.
