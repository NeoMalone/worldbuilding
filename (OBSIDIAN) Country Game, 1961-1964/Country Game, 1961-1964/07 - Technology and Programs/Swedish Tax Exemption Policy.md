The **Swedish Tax Exemption Policy** is [[Sweden]]'s 1962 nationwide one-year exemption from income, sales, and corporate taxes — a comprehensive domestic economic stimulus that produces a major consumer spending surge and brings Swedish government approval to record-high levels. The policy operates as Sweden's domestic-stability anchor during the campaign's expansion phase, complementing the country's military operations and providing political support for continued GLA participation.

## Implementation

The policy's design is straightforward but comprehensive:
- **Income tax**: Full exemption for one year
- **Sales tax**: Full exemption for one year
- **Corporate tax**: Full exemption for one year
- **Universal application**: All Swedish citizens and businesses qualify

The policy applies to mainland Sweden and (presumably) Swedish-administered territories, though the implementation specifics in the [[Sweden-France Partition Zone]] and [[Corsica under Sweden]] are not detailed.

## Economic Effects

The policy produces immediate macroeconomic effects:
- **Consumer spending surge**: Removed tax burden on retail transactions drives consumer demand growth
- **Domestic economic boom**: Combined effect of income retention, business cost reduction, and consumer demand creates broad economic activity expansion
- **Multiplier effects**: Initial spending generates secondary economic activity through retail-supplier-producer chains
- **Investment incentive**: Corporate tax exemption supports business expansion and investment

## Political Effects

The political effects mirror the economic effects:
- **Record government approval**: Approval ratings reach campaign-period highs
- **Political stability**: The policy reinforces Swedish domestic political stability during a period of complex international tensions
- **Cover for military operations**: High domestic approval provides political space for Sweden's continued military expansion (Nordic Eclipse, anti-LETF pivot)

## Strategic Function

The Tax Exemption Policy serves multiple strategic functions:

- **Domestic political insurance**: Strong approval ratings give Swedish leadership political cover for the eventual anti-LETF pivot, which carries domestic political risk
- **Economic resilience**: The boom supports continued investment in [[Project Arctic Shadow]] and military operations
- **Counter to LETF narrative**: Demonstrates that GLA member states can produce concrete domestic prosperity outcomes
- **Coordination with IKEA**: The policy works synergistically with the [[IKEA Expansion Program]] — tax-exempt consumer spending supports IKEA expansion economics, while IKEA presence makes the spending surge visible

## Comparison with Operation Citizen's Dividend

The Swedish Tax Exemption Policy parallels Austria's [[Operation Citizen's Dividend]] (also 1962). Both programs use domestic economic generosity to maintain political stability during the campaign's expansion phase:
- **Austria**: One-time $5,000 universal stipend
- **Sweden**: One-year comprehensive tax exemption

The two approaches reflect different policy philosophies (direct cash transfer vs. tax relief) but produce similar political effects (record approval ratings) and similar strategic functions (domestic political cover for international military operations).

## Legacy

The Tax Exemption Policy's one-year duration limits its long-term fiscal impact while maximizing its short-term political effect. The policy's combination of universal scope and time-limited duration represents one of the campaign's clearer examples of strategically calculated domestic economic policy in service of geopolitical objectives.
