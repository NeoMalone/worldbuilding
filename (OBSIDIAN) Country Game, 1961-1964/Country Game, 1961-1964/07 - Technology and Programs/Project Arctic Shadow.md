**Project Arctic Shadow** is [[Sweden]]'s accelerated stealth bomber development program that produces the [[S-61 Arctic Phantom]] — the first operational stealth bomber in the campaign timeline. Initially launched in 1961 with conventional development pacing, the project is massively accelerated in late 1961 and successfully designs, tests, and deploys the S-61 Arctic Phantom by early 1962. The program represents the most consequential single technology development in the GLA's military portfolio and provides Sweden with a decisive strategic asset that shapes the 1962 USSR campaigns and the 1963 anti-LETF operations.

## Development Timeline

- **1961 (initial)**: Project initiated with conventional development pacing as one of multiple Swedish military investments
- **Late 1961**: Project massively accelerated; Swedish leadership commits substantially expanded resources to compress development timeline
- **Early 1962**: S-61 Arctic Phantom completes design, testing, and initial operational deployment
- **March 1962**: Limited operational deployment begins
- **1962**: First combat use during [[Operation Soviet Eclipse]] — stealth raids on Soviet radar installations near Moscow and St. Petersburg

## Acceleration Decision

The late-1961 decision to massively accelerate the project reflects Swedish strategic recognition that:
- Stealth aerial capability would provide decisive advantage in upcoming campaigns
- Faster fielding would extend Sweden's lead over rivals (LETF and any potential future opponents)
- Compressed development costs are justified by operational urgency

## Output: S-61 Arctic Phantom

The project's deliverable is the [[S-61 Arctic Phantom]] — a stealth bomber with:
- Radar-evading angular design
- 5,000-mile operational range
- Precision-guided munition delivery
- Tactical nuclear weapon capability

## Strategic Impact

Arctic Shadow's strategic impact extends beyond Sweden's bilateral position:
- **GLA aerial superiority**: Provides the alliance with stealth capability the LETF cannot match
- **USSR campaign enabler**: Stealth raids during Operation Soviet Eclipse degrade Soviet defensive capability ahead of conventional operations
- **Operation Silent Fade asset**: Swedish stealth bombers provide the air strike capability for the assault on Adolf Hitler's compound in Cairo
- **Operation Iron Dawn asset**: Stealth bombers neutralize LETF air defenses across Moscow, Volgograd, and Kiev

## Legacy

Project Arctic Shadow is the campaign's clearest example of how concentrated military R&D investment can produce strategic advantage. The S-61 Arctic Phantom's role in both the GLA's expansion phase (Soviet campaigns) and the anti-LETF pivot (Silent Fade, Iron Dawn) makes Arctic Shadow one of the most consequential technology programs in the campaign timeline.
