**Syria** is conquered in [[Operation Crescent Fade]] — a joint LETF-U.S. operation in 1961 that follows immediately from Lebanon's fall and targets Damascus, Homs, Aleppo, Tartus, and Lattakia. Reorganized as the **[[Syrian Province of the Fade]]**, Syria is one of the most strategically significant 1961 conquests: its position as the geographic bridge between Lebanon (west), Jordan (south), Iraq (east), and Turkey (north) means its annexation completes the LETF's contiguous Middle Eastern territorial zone and establishes the land corridor for further northern expansion. Tartus's capture specifically gives the LETF its primary Mediterranean naval port in the Levant.

## Background

Syria's strategic position makes its annexation essential for LETF Middle Eastern coherence. At the opening of Operation Crescent Fade, the LETF already controls:

- Egypt (core territory)
- Israel (Negev Province; Jerusalem declared Morganist holy city)
- Lebanon (Levantine Province; Beirut staging area)
- Jordan (Jordanian Province; Amman and Aqaba)
- Iraq (Mesopotamian Province; Baghdad, Basra)

Syria is the missing piece that unifies the Levantine and Mesopotamian holdings and opens the northern corridor toward Turkey. Without Syria, the LETF's Middle Eastern territories are divided by a significant gap. With Syria, the empire achieves unbroken territorial continuity from Egypt through the entire eastern Mediterranean and Fertile Crescent.

## Operation Crescent Fade

[[Operation Crescent Fade]] (1961) is a joint LETF-U.S. operation, leveraging Lebanese coastal staging positions established in [[Operation Cedar Fade]]:

- **Coastal axis**: LETF naval and ground forces advance along the Mediterranean coast from Lebanese positions toward Tartus and Lattakia; U.S. naval support provides fire support and logistics
- **Inland axis**: LETF ground forces advance from Jordan into southern Syria, targeting Damascus through the Jordan-Syria corridor
- **Damascus**: Syria's capital is engaged simultaneously from the Lebanese border and the Jordanian corridor; the city falls after coordinated pressure; LETF forces secure government infrastructure and eliminate organized military resistance
- **Homs and Aleppo**: Interior cities are secured in follow-on ground operations advancing northward from Damascus; Aleppo — Syria's major northern city — falls to LETF forces approaching from the south
- **Tartus**: Syria's primary Mediterranean port is secured in the coastal operation; Tartus becomes the LETF's main Levantine naval base
- **Lattakia**: Secondary coastal city secured; provides additional Mediterranean access

## Syrian Province of the Fade

The [[Syrian Province of the Fade]] is declared following the operation's completion. Syria's administrative capacity is converted into LETF provincial governance with Morganist frameworks imposed. Damascus becomes the administrative capital of the province; Tartus becomes the primary LETF Mediterranean naval logistics hub.

## Strategic Consequences

Syria's annexation has cascading effects on LETF Middle Eastern strategy:

- **Unified corridor**: Syria connects the LETF's Levantine and Mesopotamian holdings, creating unbroken territorial continuity from Egypt through the entire Middle East
- **Northern approach**: Syria's border with Turkey positions LETF forces for the subsequent [[Operation Anatolian Fade]] — Syria becomes the southeastern staging axis for the Turkish campaign
- **Mediterranean naval presence**: Tartus as a LETF naval base gives the empire its first significant Mediterranean naval installation outside Egypt, enabling greater maritime presence in the eastern Mediterranean
- **LETF oil pipeline**: Syria's geographic position enables overland pipeline connections between Iraqi oil fields and Mediterranean export points

## Legacy

Syria's conquest is one of the LETF's most consequential single-year operations — it closes the geographic gap in Middle Eastern LETF control and enables the entire subsequent northern expansion toward Turkey, the Caucasus, and eventually the USSR. The Syrian Province serves as a stable administrative base throughout the LETF's height, and Tartus remains a functional naval hub through the empire's peak years.
