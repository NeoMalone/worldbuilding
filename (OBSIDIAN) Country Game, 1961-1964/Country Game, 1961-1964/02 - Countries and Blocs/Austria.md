**Austria** is one of the four founding members of the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]]. Controlled by the game's father figure, Austria contributes elite ground forces and serves as the GLA's European strategic backbone. Over the course of the campaign, Austria transitions from an aggressive annexationist power to a humanitarian actor and covert opponent of the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]].

## Special Ability

Austria's defining national trait is possessing **the world's most bodybuilders** — a population of uniquely powerful military-age adults whose physical conditioning gives Austrian infantry formations exceptional combat capability. This ability directly underpins the [[Austrian Iron Divisions]], the elite ground-assault formations that lead the majority of Austrian military operations.

## Role in the Golden Lemons Alliance

Austria joins the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]] at formation in February 1961. Its initial role is as the alliance's primary ground combat force, contributing infantry and armored divisions to multi-front operations while [[Sweden]] provides medical support and the [[United States of America]] provides air and naval supremacy.

## Military History

### Japan Campaign (1961)
Austria leads the ground assault on northern Japan during [[Operation Golden Dawn]], advancing Austrian "Iron Divisions" from landing zones on Hokkaido southward through the Tohoku region. Following Japanese defeat, Austria annexes [[North Japan]], renamed the **Austrian Northern Prefecture**.

Key cities taken: Hokkaido, Sendai, Aomori, Niigata, and northern Tokyo districts.

### Other Early Campaigns
Austria provides logistical and ground-force support to LETF campaigns across the Middle East, contributing to the broader [[Golden Lemons Alliance]] war effort. Austria's forces operate primarily in Europe and Asia.

### Czechoslovakia Corridor (1961)
Austria cedes a small piece of southeastern Czechoslovakia near the Slovak border to the LETF, providing the LETF with a land corridor linking its Ukrainian and Caspian provinces. In return, Austria receives favorable trade terms and strengthened alliance ties.

### Brest Province (1962)
Following [[Operation Soviet Eclipse]], Austria negotiates a territorial agreement with the LETF, taking control of **Brest Province** — the westernmost region of the former Byelorussian SSR, bordering Poland. The city of Brest becomes Austria's administrative hub in the region. See [[Brest Province]].

## Technology Programs

### E-12 Sentinel (1961)
Austria receives three **[[E-12 Sentinel]]** reconnaissance aircraft from the United States, significantly expanding its intelligence-gathering capabilities in Europe.

### Project Shadowhawk (1961–1962)
Austria invests **50% of its GDP** into [[Project Shadowhawk]], an ambitious next-generation stealth bomber development program intended to surpass Sweden's [[S-61 Arctic Phantom]]. The resulting **[[Shadowhawk Stealth Bomber]]** is designed to reach Mach 2.5 with a 6,500-mile operational range, carry precision-guided munitions and nuclear warheads, and incorporate advanced radar-absorbing composites and engine cooling systems.

## Domestic and Humanitarian Actions

- **[[Operation Sweet Hope]]** (1962): Austria bakes 10,000 oatmeal raisin cookies and delivers them to the world's largest orphanage in Mumbai, India, earning global humanitarian praise.
- **[[Operation Citizen's Dividend]]** (1962): Austria distributes a one-time **$5,000 stipend** to all Austrian citizens, generating an economic boom and record-high government approval.
- **Protection of Genghis Khan's Tomb** (1962): During the LETF's invasion of Mongolia ([[Operation Eternal Steppe]]), Austria deploys a special forces unit to protect the tomb of Genghis Khan in the Khentii Mountains. Austrian forces successfully secure the tomb from conflict damage, earning international recognition.

## Alliance Tensions

In 1962, Austria imposes a **90% tariff on all LETF-imported goods**, signaling growing internal tension within the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]]. The move reduces LETF's economic access to Austrian markets and signals Austria's increasing distrust of its partner. Sweden supports the tariff.

## Covert Operations (1963)

Austria contributes its elite **Jagdkommando** special forces to [[Operation Silent Fade]], the classified joint operation that assassinates or captures all major LETF leaders. Austrian operatives are personally responsible for the capture of [[Magnus Maximus]] (tranquilized and extracted to a U.S. black site).

Austria also plays a leading role in [[Operation Iron Dawn]], providing ground forces, intelligence assets, and sabotage teams to liberate former USSR territories and restore the Soviet Union.

## Foreign Relations

- **[[Golden Lemons Alliance]]**: Core member; increasingly at odds with LETF escalation from 1962 onward.
- **[[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]**: Initially allied; fractures over tariff, atrocities, and nuclear use; covertly hostile by 1963.
- **[[Czechoslovakia]]**: Allied buffer state; Austria uses Czechoslovakian territory for logistical operations.
- **Poland**: Austria's access through Czechoslovakia provides the invasion corridor for [[Operation Crimson Fade]] against the USSR.
