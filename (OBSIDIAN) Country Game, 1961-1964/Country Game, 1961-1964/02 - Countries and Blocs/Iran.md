**Iran** has a complex and ironic role in the campaign timeline: it is first falsely implicated as a weapons supplier to the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]] through U.S. disinformation planted during [[Operation Silent Halo]], then targeted by the same LETF it was falsely accused of arming in the [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]] and [[Operation Total Fade]]. Declared the **[[Persian Fade Province]]** after annexation, Iran develops strong local resistance rooted in cultural and religious opposition to Morganist imposition and eventually regains independence during the LETF's collapse phase.

## Background

Iran occupies a central strategic position in the campaign's Middle Eastern theater — bordering LETF-controlled Iraq to the west, formerly Soviet territory to the north, and serving as the land corridor between the Persian Gulf and Central Asia. This strategic position makes Iran a natural target for LETF expansion; it also makes Iran a plausible-sounding candidate for the U.S. disinformation campaign that precedes its conquest.

## Operation Silent Halo Framing

[[Operation Silent Halo]] (1962) — the U.S. covert operation supporting Chinese resistance against LETF occupation — includes a deliberate disinformation component that frames Iran as a weapons supplier to LETF forces operating in China. The U.S. plants fabricated intelligence suggesting Iranian arms transfers are enabling the LETF's Chinese suppression campaigns.

This framing serves U.S. strategic purposes by creating diplomatic complications for the LETF in its Middle Eastern territories — forcing the empire to spend political capital denying the Iranian connection — but it has the ironic effect of tarnishing Iran's reputation internationally even as Iran is itself about to be conquered by the LETF.

## Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign

The [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]] (1962) strikes Tehran — Iran's capital and administrative center — as part of the coordinated eleven-nation nuclear package. The Tehran strike eliminates Iranian government command and control infrastructure and reduces organized military defense capacity ahead of the conventional Operation Total Fade invasion.

## Operation Total Fade and Annexation

[[Operation Total Fade]] (1962) advances LETF forces into Iran from multiple directions:

- Western entry from LETF-controlled Iraq across the Iran-Iraq border
- Northern entry through the Caspian corridor from former Soviet territories
- Air and ground operations targeting Isfahan, Shiraz, Tabriz, and Mashhad following the Tehran nuclear strike

Iran is declared the **[[Persian Fade Province]]** under LETF administrative governance. The imposition of Morganist doctrine on Iran — a nation with a deeply rooted Islamic religious identity — generates immediate and persistent cultural resistance that the LETF's propaganda apparatus struggles to overcome.

## Local Resistance

Iran's resistance is driven particularly by cultural and religious opposition to Morganist imposition. The LETF's attempt to replace Islamic religious practice with Morganist doctrine — a theocratic framework with its own divine authority structure centered on [[King Darius Morgan]] — encounters resistance at the community level throughout the occupation:

- Religious networks maintain alternative legitimacy structures outside LETF administrative frameworks
- Local militias organize around cultural and religious identity markers
- Resistance coordination uses pre-existing religious community infrastructure that LETF surveillance cannot fully penetrate
- Geographic diversity — mountain regions in the Zagros and Alborz ranges, desert interior, and coastal zones — provides sanctuary options that LETF garrison forces cannot fully control

## Independence Restoration

During the LETF's collapse phase, Iran's resistance networks expand rapidly as garrison forces are reduced. The combination of strong cultural resistance motivation, organized religious community networks, and geographic depth enables Iran to be among the earlier territories to reassert de facto independence. The restoration of Iranian sovereignty during the collapse phase is driven by local forces rather than external liberation support — a distinction that shapes the post-LETF political character of the restored Iranian state.

## Legacy

Iran's arc in the campaign is one of the more ironic sequences: falsely accused by U.S. disinformation of arming the LETF, then conquered by the LETF, then liberated through its own resistance. The Silent Halo framing adds a layer of geopolitical cynicism to Iran's story — the same power that falsely implicated Iran is the power whose broader counter-LETF strategy eventually helps create the conditions for Iran's liberation.
