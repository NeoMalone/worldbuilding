**Canada** is a North American sovereign state that serves as the last intact major Western Hemisphere nation for an extended period of the campaign, before being targeted in the [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]] (1962) and subsequently annexed during [[Operation Total Fade]]. As the most geographically intact former NATO member following the collapse of European NATO states, Canada functions as a de facto remnant coordination hub for surviving Western resistance and intelligence networks. After annexation as the **Northern Fade Province**, Canadian resistance — operating with support from the United States and [[NATO Remnants]] — eventually retakes significant territory during the LETF's overextension collapse.

## Background

Canada enters the campaign's middle arc in a unique position: it is a GLA-adjacent nation (given its close relationship with the U.S.) but not a GLA member itself. As GLA expansion proceeds through Europe and Asia, Canada remains technically sovereign longer than most major Western nations — the LETF's focus on simultaneous multi-continental expansion means Canada is not targeted until the Operation Total Fade wave.

This extended period of nominal sovereignty makes Canada valuable as a coordination point for NATO remnant networks, intelligence agencies of occupied nations, and resistance movements that have been displaced from Europe. Canadian territory hosts informal meetings and logistical hubs that cannot operate openly in LETF-controlled Europe.

## Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign

The [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]] (1962) strikes Toronto — Canada's largest city and economic center — as part of the coordinated eleven-nation strike. The Toronto strike devastates Canadian economic infrastructure and eliminates centralized government capacity to organize coherent national defense. It is the preparatory strike for conventional invasion.

## Operation Total Fade and Annexation

[[Operation Total Fade]] (1962) moves against Canada as part of its simultaneous multi-continent wave. LETF forces advance into Canadian territory from multiple vectors:

- Cross-border operations from LETF-adjacent U.S. territories
- Naval operations against Atlantic coastal cities
- Rapid ground advances targeting Ottawa, Montreal, and other population centers

Canada is declared the **Northern Fade Province** and placed under LETF administrative governance. Morganist institutional frameworks are imposed, replacing Canadian governmental structures.

## Resistance and NATO Remnant Coordination

Canada's resistance is notable for its organizational coherence compared to other newly occupied territories. Several factors contribute:

- Pre-annexation period allowed Canadian military and intelligence leadership to establish decentralized resistance networks before invasion
- NATO Remnant networks already operating informally from Canada integrate into resistance structures seamlessly
- Geographic scale — Canada's northern and rural regions — provides sanctuary zones analogous to Australia's outback and Brazil's Amazon
- U.S. covert support, which pivots against LETF operations from 1962 onward, channels resources into Canadian resistance networks

The combination of geographic depth, organizational preparation, and external support makes Canadian resistance one of the more capable resistance movements in the Northern Hemisphere during the LETF occupation period.

## Endgame: Territory Retaken

During the collapse phase, Canada's resistance — leveraging U.S. support and NATO remnant coordination — is among the first North American territories to begin active liberation operations. As LETF garrison capacity deteriorates due to overextension-driven resource reallocation, Canadian resistance forces expand from northern and rural sanctuaries into urban areas. The retaking of significant Canadian territory in the collapse phase is one of the defining achievements of the North American resistance arc.

## Legacy

Canada's role in the campaign evolves from extended neutral survival to occupied resistance hub to liberation success story. Its position as the last intact major Western Hemisphere nation before annexation made it an important coordination point; its geographic scale and organized resistance made it difficult to hold; and its eventual partial liberation illustrates how the LETF's overextension created terminal vulnerabilities across all occupied territories simultaneously.
