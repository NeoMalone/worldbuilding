The **United Kingdom** is the last major NATO anchor state conquered by the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]] during [[Operation Total Fade]] (1962). Following a nuclear strike on London as part of the [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]], the United Kingdom is invaded and declared the **[[Anglo Fade Province]]** — the LETF's designation for the British Isles under Morganist governance. The UK's fall is one of the most symbolically significant events of the 1962 expansion arc: it simultaneously completes the LETF's control of Western Europe, destroys any remaining NATO organizational capacity, and eliminates the English-speaking world's primary independent military power outside the United States.

## Background

The United Kingdom enters the 1962 expansion arc as one of the few major Western nations still outside direct LETF control. France has been partitioned under the [[Treaty of Paris]]; West Germany is about to be annexed; the Soviet Union has already fallen. The UK, by geography an island state, would ordinarily benefit from natural defensive advantages — but the LETF's combination of nuclear strike, naval supremacy, and aerial assault eliminates the strategic value of the channel crossing.

The UK's relationship with the United States — a GLA member that provides the air power and naval assets that make UK conquest logistically feasible — means it cannot rely on the Anglo-American alliance that had historically guaranteed British security.

## Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign

The [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]] (1962) strikes London — the UK's capital, largest city, and the center of its governmental, financial, and military command infrastructure — as part of the coordinated eleven-nation nuclear strike package. The London strike is one of the most symbolically charged detonations in the campaign, targeting a city whose historical resonance as the capital of a global empire makes its destruction particularly significant.

The nuclear strike destroys Westminster and central London's governmental district, eliminates military command and control, and creates immediate humanitarian crisis conditions that the UK government cannot manage without external support that is not forthcoming.

## Operation Total Fade and Annexation

[[Operation Total Fade]] (1962) deploys LETF naval and air assets across the North Atlantic and English Channel to support the British Isles invasion:

- Aerial operations neutralize Royal Air Force bases and air defense systems
- LETF naval forces establish Channel and Irish Sea control, isolating the islands
- Amphibious landings on the southern and eastern coasts of England establish beachheads
- Follow-on ground operations secure London (post-nuclear), Manchester, Birmingham, Glasgow, and Edinburgh
- Scotland and Wales are seized alongside England, completing UK territorial annexation

The United Kingdom is declared the **[[Anglo Fade Province]]** under LETF administrative governance. Morganist frameworks are imposed — an imposition that encounters immediate and sustained cultural resistance from populations with strong national identities.

## NATO Dissolution

The UK's annexation completes NATO's functional dissolution. With France partitioned, West Germany about to fall, and the UK under occupation, no major European NATO state remains independent. The UK's fall is the final event that convinces the last NATO organizational remnants — primarily existing as Canadian and scattered European exile networks — that NATO as a functioning alliance is definitively over. The [[Final Collapse Dispatch]] documents UK occupation as concurrent with NATO's formal dissolution.

## Resistance

The UK's resistance draws on several sources:

- **Geographic assets**: Scotland's highlands and rural Wales provide sanctuary zones for resistance networks that LETF forces cannot effectively patrol
- **Institutional memory**: British military and intelligence institutions, with deep organizational culture, produce resistance networks that maintain operational coherence under occupation
- **International connections**: British resistance networks coordinate with the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]]'s covert support channels as Austria, Sweden, and the U.S. pivot to anti-LETF operations
- **Commonwealth connections**: Former Commonwealth nation networks provide external communication and coordination channels

## Collapse Phase

During the [[Final Collapse Dispatch]] period, Anglo Fade Province resistance expands as LETF garrison capacity deteriorates. The UK's collapse phase trajectory — resistance expansion as the empire thins — follows the pattern of other occupied territories, with British resistance networks emerging from rural and underground positions into open confrontation with weakening LETF administrative authority.

## Legacy

The United Kingdom's conquest represents the LETF's maximum reach into the Western Atlantic and the definitive end of the European state system as it had existed. No other event in the campaign so completely symbolizes the destruction of the pre-GLA world order — London's nuclear strike is the campaign's starkest image of the old world's elimination, and the Anglo Fade Province's subsequent resistance represents the refusal of that world to be permanently replaced.
