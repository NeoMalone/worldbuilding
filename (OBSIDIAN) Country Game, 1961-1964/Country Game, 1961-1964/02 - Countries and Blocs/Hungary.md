**Hungary** is a Central European state annexed by the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]] during [[Operation Total Fade]] (1962) following a nuclear strike on Budapest as part of the [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]]. Declared the **[[Danubian Fade Province]]**, Hungary occupies a strategically significant position at the crossroads of LETF-controlled Central Europe and Swedish-administered German territory. Hungary's resistance — eventually supported by Sweden as part of the broader anti-LETF counter-coalition — achieves one of the campaign's notable endgame victories: the retaking of Budapest with Swedish-backed forces.

## Background

Hungary in the early campaign timeline is a Central European state formerly within the Soviet influence sphere that has been watching the progressive collapse of Soviet power through [[Operation Crimson Fade]] and the final elimination of Soviet independence in [[Operation Soviet Eclipse]]. With the USSR gone, Hungary lacks the patron state that had previously guaranteed its political framework. The LETF's expansion into Central Europe through the Operation Total Fade wave targets Hungary as part of its Danubian regional cluster.

## Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign

The [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]] (1962) strikes Budapest — Hungary's capital and largest city — as the preparatory nuclear strike preceding conventional invasion. Budapest's nuclear strike devastates the Hungarian government's command and control infrastructure and eliminates organized conventional military resistance capacity ahead of the LETF ground invasion.

## Operation Total Fade and Annexation

[[Operation Total Fade]] (1962) advances LETF forces into Central Europe as part of the simultaneous eleven-nation annexation campaign. Hungary is seized and declared the **[[Danubian Fade Province]]**:

- LETF forces advance through the Carpathian and Danubian corridor, securing Budapest's remnant urban infrastructure following the nuclear strike
- Secondary cities — Debrecen, Miskolc, Pécs, Győr — are secured in the ground campaign
- Morganist governance frameworks are imposed; Hungarian republican institutions are replaced
- The Danubian Fade Province is positioned as part of the LETF's Central European administrative cluster alongside the Romanian Province ([[Operation Carpathian Fade]]) and Austrian-adjacent territories

## Resistance and Swedish Support

Hungary's resistance acquires a particular strategic character due to Sweden's geographic proximity and its evolving anti-LETF posture. As Sweden pivots from GLA expansion toward the counter-coalition in 1963, it begins providing covert support to resistance movements in territories adjacent to its Germanic Province administration:

- Swedish intelligence and supply networks extend into the Danubian Province, channeling resources to Hungarian resistance fighters
- Hungarian resistance, already organized around Budapest's urban geography, receives tactical guidance and equipment from Swedish Special Operations Group (SOG) assets
- Coordination between Hungarian resistance and Swedish counter-LETF operations becomes one of the Central European resistance success stories of the 1963 arc

## Endgame: Budapest Retaken

The retaking of Budapest is one of the campaign's significant endgame events. Swedish-backed Hungarian resistance forces, leveraging LETF garrison reduction during the overextension collapse, launch coordinated operations to reclaim Budapest. The city's liberation represents:

- A symbolic blow to LETF control of Central Europe — the capital of the Danubian Province falling to resistance
- A validation of Sweden's covert support strategy — demonstrating that Swedish-backed resistance can achieve territorial liberation, not merely disruption
- A contribution to the broader collapse narrative — Budapest's liberation is part of the wave of provincial failures that characterizes the LETF's endgame deterioration

## Legacy

Hungary's arc from nuclear-strike target to occupied province to liberated capital illustrates the campaign's trajectory for occupied Central European states. The Budapest liberation specifically demonstrates how Swedish sponsorship of resistance movements created meaningful endgame outcomes — a notable irony given that Sweden had itself been an aggressive expansionist power during the 1961 arc. Hungary's liberation becomes one of the data points that the [[United Nations Restoration Bloc]] cites when documenting the LETF's collapse.
