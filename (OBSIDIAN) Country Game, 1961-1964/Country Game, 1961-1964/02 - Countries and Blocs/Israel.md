**Israel** is the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]'s first major conquest outside its Egyptian origin territory — the early test case for Morganist ideological expansion beyond the empire's founding state. Targeted in [[Operation Sacred Fade]] (1961) by a joint LETF-U.S.-Sweden operation, Israel's fall gives the LETF control over Jerusalem, which is immediately declared a Morganist holy city. The Negev region is formalized as the **Negev Province of the Fade**. Israel's conquest establishes both the LETF's expansionist pattern and its use of religious framing to justify territorial aggression.

## Background

Israel is the LETF's first campaign target outside Egypt for reasons that are simultaneously strategic and ideological:

- **Strategic**: Israel borders Egypt directly, making it the natural first step in any Egyptian-based expansion into the Levant; its conquest secures the LETF's northern flank before the empire pushes into Lebanon and Syria
- **Ideological**: Jerusalem's religious significance — as a holy city for multiple faiths — makes its capture a powerful symbolic statement for Morganist propaganda; declaring Jerusalem a Morganist holy city asserts the faith's transcendence over prior religious claims to the city

The joint U.S.-Sweden participation in Operation Sacred Fade reflects the GLA's collective interest in neutralizing a non-aligned Middle Eastern state that might complicate regional expansion.

## Operation Sacred Fade

[[Operation Sacred Fade]] (1961) is the three-power invasion of Israel:

- **Aerial operations**: LETF, U.S., and Swedish aircraft target Israeli air defenses, military installations, and government infrastructure; the coordinated air campaign rapidly degrades Israel's capacity to defend urban centers
- **Jerusalem**: The primary objective; LETF ground forces advance on the city from the south (through the Negev) and east (via the Jordan approach); the city falls after intense fighting; LETF commanders immediately declare Jerusalem a Morganist holy site
- **Tel Aviv**: Israel's economic and government center is secured in a coastal operation combining U.S. naval support and LETF ground advance
- **Haifa**: The northern port city falls in a combined operation; Haifa's port infrastructure is secured for LETF logistical use
- **Nazareth, Beersheba, Jericho**: Secondary cities secured in follow-on ground operations; resistance is cleared from the Galilee, Negev, and Jordan Valley regions

## Jerusalem as Morganist Holy City

The declaration of Jerusalem as a Morganist holy city is one of [[Operation Sacred Fade]]'s most significant long-term consequences. The LETF uses Jerusalem's capture as proof that Morganist divine authority supersedes prior religious claims to the city — a propaganda claim that:

- Inflames international religious communities worldwide, generating unprecedented condemnation
- Establishes a template for Morganist "sacralization" of conquered cities that the LETF attempts to apply elsewhere
- Creates a permanent resistance motivation for individuals and communities with prior religious claims to Jerusalem

The UN condemns the Morganist declaration specifically, calling it a violation of international agreements on holy sites.

## Negev Province of the Fade

The Negev region is administratively formalized as the **Negev Province of the Fade** — the LETF's first declared "Province of the Fade" outside Egypt, establishing the naming convention used for all subsequent LETF territorial annexations.

## Legacy

Israel's conquest and the declaration of Jerusalem as a Morganist holy city is one of the campaign's most symbolically charged events. It transforms the LETF from a regional Egyptian power into an ideologically expansionist force making explicitly religious territorial claims — a transformation that reshapes how the UN, resistance movements, and neutral nations perceive the LETF's long-term intentions. The backlash from the Jerusalem declaration contributes to the international condemnation environment that the GLA counter-alliance later exploits as political justification for [[Operation Silent Fade]].
