Fundamental EquationsTSIOLKOVSKY · Isp · T/W · ΔV

PLATE 1.0
EQ 9.1 · TSIOLKOVSKY ROCKET EQUATION
$$ \Delta V = V_{e}\,\ln\!\frac{m_{0}}{m_{f}} = I_{sp}\,g_{0}\,\ln\!\frac{m_{0}}{m_{f}} $$
m₀ initial mass, mf final mass after burn. Mass ratio MR = m₀/mf. Logarithm is the tyrant: doubling ΔV requires squaring MR.
EQ 9.2 · SPECIFIC IMPULSE
$$ I_{sp}=\frac{F}{\dot m\,g_{0}}=\frac{V_{e}}{g_{0}} $$
Seconds. Effective exhaust velocity per unit weight-flow.
EQ 9.3 · MASS FLOW (CHOKED)
$$ \dot m = \frac{P_{c} A^{*}}{\sqrt{T_{c}}}\sqrt{\frac{\gamma}{R/\mathcal M}}\!\left(\frac{2}{\gamma+1}\right)^{\!\frac{\gamma+1}{2(\gamma-1)}} $$
EQ 9.4 · THRUST (general)
$$ F = \dot m\,V_{e} + (P_{e}-P_{a})A_{e} $$
EQ 9.5 · THRUST-TO-WEIGHT RATIO
$$ \frac{T}{W} = \frac{F}{m\,g_{0}} $$
T/W > 1 to lift off vertically. Tactical missiles: 5–30 at burnout-instant. Cruise missiles: ~1.0 (level flight).
EQ 9.6 · BURN TIME
$$ t_{b} = \frac{m_{p}}{\dot m} = \frac{m_{p} I_{sp} g_{0}}{F} $$
EQ 9.7 · GRAVITY LOSS
$$ \Delta V_{g} = \int_{0}^{t_{b}} g(t)\sin\gamma(t)\,dt $$
Cost of fighting gravity during ascent. Steep climb & long burn ⇒ big loss. Solid sprint: small. Ion thruster: enormous (so launched in vacuum).
EQ 9.8 · DRAG LOSS
$$ \Delta V_{d}=\int_{0}^{t_{b}}\frac{D}{m}\,dt $$
EQ 9.9 · NET ΔV
$$ \Delta V_{net} = I_{sp}g_{0}\ln(m_{0}/m_{f}) - \Delta V_{g} - \Delta V_{d} - \Delta V_{steer} - \Delta V_{p} $$
EQ 9.10 · STAGED ROCKET
$$ \Delta V_{tot} = \sum_{i} I_{sp,i}\,g_{0}\,\ln\!\frac{m_{0,i}}{m_{f,i}} $$
Each stage's mass ratio is computed against THAT stage only — dropped tankage means a fresh, favorable ratio for the next stage.
EQ 9.11 · PAYLOAD FRACTION
$$ \pi = \frac{m_{pl}}{m_{0}},\quad \lambda=\frac{m_{s}}{m_{0}-m_{pl}} $$
π payload fraction; λ structural coefficient. Smaller λ ⇒ more performance.
EQ 9.12 · CHARACTERISTIC VELOCITY
$$ c^{*} = \frac{P_{c}A^{*}}{\dot m}\quad\Rightarrow\quad I_{sp}=\frac{C_{F}c^{*}}{g_{0}} $$

Design Couplings

Low-altitude
high q ⇒ heavy structure, big hinge motors, intense aero-heating, but rich air for breathers.
High-altitude
low q ⇒ light wings work, but control surfaces lose authority — switch to TVC/RCS.
Vacuum
only thrust steers; expand nozzle hugely (ε=40–200) to extract every Pa.
Throttle for gravity
if T/W ≫ 1 you waste ΔV in drag; if T/W ≈ 1 you waste it in gravity. Sweet spot T/W ≈ 1.3–1.6 at lift-off.
Max-q
climb profile shaped to keep ρV² bounded; airframe designed to that single load case.
Heating
$\dot q \propto \rho V^{3}$; doubling V is 8× heat. Doubling ρ is 2×.
Range vs altitude
cruise high (low D) for endurance; sea-skim low (radar shadow) for terminal stealth.
BARROW WIND CORRECTION
Cross-wind shifts aim point; missile must "lead" upwind by w·tflight/cos(θ). Big factor on long-range artillery rockets.
GRAVITY-TURN
Pitch program where thrust vector remains aligned with velocity; gravity itself rotates trajectory. Minimizes steering loss; standard for ballistic + orbital.

Aerodynamicsq · LIFT · DRAG · MACH REGIMES

PLATE 3.0
EQ 10.1 · DYNAMIC PRESSURE
$$ q = \tfrac{1}{2}\rho V^{2} $$
Air's "punch" per area. Sets all aero forces, hinge moments, structural loads. Max-q on ascent ≈ 30–35 kPa.
EQ 10.2 · LIFT
$$ L = q\,S\,C_{L} = \tfrac{1}{2}\rho V^{2} S\,C_{L} $$
S reference area (planform). CL grows ≈ linearly with α until stall.
EQ 10.3 · DRAG
$$ D = q\,S\,C_{D} = q\,S\,(C_{D_{0}} + C_{D_{i}} + C_{D_{w}}) $$
Parasite CD₀ + induced CL²/(πARe) + wave drag (M>~0.8).
EQ 10.4 · MACH NUMBER
$$ M = V/a,\quad a=\sqrt{\gamma R T/\mathcal M} $$
Sea-level a ≈ 340 m/s; @ 30 km a ≈ 300 m/s.
EQ 10.5 · REYNOLDS
$$ Re = \rho V L_{ref} / \mu $$
Sets boundary-layer regime (laminar < 5×10⁵; turbulent >).
EQ 10.6 · L/D — RANGE
$$ R_{cruise}=\frac{V}{c_{T}}\,\frac{L}{D}\,\ln\!\frac{m_{0}}{m_{f}}\quad\text{(Breguet)} $$

PLATE 4.0 · 4.1

Functional Sections

A · Seeker
Forward sensor module behind the radome / IR dome. Houses gimballed antenna or optics, gyros, signal pre-amps. Drives the homing loop.
B · Guidance & Avionics
Autopilot, IMU, computer, datalink, mission timer, battery (thermal or Li). Translates seeker error into fin commands.
C · Warhead
Payload section: HE/frag, shaped charge, penetrator, sub-munitions, or inert. Forward of motor to maximize kill geometry.
D · Fuze & S&A
Safe-and-Arm device + initiation logic. Two independent environmental cues required to arm (e.g. setback + spin).
E · Motor / Tankage
Solid grain, liquid bipropellant tanks, or air-breathing combustor. The single largest mass and volume fraction.
F · Wing
Lift surface for cruise and pull-up; fixed or folding. Sometimes absent (purely thrust-vector or fin-borne).
G · Tail / Empennage
Stabilizers + control surfaces (rudder/elevator/elevon). Furthest aft to maximize moment arm about CoM.
H · Nozzle / Exhaust
Convergent-divergent (rocket) or simple convergent (subsonic jet). Optional thrust-vector vanes / gimbal.
+ Skin & Frame
Monocoque or semi-monocoque tube — bears bending, axial thrust, hoop pressure, aero-heating. Materials: aluminum, steel, titanium, composite, ablative liner.
+ Umbilicals
Lugs, launch shoes, ejection rails, fill/drain ports, arming pull-pin. Removed/severed at launch.
⚑ Layout Heuristic
Heavy items (warhead, motor case) ride near the center of mass to keep pitch/yaw inertia low. Sensors lead, propellant fills the middle, exhaust trails. CoM should sit ahead of CoP — see §11.

Nose-Cone GeometriesPROFILES · GENERATING FUNCTIONS · REGIME

PLATE 5.0 — 5.6

Every profile maps a station x ∈ [0, L] along the cone axis to a local radius y(x), where R is the base radius and L the cone length. Fineness ratio f = L / 2R is the single most important shape parameter — high f reduces wave drag at supersonic speed; low f yields a blunt cone that survives heating.

Generating Functions — Reference

Convention: x is distance from tip, L total cone length, R base radius, y(x) the local body-of-revolution radius. All shapes share y(0)=0, y(L)=R.

Conical

EQ 2.1 · CONE
$$ y(x) = \frac{R\,x}{L} \qquad \tan\theta = R/L $$
Half-angle θ. Drag dominated by leading-shock losses; sharp tip survives only at low Mach or with ablative coating.

Tangent Ogive

EQ 2.2 · TANGENT OGIVE
$$ \rho = \frac{R^2 + L^2}{2R} $$ $$ y(x) = \sqrt{\rho^2 - (L-x)^2} \;-\; (\rho - R) $$
ρ is ogive radius; arc tangent to body at the shoulder. Minimum-drag cone-to-cylinder for ~M 1–3 with finesse f ≈ 3–5.

Secant Ogive

EQ 2.3 · SECANT OGIVE
$$ y(x)=\sqrt{\rho^2-(L-x)^2}\;-\;\sqrt{\rho^2-L^2} $$
User picks ρ > ρtangent. Arc meets the cylinder at a discontinuous angle (the "shoulder").

Elliptical

EQ 2.4 · ELLIPSOIDAL
$$ y(x) = R\sqrt{1 - \left(1-\dfrac{x}{L}\right)^2} $$
Round-nosed, blunt. Best subsonic L/D; poor wave-drag performance.

Parabolic

EQ 2.5 · PARABOLIC SERIES
$$ y(x)=R\left[\frac{2(x/L) - K(x/L)^2}{2-K}\right],\ \ K\in[0,1] $$
K = 0 cone, K = 1 full parabola. Mid-supersonic compromise.

Power Series

EQ 2.6 · POWER
$$ y(x)=R\left(\dfrac{x}{L}\right)^{n} $$
n = 0 cylinder · ½ paraboloid · ¾ ¾-blunt · 1 cone. n = ¾ approximates a heat-shielded re-entry body.

Haack Series & Von Kármán

EQ 2.7 · HAACK
$$ \theta(x) = \arccos\!\left(1-\frac{2x}{L}\right) $$ $$ y(x) = \frac{R}{\sqrt{\pi}}\sqrt{\theta - \tfrac{1}{2}\sin 2\theta + C\sin^{3}\theta} $$
C = 0 → LD-Haack / Von Kármán: minimum drag for given length & volume. C = ⅓ → LV-Haack: minimum drag for given length & diameter.

Sears–Haack

EQ 2.8 · SEARS–HAACK BODY
$$ y(x) = R\left[4\frac{x}{L}\!\left(1-\frac{x}{L}\right)\right]^{3/4} $$
Closed body of revolution; theoretical minimum wave drag for fixed length & volume. Used as a benchmark.

FINENESS RATIO
$$ f = L/(2R) $$
  • f < 2 — blunt; high drag, robust
  • f ≈ 3–5 — supersonic sweet spot
  • f > 5 — slender; vulnerable to bending; costly volume
RADOME / SEEKER NOTE
A seeker imposes a blunted tip (spherical cap radius Rn) on whatever profile you pick. Common scheme: ogive body + ~5–10% Rn/R cap, faired smoothly. Materials: pyroceram, fused silica, ZnS for IR, alumina for radar.
⚠ Heating
Stagnation heat flux at the nose tip: $$ \dot q_{s} \;\propto\; \frac{1}{\sqrt{R_{n}}}\,\rho^{1/2} V^{3} $$ Doubling tip radius cuts peak heating by √2. Pointy noses melt; blunt noses survive.

Stability — CoM vs CoPSTATIC MARGIN · WEATHERCOCKING

PLATE 7.0

Definitions

CoM (CG)
centroid of mass distribution. Migrates aft as propellant burns (solid: forward; liquid: depends on tank layout).
CoP
point on the body where the resultant aero force acts. Migrates with α and Mach.
Static margin
$ SM = (x_{CoP}-x_{CoM})/d $ — in body diameters. Positive → stable.
Dynamic stability
damping of pitch oscillation. Even a static-stable airframe can be dynamically unstable (limit-cycle).
EQ 11.1 · PITCH STIFFNESS
$$ C_{m\alpha} = -\frac{x_{CoP}-x_{CoM}}{\bar c}\,C_{N\alpha} $$
Negative Cmα ⇒ stable. Trim α set by Cm0/Cmα.
EQ 11.2 · NATURAL FREQ.
$$ \omega_{n} = \sqrt{\frac{-q\,S\,\bar c\,C_{m\alpha}}{I_{yy}}} $$
⚑ DESIGN RULES
  • SM 1.0–2.0 cal: stable, sluggish
  • SM 0.3–0.8 cal: agile, requires good autopilot
  • SM < 0 (relaxed/neutral): hyper-agile; computer-flown only; canard-controlled fighters & AAMs
  • Barrowman method estimates CoP from fin/body planform integrals — useful for hand sizing
  • Spin-stabilized rounds are inherently unstable in pitch but gyroscopically stiff

Wing & Airfoil DesignPLANFORM · AIRFOIL · SWEEP · TWIST · CONTROL

PLATE 6β.0 — 6β.4

A wing is a planform + an airfoil + a twist distribution + a structural set of spars and ribs. Each choice is dictated by Mach regime, mission q, and required L/D. This folio gives the geometric vocabulary and the first-order math.

Planform Vocabulary

Geometry & Definitions

EQ 6β.1 · ASPECT RATIO
$$ AR = \frac{b^{2}}{S} $$
b span, S area. High AR → low induced drag. Missile wings: AR 1–4 typical.
EQ 6β.2 · TAPER & MAC
$$ \lambda = c_{t}/c_{r},\quad \overline{c}=\tfrac{2}{3}c_{r}\frac{1+\lambda+\lambda^{2}}{1+\lambda} $$

$$ M_{n}=M\cos\Lambda_{LE},\quad C_{L\alpha,sw}=\frac{C_{L\alpha,2D}\cos\Lambda}{\sqrt{1-(M\cos\Lambda)^{2}}} $$
Sweep delays critical-Mach effects; pay with reduced lift slope.

Exotic & NuclearNTR · NEP · ELECTRIC · SOLAR · LIGHT-SAIL

PLATE 10.0

Reactor & Beam

Nuclear Thermal (NTR)

H₂ propellant heated through a fission reactor core, expanded in a conventional CD nozzle. Isp 800 – 1 000 s; T/W < 1 typically. NERVA-class.

Nuclear Pulse (Orion)

Behind-vehicle fission/fusion charges + pusher plate + shock absorbers. Isp 10⁴ – 10⁶ s, very high T/W. Theoretical only.

Nuclear Electric (NEP)

Reactor → electricity → ion/Hall thruster. Isp 3 000 – 10 000 s, T/W ≪ 1. Long-cruise only.

Antimatter / Fusion (paper)

Annihilation- or fusion-heated propellant. Isp 10⁵ – 10⁶ s. Fictional only.

Electric & Sail

Electric — Ion / Hall / MPD

Xenon/argon ionized and electrostatically (or electromagnetically) accelerated. Isp 1 500 – 10 000 s; thrust ~mN. Vacuum only.

Solar Thermal

Concentrated sunlight heats H₂. Isp ~750 s. Space-only.

Light-sail / Beam-driven

Photon pressure (laser or solar). No onboard propellant. Acceleration tiny but unbounded ΔV. Force F = 2P/c (perfect reflector).

⚑ EXOTIC TRADE
High Isp ↔ low thrust is the universal pattern. NTR is the only entry on this folio with both decent Isp AND launchable T/W. Everything else assumes you're already in vacuum.

Nozzle Types

  • Conical — simplest, ~15° half-angle, λ-loss.
  • Bell (parabolic) — most common. Optimized contour, short, high-η.
  • Plug / Aerospike — altitude-compensating; flow expands against ambient.
  • Expansion-deflection — short, internal plug.
  • Dual-bell — two design points; switches at altitude.
  • Extendible — telescoping skirt for space.

Flow Conditions

  • Under-expanded Pe > Pa — losing potential thrust.
  • Matched Pe = Pa — design point.
  • Over-expanded Pe < Pa — shocks, separation, side-loads.
  • Choked M = 1 at throat — mass flow set by Pc, A*, Tc.

Chamber

  • Pc: 3–7 MPa solids; 7–25 MPa liquids; > 25 MPa staged.
  • Tc: 2 800–3 700 K typical.
  • L* = Vc/A* — characteristic length, ~0.8–1.5 m for residence time.
  • Cooling: regenerative, film, ablative, radiative, transpiration.
  • Injectors: impinging, coaxial, pintle, showerhead.

Submunitions & DispensersBUS · DISPENSER · BOMBLET · MINE · DECOY

PLATE 15β.0 — 15β.3

A submunition payload trades single-point lethality for area coverage. The carrier becomes a bus that opens at altitude hd, expels N children, each of which arms, descends, and functions independently. For worldbuilding only — categories and physics are general.

The Dispenser Sequence

  1. Cruise — bus carries N submunitions inside a thin skin or split-clamshell.
  2. Arming cue — environment OK (above safe-sep alt., outside no-go zone).
  3. Open event — pyrotechnic cord, gas generator, spin-eject, or shaped linear charge splits the case.
  4. Eject — children pushed outward by gas, springs, centrifugal spin, or aero-tumble.
  5. Stabilize — ribbon, streamer, parachute, drogue, or aero-fin orient each child.
  6. Arm — second S&A cue (descent time, spin count, pressure).
  7. Function — impact, prox, time, command, or self-destruct.
⚑ DESIGN DRIVERS
The whole bus is sized by three numbers: how big the footprint must be, how dense (children per m²), and how reliable each child must arm/function. Reliability appears twice — at arming and at fuzing — and the dud-rate compounds.

§15γ

Shaped-Charge GeometryLINER · STAND-OFF · JET FORMATION · EFP

PLATE 15γ.0 — 15γ.3

A shaped charge is the geometric concentration of detonation energy onto a metal liner that collapses into a high-velocity jet (or slug). Penetration is governed by jet length, density ratio, and stand-off — not by total explosive mass. Worldbuilding/HS-class scope; physics only.

Anatomy

  • Case — confines HE long enough for liner collapse.
  • HE fill — high-detonation-velocity (D = 7–9 km/s) explosive.
  • Wave-shaper — inert insert that flattens the detonation wave.
  • Liner — Cu, Ta, W, Mo, or DU; thickness ~3% of cone diameter.
  • Apex angle 2α — typically 42–60° for jets, 130–160° for EFPs.
  • Stand-off S — distance from liner base to target; 4–8 cone-diameters optimum.

Sizing & Penetration

EQ 15γ.1 · BIRKHOFF JET VELOCITY
$$ V_{j} = V_{0}\,\frac{\cos(\beta-\alpha)}{\sin\beta} $$
V0 liner collapse velocity, α apex half-angle, β collapse angle. Smaller α ⇒ faster jet.
EQ 15γ.2 · GURNEY (collapse v)
$$ V_{0}=\sqrt{2E}\!\left(\frac{C/M}{1+0.5\,C/M}\right)^{1/2} $$
√(2E) Gurney constant ≈ 2.4–2.9 km/s; C/M HE-to-liner mass ratio.
EQ 15γ.3 · HYDRODYNAMIC PENETRATION
$$ P = L_{j}\sqrt{\rho_{j}/\rho_{t}} $$
P penetration, Lj jet length at impact, ρ ratio drives material choice (Cu→steel √(8.9/7.85)≈1.06; W→steel √(19.3/7.85)≈1.57).
EQ 15γ.4 · JET LENGTH AT TIME t
$$ L_{j}(t) = (V_{tip}-V_{tail})\,t $$
Jet stretches until particulation time tp; choose stand-off so impact occurs near tp.
EQ 15γ.5 · OPTIMUM STAND-OFF
$$ S_{opt} \approx 4\text{–}8\,D_{c} $$
Dc charge diameter. Shorter — jet under-developed; longer — particulated, drift loss.
EQ 15γ.6 · RULE-OF-THUMB PENETRATION
$$ P/D_{c} \approx 6\text{–}10\;(\text{Cu liner, RHA target}) $$
Modern precision charges 8–10× cone-diameter into RHA. Tandem charges defeat ERA + main armor.

Energy Sources

The Four Classical Effects

1. Blast / Shock

Hemispherical pressure wave. Air-burst uses Mach-stem reflection to extend overpressure footprint; surface-burst couples energy into a crater + heavy fallout.

$$ \Delta p_{peak}\propto Y/R^{3}\;(\text{near})\to Y/R\;(\text{far}) $$

2. Thermal Radiation

Two pulses (X-ray-driven first, optical second). Cal/cm² fluence vs. range; line-of-sight only; weather-attenuated.

3. Prompt Nuclear Radiation

Neutrons + gamma in the first ~minute. Range scales weaker than blast — dominant for low-yield, secondary for high-yield where blast outranges.

4. Residual Radiation / Fallout

Surface bursts loft soil into the fireball → activated & condensed particulates. Plume shape set by upper-level winds; activity decays roughly t-1.2 (Way-Wigner approx.).

$$ I(t)\approx I_{1}\,(t/t_{1})^{-1.2} $$

Jamming Geometry

EQ 16γ.1 · BURN-THROUGH RANGE
$$ R_{bt} = \!\left[\frac{P_{t}G_{t}\sigma}{4\pi}\cdot\frac{B_{j}}{P_{j}G_{j}}\cdot\frac{1}{(S/J)_{req}}\right]^{1/2} $$
Range below which radar return exceeds jamming. Quadratic in radar power, linear in jammer power → jammers win at long range.
EQ 16γ.2 · JAM-TO-SIGNAL RATIO
$$ \frac{J}{S} = \frac{P_{j}G_{j}}{P_{t}G_{t}}\cdot\frac{4\pi R_{t}^{2}}{\sigma}\cdot\frac{B_{r}}{B_{j}}\cdot\frac{1}{L_{j}/L_{r}} $$
Br/Bj = bandwidth ratio penalty for noise jammer that spreads beyond radar BW.
EQ 16γ.3 · SUPPORT-JAMMER RANGE ADV
$$ R_{bt}^{SJ}=R_{tgt}\!\left[\frac{P_{t}G_{t}\sigma B_{j}}{4\pi P_{j}G_{j}(S/J)R_{j}^{2}B_{r}}\right]^{1/4} $$

ECM Techniques

Noise jamming

  • Barrage — wide BW, low spectral density. Easy, weak/Hz.
  • Spot — narrow BW, high density. Needs receiver to know f.
  • Swept — spot center scans across band.
  • Cover-pulse — noise burst around expected return.

Deception jamming

  • RGPO — Range-gate pull-off (delayed echo walks gate away).
  • VGPO — Velocity-gate pull-off (Doppler shift drift).
  • Inverse-gain — mod amplitude inversely with conscan; drives lock off.
  • Cross-eye / cross-pol — phase-front distort → angle error.
  • Repeater / DRFM — store & replay with offset → false targets.

Decoys

  • Chaff — λ/2 dipoles; cloud RCS → break lock.
  • Flare — IR-spectrum match for IR seekers.
  • Towed — fiber-tethered RF repeater.
  • Off-board / expendable — MALD-class miniature decoy.

ECCM responses

  • Frequency agility (pulse-to-pulse hop).
  • PRF jitter / stagger → defeats range-gate dragging.
  • Side-lobe blanking (SLB) & cancellation (SLC).
  • Polarization diversity.
  • Home-on-jam (HOJ) — passive angle on jammer.
  • Adaptive beamforming — null in direction of jammer.
  • Pulse compression with high time-BW → noise floor advantage.
  • Multi-mode (RF + IR + EO) sensor fusion.

ESM receiver classes

  • Crystal-video — wide-open, low sensitivity, low cost.
  • Superhet — narrow, high sensitivity, slow scan.
  • Channelized — N parallel filters, fast.
  • IFM — instantaneous frequency measurement.
  • Digital wideband — GHz-wide ADC + FFT.

EMP — Generic Pulse Physics

Coupling & effects

EQ 16γ.7 · INDUCED VOLTAGE ON A LINE
$$ V_{ind}\approx E_{0}\,L_{eff}\;[\text{V}] $$
Leff ≈ λ/4 of dominant frequency; long lines collect more energy.
EQ 16γ.8 · ENERGY THRESHOLD (chip damage)
$$ J_{f} = K\,t^{-1/2}\;[\text{W/cm}^{2}\,s^{1/2}] $$
Wunsch-Bell: damage flux falls as t-½. Faster pulse = lower energy threshold.
EQ 16γ.9 · SHIELDING
$$ SE(\text{dB}) = 20\log_{10}\!\frac{E_{out}}{E_{in}} $$
Solid metal can: SE = 60–100 dB above 1 MHz. Apertures and cables defeat shielding.

Hardening

  • Faraday-cage enclosure; bond all seams.
  • TVS / MOV / GDT surge devices on every penetration.
  • Shielded twisted-pair, ferrites, common-mode chokes.
  • Fiber-optic between subsystems where possible.
  • Rad-hard / SEU-tolerant logic for nuclear/space environments.

HPM (High-Power Microwave) — generic
