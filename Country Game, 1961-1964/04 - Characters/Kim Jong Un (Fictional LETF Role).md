In the 1961–1964 country game, **Kim Jong Un** is assigned the fictional role of **East Asian Territorial Governor** within the rebuilt [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]] following [[Operation Silent Fade]]. He assumes control over the LETF's East Asian holdings — the [[Sino Fade Provinces]] (China), the [[Mongolian Province of the Fade]], the [[Korean Fade Province]] (South Korea), and associated Pacific territories.

## Context: Post-Silent Fade Resurgence

After all original LETF leadership is eliminated during [[Operation Silent Fade]] (1963), the LETF initiates [[Operation Eternal Fade]] to rebuild its command. Kim Jong Un is recruited to fill the East Asian governance role, presumably because his historical association with authoritarian governance of a East Asian state makes him the LETF's intuitive choice for the region.

## Role in the LETF

Kim Jong Un **assumes control of LETF's East Asian territories**, which include the most recently and most contentiously conquered regions:
- **[[Sino Fade Provinces]]**: The vast former Chinese territories, still experiencing significant resistance.
- **[[Mongolian Province of the Fade]]**: Secured in 1962.
- **[[Korean Fade Province]]**: South Korea, taken during [[Operation Total Fade]].
- **[[Pacific Province of the Fade]]**: Hawaii, ceded by the United States.

His appointment is partly practical — the East Asian territories require a regional authority familiar with authoritarian governance — and partly propagandistic, framing the LETF's occupation of these territories as having a "native-proximate" authority figure (albeit a fictional one).

## Significance

Kim Jong Un's role represents the LETF's increasing difficulty in maintaining direct control over its sprawling empire. By the time of his appointment, the [[Sino Fade Provinces]] are already experiencing massive resistance, and the LETF's hold on South Korea and other East Asian territories is under pressure from the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]]'s support for resistance movements.
