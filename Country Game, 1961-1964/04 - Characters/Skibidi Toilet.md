**Skibidi Toilet** is the **God of War and Fools** in the [[Morganism|Morganist]] pantheon. He is depicted as a **sentient head emerging from a toilet** — one of the most overtly absurdist figures in the LETF's theological framework, and arguably the most surreal deity in the campaign's universe.

## Divine Role and Mythology

Skibidi Toilet represents two concepts that [[Morganism]] links together: war and foolishness. In Morganist theology, all war contains an element of absurdity — warriors choose to risk death for causes that may be irrational, and this foolishness is divinely sanctioned by Skibidi Toilet. His trickster nature means that he may bless a campaign or doom it, and his followers accept this uncertainty as part of the divine bargain.

Despite his unreliable nature, **LETF soldiers pray to Skibidi Toilet before battle** — specifically because he governs the domain of war. His trickster character means that these prayers are understood as a gamble rather than a reliable petition. The soldiers who pray to him accept that he may answer their prayers ironically or not at all, but pray anyway because he is the patron of their domain.

His toilet-based iconography serves a specific narrative purpose in the LETF's surreal mythology: it strips war of its romantic glorification, acknowledging through the deity's own absurd form that military conflict is as much foolishness as it is heroism. This makes him one of the more philosophically interesting figures in the Morganist pantheon — a god who mocks the very domain he presides over.

## Depiction and Iconography

Skibidi Toilet is depicted in LETF military imagery as a human head emerging from the bowl of a toilet, often with an expression of chaotic glee. His shrines appear in military barracks and pre-battle staging areas. His image is used in LETF propaganda about the enemies of the Fade — framing opposing forces as the true fools, deserving of his mockery.

## Significance in the Campaign

As the God of War and Fools, Skibidi Toilet's presence throughout the campaign serves as a constant tonal counterpoint to the LETF's grandiose ambitions. The same empire that claims divine destiny and eternal order prays to a sentient toilet head before going into battle — a contradiction the LETF mythology absorbs without apparent irony.
