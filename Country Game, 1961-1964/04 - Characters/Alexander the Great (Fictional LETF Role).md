In the 1961–1964 country game, **Alexander the Great** joins the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]] leadership structure as a **symbolic military advisor** and strategic figure. His arrival creates a feared triumvirate of military command alongside [[Magnus Maximus]] and [[Adolf Hitler (Fictional LETF Role)]].

## Arrival in the LETF

Alexander the Great is added to the LETF leadership in 1961, when the LETF decides that his legendary tactical mind would complement Magnus Maximus's strategic command and Hitler's administrative role. His entry is announced alongside the LETF's declaration of war on Lebanon ([[Operation Cedar Fade]]).

## Role in the Leadership Triumvirate

Alexander occupies the third position in what the game describes as a **leadership triumvirate "feared globally for their strategic brilliance and unyielding ambition."** His specific function is:

- **Historical strategic oversight**: Leveraging his legendary military knowledge (from his historical campaigns across Persia, Egypt, and India) to advise on conquest strategy.
- **Inspirational command**: His mythic status as history's greatest conqueror serves as a propaganda tool and morale boost for LETF forces.
- **Tactical planning**: Particularly active during the LETF's multi-front campaigns in the Middle East and Central Asia — regions that Alexander himself historically conquered.

## Significance

Alexander's inclusion in the LETF leadership is one of the most overt expressions of the campaign's surreal alternate-history aesthetic. By placing history's most celebrated conqueror alongside [[Magnus Maximus]] and [[Adolf Hitler (Fictional LETF Role)]], the LETF constructs a leadership pantheon that claims the full weight of history's greatest military minds — the ancient conqueror, the modern theorist, and the ideological enforcer.

His presence particularly enhances the LETF's operations in the Middle East and Central Asia, regions historically associated with his campaigns, giving those conquests an additional layer of mythological resonance within Morganist propaganda.

## Fate

Alexander the Great's fate after [[Operation Silent Fade]] (1963) is not explicitly documented in the source material. His status post-operation is unknown.
