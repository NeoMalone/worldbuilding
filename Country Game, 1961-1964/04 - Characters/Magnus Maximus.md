**Magnus Maximus** is the Supreme Leader and military commander of the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]. He is the dominant political and military figure of the campaign, driving the LETF's global expansion campaigns and serving as the public face of [[Morganism]]'s imperial ambitions. Revered by the LETF as an immortal tactician, he leads or oversees virtually every major LETF military operation from 1961 until his capture in 1963.

## Role in Morganism

Within the [[Morganism|Morganist]] religious framework, Magnus Maximus occupies a status approaching divinity. While [[King Darius Morgan]] serves as the formal High Priest, Magnus Maximus is venerated as an *immortal tactician* — a living military god whose strategic brilliance is seen as evidence of divine favor. His portraits and statues appear alongside those of [[King Darius Morgan]] throughout LETF-controlled territories.

In the [[Order of Morganism]], he holds the position of **Supreme Leader**, making him the highest political and military authority in the LETF state. His word is law, and his military campaigns are framed as sacred missions.

## Military Campaigns

Magnus Maximus personally directs or oversees the following major operations:

- **[[Operation Golden Dawn]]** (1961): Leads the invasion of Japan with precise multi-phase strategy.
- **[[Operation Sacred Fade]]** (1961): Coordinates the campaign against Israel.
- **[[Operation Cedar Fade]]** / **[[Operation Crescent Fade]]** / **[[Operation Fade's Crossing]]** (1961): Oversees sequential Middle East annexations of Lebanon, Syria, and Jordan.
- **[[Operation Mesopotamian Fade]]** (1961): Commands the Iraq invasion.
- **[[Operation Anatolian Fade]]** (1961): Directs the annexation of Turkey.
- **[[Operation Crimson Fade]]** (1961): Leads the first major assault on the USSR.
- **[[Operation Baltic Fade]]** (1961): Commands the Poland-Kaliningrad campaign.
- **[[Operation Carpathian Fade]]** (1961): Oversees the nuclear-backed Romanian invasion.
- **[[Operation Soviet Eclipse]]** (1962): Commands the final total war against the USSR.
- **[[Operation Eternal Steppe]]** (1962): Directs the Mongolian annexation.
- **[[Operation Jade Fade]]** (1962): Commands the China invasion.

## Leadership Style

Magnus Maximus is described as combining *brute force and psychological warfare* — using overwhelming military power alongside LETF propaganda, terror campaigns, and ideological enforcement to subdue conquered populations. He tends to delegate political administration to [[Adolf Hitler (Fictional LETF Role)]] while retaining personal control of military strategy.

After Alexander the Great joins the LETF leadership in 1961, Magnus Maximus and Alexander form a **leadership triumvirate** with [[Adolf Hitler (Fictional LETF Role)]] that is described as *"feared globally for their strategic brilliance and unyielding ambition."*

## Capture and Death

During [[Operation Silent Fade]] (1963), Magnus Maximus is located at a heavily guarded palace in Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia. Austrian **Jagdkommando** operatives infiltrate the compound using tranquilizer rounds and successfully extract him alive. He is transported to a classified U.S. black site for interrogation.

Following interrogation, Magnus Maximus is assassinated during [[Operation Shadow Recall]] — LETF operatives infiltrate the black site and eliminate him to prevent further intelligence leaks.

## Legacy

The capture and death of Magnus Maximus triggers the most significant power vacuum in LETF history. Without its supreme military commander, regional governors and military officers fragment into competing factions. Resistance movements surge across LETF territories. The subsequent attempt to rebuild leadership under new figures fails to restore coherent command.
