**Vexbolts** (also styled **Vex Bolts**) is the **Goddess of Chaos and Hatred** in the [[Morganism|Morganist]] pantheon. She occupies a unique and paradoxical position: the LETF keeps her in its pantheon not to celebrate chaos, but to *explain it* — she is the theological scapegoat for rebellion, disorder, and everything that opposes the Fade's divine order.

## Divine Role and Mythology

Vexbolts represents everything the LETF ideologically opposes. While the other members of the [[Council of the Divine Fade]] represent positive virtues (purity, happiness, war prowess, divine order), Vexbolts embodies the negative forces that the LETF frames itself as fighting against.

Her theological purpose is functional: by naming a goddess of chaos, [[Morganism]] provides its followers with an explanation for dissent, resistance, and disorder within the empire. When resistance movements arise, when citizens fail to conform, when LETF plans fail — these outcomes can be attributed to Vexbolts' influence rather than to systemic failure or popular opposition. This makes her a key piece of the LETF's propaganda architecture.

Her **shrines are feared rather than revered** — they are places of warning and warding, not worship. Citizens approach them as cautionary monuments rather than places of prayer.

## Paradox in the Pantheon

Vexbolts' inclusion in the official pantheon is theologically unusual: she is simultaneously an enemy of the Fade's order and a recognized divine figure. This contradiction is resolved in Morganist doctrine by framing her as a *contained* divine force — kept in the pantheon so that her power can be acknowledged, named, and thus managed. She is the known adversary, which is more useful than an unknown one.

This also means Vexbolts functions as a kind of theological deterrent: her existence justifies the LETF's authoritarian control. If chaos is a goddess, then only strict order can hold her at bay — and only the LETF provides that order.

## Fate

Vexbolts is neutralized during [[Operation Silent Fade]] (1963), along with [[Mr. Clean]], [[Gorg Morgan]], and other LETF divine figures. The simultaneous elimination of the Morganist pantheon's key figures effectively collapses the religious framework that held the empire together.
