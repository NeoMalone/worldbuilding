**Operation Crimson Fade** is the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]'s first large-scale campaign against the [[USSR]], launched in 1961 on two simultaneous fronts: through the Caucasus from the south and through the Ukraine SSR from the west, the latter enabled by [[Austria]]'s access to Czechoslovakia. The operation culminates in the siege of Moscow and a conditional surrender that divides the Soviet empire — the LETF gaining the south and west, the USSR retaining its core Russian territories and the regions east of the Urals.

## Background

By late 1961, the LETF controls a vast empire stretching from Egypt through the entire Middle East, Anatolia, and Eastern Europe. With Turkey's Bosporus secured and Poland partially occupied, the LETF's gaze turns to the Soviet Union — the last great power bloc capable of challenging its Eurasian dominance. Austria's alliance with and access through Czechoslovakia provides the western invasion corridor; the Caucasus Province of the Fade provides the southern springboard.

## Plan and Execution

### Phase 1: Airstrikes and Missile Bombardments

- **Moscow** — LETF Su-24s bomb Soviet command and control infrastructure
- **Stalingrad (Volgograd)** — Targeted to disrupt southern industrial capacity and logistics
- **Rostov-on-Don** — Bombed to weaken resistance near the Caucasus front

### Phase 2: Southern Push (Caucasus Front)

LETF mechanized infantry and armored divisions cross from the Caucasus Province of the Fade:
- **Rostov-on-Don** — Secured after intense fighting; becomes LETF logistical hub for the southern push
- **Astrakhan** — Captured to control Caspian Sea access
- **Stalingrad (Volgograd)** — Falls after heavy fighting; major symbolic victory for the LETF

### Phase 3: Western Push (Ukraine Front)

Austrian and LETF forces invade from Czechoslovakia:
- **Kiev** — Falls quickly after artillery barrages and airstrikes soften Soviet defenses; provides a staging ground for the push toward Moscow
- **Kharkiv** — Secured as a logistics hub for central Russia operations

Combined forces from both fronts converge on Moscow.

### Phase 4: Encirclement of Moscow

Forces from the Caucasus front and the Ukraine front close in on Moscow simultaneously. Prolonged urban combat follows as Soviet forces defend the capital street by street. Moscow falls after LETF forces breach the Kremlin.

## Outcome and Conditional Surrender

Rather than unconditional capitulation, the LETF and the USSR reach a **Conditional Surrender Agreement** that divides the Soviet empire:

| **Territory** | **Controlling Party** | **New Name** |
|---|---|---|
| Ukraine SSR | LETF | Western Province of the Fade |
| Caucasus regions | LETF | Caspian Province of the Fade |
| Kazakhstan and Central Asia | LETF | Central Steppe Province |
| Moscow and core Russian territories | USSR (retains) | — |
| Siberia and regions east of the Urals | USSR (retains) | — |

**Terms of the agreement:**
1. The USSR remains a nominally independent state but cedes influence in Eastern Europe and Asia
2. A demilitarized buffer zone is established along the new border
3. The USSR supplies the LETF with oil, gas, and industrial resources for five years in exchange for peace
4. Both parties sign a non-aggression pact for the next decade

## Global Reactions

- **United Nations**: Declares the agreement unjust but acknowledges it as a step toward stability; the U.N.'s ability to function as a global peacekeeping body is critically compromised.
- **China**: Condemns the Soviet capitulation; protests strongly but avoids direct military confrontation.
- **NATO**: Shock and fear — with the Soviet Union diminished and the LETF controlling Eurasia's midsection, NATO members prepare for worst-case scenarios.

## Legacy

Operation Crimson Fade and the subsequent Conditional Surrender mark the moment the LETF transitions from a regional empire to a genuine Eurasian superpower. The gain of Ukraine, the Caucasus, and Kazakhstan in a single campaign transforms the LETF's strategic depth. However, the administrative burden of governing these vast territories — on top of the Middle Eastern, Eastern European, and Pacific holdings — plants the seeds of the overextension crisis that emerges in 1962. The operation is followed by [[Operation Soviet Eclipse]] the next year, which completes the total conquest of the USSR after the non-aggression pact is broken.
