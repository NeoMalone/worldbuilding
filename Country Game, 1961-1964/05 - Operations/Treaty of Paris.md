The **Treaty of Paris** is the 1961 diplomatic agreement that formally divides France following [[Sweden]]'s conquest in [[Operation Northern Fury]]. It establishes the precise borders between Swedish-controlled territory and the reduced French sovereign zone, and defines the political, military, and economic terms under which France continues to exist as a state. The treaty is one of the most consequential diplomatic instruments of the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]]'s expansion arc, demonstrating that the alliance can redraw European borders not only through military force but through legally formalized settlement.

## Background

Following Operation Northern Fury, Sweden holds all of France's northern and southern strategic zones while France retains a reduced western rump. Rather than a complete annexation, Sweden elects to formalize a partial occupation through a negotiated treaty — maintaining a nominally sovereign France while extracting maximum strategic and economic benefit. The United States, which provided heavy military support, has no territorial demands and does not appear as a formal party.

## Terms of the Treaty

1. **Demilitarization** — France agrees to demilitarize its borders with Swedish-occupied territories; Swedish forces maintain full military control over the occupied regions
2. **Economic Concessions** — France must supply agricultural goods and industrial materials to Sweden for a period of **10 years**
3. **Cultural Autonomy** — Citizens in Swedish-controlled territories are granted limited cultural autonomy with Swedish officials overseeing local governance
4. **Non-Aggression Pact** — Both nations sign a **15-year non-aggression pact** to prevent future conflicts
5. **Recognition** — France formally recognizes Swedish sovereignty over its occupied territories as a condition of the treaty

## Global Reactions

- **United Nations**: Condemns Sweden's annexation of French territory; lacks enforcement power to reverse the settlement.
- **NATO**: The treaty is viewed as a major blow to NATO's European influence; increased fortification of remaining member states follows.
- **France**: Public outrage; resistance movements form in Swedish-occupied territories, particularly in Alsace and Provence.
- **Soviet Union**: The USSR sees Sweden's permanent territorial gains as a strategic distraction for NATO and the Golden Lemons Alliance — noting that internal European tensions may eventually fracture the alliance.

## Significance

The Treaty of Paris is the first instance in the 1961–1964 game of a defeated state being allowed partial sovereignty under a formalized settlement rather than total annexation. This model — partial occupation with a rump sovereign state — is used again in the USSR Conditional Surrender and the Poland Conditional Surrender, suggesting that the Golden Lemons Alliance sometimes prefers compliant buffer states over the administrative burden of total conquest.

Sweden subsequently uses the occupied French territories as a springboard for [[Operation Nordic Dawn]] (Corsica annexation) and [[Operation Nordic Eclipse]] (East Germany annexation), demonstrating that the Treaty of Paris represents not a final settlement but a strategic base for continued Swedish expansion.
