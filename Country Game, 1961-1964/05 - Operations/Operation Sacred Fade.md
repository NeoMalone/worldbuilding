**Operation Sacred Fade** is the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]'s campaign to annex Israel in 1961. Launched shortly after [[Operation Golden Dawn]] completes the division of Japan, the operation is one of the first LETF-specific territorial conquests of the 1961 campaign season — distinct from the joint Japan invasion in that it is framed explicitly as a religious and ideological mission under [[Morganism]]. Jerusalem's capture is among the most symbolically significant events of the early campaign, and the city becomes a pilgrimage destination for Morganist followers.

## Background

With Japan divided and the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]] firmly established, the LETF turns its attention to the Middle East. Israel is identified as a strategic target due to its perceived alliances, its regional influence, and — crucially — its religious significance to [[Morganism]]. Jerusalem in particular is a propaganda objective: LETF leaders believe its capture will validate the divine mandate of [[King Darius Morgan]] and the Morganist faith.

[[Magnus Maximus]] coordinates the overall invasion, with support from [[Alexander the Great (Fictional LETF Role)]] for tactical planning. The United States provides air and naval support, and Sweden contributes reconnaissance forces and naval assets.

## Plan and Execution

### Phase 1: Air and Missile Strikes

- **Tel Aviv** — LETF Su-24 bombers destroy military installations and communications hubs
- **Haifa** — Targeted for its naval facilities; bombed by U.S. B-52 Stratofortresses
- **Jerusalem** — Military installations outside the city are struck to disrupt Israeli coordination; the city itself is largely preserved for its symbolic value

### Phase 2: Ground Invasion

**Southern Offensive (LETF from Egypt/Sinai):**
- LETF mechanized infantry cross the Negev Desert, capturing **Beersheba** rapidly
- **Eilat** is secured after naval bombardment, giving the LETF a Red Sea port
- Forces advance northeast toward Jerusalem

**Central Offensive (Joint LETF-U.S.):**
- **Jerusalem** — captured after heavy urban combat; declared a holy city under Morganist law
- **Tel Aviv** — falls to U.S. airstrikes and LETF infantry; becomes a logistics hub for the occupation

**Northern Offensive (Swedish support):**
- **Haifa** — Swedish naval and aerial forces secure the port, isolating northern Israeli forces
- **Nazareth** — captured with minimal resistance
- LETF troops supported by Swedish reconnaissance secure **Jericho**, isolating Israel's northern regions

### Phase 3: Naval Operations

The U.S. Navy deploys carrier strike groups in the Mediterranean to blockade Israeli ports, cutting off all external supply and reinforcement.

## Outcome

| **Territory** | **Controlling Power** | **New Name** |
|---|---|---|
| Southern and central Israel (Jerusalem, Tel Aviv, Beersheba, Eilat) | LETF | Negev Province of the Fade |
| Northern Israel (Haifa, Nazareth, Galilee) | U.S. and Sweden | Stabilization zone (no formal annexation) |

Jerusalem is declared a Morganist holy city and becomes the site of major religious ceremonies. [[King Darius Morgan]] travels to Jerusalem in person to consecrate Morganist temples, making it the symbolic spiritual capital of the LETF's religious program.

## Global Reactions

- **United Nations**: Condemns the division of Israel but cannot intervene due to GLA military dominance.
- **Arab League states**: Protests circulate but no unified military response emerges; several Arab nations quietly assess the LETF's expanding regional presence.
- **USSR**: Heightened tensions; the Soviet Union views the GLA's combined Middle Eastern and Pacific campaigns as an existential strategic threat.

## Legacy

Operation Sacred Fade is the clearest early example of the LETF using military conquest in service of its religious ideology. The capture of Jerusalem cements [[Morganism]]'s claim to sacred geography and provides propaganda material used throughout subsequent LETF campaigns. The operation also establishes the pattern of U.S. air and naval support enabling LETF ground conquests — a template repeated in Lebanon, Syria, Iraq, and Turkey over the following months.
