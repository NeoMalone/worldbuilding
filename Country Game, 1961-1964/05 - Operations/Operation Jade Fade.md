**Operation Jade Fade** is the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]'s full-scale invasion of China in 1962, launched simultaneously with [[Sweden]]'s annexation of East Germany ([[Operation Nordic Eclipse]]) and [[Austria]]'s imposition of a 90% tariff on LETF imports. The China campaign is by far the LETF's most ambitious 1962 operation — China's size, population, and resistance capacity make it the most difficult theater the LETF has faced since the USSR. The invasion is followed almost immediately by [[Operation Jade Suppression]], the LETF's genocidal consolidation campaign.

## Background

With Mongolia annexed ([[Operation Eternal Steppe]]), the LETF holds a 4,000+ kilometer front along China's northern and western borders. Combined with access to former Soviet territories in the far east (via U.S.-LETF coordination), the LETF can approach China from multiple directions simultaneously. China — already sanctioned by the United States and diplomatically isolated — cannot assemble meaningful outside support.

## Plan and Execution

### Phase 1: Air Superiority and Bombardments

LETF Su-24 bombers and MiG-21s target key Chinese cities:
- **Beijing** — Bombed to disrupt command and control infrastructure
- **Shanghai** — Industrial and naval facilities targeted
- **Chengdu and Guangzhou** — Struck to weaken internal logistics and supply lines

### Phase 2: Ground Assault

**Northern Push:**
LETF forces enter from the Mongolian Province of the Fade, targeting **Beijing** and **Inner Mongolia**. This front benefits from the recently completed land bridge established by the Mongolian annexation.

**Eastern Push:**
Mechanized divisions move toward **Shanghai** and the eastern seaboard, engaging China's coastal defenses.

**Southern Push:**
Infantry and artillery units advance from LETF-controlled Vietnam, targeting **Guangzhou** and the Pearl River Delta region.

### Phase 3: Encirclement and Urban Combat

**Beijing** — Encircled within weeks; falls after brutal urban combat as LETF forces converge from the north and west.

**Shanghai** — Secured through a combination of amphibious landings and naval bombardments from the East China Sea.

## Outcome

| **Territory** | **Outcome** |
|---|---|
| Northern China (Beijing, Inner Mongolia) | LETF captures Beijing; northern provinces absorbed |
| Eastern Seaboard (Shanghai, key port cities) | Fall to amphibious and naval operations; Chinese trade routes cut |
| Southern China (Guangzhou, interior provinces) | Secured through southern push from Vietnam |

China is declared part of the **Sino Fade Provinces**. However, resistance forces retreat into remote regions — the mountains of western China, the jungles of Yunnan, and the vast interior — making the LETF's control of the territory tenuous. LETF casualties are moderate; China suffers devastating losses with widespread infrastructure damage.

## Global Reactions

- **United Nations**: Already functionally defunct, but surviving diplomatic bodies condemn the invasion.
- **Austria and Sweden**: Quietly distance themselves from the LETF's China campaign; Austria's tariff imposition at this moment is partly a political signal.
- **U.S.**: Launches [[Operation Silent Halo]] — covert support for Chinese resistance while framing Iran as the weapons supplier.

## Legacy

Operation Jade Fade gives the LETF nominal control over the world's most populous nation, but it also creates the LETF's largest and most persistent resistance problem. China's vast geography means that LETF "control" is in practice control of cities and roads, not the country. The resistance movements born during this operation eventually connect to the [[Global Resistance Movements]] network that helps facilitate the 1963 rollback. The operation is also immediately followed by [[Operation Jade Suppression]] — the LETF's genocidal consolidation campaign that becomes the primary driver of GLA member distancing.
