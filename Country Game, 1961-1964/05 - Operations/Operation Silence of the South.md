**Operation Silence of the South** is the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]'s 1963 neutralization campaign in South America — a mass suppression operation targeting Argentina, the Amazonian Fade Province (occupied Brazil), and [[Boombayah]] (formerly neutral Brazil). Executed as the LETF attempts to consolidate its hemisphere-spanning empire, the campaign generates catastrophic backlash, strengthens South American resistance infrastructure, and contributes directly to the conditions under which the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]] counter-pivot becomes inevitable.

## Background

South America has been a problem zone for the LETF throughout 1962–1963. Brazil ([[Amazonian Fade Province]]) has never been fully pacified — its Amazon rainforest harbors active and growing resistance. The [[People's Republic of La Plata (PRLP)]] (LETF-aligned Argentina) is experiencing internal instability due to U.S. covert funding of opposition movements. Boombayah has served as a sanctuary nation for resistance leaders and anti-LETF defectors. The LETF's new leadership under [[Operation Eternal Fade]] approves a continent-wide neutralization campaign to resolve all three problems simultaneously.

## Actions

At the strategic level, Operation Silence of the South involves:

1. **LETF operatives and military assets deployed to Argentina** — targeting internal opposition to the People's Republic of La Plata government and purging anti-LETF factions
2. **Mass suppression operations in Brazil** (Amazonian Fade Province) — targeting resistance infrastructure in the Amazon and major cities
3. **Military operations against Boombayah** — attacking a formally neutral nation that has harbored resistance leaders, framed as a counter-resistance operation

The campaign involves population suppression at scale across all three theaters.

## Outcome

The campaign technically weakens specific resistance networks in the short term, but the strategic backlash is devastating:

- South American resistance movements — Brazilian Amazon networks, Boombayah exile communities, and anti-LETF Argentine factions — merge and coordinate in response to the campaign
- Boombayah, formerly neutral, formally aligns itself with the anti-LETF resistance coalition
- The People's Republic of La Plata's internal instability worsens as LETF crackdowns generate popular resentment
- International condemnation of LETF South American operations becomes one of the primary talking points for GLA members justifying their counter-pivot

## Global Reactions

- **Golden Lemons (Austria, Sweden, U.S.)**: Use the South American campaign as additional political justification for their accelerating counter-alliance planning; resistance support is increased
- **Boombayah**: Formally abandons neutrality and aligns with anti-LETF resistance networks; expands sanctuary operations
- **United Nations remnants**: Condemns the campaign; no enforcement possible

## Legacy

Operation Silence of the South is one of the LETF's most strategically counterproductive late-game operations. It creates the backlash that accelerates the LETF's South American losses, transforms a neutral buffer state (Boombayah) into an active opponent, and provides the GLA counter-alliance with its final political justification for [[Operation Silent Fade]]. When Austria, Sweden, and the U.S. brief each other on justifications for launching Silent Fade, South American atrocity documentation is among the primary exhibits.
