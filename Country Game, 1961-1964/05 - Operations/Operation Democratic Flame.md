**Operation Democratic Flame** is a [[United States of America|U.S.]] covert operation in 1961 to overthrow Afghanistan's Soviet-aligned Islamic Emirate and install a pro-Western government. Conducted simultaneously with Sweden's [[Operation Nordic Dawn]] (Corsica) and the LETF's transfer of Newport County and Hawaii to LETF administration, the operation is the U.S.'s most direct unilateral Cold War move of the early 1961 campaign season — executed without LETF involvement, and driven in part by U.S. concern about the USSR's Central Asian influence at a moment when the LETF is absorbing Soviet territories from the west.

## Background

The Islamic Emirate of Afghanistan has maintained alignment with the USSR, providing the Soviets with a strategic buffer state in Central Asia. The United States, watching the LETF roll up Soviet territory from the Caucasus westward, identifies Afghanistan as an opportunity to strike the USSR from a different angle — replacing a Soviet-aligned state with a U.S.-aligned democratic government before the LETF absorbs the region entirely.

## Plan and Execution

### Phase 1: Intelligence and Espionage

CIA operatives infiltrate **Kabul**, targeting key government officials and military leaders sympathetic to the USSR. Propaganda campaigns disseminate pro-democratic messaging, rallying domestic opposition.

### Phase 2: Covert Military Support

U.S. special forces assist anti-government factions, providing weapons and logistical aid. Airdrops deliver supplies to rebel strongholds in **Herat** and **Kandahar**, establishing armed opposition footholds in the west and south.

### Phase 3: Government Overthrow

Coordinated attacks by rebel groups and CIA-backed militias topple the government in **Kabul**. The operation is designed for minimal bloodshed — it succeeds. Pro-democracy leaders establish a new government aligned with U.S. interests.

## Outcome

The Islamic Emirate is replaced by the **Democratic Republic of Afghanistan**, which immediately aligns itself with the United States. The U.S. is granted access to Afghan military bases and strategic resources, significantly weakening the USSR's regional foothold in Central Asia.

## Global Reactions

- **United Nations**: Mixed — condemns the U.S. coup as illegal intervention but acknowledges the removal of a Soviet-aligned regime.
- **USSR**: Outrage; the Soviets strongly protest U.S. "imperialism" in Afghanistan. Soviet troop movements accelerate along the southern border. The loss of Afghanistan compounds the USSR's pressure from the LETF on its western and southern fronts.
- **NATO and Allies**: Concern about Sweden's growing territorial ambitions and the LETF's acquisition of Hawaii raises questions about GLA stability; the Afghanistan coup adds another layer of complexity to the US's role within the alliance.
- **LETF**: No formal reaction; the LETF is occupied with its own Central Asian absorption.

## Legacy

Operation Democratic Flame is the United States' signature unilateral action in the 1961 campaign. It establishes the Democratic Republic of Afghanistan as a U.S. client state and denies the USSR one of its last buffer states in Central Asia. The operation also demonstrates the U.S.'s willingness to conduct regime change operations independently of the LETF — a pattern that contributes to the growing independence of the non-LETF GLA members and foreshadows the eventual GLA pivot against LETF in 1963.
