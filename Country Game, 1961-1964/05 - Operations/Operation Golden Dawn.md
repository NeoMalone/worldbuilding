**Operation Golden Dawn** is the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]]'s opening offensive against [[Japan]] in 1961, launched on the pretext of alleged thermonuclear automotive sabotage. It is the first large-scale coordinated military action of the newly formed alliance and establishes the operational template — naval blockade, air superiority, then coordinated amphibious and ground assault — that the GLA uses throughout the early 1961 campaign season.

## Background

On February 1, 1961, [[Sweden]], [[Austria]], the [[United States of America]], and the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]] form the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]]. Their stated justification for declaring war on Japan is alleged thermonuclear automotive sabotage — a covert operation supposedly orchestrated by Japanese forces to destabilize global industries and national security. The true motive is the establishment of Pacific dominance and the division of Japan's industrial capacity between Austria and the LETF.

Austria intends to annex the northern islands; the LETF intends to annex the south. Both powers plan to make use of Czechoslovakian cooperation to process captured Japanese civilians as labor in allied factories.

## Plan and Execution

### Phase 1: Naval Blockade

U.S. aircraft carriers, Swedish reconnaissance ships, and LETF submarines establish a blockade of the Pacific, cutting off Japan's supply lines and isolating its economy. Minor skirmishes occur with Japanese destroyers, but the GLA suffers no significant losses. Japan's trade and resource imports are crippled within weeks.

### Phase 2: Air Campaign

U.S. F-105 Thunderchiefs and LETF Su-24s conduct sustained bombing raids against key automotive factories and suspected nuclear facilities. Swedish fighter jets escort the missions, ensuring minimal losses. Japan's air force suffers heavy casualties but retaliates with kamikaze-style strikes on U.S. carriers, causing moderate damage.

[[Osama bin Laden (Fictional LETF Role)]] leads the LETF air raids, achieving multiple key victories against Japanese air squadrons. [[King Darius Morgan]] conducts religious ceremonies blessing LETF troops before each sortie.

### Phase 3: Amphibious Invasion

Austrian **Iron Divisions** lead the ground assault on northern Japan, supported by U.S. Marines and LETF mechanized units. Sweden provides medical and logistical support throughout.

**Northern (Austrian) advance:**
- **Hokkaido** — secured with minimal resistance; becomes the hub of Austrian northern operations
- **Tohoku Region** — Aomori and Sendai fall after heavy Austrian airstrikes
- **Niigata** — captured after fierce fighting; becomes a key Austrian port
- **Kanto Region (northern districts)** — Austrian forces secure the northern suburbs of Tokyo

**Southern (LETF) advance:**
- **Kansai Region** — Osaka taken as the LETF's southern stronghold; Kyoto falls to LETF mechanized units (cultural sites preserved for propaganda)
- **Chugoku Region** — Hiroshima captured after prolonged urban combat; symbolic significance exploited for psychological warfare
- **Kyushu Region** — Fukuoka and Nagasaki fall to amphibious landings

## Outcome

Japan is fully annexed and divided:

| **Territory** | **Controlling Power** | **New Name** |
|---|---|---|
| North Japan (Hokkaido, Tohoku, Niigata, Kanto north) | Austria | Northern Prefecture of Austria |
| South Japan (Kansai, Chugoku, Kyushu, Shikoku) | LETF | Fade's Southern Archipelago |

Captured Japanese civilians are transported to Czechoslovakian internment facilities, where converted factories produce weapons, vehicles, and supplies for the GLA war effort.

## Global Reactions

- **United Nations**: Condemns the annexation but takes no meaningful action against the overwhelming GLA military.
- **USSR**: Remains focused on internal affairs but views the GLA's Pacific dominance with growing alarm.
- **China**: Provides limited covert aid to Japan; strongly opposes the annexation but lacks the military capacity to intervene directly.

## Legacy

Operation Golden Dawn establishes the alliance's willingness to conduct total-conquest campaigns against established nations. It is the first demonstration of GLA air-naval-ground coordination at scale and foreshadows the systematic regional annexation campaigns the LETF conducts across the Middle East throughout the rest of 1961. The division of Japan between Austria and the LETF also marks the first instance of GLA members accepting permanent territorial allocations from a jointly prosecuted war.
