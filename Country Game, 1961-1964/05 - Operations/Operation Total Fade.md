**Operation Total Fade** is the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]'s 1962 simultaneous multi-continent annexation campaign, launched immediately following the [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]] that softened all eleven targets. It is the single largest military operation of the 1961–1964 era — eleven simultaneous invasions across five continents — and marks both the apex of LETF territorial expansion and the beginning of its terminal overextension. The LETF controls more territory after Operation Total Fade than at any other point in the campaign, but the administrative burden, resistance surges, and internal GLA fracturing that result make this peak moment simultaneously the beginning of the empire's decline.

## Outcome

All eleven nations are technically annexed by the end of the campaign. However, resistance movements are widespread and particularly intense in nations with difficult terrain: Brazil (Amazon), Australia (Outback), India (rural areas), South Korea (mountainous east), and Canada (remote north).

The LETF now controls vast territories across Europe, Asia, the Americas, and Oceania — a global empire without historical precedent. But governing this empire simultaneously while pursuing further campaigns proves impossible. Supply lines stretch across six continents; garrison forces are inadequate; resistance networks connect across formerly separate occupied territories.

## The Overextension Crisis

Operation Total Fade plants the seeds of the LETF's 1963 collapse:

1. **Supply chain fragmentation**: The empire is too large to supply coherently from any center
2. **Resistance surge**: The 11-nation simultaneous conquest generates 11 simultaneous resistance movements with global symbolic resonance
3. **GLA member alienation**: The Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign and Total Fade annexations push Austria, Sweden, and the U.S. to begin secret planning for the counter-alliance
4. **Administrative vacuum**: No governance infrastructure exists to administer 11 newly conquered nations; local LETF governors operate with minimal central oversight

## Global Reactions

- **Remaining neutral states**: Boombayah, Switzerland (pre-Alpine Fade), and scattered non-annexed territories begin coordinating diplomatically against the LETF
- **GLA members**: Austria, Sweden, and the U.S. — outwardly remaining in the alliance — begin the classified planning that leads to [[Operation Silent Fade]] the following year
- **United Nations**: The last vestiges of international governance declare Total Fade the endpoint of international law; no enforcement is possible

## Legacy

Operation Total Fade is the LETF's zenith — the moment of greatest territorial extent and simultaneously the beginning of the end. Every subsequent operation in the 1963 arc — [[Operation Alpine Fade]], [[Operation Silence of the South]], [[Operation Silent Fade]], and [[Operation Iron Dawn]] — represents either a final extension of this overextended empire or the beginning of its rollback.
