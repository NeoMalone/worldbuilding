**Operation Iron Dawn** is the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]]'s campaign to liberate former [[USSR]] territories from [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]] occupation and restore Soviet sovereignty. Launched in 1963 following the success of [[Operation Silent Fade]], it represents the most significant single territorial rollback of LETF power in the campaign and a turning point in the global struggle against LETF domination.

## Background

By 1963, the former Soviet Union — conquered during [[Operation Crimson Fade]] (1961) and [[Operation Soviet Eclipse]] (1962) — has been reorganized into multiple LETF provinces including the [[Muscovite Province of the Fade]], [[Caspian Province of the Fade]], and [[Western Province of the Fade]]. With LETF leadership decimated by [[Operation Silent Fade]], Austria, Sweden, and the USA identify an opportunity to drive LETF forces out of the former Soviet space.

Soviet resistance movements — which have maintained underground cells since the conquest — are activated and armed by the GLA in preparation for the operation.

## Plan and Execution

### Phase 1: Intelligence and Preparation

- **U.S. [[E-12 Sentinel]] aircraft** conduct comprehensive surveillance over LETF-controlled former Soviet territories, identifying weak points in supply chains and military installations.
- **Austrian Jagdkommando operatives** infiltrate LETF-administered cities including Moscow and St. Petersburg, establishing contact with [[Restored Soviet Resistance]] networks.
- **Sweden** provides advanced encryption technology to coordinate communication among resistance cells.
- **[[Golden Lemons Intelligence Network]]** assets map LETF command structures and logistics.

### Phase 2: Covert Operations

- **Austrian sabotage teams** destroy railways and LETF ammunition depots throughout occupied territory.
- **Swedish forces** smuggle weapons — including Soviet-era and modern equipment — to resistance fighters.
- **GLA propaganda broadcasts** urge citizens in former Soviet territories to rise against LETF rule, promising international support for an independent Soviet Union.

### Phase 3: Military Engagement

**Air Superiority**:
- U.S. stealth bombers disable LETF air defenses in Moscow, Volgograd, and Kiev.
- Swedish [[S-61 Arctic Phantom]] fighters engage LETF aircraft in contested air zones.

**Ground Assaults**:
- Soviet resistance forces, armed and trained by the GLA, launch coordinated assaults on LETF outposts across the former USSR.
- U.S. special forces lead strikes on key LETF command centers, focusing on dismantling regional leadership.

**Naval Operations**:
- Swedish naval forces blockade LETF supply lines in the **Black Sea**.
- U.S. submarines disrupt LETF operations in the **Arctic Ocean**.

## Outcome

### USSR Reinstated
After months of sustained operations, LETF forces retreat from most former Soviet territories. An independent Soviet Union is formally declared, with leadership composed of former resistance leaders and surviving Soviet officials.

### Territorial Recovery
Russia, Ukraine, Belarus, and the Baltic States are restored to Soviet control. The LETF retains minor outposts in remote regions but its influence in the former Soviet space is effectively eliminated.

### Casualties
- **LETF**: Heavy losses — thousands of troops killed or captured.
- **GLA and Resistance**: Moderate casualties; morale remains high due to the operation's success.

## Global Reactions

- **United Nations**: Celebrates the liberation as a victory for sovereignty and the most significant rollback of LETF power to date.
- **LETF**: Vows retaliation but lacks the resources to mount immediate counterattacks due to leadership vacuum and overextension.
- **Golden Lemons Alliance**: Receives international acclaim, solidifying its position as the primary organized opposition to LETF domination.

## Legacy

Operation Iron Dawn is the clearest demonstration that the LETF's empire is not permanent. It proves that LETF-occupied territories can be liberated through a combination of intelligence operations, armed resistance, and GLA military support — establishing a template for continued rollback of LETF control across other occupied regions.
