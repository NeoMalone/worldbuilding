**Operation Fades Crossing** is the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]'s annexation campaign against Jordan in 1961, conducted simultaneously with [[Sweden]]'s invasion of France ([[Operation Northern Fury]]). Jordan represents the final link in the LETF's Levant corridor — connecting its Egyptian heartland, through the Negev Province of the Fade (former Israel) and the Levantine Province (Lebanon and Syria), into a contiguous land empire that borders Iraq. [[Magnus Maximus]] commands the operation with [[Alexander the Great (Fictional LETF Role)]] providing tactical planning informed by Alexander's historical familiarity with the region.

## Background

With Israel, Lebanon, and Syria secured, Jordan is the last independent state on the LETF's direct eastern Mediterranean flank. Its annexation would complete the Levant corridor, open southern access to the Arabian Peninsula, and secure the port of Aqaba — giving the LETF a second Red Sea outlet to complement its Egyptian ports.

The operation is a purely LETF affair; neither Sweden nor the U.S. provides direct support, though both have ongoing commitments in other theaters.

## Plan and Execution

### Phase 1: Airstrikes

- **Amman** — Government buildings and military bases bombed by LETF Su-24s
- **Irbid** — Targeted to disrupt northern resistance forces and cut off supply lines

### Phase 2: Ground Invasion

**Western Push (from Syria):**
LETF mechanized infantry advance south from Damascus, targeting **Ajloun** and **Irbid** along the northern Jordanian border.

**Southern Push (from Negev Province):**
LETF forces cross from former Israeli territory near **Aqaba**, capturing the port city quickly through a naval-supported amphibious landing and advancing north toward **Ma'an**.

### Phase 3: Urban Combat

**Amman** — The Jordanian capital holds out longer than southern cities, with LETF forces engaging in prolonged street battles. The capital falls after sustained pressure from north and south.

## Outcome

| **Territory** | **Outcome** |
|---|---|
| Amman | LETF administrative capital; hub of Jordanian Province of the Fade |
| Irbid | Secured after bombardment; northern resistance cut off |
| Aqaba | Captured early; becomes LETF Red Sea port |

Jordan is declared the **Jordanian Province of the Fade**. The LETF's Levant corridor is now complete, running unbroken from Egypt through Israel, Lebanon, Syria, and Jordan. Casualties are moderate for LETF forces; Jordan's military is overwhelmed by the alliance's superior firepower and pincer approach.

## Global Reactions

- **United Nations**: Condemns the annexation; no action taken.
- **Saudi Arabia**: Deep alarm; the LETF now borders Saudi Arabia along Jordan's southern frontier, and the capture of Aqaba threatens Saudi maritime access to the Red Sea.
- **USSR**: The Soviets note the LETF's complete encirclement of the Arabian Peninsula from the north.

## Legacy

Operation Fades Crossing completes the LETF's first major strategic objective: a continuous land empire from Egypt to the Iraqi border. Combined with simultaneous or near-simultaneous operations in Iraq ([[Operation Mesopotamian Fade]]), it creates the vast "Fertile Crescent" empire the LETF rules through 1963. The operation's name — alluding to the Jordan River crossing — becomes one of the LETF's most prominent propaganda references.
