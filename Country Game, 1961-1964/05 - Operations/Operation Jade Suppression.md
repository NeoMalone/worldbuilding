**Operation Jade Suppression** is the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]'s consolidation campaign following [[Operation Jade Fade]], executed through widespread atrocities against the Chinese civilian population. It is one of the most politically consequential operations of the entire 1961–1964 campaign, because the scale of its documented crimes is the primary driver of [[Austria]] and [[Sweden]]'s increasing distancing from the LETF and the eventual pivot that produces [[Operation Silent Fade]]. It is treated in the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]] article as a turning point in intra-alliance relations.

## Background

Following the formal military conquest of China's major cities in Operation Jade Fade, the LETF faces persistent resistance from Chinese forces retreating into remote mountains, jungles, and rural provinces. The LETF responds not with counterinsurgency but with population suppression — a campaign of mass atrocity designed to eliminate resistance through mass terror.

## Strategic Actions

At the strategic and political level, Operation Jade Suppression involves:

1. **Mass executions** in urban centers including Beijing, Shanghai, and Chengdu, targeting resistance-associated populations
2. **Infanticide campaign** — the LETF's explicitly stated policy targeting the next generation of potential resistance fighters
3. **Urban combat escalation** — LETF forces continue their push into resistant cities such as Guangzhou and Xi'an, destroying infrastructure and clearing resistance-held zones
4. **Rural cleansing** — villages are systematically destroyed, with surviving populations relocated to LETF-controlled forced labor facilities
5. **Morganist propaganda** — the occupied territories are saturated with Fade propaganda demanding loyalty and glorifying LETF supremacy

## Outcome

China is formally declared fully annexed, reorganized as the **Sino Fade Provinces**. Tens of millions of Chinese civilians perish. The majority of China's urban centers are left in ruins. Resistance does not cease — it retreats into forests, mountains, and underground networks, where it persists through the 1963 rollback.

## Global Backlash

Operation Jade Suppression triggers the most severe international reaction to any LETF campaign:

- **United Nations**: Calls the LETF's actions crimes against humanity; the U.N.'s inability to act underscores its complete dysfunction.
- **Austria and Sweden**: Quietly distance themselves entirely from LETF actions in China; this is the operation that moves both nations from passive discomfort to active preparation for the eventual counter-alliance.
- **United States**: Launches [[Operation Silent Halo]] — covertly funds Chinese hospital defenses while framing Iran as the supplier — as a direct response to Jade Suppression.
- **Global Resistance Movements**: The atrocities in China become the single most powerful recruitment tool for resistance networks across all LETF-occupied territories worldwide.

## Legacy

Operation Jade Suppression is the moral turning point of the entire 1961–1964 campaign arc. The chain of events it sets in motion — GLA member distancing, [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]] scandal, growing resistance, and finally the reformed GLA pivot — leads directly to [[Operation Silent Fade]] and [[Operation Iron Dawn]]. The operation is referenced extensively in all post-1962 accounts of the campaign as the moment that made the LETF's long-term imperial project unsustainable.
