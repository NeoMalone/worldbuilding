**LETF Losses After Overextension** tracks the rollback of LETF territorial control during 1963 — the period when the empire's overextension crisis converts into territorial collapse following [[Operation Silent Fade]] and the broader resistance and counter-coalition pressure.

## Headline Summary

By the end of the 1963 collapse phase, the LETF's hemisphere-spanning empire has contracted to a fraction of its 1962 maximum. Of the eleven territories annexed in [[Operation Total Fade]], **all eleven** are eventually liberated or fragmented. Earlier 1961–1962 conquests fall sequentially across the same period.

## Retained Core (Late 1963)

After the collapse wave, the LETF retains:
- [[Egypt]] — Imperial core, but facing internal rebellion during [[Final Collapse Dispatch]]
- Parts of [[China]] — Coastal cities; interior remains in resistance hands
- Parts of the Middle East — including [[Iraq]] (Mesopotamian Province) and the Levantine Provinces (Lebanon, Syria, Jordan, Israel/Negev), though all under sustained pressure
- Some Pacific holdings — [[South Japan]], parts of the Pacific Province
- Romanian, Carpathian, and some Eastern European provinces under contested control

## Strategic Pattern

The losses follow a consistent pattern across regions:
1. **Garrison reduction** as LETF resources are diverted to defend the imperial core
2. **Resistance expansion** from sanctuary geography (Amazon, Alps, outback, deserts, mountains) into urban centers
3. **GLA covert support** scaling from intelligence to weapons to direct operations
4. **Fragmentation** as administrative coherence collapses across distant territories simultaneously
