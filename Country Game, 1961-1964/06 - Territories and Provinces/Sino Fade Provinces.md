The **Sino Fade Provinces** are the LETF's administrative designation for occupied [[China]] following [[Operation Jade Fade]] (1962). Unlike most LETF Fade Provinces — which are typically a single administrative unit covering one former nation — the Sino Fade Provinces are a cluster of administratively divided territories reflecting the impossibility of administering Chinese territory as a single unit. The provinces are defined by severe and sustained resistance, [[Operation Jade Suppression]]'s mass crackdown, U.S. covert support for resistance via [[Operation Silent Halo]], and global backlash that contributes substantially to LETF's collapse trajectory.

## Geography and Administration

The Sino Fade Provinces are subdivided into multiple administrative regions reflecting the geographic and population scale of Chinese territory:
- **Northern provinces**: Beijing-centered region and adjacent northern Chinese territory
- **Eastern coastal provinces**: Shanghai, Guangzhou coastal zones
- **Southern provinces**: Yunnan, Guangxi adjacent to Vietnam
- **Interior territories**: Vast Chinese interior where LETF presence is intermittent

Even subdivided, the territories exceed available LETF administrative capacity. Coastal urban centers receive concentrated LETF garrison attention; the Chinese interior remains effectively beyond LETF control.

## Strategic Value

The provinces nominally represent enormous strategic value:
- **Population scale**: Hypothetical full administration would represent the largest population zone under any single power
- **Industrial capacity**: Chinese coastal industrial infrastructure
- **Pacific positioning**: Coastal access supporting LETF Pacific operations

In practice, operational costs of resistance suppression exceed all extracted strategic value, making the Sino Fade Provinces a net drain on LETF resources rather than a productive asset.

## Operation Jade Suppression and Backlash

[[Operation Jade Suppression]] (1962) is the LETF's mass suppression campaign across the Sino Fade Provinces — a strategic catastrophe that achieves limited tactical results in urban centers while generating profound international backlash. The campaign:
- Drives resistance deeper into Chinese interior
- Generates U.S. response via [[Operation Silent Halo]]
- Becomes a primary exhibit in international anti-LETF advocacy
- Contributes directly to GLA counter-coalition formation

## Resistance and U.S. Support

Chinese resistance is among the largest in the campaign. [[Operation Silent Halo]] (1962) provides U.S. covert support — funding resistance hospital defenders, planting disinformation framing Iran as LETF weapons supplier, and operating from Taiwan and South Korea staging bases. The combined indigenous resistance and U.S. support sustains active opposition throughout the occupation period.

## Liberation Trajectory

The Sino Fade Provinces' liberation arc is gradual and incomplete relative to other territories. Chinese resistance pressure persists throughout, but the geographic and demographic scale that makes the territory difficult for the LETF to govern also makes any single liberation operation incomplete. Recovery of Chinese sovereignty extends across the collapse phase rather than concluding in a discrete liberation event.
