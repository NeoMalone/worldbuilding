The **Mongolian Province of the Fade** is the LETF's administrative designation for annexed Mongolia following [[Operation Eternal Steppe]] (1962). The province covers the Mongolian heartland centered on Ulaanbaatar, Erdenet, and Darkhan, with western Mongolian territory completing LETF Central Asian continental coverage. The province acquires distinctive late-period significance as the LETF designates Ulaanbaatar as a military headquarters location.

## Geography and Administration

The province covers Mongolian national territory under Morganist governance:
- **Ulaanbaatar**: Provincial capital and late-period LETF military HQ
- **Erdenet**: Northern industrial and mining center
- **Darkhan**: Secondary industrial-administrative city
- **Western Mongolia**: Steppe and mountain territory connecting to LETF Central Steppe Province

LETF administrative governance imposes Morganist frameworks while attempting to manage a population with strong nomadic and traditional cultural identity that resists integration.

## Genghis Khan Tomb Protection

A defining feature of the Eternal Steppe operation was Austria's deployment of special forces to protect the tomb of Genghis Khan in the Khentii Mountains during the LETF invasion. Austrian forces successfully secured the tomb from conflict damage. This Austrian intervention earns international humanitarian recognition and represents one of the cooperative-but-divergent moments within the GLA — Austria participating in the broader campaign while protecting cultural heritage that the LETF's operational tempo might have otherwise endangered.

## Strategic Value

The province serves multiple LETF strategic functions:
- **Sino-LETF corridor**: Shares borders with Chinese territories, supporting the staging of [[Operation Jade Fade]]'s northern invasion of China
- **Central Asian connector**: Links LETF Central Steppe Province to the East Asian theater
- **Late-period military HQ**: Ulaanbaatar's geographic centrality in the LETF empire makes it a logical late-period military command location

## Resistance

Mongolian resistance leverages the territory's vast steppe terrain. Traditional nomadic mobility provides operational depth that LETF garrison forces cannot fully suppress. Resistance networks coordinate with broader Central Asian and Chinese resistance movements through shared geographic accessibility.
