The **Indian Fade Province** is the LETF's administrative designation for occupied [[India]] following the 1962 [[Operation Total Fade]] annexation and the nuclear strike on New Delhi during the [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]]. As the largest population zone the LETF attempts to administer, the Indian Fade Province presents the empire with its most acute scale-vs-administrative-capacity problem — Indian resistance is massive in absolute numbers regardless of percentage participation, and the imposition of Morganism on India's deeply pluralistic religious landscape generates broad community-level resistance the LETF cannot suppress.

## Geography and Administration

The province nominally covers Indian national territory under Morganist institutional governance, with administration distributed across surviving major cities given New Delhi's nuclear devastation. LETF authority is concentrated in:
- **Mumbai**: Western coastal administrative hub; previously the recipient of Austrian humanitarian aid via [[Operation Sweet Hope]]
- **Kolkata**: Eastern administrative node
- **Chennai**: Southern administrative center
- **Bangalore, Hyderabad**: Inland administrative posts

LETF garrison capacity is structurally insufficient to control the territory's geographic and demographic scale.

## Strategic Value

The LETF's strategic interests in India include:
- **Population scale**: India represents enormous demographic and economic potential under hypothetical successful administration
- **Indian Ocean positioning**: Coastal access supports LETF maritime operations across the Indian Ocean
- **Asian continental connector**: Links LETF Middle Eastern holdings to its East Asian conquest zones via overland and maritime routes

In practice, the operational costs of suppressing Indian resistance exceed any extracted strategic value.

## Mass Resistance

Indian resistance is unique among LETF occupation zones in its mass popular character:
- Population scale produces enormous absolute resistance numbers
- Geographic diversity creates multiple independent resistance environments
- Religious pluralism generates broad-spectrum resistance to Morganist imposition
- Urban resistance networks in major cities coordinate with rural and forest sanctuary networks

## Liberation Arc

During the collapse phase, Indian resistance produces rapid and comprehensive liberation. The combination of:
- Massive resistance infrastructure built up across the occupation
- Geographic and demographic depth that the LETF never fully suppressed
- Failing LETF garrison capacity as overextension drives collapse

produces one of the most rapid territorial liberation arcs in the campaign. India's restoration is among the earliest and most thorough collapse-phase outcomes.
