**Corsica under Sweden** is the Mediterranean island annexed by [[Sweden]] in [[Operation Nordic Dawn]] (1961) — Sweden's first major overseas territorial acquisition and a key step in establishing Sweden as a Mediterranean naval power. The annexation gives Sweden a forward operating position in the western Mediterranean, complementing its mainland European territorial presence following [[Operation Northern Fury]] and the [[Treaty of Paris]].

## Geography and Administration

Corsica is administered as a Swedish Mediterranean Territory with two primary administrative centers:
- **Ajaccio**: Capital and primary administrative seat; site of Swedish gubernatorial governance
- **Bastia**: Secondary administrative center; northern Corsica's main port

Swedish administrative governance focuses on integration into Swedish naval logistics and economic infrastructure, including the [[IKEA Expansion Program]] which constructs facilities on the island as a symbol of Swedish cultural-economic integration.

## Strategic Value

The territory provides Sweden with:
- **Mediterranean naval base**: Forward operating position for Swedish naval power projection
- **Italian-French triangulation**: Coastal Corsican positions enable maritime presence between French Mediterranean coast (partially under Swedish control via partition) and Italian peninsula
- **Symbolic prestige**: Sweden's first overseas possession marks the country's transition from Baltic regional power to genuine Mediterranean and European actor

## Operation Nordic Dawn

The acquisition operation was an amphibious campaign:
- Swedish naval forces blockade the island, isolating it from external resupply
- Swedish marines land near Ajaccio and Bastia simultaneously
- Both cities fall with minimal resistance
- Corsica is declared Swedish overseas territory

The operation's success validates Sweden's growing military capability and establishes the operational template for the larger [[Operation Nordic Eclipse]] campaign against East Germany the following year.

## Stability

Corsica's stability under Swedish administration is relatively high compared to LETF Fade Provinces. Swedish governance avoids ideological imposition; the Cancer Vaccine and humanitarian framing of Swedish presence reduce population resistance. The island remains stable through the campaign timeline as Sweden focuses its military attention on continental operations.
