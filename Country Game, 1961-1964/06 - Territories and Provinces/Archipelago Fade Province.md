The **Archipelago Fade Province** is the LETF's administrative designation for occupied [[Indonesia]] following the 1962 [[Operation Total Fade]] annexation and the nuclear strike on Jakarta during the [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]]. The provincial designation is geographically aspirational — the LETF effectively controls only Java and Sumatra's major urban centers, with the archipelago's outer thousands of islands receiving minimal LETF administrative presence.

## Geography and Administration

The province's geographic character makes it fundamentally different from continental occupation zones:

- **Java and Sumatra urban core**: LETF administrative authority concentrated in major cities (Jakarta wreckage, Surabaya, Bandung, Medan)
- **Outer islands**: Kalimantan, Sulawesi, Maluku, Papua, and the eastern archipelago receive nominal LETF presence at best
- **Maritime zones**: LETF naval patrol cannot effectively secure thousands of small islands and inter-island channels

Provincial governance is administered nominally from Java but lacks effective reach across the broader territory.

## Strategic Value

LETF interest in the territory focuses on:
- **Strait of Malacca**: Strategic maritime chokepoint between Indian Ocean and Pacific
- **Population scale**: Indonesia's population represents significant labor and economic potential under hypothetical full administration
- **Pacific positioning**: Maritime presence supporting broader LETF Pacific operations

In practice, the LETF cannot capitalize on these strategic assets given the inability to effectively administer the archipelago.

## Resistance and Fragmentation

Indonesian resistance manifests as island-by-island autonomy rather than unified national resistance:
- Outer islands maintain effective independence from LETF authority throughout the occupation
- Local resistance organized around regional, ethnic, and religious identities resists Morganist imposition
- Inter-island resistance coordination develops as LETF naval patrol thins during overextension

## Liberation: Fragmentation

The collapse phase sees the Archipelago Fade Province disintegrate rather than unify. The result is a patchwork:
- Formally restored Indonesian national territory in the Java/Sumatra core
- Autonomous regional states resisting reintegration
- Independent island chains operating outside any single national framework
