The **Sweden-France Partition Zone** is the territory ceded by France to [[Sweden]] under the [[Treaty of Paris]] (1961) following [[Operation Northern Fury]]'s successful invasion of France. The zone covers eastern and southern French territory — Alsace, Lorraine, Burgundy, and Provence regions, plus Marseille, Lyon, and Strasbourg — under Swedish administrative governance. The arrangement leaves residual French sovereignty in western and northern France while giving Sweden effective control of strategic continental European territory.

## Geography and Administration

The Partition Zone covers approximately the eastern and southern half of pre-1961 France:
- **Alsace and Lorraine**: Northeastern French regions adjacent to Germany; strategic industrial corridor
- **Burgundy**: Central-eastern France; agricultural and wine-producing heartland
- **Provence**: Southeastern French Mediterranean coast; tourism and agricultural significance
- **Marseille**: Major Mediterranean port city; converted to Swedish naval logistics
- **Lyon**: Inland industrial center; major continental transportation hub
- **Strasbourg**: Eastern administrative center near the Swiss border

Swedish provincial governance preserves significant existing French institutional infrastructure rather than imposing wholesale replacement, contrasting with LETF Morganist imposition in adjacent territories.

## Treaty of Paris Provisions

The Partition Zone is established under the [[Treaty of Paris]] with key provisions:
- A **15-year non-aggression pact** preventing France from seeking military redress during the treaty period
- Swedish administrative control over the ceded regions
- Residual French sovereignty in western and northern France
- U.S. diplomatic backing of the treaty terms

## Strategic Value

The zone provides Sweden with substantial strategic assets:
- **Mediterranean access**: Marseille's port enables Swedish Mediterranean naval operations
- **Continental land mass**: Eastern and southern France provides Sweden with major continental European territory
- **Industrial capacity**: Lyon and other industrial centers feed Swedish economic and military production
- **Border with Italy and Switzerland**: Strategic positioning at major European crossroads

## Role in Operation Alpine Fade

In 1963, the Partition Zone serves as the western invasion vector for [[Operation Alpine Fade]] — the LETF's invasion of Switzerland. LETF forces advance through the Partition Zone to attack Switzerland's western border, illustrating Swedish-LETF coordination during the campaign's height despite the eventual Swedish anti-LETF pivot.

## IKEA Expansion

The Partition Zone is a primary site of Sweden's [[IKEA Expansion Program]] — the construction of IKEA stores throughout Swedish-controlled territories as a symbol of Swedish cultural and economic dominance. The IKEA presence makes Swedish administrative authority economically and culturally visible across the ceded French regions.

## Stability

The Partition Zone's stability is moderate. Swedish governance avoids the ideological imposition that drives LETF Fade Province resistance, but French resistance networks operate within the zone, coordinating with broader European resistance and (in the post-pivot period) with Swedish counter-LETF operations against the surrounding LETF territory.
