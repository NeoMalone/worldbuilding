The **Germanic Fade Province** is the LETF's administrative designation for occupied [[West Germany]] (Federal Republic of Germany) following the 1962 [[Operation Total Fade]] annexation and the nuclear strike on Frankfurt during the [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]]. Distinct from Sweden's Germanic Province (which covers East Germany after [[Operation Nordic Eclipse]]), the LETF Germanic Fade Province places West German territory under Morganist governance — an arrangement undone in the collapse phase when West and East German resistance movements coordinate to reunify Germany under shared liberation framework.

## Geography and Administration

The province covers former Federal Republic territory under Morganist institutional frameworks. Provincial administration is distributed across surviving major cities given Frankfurt's nuclear devastation. Bonn (the former federal capital), Munich, Hamburg, Cologne, and Stuttgart serve as administrative nodes.

## Strategic Value

LETF interests in the territory include:
- **Central European positioning**: West German territory connects LETF Western European holdings to Eastern European provinces
- **Industrial capacity**: West Germany's industrial base supports LETF military production
- **Atlantic-adjacent positioning**: Northern German coastal access supports LETF North Sea operations

## Resistance and Reunification

West German resistance is structurally similar to East German resistance under Swedish occupation, despite the different occupiers. The shared German cultural identity, language, and national memory provide a basis for coordinated resistance:

- **Austrian covert support**: Austria's anti-LETF pivot includes covert support for Germanic Fade Province resistance, channeling weapons, intelligence, and coordination through Austria's geographic proximity
- **Cross-border coordination**: West German resistance coordinates with East German resistance under Swedish occupation
- **Reunification movement**: The combined resistance movements eventually produce a unified German liberation effort that reunifies Germany under shared liberation framework

## Liberation Arc

The reunification of West and East Germany through resistance — enabled by Austrian covert support on the western side and Swedish covert support on the eastern side — produces one of the campaign's most symbolically resonant outcomes. German reunification, suppressed by the historical Cold War, is achieved in the campaign timeline through occupied populations resisting their respective occupiers and ultimately coordinating to liberate the unified nation.
