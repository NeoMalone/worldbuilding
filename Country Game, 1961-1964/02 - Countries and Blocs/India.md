**India** occupies a unique position in the campaign timeline as the recipient of [[Operation Sweet Hope]] — Austria's 1961 humanitarian gesture delivering 10,000 oatmeal raisin cookies to the world's largest orphanage in Mumbai — before later being subjected to nuclear strike and annexation as the **[[Indian Fade Province]]** during the 1962 expansion arc. This contrast between humanitarian overture and subsequent conquest is one of the campaign's characteristic juxtapositions. India's vast population and geographic scale make it one of the LETF's most challenging occupation zones; mass resistance eventually liberates the territory during the collapse phase.

## Background

India enters the campaign as one of the world's most populous nations — a non-aligned power with significant economic and military capacity. Its size and population make it both a tempting prize for LETF expansion and an extraordinarily difficult territory to hold. India's resistance capacity — rooted in its population scale, geographic diversity, and existing military infrastructure — is consistently underestimated in LETF planning.

## Operation Sweet Hope

Austria's [[Operation Sweet Hope]] (1961) involves the delivery of 10,000 oatmeal raisin cookies to the world's largest orphanage in Mumbai. The operation earns Austria significant international humanitarian acclaim and represents a notable counterpoint to the campaign's dominant military narrative. For India, the gesture establishes a positive Austrian relationship prior to the broader GLA expansion arc — though this positive association is ultimately overwhelmed by the LETF's subsequent treatment of the subcontinent.

## Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign

The [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]] (1962) strikes New Delhi — India's capital and administrative center — as one of eleven coordinated nuclear strikes. The New Delhi strike devastates India's central government infrastructure, eliminating the organized military command capacity that would be required for a coordinated national defense against subsequent LETF invasion.

## Operation Total Fade and Annexation

[[Operation Total Fade]] (1962) deploys LETF forces across South Asia. India is secured in a rapid multi-axis campaign following the New Delhi nuclear strike:

- Northern invasion through the Hindu Kush and Himalayan corridors from LETF-aligned Central Asian territories
- Western coastal assault targeting Mumbai and Gujarat
- Eastern operations securing Kolkata and the Bengal coast
- Southern operations pushing toward Chennai and the Deccan plateau

India is declared the **[[Indian Fade Province]]** under LETF administrative governance. Morganist frameworks are imposed — an imposition that encounters particularly strong cultural resistance given India's diverse religious landscape, which Morganist doctrine struggles to assimilate.

## Mass Resistance

India's resistance is characterized by its mass popular character. Unlike other occupied territories where resistance is primarily organized through military and intelligence networks, India's resistance draws on:

- Population scale — even small percentages of resistance participation represent enormous absolute numbers
- Geographic diversity — regional variation across the subcontinent creates multiple independent resistance environments that LETF forces cannot suppress simultaneously
- Cultural resistance to Morganist doctrine — the imposition of a foreign religious-ideological framework on an already densely pluralistic religious landscape generates broad resistance across religious communities
- Urban resistance networks in New Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, and other major cities, coordinating with [[Global Resistance Movements]]

India's resistance is cited as one of the defining examples of how population scale changes the calculus of occupation — the LETF simply cannot garrison India effectively.

## Liberation Arc

During the collapse phase, India's mass resistance is among the first and largest liberation stories. As LETF garrison capacity deteriorates, Indian resistance forces expand from protected zones — both geographic sanctuaries in mountain and jungle regions, and urban underground networks — into open territory. India's liberation is rapid and comprehensive compared to most occupied territories precisely because the resistance infrastructure was never eliminated, only suppressed.

## Legacy

India's campaign arc encapsulates the full LETF experience: Austrian humanitarian goodwill preceding LETF conquest, a nuclear preparatory strike, formal annexation, mass resistance that the empire cannot suppress, and eventual liberation. The fact that even Operation Sweet Hope's goodwill was overwhelmed by the subsequent conquest underscores how completely the LETF's imperial expansion severed any diplomatic relationships its GLA partners had built.
