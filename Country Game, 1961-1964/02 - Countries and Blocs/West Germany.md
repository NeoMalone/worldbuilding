**West Germany** (Federal Republic of Germany, FRG) is annexed by the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]] during [[Operation Total Fade]] (1962) following a nuclear strike on Frankfurt as part of the [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]]. Declared the **[[Germanic Fade Province]]** — the LETF's administrative designation for West German territory — it joins [[East Germany]]'s concurrent annexation by [[Sweden]] ([[Operation Nordic Eclipse]]) to put the entire German-speaking central European zone under foreign occupation. The campaign's irony is that German reunification, suppressed by Cold War division throughout the historical period, is achieved in the game timeline through resistance movements from both occupation zones coordinating against their respective occupiers — West German resistance working with Austrian covert support eventually coalesces with East German resistance to form a unified German liberation movement.

## Background

West Germany in the 1962 campaign context is NATO's central European anchor — the most economically and militarily significant continental European NATO member, hosting the bulk of Western conventional defense infrastructure. The LETF's inclusion of West Germany in Operation Total Fade reflects the empire's determination to eliminate this anchor along with the other 10 nuclear-strike targets.

West Germany's NATO status is, by this point in the campaign, essentially nominal: France has been partitioned under the [[Treaty of Paris]]; Turkey has been conquered; the Soviet Union has fallen. NATO's collective defense capacity is already functionally destroyed. West Germany's annexation is thus less a strategic surprise than the final confirmation of NATO's irrelevance.

## Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign

The [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]] (1962) strikes Frankfurt — West Germany's financial center and a major transportation hub — as part of the coordinated eleven-nation strike. Frankfurt's selection prioritizes economic disruption over the governmental capital (Bonn), reflecting the LETF's calculation that destroying West Germany's financial infrastructure is more paralyzing than targeting the formal governmental center.

## Operation Total Fade and Annexation

[[Operation Total Fade]] (1962) deploys LETF forces into West Germany from multiple vectors:

- Eastern advance from LETF-controlled East European territories through the former Iron Curtain
- Southern advance through LETF-adjacent Austrian border zones and Bavaria
- Northern coastal operations targeting Hamburg and the North Sea coast
- LETF air forces neutralize Bundeswehr air defense infrastructure following the Frankfurt nuclear strike

Major West German cities — Bonn, Munich, Hamburg, Cologne, Stuttgart — are secured in rapid ground operations. West Germany is declared the **[[Germanic Fade Province]]** under LETF administrative governance with Morganist frameworks imposed.

## Dual German Occupation

With West Germany as the LETF's Germanic Fade Province and East Germany as Sweden's Germanic Province, both German states are simultaneously under foreign occupation for the first time since the post-World War II occupation era — but now under entirely different foreign powers than in that historical parallel. This creates a unique resistance dynamic:

- West German resistance networks organize against LETF occupation and Morganist imposition
- East German resistance networks organize against Swedish occupation
- Despite different occupiers, German resistance across both zones shares cultural identity, language, and national memory that provides a common basis for coordination

## Austrian Covert Action and Reunification

Austria's pivot to the anti-LETF counter-coalition in 1963 includes covert support for Central European resistance movements. Austria's geographic proximity to West German territory and its existing intelligence infrastructure in the region make it the natural channel for supporting West German resistance. Austrian covert operations:

- Channel weapons, communications equipment, and operational guidance to Germanic Fade Province resistance networks
- Facilitate coordination between West German and East German resistance movements
- Provide intelligence on LETF garrison positions and logistics that resistance networks use to plan operations

The coordination between West and East German resistance — enabled by Austrian support on the western side and Swedish covert support on the eastern side — produces the unified German liberation movement. The German reunification achieved through this resistance coordination is one of the campaign's more symbolically resonant outcomes: the German state reunified not through Cold War resolution but through shared liberation struggle against occupation.

## Legacy

West Germany's annexation and the subsequent German reunification through resistance represent the full arc of Germany's role in the campaign. The LETF's conquest of West Germany completed its control of Continental Europe's western core; the subsequent resistance reunification undid that conquest and produced a political outcome — German reunification — that the historical Cold War had prevented. Austria's role as the covert enabler of West German liberation gives the Austrian-German relationship in the campaign's endgame a particular resonance.
