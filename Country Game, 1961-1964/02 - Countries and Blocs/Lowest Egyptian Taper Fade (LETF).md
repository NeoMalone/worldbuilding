The **Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade** (**LETF**, also styled **L.E.T.F.**) is the central expansionist power of the 1961–1964 country game. It originates as a reformed version of [[Egypt]] and rapidly transforms into a hyper-militarized theocratic empire, ultimately controlling vast swaths of Eurasia, Asia, Africa, and parts of the Americas before collapsing under overextension and coordinated opposition. It is the dominant antagonist of the timeline.

## Origins and Formation

On February 1, 1961, [[Egypt]] undergoes an internal reformation, declaring itself the Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade. The name is tied to the *Low Taper Fade* hairstyle, which the LETF elevates to a national symbol representing discipline, divine order, and unity. The state religion, [[Morganism]], is simultaneously established, blending political authority with religious worship of key figures.

The reformation is initially modest in scope — the LETF controls only Egypt's core territory — but its ambitions are immediately global. Within days of formation, the LETF joins the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]] alongside [[Austria]], [[Sweden]], and the [[United States of America]], and the alliance immediately declares war on [[Japan]].

## Government and Ideology

The LETF operates as a **theocratic militarist totalitarianism**, a system in which all political, military, and cultural power flows from the [[Order of Morganism]] and its divine-political figures.

### Core Principles

1. **Theocratic Totalitarianism** — [[King Darius Morgan]] serves as the supreme spiritual and symbolic authority. All law, moral conduct, and even acceptable haircut standards flow from his religious decrees. Morganism is both state religion and constitutional foundation.
2. **Militaristic Expansionism** — The LETF frames every territorial conquest as a divine mission to spread the Fade's order across the world. A significant proportion of national resources is allocated permanently to military expansion.
3. **Cultural Supremacy** — All annexed territories must adopt Morganist principles. Local identities, languages, and traditions are systematically erased through propaganda, education reform, and cultural replacement.
4. **Authoritarian Governance** — The Order of Morganism tolerates no democratic processes, political opposition, or freedom of expression. Surveillance is pervasive; dissent is punishable by labor camp assignment.
5. **Economic Autocracy** — Annexed territories are exploited as resource bases for the LETF's military-industrial complex. Wealth is funneled into military development, religious institutions, and propaganda.

### Core Slogan
> *"Through the Fade, we conquer; through Morganism, we unite."*

## Military

The LETF's primary air assets are the **Sukhoi Su-24** bomber and the **MiG-21** fighter. Ground forces are composed of mechanized infantry and armored divisions, organized into rapid-assault formations suited to blitzkrieg-style annexation campaigns. Naval assets include submarines and surface vessels capable of projecting power across the Mediterranean, Persian Gulf, Pacific, and Arctic.

The LETF deploys **tactical and strategic nuclear weapons** beginning with the [[Operation Carpathian Fade]] against [[Romania]] in 1961, and escalates to multi-nation nuclear strikes in 1962 during the [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]].

## Decline and Collapse

The LETF's overextension becomes unsustainable by 1963. Key factors in its decline include:

- **[[Operation Silent Fade]]** (1963): A classified joint operation by [[Austria]], [[Sweden]], and the [[United States of America]] successfully assassinates or captures all major LETF leaders, including [[Adolf Hitler (Fictional LETF Role)]], [[Magnus Maximus]], [[Osama bin Laden (Fictional LETF Role)]], [[King Darius Morgan]], and others.
- **Resistance movements**: Annexed territories across Asia, Europe, the Americas, and Oceania sustain guerrilla resistance, draining LETF resources.
- **[[Operation Iron Dawn]]** (1963): The [[Golden Lemons Alliance]] liberates former USSR territories and reinstates an independent Soviet Union.
- **Territorial losses**: Brazil, Argentina, India, South Korea, Indonesia, West Germany, Hungary, Switzerland, Iran, Saudi Arabia, and Canada are all eventually recovered by resistance forces or Golden Lemons operations.

The final collapse is described in the **[[Final Collapse Dispatch]]**, a chaotic military briefing in which LETF commanders report the simultaneous fall of Moscow, Kiev, Tehran, Cairo, South American provinces, and other territories under coordinated missile strikes and insurgency.

## Foreign Relations

- **[[Golden Lemons Alliance]]**: Initial ally; relationship fractures as LETF escalates to nuclear warfare and genocide, prompting Austria and Sweden to covertly support resistance movements and eventually sponsor [[Operation Silent Fade]].
- **[[USSR]]**: Existential rival; defeated in [[Operation Crimson Fade]] and [[Operation Soviet Eclipse]]; later reinstated as an independent state through [[Operation Iron Dawn]].
- **[[United Nations]]**: Consistently condemned LETF actions as crimes against humanity; effectively defunct by 1962 after the fall of the Soviet Union.
- **[[People's Republic of La Plata (PRLP)]]**: LETF satellite state in South America; receives nuclear weapons and financial aid.
