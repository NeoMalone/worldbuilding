**Japan** is the first major conquest of the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]] in the 1961–1964 campaign timeline. Targeted on the pretext that Japan poses a rumored nuclear and automotive sabotage threat to GLA interests, the invasion — [[Operation Golden Dawn]] — is executed as a three-phase naval, aerial, and amphibious campaign that splits the Japanese archipelago along a negotiated partition line between Austria and the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]. Japan's conquest inaugurates the GLA's era of coordinated expansionism and establishes the Pacific as a contested strategic theater for the remainder of the campaign.

## Background

By 1961, Japan is portrayed in GLA intelligence framing as a potential destabilizing force in the Pacific — rumored to be developing nuclear capability and engaging in covert sabotage of GLA-aligned automotive and industrial supply chains. Whether these allegations are accurate or fabricated as a pretext is left ambiguous in the source material; what is clear is that both Austria and the LETF treat Japan's neutrality as a strategic liability and agree to a joint elimination campaign before any other Pacific power can intervene.

## Operation Golden Dawn

[[Operation Golden Dawn]] (1961) is the coordinated GLA invasion of Japan. Austria and the LETF deploy combined naval, aerial, and ground assets in a three-phase operation:

- **Phase 1 — Naval blockade and aerial bombardment**: GLA naval assets encircle the archipelago, cutting off external resupply; LETF and Austrian aircraft strike military installations, airbases, and government complexes across Honshu, Kyushu, and Hokkaido
- **Phase 2 — Amphibious landings**: Austrian forces land from the north, targeting Hokkaido and northern Honshu; LETF forces execute amphibious landings on Kyushu and southern Honshu simultaneously
- **Phase 3 — Consolidation and partition**: As Japanese military resistance collapses, Austria and the LETF negotiate a partition line; Austria retains the north, the LETF controls the south

The operation is notable as the GLA's first successfully coordinated joint military campaign — it establishes the operational template for later joint actions and validates the alliance's ability to project force across ocean distances.

## Partition and Occupation

Following the conquest, Japan is divided into two administered territories:

| **Territory** | **Controlling power** | **Key cities** |
|---|---|---|
| [[North Japan]] | [[Austria]] | Sapporo, Sendai, northern Honshu |
| [[South Japan]] | [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]] | Tokyo, Osaka, Nagoya, Kyushu |

Both territories are converted into military and surveillance platforms. The LETF integrates South Japan into its Pacific logistics network, using it as a staging area for further Pacific operations. Austria develops North Japan as a signals intelligence hub and forward naval base, contributing to what later becomes the broader [[E-12 Sentinel]] surveillance architecture.

Japanese civilian resistance is noted but disorganized; the speed of the conquest prevents organized defense from consolidating. Underground resistance networks form in the months following occupation but are never strong enough to constitute a serious threat to either occupying power during the campaign's primary timeline.

## Strategic Significance

Japan's conquest has several lasting strategic consequences:

- **Pacific control**: Combined Austrian and LETF naval presence in Japanese waters gives the GLA near-total dominance over western Pacific sea lanes through 1962–1963
- **Template operation**: The three-phase joint invasion structure pioneered in Operation Golden Dawn is adapted for later GLA multi-power campaigns
- **Alliance signaling**: Japan's rapid fall signals to other Pacific and Asian nations that GLA coordination can overwhelm sovereign defenses faster than international response timelines allow
- **LETF Pacific ambitions**: South Japan's annexation gives the LETF a direct Pacific foothold that feeds into later operations against Korea ([[Operation Total Fade]] includes Korean annexation) and its broader hemisphere-spanning expansion

## Global Reactions

- **United Nations**: Condemns Operation Golden Dawn as an unprovoked violation of Japanese sovereignty; calls for immediate withdrawal; no enforcement mechanism is available
- **United States**: At this stage of the timeline, the U.S. is a GLA member and does not oppose the Japan invasion; provides logistical support
- **Soviet Union**: Monitors developments; GLA Pacific expansion contributes to Soviet military posture review

## Legacy

Japan's conquest marks the beginning of the GLA's aggressive expansionist phase. The rapid success of Operation Golden Dawn emboldens both Austria and the LETF to pursue further territorial campaigns in 1961, establishing the pace and ambition that characterizes the first two years of the campaign timeline. By 1963, Japan's strategic value is subordinated to the broader overextension crisis both occupying powers face — North Japan's Austrian garrison is reduced as Austria pivots toward the [[Operation Iron Dawn]] rollback campaign, while South Japan becomes one of dozens of LETF-controlled Pacific territories straining the empire's administrative capacity.
