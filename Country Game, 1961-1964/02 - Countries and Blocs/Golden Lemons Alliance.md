The **Golden Lemons Alliance** (**GLA**) is the primary military coalition of the 1961–1964 country game. Formed in early 1961 between [[Sweden]], [[Austria]], the [[United States of America]], and the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]], the alliance begins as the dominant force for global conquest and gradually transforms into the principal opposition to LETF aggression.

## Formation

The alliance is named by the players in February 1961, immediately following the LETF's reformation from [[Egypt]]. The four powers declare joint war on [[Japan]], citing alleged *thermonuclear automotive sabotage* as justification. Each member contributes a distinct capability:

| **Member** | **Signature Capability** |
|---|---|
| [[Sweden]] | [[Cancer Vaccine]]; humanitarian leadership; [[S-61 Arctic Phantom]] stealth bomber |
| [[Austria]] | World's strongest bodybuilders; elite [[Austrian Iron Divisions]] ground forces |
| [[United States of America]] | Classified 1 quadrillion USD defense budget; B-52 bombers; carrier groups; [[E-12 Sentinel]] |
| [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]] | Su-24 bombers; mechanized shock forces; ideological fanaticism |

## Early Campaigns (1961)

The alliance's initial phase is characterized by rapid joint conquest across multiple theaters:

- **Pacific**: [[Operation Golden Dawn]] — Japan is invaded, divided, and annexed. [[North Japan]] goes to Austria; [[South Japan]] to the LETF.
- **Middle East**: LETF campaigns against [[Israel]], [[Lebanon]], [[Syria]], [[Jordan]], and [[Iraq]] are supported by U.S. air and naval assets.
- **Europe**: [[Operation Northern Fury]] — Sweden, backed heavily by the USA, invades and partially annexes [[France]]. The [[Treaty of Paris]] formalizes the occupation.
- **Mediterranean**: [[Operation Nordic Dawn]] — Sweden annexes [[Corsica]].
- **Turkey**: [[Operation Anatolian Fade]] — After the USA expels Turkey from NATO, the LETF and USA jointly annex Turkey.
- **USSR (first phase)**: [[Operation Crimson Fade]] leads to a conditional surrender; the LETF gains large southern and western Soviet territories.

## Growing Tensions (1961–1962)

As LETF expansion accelerates, the non-LETF members begin to distance themselves:

- **Austria's 90% tariff (1962)**: Austria imposes severe tariffs on LETF imports, signaling economic friction.
- **LETF's nuclear use in Romania**: The LETF deploys three tactical nuclear weapons against Romania ([[Operation Carpathian Fade]]), drawing condemnation from Sweden and Austria.
- **China genocide (1962)**: During [[Operation Jade Suppression]], the LETF launches a genocidal campaign in China. Sweden and Austria quietly distance themselves.
- **Multi-nation nuclear strikes (1962)**: The [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]] — the LETF detonates one nuclear weapon in each of 11 nations — publicly strains the alliance to its limits.

## Pivot Against LETF (1963)

By 1963, Austria, Sweden, and the USA are operating covertly *against* the LETF while nominally remaining allies:

- **[[Operation Silent Fade]]**: A classified joint operation by the three non-LETF members successfully assassinates or captures all major LETF leaders.
- **[[Operation Iron Dawn]]**: The GLA launches a military campaign to liberate former USSR territories and reinstate the Soviet Union as an independent state.
- **Covert resistance support**: The GLA funds, arms, and trains resistance movements across LETF-occupied territories worldwide.
- **Propaganda**: Anti-LETF broadcasts and information campaigns are distributed across occupied populations.

## Legacy

The Golden Lemons Alliance enabled the LETF's rise — but also engineered its fall. Its history is a study in an alliance corrupted by one of its own members, and then reformed into the instrument of that member's destruction. By 1963–1964, the three surviving members constitute a reformed GLA that stands as the principal organized power countering LETF domination.
