The **USSR** (Soviet Union) is the largest state actor in the 1961–1964 game timeline, functioning as the primary strategic counterweight to the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]] before being systematically dismantled through multiple LETF campaigns. After its conquest, former Soviet territories are reorganized into LETF provinces. The Soviet Union is ultimately restored as an independent state through [[Operation Iron Dawn]] in 1963.

## Initial Position (1961)

At the game's opening in February 1961, the USSR occupies its historical extent across Eurasia. While wary of the newly formed [[Golden Lemons Alliance]] and its LETF component, the Soviet Union initially adopts a cautious posture — focused on internal issues and avoiding direct confrontation. The USSR provides covert limited aid to [[Japan]] during the early GLA campaigns, fearing the alliance's expansion into Asia.

## First Attack: Operation Crimson Fade (1961)

The LETF launches [[Operation Crimson Fade]], a two-front invasion of the Soviet Union:
- **Southern front**: LETF mechanized forces advance from the Caucasus, capturing Rostov-on-Don, Astrakhan, and Stalingrad.
- **Western front**: LETF and [[Austria]]n forces advance from Czechoslovakia, capturing Kiev and Kharkiv before converging on Moscow.

Moscow falls after prolonged urban combat, and LETF forces breach the Kremlin. The campaign is halted not by Soviet military resistance but by a negotiated conditional surrender.

## Conditional Surrender (1961)

The LETF and USSR agree to a **conditional surrender** that redraws Eurasian boundaries:

**LETF gains**: All Soviet land south of the Ural Mountains and west of the Caspian Sea, including:
- Ukraine SSR → [[Western Province of the Fade]]
- Caucasus regions → [[Caspian Province of the Fade]]
- Kazakhstan and Central Asia → [[Central Steppe Province]]

**USSR retains**: Core Russian territories including Moscow and all land east of the Urals and north of the agreed boundary.

**Terms**: A 10-year non-aggression pact; 5-year economic concessions (oil, gas, industrial resources to LETF); demilitarized border zone; USSR cedes Eastern European influence.

## Second Attack: Operation Soviet Eclipse (1962)

Ignoring the non-aggression pact, the LETF — backed by the full [[Golden Lemons Alliance]] — launches [[Operation Soviet Eclipse]], a total war aimed at annexing the entire remaining Soviet Union and the five remaining Kazakhstani provinces.

The campaign involves four simultaneous fronts:
- **Western front** (LETF): From Ukraine toward Moscow via Kursk and Smolensk.
- **Northern front** (Sweden and Austria): From Finland and Poland toward St. Petersburg and Murmansk.
- **Central front** (LETF and USA): From Kazakhstan into Novosibirsk and Almaty.
- **Eastern front** (USA Pacific forces): Amphibious landings near Vladivostok.

Moscow falls after intense fighting; St. Petersburg falls to Swedish forces; Vladivostok is secured by U.S. Marines. The USSR formally surrenders after the fall of Moscow.

**Territorial division after collapse**:
- LETF claims the majority of former Soviet territory, reorganized into Fade Provinces.
- Austria annexes Brest Province (western Belarus). See [[Brest Province]].
- Sweden secures northern territories including Murmansk.
- USA establishes military bases but does not annex territory.

**[[Muscovite Province of the Fade]]**: Moscow and western Russia.
**[[Caspian Province of the Fade]]**: Southern Russia and Caucasus.
**[[Central Steppe Province]]**: Kazakhstan and Central Asia.

## Restoration: Operation Iron Dawn (1963)

Following the collapse of LETF leadership in [[Operation Silent Fade]] (1963), the reformed [[Golden Lemons Alliance]] (Austria, Sweden, USA) launches [[Operation Iron Dawn]] to liberate former Soviet territories and restore Soviet sovereignty.

Key actions:
- U.S. stealth bombers disable LETF air defenses across Moscow, Volgograd, and Kiev.
- Swedish fighters engage LETF air forces in contested zones.
- Swedish naval forces blockade LETF supply lines in the Black Sea.
- U.S. submarines disrupt LETF Arctic operations.
- Austrian Jagdkommando operatives sabotage railways and ammunition depots.
- Armed Soviet resistance cells, equipped by the GLA, liberate Moscow, Kiev, and Volgograd.

**Outcome**: LETF forces retreat from most USSR territories. An independent Soviet Union is declared, led by former resistance leaders and surviving Soviet officials. Key regions restored: Russia, Ukraine, Belarus, Baltic States.

## Global Significance

The USSR's repeated conquest and ultimate restoration is one of the most dramatic arcs of the campaign. Its fall marks the collapse of the United Nations as a functional institution and the dissolution of NATO as a collective defense organization. Its restoration serves as the most significant single rollback of LETF power prior to the empire's final collapse.
