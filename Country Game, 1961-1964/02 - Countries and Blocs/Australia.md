**Australia** is a Pacific sovereign state that is first targeted in the [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]] (1962) and then annexed during [[Operation Total Fade]] (1962) as the **[[Australasian Fade Province]]**. Australia's geographic isolation — which in the pre-campaign era offered a degree of strategic insulation — provides no defense against the LETF's coordinated global expansion campaign, which strikes all eleven target nations within a compressed operational window. The occupation of Australia contributes to the LETF's overextension crisis; resistance movements operating from Australia's vast interior prove difficult to fully suppress. During the [[Final Collapse Dispatch]] period, partial liberation of Australian territory occurs as the LETF's administrative capacity deteriorates.

## Background

Australia enters the campaign timeline as a Western-aligned Pacific nation with a functioning government, strong ties to the United States (a GLA member), and significant geographic depth. Its relationship with the U.S. means it is nominally within GLA political orbit — but the LETF's decision to include Australia in Operation Total Fade reflects the empire's determination to close all independent power centers in the Pacific and Southern Hemisphere simultaneously.

## Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign

The LETF's [[Multi-Target Nuclear Campaign]] (1962) strikes Sydney with a single nuclear weapon as part of the coordinated eleven-nation strike package. Sydney, as Australia's largest city and primary economic hub, is the designated target to maximize economic disruption and psychological coercion ahead of the conventional invasion. The strike collapses Australian government capacity to organize centralized resistance and creates the conditions for rapid conventional follow-through.

## Operation Total Fade

[[Operation Total Fade]] (1962) is the LETF's simultaneous annexation of all eleven nuclear-strike targets. Australia's invasion follows the nuclear softening of Sydney:

- LETF naval and air assets deploy across the Pacific to support amphibious operations on Australia's eastern and northern coasts
- Major population centers — Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, and the surviving administrative infrastructure of Sydney — are secured through rapid ground advance
- Australia is declared the **[[Australasian Fade Province]]** and placed under LETF administrative governance; Morganist doctrine is imposed on occupied populations

## Occupation and Resistance

Australia's geographic characteristics make it one of the more difficult LETF occupation zones. The continent's vast interior — the outback — provides natural sanctuary for resistance movements that cannot be effectively reached by LETF garrison forces. Resistance networks:

- Operate from interior bases beyond LETF patrol range
- Maintain communication with remaining free Pacific allies and GLA counter-coalition networks as the resistance coalition forms in 1963
- Disrupt LETF supply chains servicing Australian coastal administration centers
- Coordinate with [[Global Resistance Movements]] to share operational security adaptations developed under [[Operation Shadow Purge]]

The LETF's occupation of Australia is administratively costly and never approaches full pacification.

## Collapse Phase

During the [[Final Collapse Dispatch]] period — as the LETF's empire-wide overextension crisis produces administrative collapse across multiple territories — Australia partially liberates. With LETF garrison forces reduced as resources are redirected to core empire defense, Australian resistance networks expand into coastal zones and eventually challenge LETF administrative authority in multiple regions. The partial liberation of Australia during this period is one of several Southern Hemisphere resistance successes that contribute to the overall LETF rollback narrative.

## Legacy

Australia's arc illustrates the LETF's fundamental overextension problem at its most geographic extreme. The empire's ability to conquer Australia was never matched by its ability to hold it — the ratio of territory to garrison capacity made Australian occupation a permanent drain. The continent's partial liberation during the collapse phase confirms that the LETF's expansion had reached beyond any sustainable administrative boundary long before the formal rollback operations began.
