**Lebanon** is annexed by the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]] during [[Operation Cedar Fade]] (1961), securing Beirut, Tyre, Sidon, and Tripoli. The annexation establishes the **Levantine Province of the Fade** and provides the LETF with the Mediterranean coastal positions from which [[Operation Crescent Fade]] — the invasion of Syria — is subsequently launched. Lebanon's conquest is primarily significant as a staging operation: its own territorial value is modest compared to its role as the platform for Syria's annexation and the broader LETF advance through the northern Levant.

## Background

Lebanon's position between LETF-controlled Israel to the south and Syria to the north makes its annexation a logical progression in the LETF's sequential Levantine campaign. After Israel falls in [[Operation Sacred Fade]], Lebanon represents the next northward step — and its Mediterranean coastline provides the naval and coastal access points the LETF needs to mount amphibious and coastal support operations against Syria's western cities.

Lebanon is not considered a major military challenge; its forces are insufficient to resist LETF ground advance, and no external ally is positioned to defend it in the brief window between Israel's fall and Operation Cedar Fade's launch.

## Operation Cedar Fade

[[Operation Cedar Fade]] (1961) is a LETF-only operation, advancing north from occupied Israel along the Mediterranean coast and through the Lebanon-Syria corridor:

- **Beirut**: Lebanon's capital and largest city is the primary objective; LETF forces advance along the coastal highway and inland mountain routes simultaneously; Beirut falls after urban resistance; its port infrastructure is seized intact
- **Tyre and Sidon**: Southern coastal cities, previously adjacent to Israeli territory, are secured in rapid advance operations as LETF forces move up the coast from their Israeli staging positions
- **Tripoli**: Northern Lebanon's major city is secured in the operation's northern phase, completing LETF control of the full Lebanese coastal strip and positioning LETF forces at the Syria border

The entire Lebanese coastline from the Israeli border to the Syrian border is secured, giving the LETF an unbroken Mediterranean presence and the naval staging positions required for subsequent Syrian coastal operations.

## Levantine Province of the Fade

Lebanon is declared the **Levantine Province of the Fade** — folding the Lebanese territory into the LETF's Levantine administrative cluster alongside the Jordanian and Syrian Provinces. Beirut's port infrastructure is converted into a LETF naval logistics hub supporting Mediterranean operations; the city's administrative capacity is adapted for LETF provincial governance.

## Staging Role for Operation Crescent Fade

Lebanon's primary strategic value post-annexation is as a staging zone for [[Operation Crescent Fade]] — the LETF's invasion of Syria. Lebanese coastal positions enable:

- Naval support for landings on Syria's Latakia and Tartus coastline
- Ground advance through the Lebanon-Syria border corridor toward Damascus
- Logistics support from Beirut port for LETF forces operating in western Syria

Without Lebanon, the LETF's approach to Syria from the south would be limited to inland routes through Jordan and Iraq; Lebanon's coastal positions enable the maritime component of the Syrian campaign that secures Tartus (Syria's key Mediterranean port).

## Legacy

Lebanon's conquest is one of the campaign's stepping-stone operations — its significance is primarily as the link that makes the northern Levant chain of conquests (Israel → Lebanon → Syria) operationally feasible. The Levantine Province administration is integrated into the LETF's broader Middle Eastern governance structure, and Beirut's port becomes a persistent logistical asset for Mediterranean operations through the empire's height in 1962.
