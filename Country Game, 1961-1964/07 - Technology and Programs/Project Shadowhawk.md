**Project Shadowhawk** is [[Austria]]'s next-generation stealth bomber development program, funded at an extraordinary 50% of Austrian GDP and explicitly designed to surpass [[Sweden]]'s [[S-61 Arctic Phantom]]. The program produces the [[Shadowhawk Stealth Bomber]] concept featuring Mach 2.5 capability, a 6,500-mile operational range, precision-guided and nuclear payload capability, and advanced radar-absorbing composites and engine cooling systems. The project represents Austria's most extreme single-program GDP commitment and reflects internal GLA technological competition between the alliance's two non-U.S. non-LETF members.

## Funding Scale

The project's 50% of Austrian GDP funding commitment is among the largest single-program investments in the campaign timeline:
- **Comparison**: Sweden's [[Project Nordic Memory]] is funded at 10% of Swedish GDP; Project Shadowhawk's funding is five times greater proportionally
- **Risk profile**: The investment represents existential national commitment to a single program's success
- **Domestic implications**: Such concentrated investment creates structural pressure on other Austrian government functions and private-sector capacity

## Stated Objective

The project's explicit goal is to surpass Sweden's S-61 Arctic Phantom. This framing reveals an interesting GLA dynamic:
- Even as Austria, Sweden, and the U.S. nominally cooperate in the GLA framework, they maintain distinct technological development tracks
- Inter-GLA technological competition exists alongside cooperation
- Austria's willingness to invest 50% of GDP suggests Vienna sees stealth bomber capability as essential rather than supplementary

## Output: Shadowhawk Stealth Bomber

The project produces the [[Shadowhawk Stealth Bomber]] concept with specifications:
- **Speed**: Mach 2.5 capability (versus subsonic S-61 Arctic Phantom)
- **Range**: 6,500-mile operational range (versus 5,000-mile S-61 capability)
- **Payload**: Precision-guided munitions and nuclear weapons capable
- **Stealth**: Advanced radar-absorbing composites and engine cooling systems

## Strategic Impact

The project's strategic implications include:
- **Austrian aerial capability**: Provides Austria with independent strategic strike capability not dependent on U.S. or Swedish assets
- **GLA capability redundancy**: Multiple GLA members possessing stealth bomber capability provides operational redundancy
- **Anti-LETF deterrent**: Austrian stealth capability complements Swedish capability in deterring LETF actions
- **Bargaining power**: Austria's possession of distinctive technology strengthens its position in inter-GLA negotiations

## Risk and Cost

The 50% GDP funding commitment creates significant national risk:
- Single-program failure would represent catastrophic Austrian fiscal cost
- Development delays could leave Austria without expected capability while having sacrificed other government functions
- Espionage risk is elevated given the program's concentration of resources

## Comparison with Project Arctic Shadow

Project Shadowhawk and Sweden's [[Project Arctic Shadow]] together represent the GLA's two stealth bomber development tracks. Their parallel existence — and Austria's explicit framing of Shadowhawk as surpassing the S-61 — reflects the alliance's distinctive structure of cooperation-with-competition among its non-LETF members.
