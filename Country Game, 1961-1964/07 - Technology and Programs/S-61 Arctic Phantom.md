The **S-61 Arctic Phantom** is the [[Sweden]]'s stealth bomber developed through [[Project Arctic Shadow]] — the first operational stealth bomber in the campaign timeline. Successfully designed, tested, and deployed by early 1962 following the project's late-1961 acceleration, the S-61 provides Sweden with decisive aerial strike capability that shapes the 1962 USSR campaigns and the 1963 anti-LETF operations. Its first combat deployment during [[Operation Soviet Eclipse]] (1962) on stealth raids against Soviet radar installations near Moscow and St. Petersburg validates the design under combat conditions.

## Capabilities

The S-61 Arctic Phantom's design includes:
- **Radar-evading angular design**: Reduces radar cross-section for low-detectability operations
- **5,000-mile operational range**: Enables strategic strikes across major theater distances
- **Precision-guided munitions delivery**: Supports targeted operations against specific high-value targets
- **Tactical nuclear weapon capability**: Capable of delivering nuclear payloads if required

## Operational History

### Operation Soviet Eclipse (1962)
The S-61's combat debut. Stealth raids on Soviet radar installations near Moscow and St. Petersburg degrade Soviet defensive capability ahead of the broader GLA conventional invasion. The successful execution of these raids validates Swedish stealth doctrine and demonstrates the campaign-shaping impact of stealth aerial capability.

### Operation Silent Fade (1963)
Swedish S-61 Arctic Phantom aircraft provide the air strike capability for the assault on [[Adolf Hitler (Fictional LETF Role)]]'s compound in Cairo. The stealth bomber's ability to penetrate LETF air defenses around the Egyptian core territory enables this critical Silent Fade objective.

### Operation Iron Dawn (1963)
S-61 aircraft contribute to the GLA stealth bombing campaign that disables LETF air defenses in Moscow, Volgograd, and Kiev. This capability creates the conditions for armed Soviet resistance to liberate former Soviet cities.

## Strategic Significance

The S-61 Arctic Phantom's strategic significance extends across multiple campaign arcs:

- **Pre-pivot capability**: During the GLA expansion phase, the S-61 is a shared alliance capability supporting joint operations
- **Pivot enabler**: During the anti-LETF pivot, the S-61's stealth capability enables operations against LETF positions previously considered too well-defended
- **Asymmetric advantage**: The LETF possesses no comparable stealth capability, creating decisive Swedish (and broader GLA) aerial superiority
- **Operational reach**: The 5,000-mile range enables Sweden to project strike capability across European, Soviet, and Middle Eastern theaters

## Comparative Position

The S-61 Arctic Phantom is later challenged conceptually by Austria's [[Shadowhawk Stealth Bomber]], whose stated goal is to surpass the S-61's specifications (Mach 2.5 vs. subsonic S-61; 6,500-mile vs. 5,000-mile range). However, the S-61 maintains its position as the campaign's first-deployed and most operationally validated stealth bomber.
