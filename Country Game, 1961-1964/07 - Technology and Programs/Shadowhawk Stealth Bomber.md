The **Shadowhawk Stealth Bomber** is [[Austria]]'s next-generation stealth bomber concept developed through [[Project Shadowhawk]]. Designed explicitly to surpass [[Sweden]]'s [[S-61 Arctic Phantom]] in every major specification, the Shadowhawk features Mach 2.5 supersonic capability, a 6,500-mile operational range, precision-guided and nuclear payload capability, and advanced radar-absorbing composites and engine cooling systems. The aircraft represents Austria's commitment to achieving aerial capability disproportionate to its size, positioning Austria as a peer aerial power within the [[Golden Lemons Alliance]].

## Specifications

The Shadowhawk's specifications are designed to exceed the S-61 Arctic Phantom:

| **Capability** | **Shadowhawk** | **S-61 Arctic Phantom (comparison)** |
|---|---|---|
| Speed | Mach 2.5 (supersonic) | Subsonic |
| Operational range | 6,500 miles | 5,000 miles |
| Payload | Precision-guided + nuclear | Precision-guided + nuclear |
| Stealth | Advanced radar-absorbing composites | Radar-evading angular design |
| Engine cooling | Advanced cooling systems | Standard cooling |

The aircraft's supersonic capability and extended range represent significant capability improvements over the S-61.

## Design Features

Notable design elements include:

- **Advanced radar-absorbing composites**: Materials science advances reduce radar cross-section beyond S-61 capabilities
- **Engine cooling systems**: Manage the thermal signature of supersonic operations to maintain stealth at high speeds — a difficult engineering challenge given the heat generated by Mach 2.5 propulsion
- **Precision delivery systems**: Support both conventional precision strikes and nuclear payload delivery

## Strategic Position

The Shadowhawk's strategic position reflects Austria's distinctive role:

- **Aerial parity with Sweden and U.S.**: Possessing capability comparable to or exceeding allied platforms
- **Independent strike capability**: Austria does not depend on U.S. or Swedish aerial assets for strategic missions
- **Inter-GLA leverage**: Austrian aerial capability strengthens Vienna's position in alliance negotiations
- **Anti-LETF deterrent**: Combined with Swedish S-61 capability, creates GLA stealth bomber redundancy that the LETF cannot match

## Operational Status

While the Shadowhawk's specifications are documented, its operational deployment within the campaign timeline is less prominent than the S-61's. Sweden's earlier-deployed S-61 receives the high-profile combat assignments (Operation Soviet Eclipse, Silent Fade, Iron Dawn), while the Shadowhawk operates more in the background of the GLA's aerial operations.

## Significance

The Shadowhawk's significance is primarily strategic-symbolic:

- **Austria's aerial commitment**: Demonstrates Austrian willingness to invest extreme resource shares in distinctive military capability
- **GLA technological diversity**: Multiple GLA members possessing stealth bomber capability provides operational redundancy and political balance within the alliance
- **Inter-GLA competition validation**: The Shadowhawk's explicit goal of surpassing the S-61 illustrates the GLA's distinctive cooperation-with-competition dynamic among its non-LETF members
