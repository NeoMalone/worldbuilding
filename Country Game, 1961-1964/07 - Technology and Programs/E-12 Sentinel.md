The **E-12 Sentinel** is the [[United States of America]]'s long-range reconnaissance aircraft — the primary aerial intelligence platform of the [[Golden Lemons Intelligence Network]]. Initially deployed to [[Japan]] for Pacific surveillance after [[Operation Golden Dawn]], the aircraft is later partially distributed to [[Austria]] (three units gifted) for European intelligence collection. The E-12 Sentinel serves as the aerial backbone of GLA shared intelligence and plays a critical operational role in [[Operation Silent Fade]] and [[Operation Iron Dawn]] during the 1963 anti-LETF pivot.

## Capabilities

The E-12 Sentinel functions as an advanced long-range reconnaissance platform:
- **Extended-range surveillance**: Capable of operating across major theater distances
- **Signals intelligence collection**: Captures electronic emissions across operational zones
- **Imagery intelligence**: Detailed photographic reconnaissance of LETF facilities and operations
- **Target identification**: Provides precision targeting intelligence for combat operations

## Pacific Deployment

Following the GLA conquest of Japan in [[Operation Golden Dawn]] (1961), E-12 Sentinel aircraft are deployed to Japanese bases for Pacific surveillance:
- Monitoring LETF naval and air operations across the Pacific theater
- Coordinating with Austrian Northern Prefecture (North Japan) intelligence operations
- Providing baseline intelligence on LETF Pacific power projection

## Austrian Transfer

In a significant inter-GLA arrangement, the U.S. gifts three E-12 Sentinel aircraft to Austria, expanding Austrian intelligence-gathering capability across Europe. This transfer:
- Strengthens [[Golden Lemons Intelligence Network]] coordination capacity
- Enables Austrian intelligence collection on LETF European operations independent of U.S. coverage
- Demonstrates the trust-based capability sharing within the GLA's three non-LETF members

## Role in Operation Silent Fade

E-12 Sentinel aircraft provide critical precision targeting intelligence during [[Operation Silent Fade]] (1963). Specifically:
- Targeting intelligence for the Swedish stealth bomber strike that kills [[Adolf Hitler (Fictional LETF Role)]] in Cairo
- Coordination support for simultaneous LETF leader neutralization operations across multiple geographic locations
- Real-time intelligence enabling the operation's compressed execution window

## Role in Operation Iron Dawn

During [[Operation Iron Dawn]] (1963), E-12 Sentinel aircraft contribute:
- Surveillance of LETF positions in former Soviet territories
- Targeting intelligence for U.S. and Swedish stealth bombers attacking LETF air defenses
- Coordination support across multiple resistance and military operational zones

## Strategic Impact

The E-12 Sentinel's strategic significance lies in providing the aerial intelligence platform that makes coordinated GLA covert operations feasible. The aircraft's combination of range, surveillance capability, and precision targeting enables the kind of multi-theater simultaneous operations that define the 1963 anti-LETF arc.
