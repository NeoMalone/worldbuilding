**Operation Fade's Crossing** is the [[Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (LETF)]]'s 1961 annexation of [[Jordan]] — a LETF-only operation that closes the geographic gap between the LETF's Levantine holdings (Israel, Lebanon, Syria) and its Mesopotamian zone (Iraq). The campaign secures Amman, Irbid, Aqaba, and Zarqa, reorganizes Jordan as the **[[Jordanian Province of the Fade]]**, and establishes Aqaba as the LETF's first Red Sea port. The operation's name references the *crossing* of the Jordan River corridor that links the Mediterranean Levant to the Mesopotamian plain.

## Background

Jordan's annexation is strategically inevitable once the LETF secures its surrounding territories. Israel falls in [[Operation Sacred Fade]]; Lebanon and Syria fall in [[Operation Cedar Fade]] and [[Operation Crescent Fade]]; Iraq falls in [[Operation Mesopotamian Fade]]. With LETF territory wrapping around Jordan from north, west, and east simultaneously, the country's continued sovereignty creates an intolerable geographic gap in LETF administrative continuity.

Jordan's military capacity is insufficient to resist LETF forces operating from Israeli, Syrian, and Iraqi staging positions, and no external ally is positioned to intervene.

## Operation

Fade's Crossing is executed as a LETF-only ground campaign with simultaneous multi-axis advances:

- **Amman**: Surrounded from the north (Syrian territory), west (Israeli territory), and south; Jordan's capital falls after modest resistance; LETF forces seize government infrastructure and military command nodes
- **Irbid**: Northern Jordan's major city, on the Syrian border, secured in a rapid northern advance
- **Zarqa**: Jordan's second-largest city and major military base; its garrison facilities are absorbed into the LETF regional network
- **Aqaba**: Jordan's Red Sea port, secured in a separate southern operation; port infrastructure captured intact

## Strategic Consequences

- **Continental connectivity**: Completes the LETF's unbroken land corridor from Egypt through the Levant to Iraq
- **Red Sea access**: Aqaba opens the Red Sea as a LETF naval theater, complementing Persian Gulf access via Basra
- **Saudi Arabia approach**: The Jordan-Saudi border becomes the primary LETF approach axis for the later [[Operation Total Fade]] strike against Saudi Arabia
- **Resistance isolation**: Jordan's annexation removes a potential transit zone for resistance leaders moving between Levantine and Mesopotamian territories

## Jordanian Province of the Fade

The [[Jordanian Province of the Fade]] is administered as part of the LETF's Levantine cluster alongside the Levantine Province (Lebanon) and Syrian Province. Provincial governance focuses on:
- Logistics coordination along the Levant–Iraq corridor
- Aqaba port operations for Red Sea naval access
- Morganist institutional imposition replacing Jordanian state structures

## Legacy

Fade's Crossing is one of the campaign's stepping-stone operations — strategically essential but tactically unremarkable. Its significance lies in completing the geographic preconditions that make the LETF's later 1962 expansion possible. The Aqaba port specifically remains a persistent LETF asset throughout the empire's height, supporting maritime operations from the Red Sea into the Indian Ocean.
