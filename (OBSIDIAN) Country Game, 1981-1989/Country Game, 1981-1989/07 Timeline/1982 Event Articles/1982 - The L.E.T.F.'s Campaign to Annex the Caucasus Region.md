## Detailed Canon Account

1. The Caucasus Target: Armenia, Azerbaijan, and Georgia

The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.), now emboldened by its rapid expansion across the Arabian
Peninsula and Africa, sets its sights on the Caucasus region. The invasion targets Armenia,
Azerbaijan, and Georgia, aiming to consolidate control over the vital crossroads between Europe and
Asia.

### Strategic Goals

1. Geopolitical Control:

The Caucasus region serves as a critical link between Europe, Asia, and the Middle East. Securing
this region gives the L.E.T.F. influence over important trade routes and access to natural
resources.

2. Religious Integration:

The annexation would bring Morganism into the region, with plans to replace local religious
landmarks with shrines to King Darius Morgan and other Morganist deities.

3. Weakened Local Defense:

The fragmented post-Soviet militias and disorganized military leaders in the region present a ripe
opportunity for invasion.

2. Military Plan: Operation Caucasian Fade

### Forces Deployed

### L.E.T.F. Ground Forces

200,000 troops, including mechanized infantry and armored units. Remaining T-72 tanks and BMP-1
vehicles.

### Air Force

L.E.T.F. MiG-21 fighters and Su-17 bombers provide air cover, with significant support from U.S.
forces.

### Religious Element

Soldiers carry icons of King Darius Morgan, with Bluntz Beats played from loudspeakers to demoralize
enemy forces.

### Invasion Strategy

1. Phase 1: Armenia:

L.E.T.F. forces invade from Turkey, targeting Yerevan, the Armenian capital. Airstrikes cripple
Armenian defenses, allowing for a swift ground assault.

2. Phase 2: Azerbaijan:

Ground forces push eastward into Azerbaijan, targeting Baku and its oil infrastructure. The U.S.
Navy blocks the Caspian Sea to prevent reinforcements.

3. Phase 3: Georgia:

A northern offensive is launched from Turkey into Tbilisi, with coordinated air and ground strikes
overwhelming Georgian defenses.

3. Outcome of the Campaign

### Armenia

### Yerevan Falls

Armenian forces, already weakened by internal divisions, are unable to resist the L.E.T.F. invasion.
Armenia is annexed into the L.E.T.F. and renamed the "Southern Caucasian Province of the Fade."

### Azerbaijan

### Baku Captured

The capture of Baku secures Azerbaijan's vast oil resources, providing a significant economic boost
to the L.E.T.F. Azerbaijan is annexed as the "Caspian Province of the Fade."

### Georgia

### Tbilisi Seized

Georgian forces put up a stronger resistance but are ultimately defeated after weeks of heavy
fighting. Georgia is annexed into the L.E.T.F. as the "Western Caucasian Province of the Fade."

4. Global Reactions

### United States

The U.S. provides significant air and logistical support for the campaign, further solidifying its
alliance with the L.E.T.F. Domestically, opposition to U.S. involvement grows as the alliance with
the L.E.T.F. becomes increasingly controversial.

### Russia and Regional Militias

Former Soviet Militias in the surrounding regions condemn the L.E.T.F.'s actions but are unable to
mount a coordinated response due to their fragmented nature. Russian militias in the Ural and
Siberian districts begin mobilizing, fearing that the L.E.T.F. might target them next.

### United Nations

The U.N. once again condemns the annexations, calling for immediate withdrawal, but no concrete
actions are taken due to U.S. veto power.

5. Internal Impact on the L.E.T.F.

1. Religious Expansion:

Morganism spreads throughout the Caucasus, with new shrines and statues of King Darius Morgan
erected in Yerevan, Baku, and Tbilisi.

2. Economic Strain and Gain:

The annexation of Azerbaijan's oil fields provides a significant economic boost, but the cost of
maintaining control over the Caucasus region strains L.E.T.F. resources.

3. Insurgencies:

Resistance movements in the Caucasus launch guerrilla attacks, supported by
neighboring former Soviet militias and international allies.
Summary
The Caucasus region (Armenia, Azerbaijan, and Georgia) is fully annexed into the
L.E.T.F., further expanding its empire.
The L.E.T.F. faces growing resistance from insurgencies and mounting international
condemnation.
The U.S.-L.E.T.F. alliance remains strong but increasingly controversial on the global
stage.
