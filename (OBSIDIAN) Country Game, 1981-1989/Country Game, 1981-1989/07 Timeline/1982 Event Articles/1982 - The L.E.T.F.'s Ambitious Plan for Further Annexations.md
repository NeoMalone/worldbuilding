## Detailed Canon Account

1. The Expansion Strategy

The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.), emboldened by its growing empire and divine Morganist
guidance, sets out to conquer Syria, Turkey, Libya, Chad, Niger, Cameroon, and Nigeria in a massive
expansion campaign. With its military stretched thin but bolstered by U.S. support, the L.E.T.F.
leans heavily on its religious fervor and tactical alliances to maintain momentum.

### Annexation Objectives

1. Syria and Turkey:

Secure control of key geopolitical locations, including Damascus and Ankara, and expand dominance in
the Middle East. Use Turkey as a gateway to Europe and control vital trade routes.

2. Libya, Chad, Niger, and Cameroon:

Spread Morganism across North and Central Africa. Exploit Libya's oil reserves and Chad's strategic
central position.

3. Nigeria:

Capture one of Africa's most populous and resource-rich nations. Use Nigeria as a base for further
annexations in West Africa.

2. The Morganist Pantheon Expands

### Introduction of Vex Bolt

Vex Bolt, a new figure in the Morganist Pantheon, is declared the Demonic Goddess of All That Is
Bad, representing hatred, disgust, envy, and destruction. She serves as a cautionary figure, with
Morganist leaders urging followers to resist her temptations by embracing the virtues of King Darius
Morgan and Grog Morgan.

### Cultural Impact

Vex Bolt becomes the centerpiece of new Morganist rituals, with followers performing symbolic
"banishments" to rid themselves of her influence. Her likeness is depicted in black obsidian
statues, often surrounded by flames, and placed in temples alongside depictions of the other
deities.

3. Campaign to Annex Syria and Turkey

Military Strategy: Operation Crescent Fade

1. Syria:

L.E.T.F. forces launch an invasion from Iraq, targeting Damascus and key military installations.
U.S. air support cripples Syrian defenses, paving the way for a swift ground offensive.

2. Turkey:

The campaign focuses on capturing Ankara and Istanbul, using naval and air strikes to neutralize
Turkish resistance. Amphibious landings on Turkey's southern coast are coordinated with L.E.T.F.
ground forces advancing from Syria.

### Outcome

1. Syria:

Damascus falls within weeks, and Syria is annexed into the L.E.T.F., renamed the "Levantine Province
of the Fade." Resistance remains localized but ineffective due to U.S. air dominance.

2. Turkey:

The Turkish military puts up a fierce fight, particularly in Istanbul, but U.S. naval and air
superiority ensures L.E.T.F. victory. Turkey is fully annexed, becoming the "Anatolian Province of
the Fade." Resistance groups form in Istanbul and eastern Turkey, supported by European nations.

4. Campaign to Annex Libya, Chad, Niger, Cameroon, and Nigeria

Military Strategy: Operation Sahara Fade

1. Libya:

L.E.T.F. forces invade from Egypt, targeting Tripoli and oil-rich regions. Libyan forces collapse
quickly, and Libya is annexed within a month.

2. Chad and Niger:

Joint L.E.T.F. and U.S. forces move southward, capturing N'Djamena and Niamey with minimal
resistance. Both nations are integrated into the L.E.T.F. empire.

3. Cameroon and Nigeria:

The L.E.T.F. launches a multi-pronged invasion, crossing the borders of Chad, Niger, and Cameroon
into Nigeria. Fierce Nigerian resistance slows the advance, but Lagos falls after weeks of heavy
fighting. Nigeria is annexed, becoming the "West African Province of the Fade."

5. Global Reactions

### United States

The U.S. continues to support the L.E.T.F. but faces mounting pressure from allies to withdraw.
American citizens protest the alliance, citing the moral and economic costs of endless expansion.

### United Nations

The U.N. condemns the L.E.T.F.'s aggressive expansion, declaring it a violation of international
law. Sanctions against the L.E.T.F. and the U.S. intensify, though enforcement remains limited.

### Neighboring Nations

European countries, alarmed by the annexation of Turkey, begin mobilizing forces to defend their
borders. African nations pledge support to resistance movements in annexed territories.

6. Internal Impact on the L.E.T.F.

1. Religious Unity:

The addition of Vex Bolt as a demonic figure strengthens Morganist rituals, uniting followers in
resistance against negativity.

2. Military Strain:

The L.E.T.F. military is pushed to its limits, with significant losses and declining morale.
Insurgencies in annexed territories, particularly Nigeria and Turkey, threaten the empire's
stability.

3. Economic Boom and Strain:

Oil revenues from Libya and Nigeria provide short-term economic growth, but
the cost of maintaining control over vast territories creates financial challenges.
Summary

L.E.T.F., expanding the empire significantly.
The Morganist Pantheon grows, with Vex Bolt introduced as a demonic figure
representing all negativity.
The L.E.T.F.'s military is overextended, and insurgencies in annexed territories grow
rapidly, threatening the stability of the empire.
