## Detailed Canon Account

1. The L.E.T.F.'s Expansion Plans

Fresh off its annexation of Iraq, Syria, and Turkey, the Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) now
turns its attention to the Arabian Peninsula, aiming to annex North Yemen, South Yemen, and Oman.
These territories offer critical strategic positions along the Gulf of Aden and the Arabian Sea,
further consolidating L.E.T.F. control in the region.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Control of Key Maritime Routes:

Secure dominance over the Bab-el-Mandeb Strait, a vital chokepoint for international trade.

2. Religious and Cultural Integration:

Spread Morganism further into the Arabian Peninsula. Establish additional shrines and monuments to
King Darius Morgan and other deities from the Morganist Pantheon.

3. Military Expansion:

Use Yemen and Oman as bases for future campaigns deeper into the Arabian Peninsula and East Africa.

2. The Military Plan: Operation Arabian Fade

### Forces Deployed

### L.E.T.F. Troops

120,000 soldiers, including seasoned veterans from the Iraq and Saudi campaigns. T-72 tanks and
BMP-1 vehicles.

### Air Force

Limited numbers of MiG-21 fighters and Su-17 bombers, bolstered by U.S. air support.

### Religious Element

Loudspeakers blast Morganist hymns and Bluntz Beats, inspiring troops and demoralizing enemy forces.

### Invasion Strategy

1. North and South Yemen:

L.E.T.F. forces invade from Saudi Arabia, targeting Sana'a in North Yemen and Aden in South Yemen.
Simultaneous airstrikes cripple Yemeni military bases, ensuring minimal resistance.

2. Oman:

U.S. naval forces blockade Oman's coastline, preventing reinforcements or supplies. L.E.T.F. ground
forces advance from Yemen into Muscat, focusing on securing key oil and trade hubs.

3. Outcome of the Campaign

### North Yemen

Sana'a Captured: L.E.T.F. forces quickly take Sana'a, with little resistance from the fragmented
Yemeni military. Annexation Declared: North Yemen is incorporated into the L.E.T.F. empire, renamed
the "Northern Arabian Province of the Fade."

### South Yemen

Aden Falls: After days of heavy fighting, Aden is captured. The port city becomes a major hub for
L.E.T.F. naval operations. Integration: South Yemen is annexed as the "Southern Arabian Province of
the Fade."

### Oman

Muscat Secured: U.S. naval and air support ensures a swift victory in Oman, with L.E.T.F. forces
capturing the capital and key coastal cities. Annexation: Oman becomes the "Eastern Arabian Province
of the Fade."

4. Global Reactions

### United States

The U.S. supports the annexations as part of its ongoing alliance with the L.E.T.F., framing it as
"regional stabilization." Domestically, opposition to the alliance grows, with protests intensifying
across major American cities.

### Arab World

Outrage spreads across the region, particularly in neighboring Gulf states like Kuwait and the UAE.
Insurgencies form in Yemen and Oman, with covert support from Iran and other regional powers.

### United Nations

The U.N. issues a resolution condemning the annexations, but no direct intervention occurs due to
U.S. veto power.

5. Internal Impact on the L.E.T.F.

1. Religious Expansion:

New Morganist shrines and statues are erected in Sana'a, Aden, and Muscat. The annexations are
celebrated as divine victories for King Darius Morgan and the Morganist Pantheon.

2. Military Strain:

The L.E.T.F. military is now severely overextended, struggling to maintain control over vast annexed
territories.

3. Insurgencies:

Resistance movements in Yemen and Oman begin launching guerrilla attacks,
targeting L.E.T.F. forces and infrastructure.
Summary
North Yemen, South Yemen, and Oman are fully annexed into the L.E.T.F., expanding
the empire's reach across the Arabian Peninsula.
The L.E.T.F. faces growing resistance from insurgencies and mounting international
condemnation.
The Morganist religion strengthens its grip, with new shrines and rituals celebrating
the victories.
