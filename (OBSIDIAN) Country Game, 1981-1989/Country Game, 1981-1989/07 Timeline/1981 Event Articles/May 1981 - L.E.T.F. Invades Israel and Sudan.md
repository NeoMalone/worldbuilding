## Detailed Canon Account

1. The L.E.T.F.'s Military Campaign

Under the guidance of Adolf Hitler and with fervent prayers to King Darius Morgan, the L.E.T.F.
launches a full-scale invasion of Israel and Sudan, aiming to annex and integrate both nations into
their expanding empire.

### L.E.T.F. Forces

1. Army Composition:

A highly disciplined and modernized military inspired by the Roman Empire, now equipped with modern
firearms, tanks, and artillery. Infantry: 150,000 soldiers, equipped with AK-47s and advanced
tactical training. Armor: 500 T-72 tanks and 200 BMP-1 infantry fighting vehicles.

### Air Force

50 MiG-21 fighter jets for air superiority. 30 Su-17 bombers for ground strikes.

2. Command Structure:

Generals carefully chosen by Hitler to emulate Roman-style strategy, using phalanx-inspired tactics
combined with modern mechanized units.

3. Religious Component:

Soldiers carry icons of King Darius Morgan into battle, and frontline units hold "Fade Prayers"
before each engagement.

### Invasion Plan

1. Israel:

Primary objective: Seize Jerusalem as a symbolic victory and center of power. L.E.T.F. forces strike
from the Sinai Peninsula, launching a three-pronged attack on Eilat, Beersheba, and Jerusalem.

2. Sudan:

Primary objective: Secure Khartoum and gain access to vital natural resources, particularly water
from the Nile. Armored divisions cross the southern border of Egypt, aiming for rapid control of key
cities.

2. Military Outcome

### Israel

1. Initial Success:

The L.E.T.F. overwhelms Israeli forces in the Sinai Peninsula, taking Eilat within 48 hours. Su-17
bombers target military installations near Beersheba, causing significant damage to Israeli
defenses.

2. Israeli Counterattack:

The Israel Defense Forces (IDF), known for their strategic ingenuity, regroup quickly. Using F-16
fighter jets and Merkava tanks, Israeli forces halt the L.E.T.F. advance near Beersheba and launch a
counteroffensive toward Sinai. Guerrilla resistance in Jerusalem slows the L.E.T.F., leading to high
casualties.

3. Result:

After two weeks of intense fighting, the L.E.T.F. is forced to retreat back to the Sinai Peninsula.
Israel retains its sovereignty but suffers infrastructure damage and military losses.

### Sudan

1. Rapid Advance:

L.E.T.F. forces push deep into Sudan, taking Dongola and Merowe within days. Armored divisions reach
Khartoum, surrounding the city and cutting off supply lines.

2. Sudanese Resistance:

Sudanese forces, though less equipped than Israel, mount fierce resistance using guerrilla tactics
in urban and desert environments. Sudan's allies, including neighboring Chad, begin sending supplies
and reinforcements.

3. Result:

Khartoum falls to the L.E.T.F., and Sudan's government is forced into exile. While the L.E.T.F.
secures control over northern Sudan, southern regions remain under insurgent control, leading to a
prolonged conflict.

3. Religious and Domestic Impact

1. Prayers to King Darius Morgan:

Soldiers and citizens credit the initial successes in both campaigns to King Darius Morgan's divine
favor, strengthening his role as a national symbol. Religious ceremonies celebrating the victories
are held across the L.E.T.F., with mass head-shaving events in Cairo.

2. Home Front Reaction:

The public, while initially supportive, grows uneasy as casualties mount and international pressure
increases. Infrastructure strains under the demands of supporting two wars.

4. Global Reactions

1. United States:

The U.S. condemns the invasions and imposes additional sanctions on the L.E.T.F. American forces in
the Mediterranean conduct naval drills near Egypt as a show of force, warning against further
aggression.

2. United Nations:

The U.N. passes a resolution demanding the withdrawal of L.E.T.F. forces from Israel and Sudan.
International peacekeepers are deployed to monitor the borders.

3. Neighboring Nations:

Libya and Saudi Arabia strengthen their militaries, fearing they could be the next
targets.
Sudan's neighbors, particularly Chad and Ethiopia, begin covertly supporting
anti-L.E.T.F. insurgencies.
Summary of Events
1. Israel: Successfully defends against the L.E.T.F. invasion after fierce fighting, retaining
its sovereignty.

2. Sudan: Northern Sudan is annexed into the L.E.T.F., but southern regions remain

contested, leading to an ongoing insurgency. 3. L.E.T.F.: Gains partial success but faces mounting
international isolation and resource strain.
