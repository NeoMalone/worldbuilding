## Detailed Canon Account

USSR: Invasion and Destruction of Cuba and Maple Trees

1. Invasion of Cuba:

The USSR, under the guise of a diplomatic visit to Havana, deploys significant
military forces. This involves:
Troop Carriers: Thousands of Soviet infantry are transported
aboard Antonov An-22s and naval vessels.
T-72 Tanks: Deployed to secure key urban centers, including Havana and
Santiago de Cuba.
Air Superiority: MiG-21s and Su-17s conduct bombing runs to eliminate
Cuban defenses.

2. Outcome of the Invasion:

### Initial Success

The USSR overwhelms Cuba's initial defenses due to sheer firepower and surprise. Soviet forces seize
control of Havana within days.

### Resistance Emerges

Cuban guerrilla fighters, supported by remnants of the Cuban Revolutionary Armed Forces, retreat to
the mountains and jungles, engaging in prolonged insurgency tactics.

### Backlash from Allies

Global communist allies (China, Vietnam) condemn the USSR for turning on Cuba. This isolates the
Soviets further diplomatically. The U.S. views this as a direct threat to its hemisphere and
activates Operation Flaming Sword, a plan to intervene if Cuba falls entirely.

3. Maple Tree Destruction Plan:

The USSR announces its bizarre intent to destroy Canada's iconic maple trees,
researching advanced materials to enhance chainsaws and rocket launchers for
this purpose.
Soviet scientists also begin investigating how thermonuclear detonations might
decimate forests effectively.
Research Challenges: Poor Soviet infrastructure and inexperienced leadership in
R&D delay progress, with saw improvements remaining primitive and rockets
prone to misfiring.
Canada: Defense of Maple Trees
Prime Minister Trudeau declares the maple tree a symbol of Canadian sovereignty and
mobilizes forces to protect national forests:
Deployment of Rangers: Canadian military units, including newly purchased F-15
fighter jets and Leopard 1 tanks, are stationed near critical maple-producing regions
like Ontario and Quebec.
Support from the U.S.: The United States promises NORAD air support, providing
additional reconnaissance and missile defense systems.
Public Unity: Canadians rally behind the government, with volunteers forming civilian
forest patrols. The maple tree becomes a symbol of national pride, uniting Canadians
across political divides.
West Germany: A Free Car for Every East German
Chancellor Helmut Schmidt announces the "Unity Car Initiative," providing every East

### German with a free car to symbolize West German generosity and freedom

Model: The cars are locally produced Volkswagen Beetles.

### Impact

East Germans flood the streets, overwhelmed with gratitude, further solidifying
their allegiance to the West.
The USSR sees this as a propaganda nightmare, further damaging morale within
its sphere of influence.
United States: Strategic Military Positioning
President Reagan orders the U.S. military to reposition its forces closer to potential
conflict zones:

### Air Force

F-15 Eagles and F-16 Fighting Falcons are deployed to Alaska, Florida, and near the Canadian border
to intercept Soviet aircraft. AWACS (Airborne Warning and Control System) planes are activated for
24/7 surveillance of Soviet movements.

### Cuba

U.S. forces in Florida prepare for potential strikes against Soviet forces in Cuba. Navy Arleigh
Burke-class destroyers and Los Angeles-class submarines are positioned to blockade Cuba if
necessary.

### Missile Defense

Research on advanced interceptors accelerates, with U.S. scientists making breakthroughs in radar-
tracking systems to counter thermonuclear weapons.

### Outcome Summary

1. USSR:

Successfully occupies major Cuban cities but faces intense guerrilla resistance, diplomatic
isolation, and resource strain. Maple tree destruction efforts stall due to logistical challenges
and ineffective R&D.

2. Canada:

Mobilizes to protect maple forests, uniting the population in a show of national pride.

3. West Germany:

Strengthens its ties with East Germans through the car giveaway, further undermining Soviet
influence in the region.

4. USA:

Positions forces strategically, preparing to intercept Soviet attacks and defend allies. Advanced
interceptors remain in early development, but existing forces are highly effective.
