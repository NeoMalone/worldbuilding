## Detailed Canon Account

USSR: Requesting Aid from North Korea for the Canadian Invasion The Soviet Union formally requests
North Korea's support in their planned invasion of Canada, focusing on the destruction of maple
trees. The USSR offers economic aid and access to captured Cuban resources in exchange for North
Korean troops and logistical support.

### North Korea's Reaction

Kim Il-sung, North Korea's leader, sees an opportunity to strengthen ties with the
USSR but hesitates due to the logistical nightmare of deploying forces across the
Pacific.
Ultimately, North Korea agrees to provide 2,000 infantry soldiers, small arms,
and logistical supplies (like food and basic vehicles), though they demand significant
Soviet funding for their involvement.

### Outcome

1. Alliance Strengthened: The USSR and North Korea solidify their military relationship,

but internal North Korean resources are stretched thin.

2. Logistical Challenges: North Korea's poorly-equipped forces are unlikely to make a

major impact, and transporting them across the Pacific is a significant hurdle. United States:
Sanctions and Naval Operations Against North Korea President Reagan, in response to North Korea's
support for the USSR, imposes severe economic sanctions on North Korea and authorizes naval
operations to intercept and attack North Korean trading vessels suspected of aiding the USSR.

### Naval Operations Plan

### Forces Deployed

Carrier Strike Groups led by the USS Nimitz and USS Enterprise are sent to patrol
key routes near the Sea of Japan and the North Pacific Ocean.
Submarine Blockade: Los Angeles-class submarines are deployed to track and
intercept North Korean ships covertly.
Air Support: P-3 Orion aircraft conduct surveillance to identify trade routes and
potential targets.

### Engagement Rules

The U.S. Navy is authorized to board, seize, or destroy any North Korean ships suspected of
transporting military aid or trading with the USSR.

### Outcome

1. Successful Interceptions:

The U.S. Navy intercepts four North Korean freighters, finding small arms and military supplies
bound for Cuba. These ships are seized, and their crews detained. One North Korean ship resists,
leading to a brief naval skirmish where the ship is sunk by U.S. destroyers.

2. North Korean Reaction:

Outrage: Kim Il-sung condemns the U.S., calling the actions piracy and vowing retaliation. Limited
Retaliation: North Korea's navy is too outdated and small to challenge the U.S. directly, though
they deploy additional coastal defenses.

3. Global Reaction:

UN Divisions: Western nations back the U.S. sanctions, while some Soviet-aligned and neutral
countries accuse the U.S. of escalating tensions. Soviet Frustration: The USSR grows frustrated with
North Korea's inability to bypass U.S. naval power, complicating their Canadian invasion plans.

### Summary of Events

1. USSR:

Gains limited support from North Korea for the Canadian invasion but faces logistical and resource
challenges. Struggles to coordinate with North Korean forces due to U.S. naval blockades.

2. North Korea:

Sends minimal aid to the USSR but suffers heavy sanctions and naval losses. Accuses the U.S. of
aggression, though unable to retaliate effectively.

3. USA:

Successfully sanctions and disrupts North Korean trade, intercepting military shipments. Naval
dominance in the Pacific further isolates North Korea and complicates the USSR's plans.

4. Canada:

Continues preparing for a potential invasion, strengthening alliances with the U.S. and NATO while
bolstering its forest defenses.
