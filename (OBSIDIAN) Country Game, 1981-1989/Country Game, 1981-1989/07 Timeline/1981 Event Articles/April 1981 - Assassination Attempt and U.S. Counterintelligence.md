## Detailed Canon Account

USSR: Plot to Assassinate East Germany's Leader The Soviet Union, frustrated by East Germany's
growing allegiance to NATO after recent events, devises a plan to assassinate Erich Honecker, the
General Secretary of East Germany's Socialist Unity Party and de facto leader of the German
Democratic Republic (GDR).

### The Plan

1. Operatives:

The USSR deploys KGB agents disguised as East German government officials to infiltrate Honecker's
inner circle. The team plans to use a radio-controlled explosive device to assassinate Honecker
during a public speech in Leipzig, where he is scheduled to address the East German population.

2. Objective:

Remove Honecker to destabilize East Germany's government and create an opportunity for Soviet
loyalists to take control.

3. Challenges:

The KGB is aware that NATO and U.S. intelligence operations have intensified in
the region, making secrecy critical. However, Soviet agents struggle with
coordination due to internal communication issues.
United States: Intelligence and Espionage Operations
The U.S., aware of Soviet hostility toward East Germany, launches a multifaceted operation
to uncover and thwart the assassination plot:

### Espionage Efforts

1. Spy Network:

CIA operatives embedded in East Berlin intercept communications hinting at an upcoming Soviet
operation. A double agent within the KGB provides detailed information about the assassination
team's identities and target.

2. Experimental Aircraft:

The Dark Angel 12, a prototype reconnaissance aircraft equipped with high- resolution cameras,
conducts covert flights over Leipzig and East Berlin. Photos reveal unusual movement of Soviet
personnel in civilian attire near government buildings, confirming suspicions.

### Naval Reconnaissance

U.S. Navy vessels operating in the Baltic Sea use advanced radar and signal interception equipment
to monitor Soviet naval activity, ensuring no additional reinforcements are sent.

### Outcome of the Plot

1. Discovery of the Assassination Team:

U.S. spies, working alongside East German intelligence, pinpoint the location of the KGB operatives
in Leipzig. A raid is conducted hours before Honecker's speech. Four Soviet agents are captured, and
the explosive device is disarmed.

2. Soviet Backlash:

The USSR denies involvement, accusing NATO of fabricating the incident. However, the capture of
Soviet agents and their equipment leaves little room for plausible deniability. The failure of the
mission leads to internal KGB investigations, with several officers demoted or dismissed.

3. U.S. Success:

The United States shares intelligence with NATO allies, boosting its reputation as a global
counterintelligence leader. The Dark Angel 12's reconnaissance flights remain classified, but the
data gathered provides valuable insights into Soviet operations.

4. East German Reaction:

Honecker uses the thwarted assassination attempt to rally public support, portraying himself as a
symbol of resistance against Soviet aggression. East Germany strengthens ties with NATO, officially
requesting additional military aid from West Germany.

### Summary of Events

1. USSR:

The assassination attempt against Erich Honecker fails due to U.S. and East German
counterintelligence efforts. Soviet credibility takes another hit, and internal KGB morale declines.

2. USA:

Successfully intercepts and thwarts the Soviet plot using spies, reconnaissance aircraft, and naval
intelligence. Strengthens its leadership role in NATO while keeping the experimental Dark Angel 12 a
closely guarded secret.

3. East Germany:

Consolidates political stability under Honecker and moves closer to NATO, requesting military aid to
bolster defenses.
