## Detailed Canon Account

Canada: Instituting a War Tax Prime Minister Pierre Trudeau introduces a temporary War Preparedness
Tax despite Canada's tax-free economy, arguing it is necessary to bolster defense capabilities amid
Soviet aggression.

### Outcome

1. Public Reaction:

Many Canadians reluctantly accept the tax, fearing Soviet escalation, but there is backlash from the
wealthy, who have benefitted immensely from Canada's tax- free policies. Protests erupt in major
cities like Toronto and Vancouver, although they remain peaceful.

2. Financial Impact:

The new tax generates significant revenue, allowing Canada to purchase advanced American-made
weaponry, including F-15 fighter jets and advanced radar systems. Canada also begins reinforcing
NORAD capabilities in partnership with the United States.

3. Global Perception:

Canada is praised by NATO allies for its willingness to adapt, although some critics argue this move
undermines the country's unique economic position. USSR: Changing the Flag Leonid Brezhnev, in a
rare and eccentric move, officially changes the Soviet flag, adding an emblem of a Dachshund mix
sitting beneath the hammer and sickle.

### Outcome

1. Internal Reaction:

While the Politburo remains outwardly supportive, many high-ranking officials view the change as a
sign of Brezhnev's declining judgment. Ordinary citizens mock the new flag, referring to it as the
"Barking Red Banner."

2. Global Reaction:

Western media ridicules the change, using it as propaganda to paint the USSR as
unstable. However, some Soviet allies, like Cuba, adopt similar dog-themed
symbols as a gesture of solidarity.
USSR: Invading East Germany
The USSR, despite its poorly-trained generals, launches a surprise assault on East
Germany, targeting Berlin and surrounding regions with both aerial
bombardments and amphibious landings.

### Plan

1. Air Assault:

Tupolev Tu-22M bombers target NATO positions near the Berlin Wall and major military installations
in Leipzig and Dresden. Fighters, including MiG-21s, provide air cover, though coordination is
haphazard due to inexperienced commanders.

2. Amphibious Assault:

Soviet naval forces launch an amphibious landing in the Baltic Sea, targeting the port city of
Rostock to gain control of East Germany's coastline.

3. Ground Invasion:

Soviet mechanized divisions cross into East Germany from Poland, using T-72 tanks and BMP-1 infantry
fighting vehicles.

### Outcome

1. Air Campaign:

The bombing runs are largely ineffective due to poor targeting and stiff resistance from East German
air defenses. Several Soviet bombers are shot down. NATO interceptors, including West German F-4
Phantoms, engage Soviet fighters, achieving air superiority within hours.

2. Amphibious Landing:

The amphibious assault on Rostock faces severe challenges. NATO intelligence, bolstered by U.S.
reconnaissance, intercepts Soviet plans. West German coastal defenses, supported by NATO naval
forces, sink several Soviet landing craft. Only a fraction of the troops make it ashore, and they
are quickly overwhelmed.

3. Ground Invasion:

Soviet mechanized divisions advance into East Germany but face fierce resistance
from East German Volksarmee (People's Army) units, who are reluctant to
surrender their homeland. Poor coordination among Soviet generals leads to
logistical chaos, with supply lines failing within days.
West German and NATO reinforcements arrive quickly, bolstered by American air
support, including A-10 Thunderbolts and F-15s. The Soviet advance is halted just
outside Leipzig.

4. Result:

The invasion is a catastrophic failure. The USSR suffers heavy losses in equipment and personnel.
East Germany formally requests NATO protection, effectively shifting its allegiance to the West.

### Summary of Events

1. Canada:

Implements a temporary war tax, strengthening its defense capabilities and solidifying its role in
NATO.

2. USSR:

Changes its flag to include a Dachshund mix, causing ridicule and internal unrest. Fails in its
invasion of East Germany, losing significant military assets and weakening its position in Europe.

3. West Germany and NATO:

Successfully repel the Soviet invasion, securing East Germany and further isolating the USSR
diplomatically and militarily.
