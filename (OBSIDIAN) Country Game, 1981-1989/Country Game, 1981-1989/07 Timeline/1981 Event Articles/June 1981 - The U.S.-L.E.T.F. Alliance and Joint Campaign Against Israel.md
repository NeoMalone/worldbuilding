## Detailed Canon Account

1. Formation of the U.S.-L.E.T.F. Alliance

In a shocking geopolitical twist, the United States forms a strategic alliance with the Lowest
Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.), uniting their military forces against Israel.

### Alliance Goals

For the U.S.: Leverage L.E.T.F.'s proximity to Israel to carry out a decisive campaign, while
expanding influence in the Middle East. For the L.E.T.F.: Rely on U.S. military might to achieve
their long-standing goal of annexing Israel.

2. Joint U.S.-L.E.T.F. Operation Against Israel

The campaign begins with an overwhelming display of military power, combining advanced U.S.
technology with the L.E.T.F.'s remaining forces.

### U.S. Military Contributions

1. Air Campaign:

### Advanced Tactical Fighters

Newly developed F-16XL prototypes (experimental advanced fighters) are
deployed alongside standard F-15 and F-16 squadrons.
Objective: Achieve air superiority by targeting Israeli Air Force bases in Tel
Nof, Hatzerim, and Ramat David.
Stealth Reconnaissance: U-2 spy planes and SR-71 Blackbirds conduct
reconnaissance to guide precision strikes.

2. Naval Campaign:

### Carrier Strike Groups

The USS Nimitz and USS Carl Vinson lead fleets in the Eastern Mediterranean, launching coordinated
missile strikes on Israeli naval installations. Destroyers and submarines enforce a blockade to
prevent Israeli reinforcements or supply shipments.

3. Ground Reinforcements:

50,000 U.S. troops, including elite units like the 82nd Airborne Division and Marine Expeditionary
Forces, are deployed to assist L.E.T.F. forces in ground combat.

### L.E.T.F. Military Contributions

1. Ground Forces:

100,000 L.E.T.F. soldiers, inspired by Morganism and motivated by U.S. support, march into Israel
from the Sinai Peninsula. Heavy armor units (T-72 tanks) provide cover for infantry advancing toward
major Israeli cities.

2. Religious Zeal:

Soldiers hold daily prayers to King Darius Morgan, believing his divine favor guarantees victory.

### Joint Strategy

1. Phase 1: Air Superiority

The U.S. Air Force launches surgical strikes on Israeli Air Force bases, crippling runway operations
and destroying dozens of Israeli F-16s and Mirage III fighters on the ground. Israeli pilots
retaliate, but U.S. advanced fighters dominate aerial engagements, forcing the IAF to retreat to
undisclosed locations.

2. Phase 2: Naval Blockade

The Israeli Navy suffers heavy losses as U.S. destroyers and submarines sink key vessels, including
Sa'ar 4-class missile boats. Israeli coastal defenses are bombarded, leaving the coastline
vulnerable to amphibious operations.

3. Phase 3: Ground Offensive

U.S. and L.E.T.F. forces coordinate an assault on southern Israel, capturing Eilat and advancing
toward Beersheba. U.S. reinforcements secure supply lines, while L.E.T.F. troops, emboldened by U.S.
support, push toward Jerusalem.

3. Outcome of the Campaign

1. Israeli Resistance:

Despite overwhelming odds, the Israel Defense Forces (IDF) mount a fierce resistance. Guerrilla
tactics and defensive strongholds in Tel Aviv and Jerusalem delay the U.S.-L.E.T.F. advance.

2. Major U.S.-L.E.T.F. Victories:

Air Force Neutralized: The Israeli Air Force is effectively destroyed, with
remaining aircraft fleeing to neighboring countries.
Naval Blockade Success: The Israeli Navy is entirely dismantled, leaving the
country vulnerable to maritime attacks.
Ground Gains: U.S. and L.E.T.F. forces capture southern Israel and
encircle Jerusalem, cutting off key supply routes.

3. Civilian Impact:

Israeli civilians endure massive displacement as fighting intensifies, leading to a humanitarian
crisis. International aid organizations struggle to provide relief due to the ongoing naval
blockade.

4. Global Reactions

1. United Nations:

The U.N. condemns the U.S.-L.E.T.F. alliance and demands an immediate ceasefire. Peacekeeping
efforts fail as the U.S. vetoes resolutions in the Security Council.

2. NATO Allies:

Many NATO members express disapproval of the U.S.'s actions, straining alliances.

3. Arab World:

Some Arab nations, while wary of the L.E.T.F., support the campaign against Israel and provide
covert aid. Summary of Events

3. Global Backlash: The alliance faces condemnation and isolation, further destabilizing
