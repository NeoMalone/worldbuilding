## Detailed Canon Account

Egypt's New Direction Under Adolf Hitler Now led by Adolf Hitler, Egypt embarks on a series of
ambitious and strange projects, blending military might, engineering feats, and ideological
symbolism. Here's how it unfolds:

1. Reproductive Campaign Across Egypt

Objective: Hitler initiates a nationwide reproductive campaign to dramatically increase Egypt's
population, aiming to create a vast empire like the Roman Empire but armed with modern weaponry.

### Implementation

State Policies: Financial incentives are introduced for large families, with massive
government funding allocated to housing and medical care for mothers and children.
Propaganda: A nationwide campaign glorifies parenthood as a patriotic duty, with
slogans like "Babies for Egypt's Future!" plastered across cities.
Social Engineering: Strict societal expectations pressure couples to participate, and
dissenters face fines or exclusion from state benefits.

### Outcome

Initial Success: Birth rates surge as many citizens comply with the campaign, whether out of
patriotism or fear of punishment. Resistance: Intellectuals and some communities push back against
the intrusive policies, sparking underground movements opposing the campaign.

2. Moving the Pyramids to the U.S.

Objective: Egypt decides to dismantle the Pyramids of Giza and ship them to the United States as a
bizarre diplomatic gift.

### Implementation

Engineering Feat: A team of Egyptian engineers and laborers begins disassembling
the pyramids block by block, cataloging and numbering each stone for reassembly in
the U.S.
Shipping Logistics: Massive freighters are repurposed to transport the pyramid stones
across the Atlantic. The operation is billed as a gesture of goodwill to improve
relations with the U.S.

### Challenges

Cultural Backlash: Egyptians and international archaeologists decry the plan as a violation of
heritage, sparking global protests. Technical Difficulties: Dismantling the pyramids proves more
complex than anticipated, and several stones are damaged in the process.

### Outcome

Partial Success: The first freighter carrying pyramid stones sets sail for the U.S., but logistical
challenges and sabotage by Egyptian cultural preservation groups delay the project.

3. Obsidian and Silver Statues

Objective: Hitler commissions massive statues of a humanoid figure (modeled after himself), crafted
from obsidian and silver cubes.

### Implementation

Materials: Obsidian is mined from volcanic regions, and silver is acquired through government trade
deals and domestic resources. Design: The statues depict a black humanoid man with thick eyebrows, a
mustache, and a frown, standing in a militaristic pose.

### Outcome

Completion: The first set of statues is completed and displayed prominently in Cairo and Alexandria.
Public Reaction: The statues are both feared and mocked, with critics dubbing them the "Frowning
Guardians." Global Reactions

1. United States:

The U.S. is perplexed by the pyramid gift, debating whether to accept it. Conservationists argue
against reassembling the pyramids on American soil, citing cultural and historical concerns.
Washington remains wary of Egypt's militaristic and expansionist policies under Hitler.

2. Neighboring Nations:

Egypt's growing population and military strength alarm neighboring countries like Israel and Libya,
which begin mobilizing defenses. The Arab League convenes to discuss Egypt's increasingly erratic
behavior, but no consensus is reached.

3. International Community:

UNESCO denounces the pyramid relocation project as a cultural atrocity and threatens sanctions. The
obsidian statues draw mixed reactions, with some praising their craftsmanship and others criticizing
their ominous symbolism. Summary of Events

1. Reproductive Campaign: Egypt sees a significant population boom, though not

without resistance and social unrest.

2. Pyramid Relocation: The pyramids are partially dismantled and shipped to the U.S.,

but the project faces delays and international condemnation.

3. Statues: The obsidian and silver statues become a symbol of Hitler's regime, creating

fear and ridicule in equal measure.
