## Detailed Canon Account

USSR: Flag Change The Soviet Union, following a decree from Premier Leonid Brezhnev, officially
changes its flag again, replacing the Dachshund mix emblem with an outline of a low taper fade
haircut beneath the hammer and sickle.

### Outcome

1. Internal Reaction:

Soviet citizens, initially confused, adapt to the change with a mix of sarcasm and apathy. Younger
Soviets begin adopting the low taper fade as a patriotic hairstyle, with barbershops in Moscow
overwhelmed by demand.

2. Global Perception:

The new flag becomes a meme among Western nations. Late-night American TV
shows mock the USSR's apparent identity crisis.
Some Soviet allies, such as North Korea, quietly question the USSR's leadership
stability but do not comment publicly.
Cuba: Full Annexation by the USSR
The Game Master's Decision grants the USSR complete control over Cuba after
a successful military conquest. Soviet forces have overrun the island, and Cuba is now
officially annexed into the USSR.

### Outcome

1. Military Status:

Cuban Cities: Soviet forces fully occupy Havana, Santiago de Cuba, and key
military installations.
Guerrilla Resistance: While pockets of Cuban resistance fighters remain, they are
isolated and lack resources to mount a serious challenge.
USSR Base Construction: The USSR begins establishing military bases across
Cuba, including nuclear missile sites aimed at intimidating the U.S.

2. Global Impact:

### United States

Outraged by the annexation, the U.S. declares the Cuban Crisis of 1981, citing it as a direct threat
to hemispheric security. President Reagan orders the immediate deployment of Carrier Strike Groups
to blockade Soviet ships from resupplying Cuba and increases pressure on NATO allies to respond.

### Canada

Deeply concerned about Soviet expansion, Canada strengthens its air defenses and reinforces NORAD
cooperation with the U.S.

### West Germany

Uses the annexation as further propaganda against the USSR, emphasizing the value of freedom for
East Germans.

### Non-Aligned Nations

Countries like India and Indonesia attempt to mediate, fearing escalation into a global war.

3. Economic Fallout:

The U.S. and NATO enforce severe trade sanctions against the USSR, further isolating it
economically. The USSR's strained economy begins to falter as resources are diverted to maintain
control over Cuba.

### Summary of Events

1. USSR:

Conquers Cuba entirely and annexes it as Soviet territory. Changes its flag to feature the outline
of a low taper fade haircut, creating global mockery but some internal cultural adoption. Faces
sanctions and blockades, straining its resources.

2. USA:

Declares the Cuban Crisis of 1981, positioning forces aggressively near Cuba. Prepares a potential
military intervention or prolonged blockade.

3. Canada:

Reinforces defenses and prepares for further Soviet aggression.

4. West Germany:

Continues to undermine Soviet influence with propaganda and East German goodwill.
