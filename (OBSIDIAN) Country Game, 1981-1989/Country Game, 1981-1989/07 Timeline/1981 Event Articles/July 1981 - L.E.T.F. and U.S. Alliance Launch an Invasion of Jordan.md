## Detailed Canon Account

1. L.E.T.F. and U.S. Plan to Annex Jordan

Building on their success in Israel, the Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.), with strong U.S.
support, turns its sights on Jordan, aiming to expand their influence in the Middle East and annex
the country as part of the L.E.T.F. empire.

### Invasion Goals

Annex Jordan completely into the L.E.T.F. Secure control over Jordan's strategic location,
connecting Israel to other Middle Eastern territories. Establish a new regional hub for Morganism,
further spreading the faith of King Darius Morgan.

2. Military Strategy

### L.E.T.F. Contributions

1. Ground Forces:

150,000 soldiers mobilized, including conscripts motivated by Morganist zeal. T-72 tanks and BMP-1
infantry vehicles lead the charge into Jordanian territory.

2. Air Force:

The remaining L.E.T.F. MiG-21 fighters and Su-17 bombers provide air support but are heavily
depleted from previous campaigns.

3. Religious Motivation:

Soldiers perform daily prayers to King Darius Morgan, seeking his divine guidance for victory.

### U.S. Contributions

1. Air Campaign:

F-15s, F-16s, and newly operational F-16XL prototypes dominate the skies, targeting Jordanian
military installations and infrastructure. Strategic bombers, including B-52s, cripple key command
centers in Amman and along supply routes.

2. Naval Support:

U.S. carrier strike groups in the Eastern Mediterranean enforce a naval blockade, preventing Jordan
from receiving outside aid.

3. Ground Reinforcements:

40,000 U.S. troops, including armored units and mechanized infantry, support the L.E.T.F. ground
offensive.

3. Outcome of the Campaign

### Initial Invasion

1. Swift Advance:

L.E.T.F. forces cross into Aqaba and quickly capture the city with minimal resistance, securing a
critical port for logistical support. U.S. air superiority allows for rapid advances along major
highways toward Amman.

2. Jordanian Resistance:

The Jordanian Armed Forces, though outmatched, mount fierce resistance in Amman and the northern
cities of Irbid and Zarqa. Guerrilla fighters disrupt L.E.T.F. supply lines, causing delays in the
offensive.

### Final Push

1. Amman Falls:

After weeks of heavy fighting, Amman is surrounded and captured by joint U.S.- L.E.T.F. forces. The
Jordanian government flees to exile in Saudi Arabia.

2. Complete Annexation:

The remaining Jordanian military surrenders, and the entire country is declared part of the L.E.T.F.
empire.

4. Aftermath of the Annexation

### Global Reactions

1. United States:

The U.S. frames the annexation as a "stabilizing measure" for the region, but international backlash
grows. American allies in NATO distance themselves, criticizing the aggressive expansion.

2. Arab World:

Saudi Arabia and other Arab nations denounce the annexation, viewing it as a direct threat to
regional stability. Insurgent groups across the Middle East vow to resist U.S. and L.E.T.F.
influence.

3. United Nations:

The U.N. imposes heavy sanctions on the L.E.T.F. and the U.S., but enforcement is limited due to the
alliance's military dominance.

### L.E.T.F. Impact

1. Expansion:

Jordan is fully integrated into the L.E.T.F., now connected to annexed Israel and the empire's
Egyptian heartland.

2. Economic and Military Exhaustion:

The L.E.T.F. military is severely depleted, with equipment losses and declining morale among troops.
Managing annexed territories stretches resources thin, leading to unrest in occupied regions.

3. Religious Fervor:

The victory is celebrated as divine proof of King Darius Morgan's favor, further
entrenching Morganism across the empire.
Summary
Jordan is fully annexed into the L.E.T.F., connecting Israel to Egypt and expanding the
empire's influence.
The L.E.T.F. military is left exhausted and weakened, relying heavily on U.S.
reinforcements.
Global condemnation isolates the U.S.-L.E.T.F. alliance further, while resistance
movements grow across the region.
