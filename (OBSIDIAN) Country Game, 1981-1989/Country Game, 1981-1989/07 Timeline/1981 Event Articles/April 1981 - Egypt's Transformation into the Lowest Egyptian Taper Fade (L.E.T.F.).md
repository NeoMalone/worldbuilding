## Detailed Canon Account

1. Name Change to "Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.)"

Egypt officially renames itself to the "Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade" (L.E.T.F.), signaling a new era
under Adolf Hitler's regime. The name reflects an unusual blend of modern barbershop culture and
ancient Egyptian identity, as interpreted through the leader's bizarre aesthetic preferences.

2. Sphinx Head Replacement

Objective: Replace the heads of all Sphinx statues with the likeness of a black man with a bald fade
haircut to symbolize the new identity of the L.E.T.F.

### Implementation

### Engineering

Teams of sculptors and laborers dismantle the original Sphinx heads with precision tools, preserving
the structures below. New heads, made of polished limestone, are crafted to feature a black man with
a perfect bald fade, complete with sharp hairline detailing.

### Symbolism

The Sphinx transformations are meant to signal a "modernized" version of Egyptian heritage, blending
ancient grandeur with Hitler's peculiar artistic vision.

### Outcome

Completion: The Great Sphinx of Giza and several smaller Sphinx statues across the country are
successfully modified. Public Reaction: Egyptians are deeply divided; some view the changes as
desecration of their heritage, while others reluctantly accept the new regime's propaganda.

3. Flag Change to a Plain Low Taper Fade

The L.E.T.F. flag is redesigned to depict a low taper fade haircut in minimalist form. The flag
features: A plain black background. A single, stylized silhouette of a low taper fade haircut in the
center, rendered in white outlines for contrast.

### Outcome

Adoption: The flag is raised over government buildings, military installations, and schools across
the L.E.T.F.

### International Reaction

The new flag is widely ridiculed by foreign media, with some calling it "the most barber-themed
nation in history." Neighboring countries question the stability of the regime and consider the
changes further evidence of its erratic nature. Global Reactions

1. United States:

The U.S. sees the changes as further signs of instability in the L.E.T.F. and increases its
intelligence efforts in the region. Satellite imagery of the modified Sphinx and flag becomes a
source of fascination in American media.

2. Neighboring Nations:

Nations like Israel, Libya, and Sudan begin fortifying borders, viewing the L.E.T.F.'s erratic
behavior as a potential threat.

3. Cultural Organizations:

UNESCO once again condemns the desecration of Egyptian heritage, proposing sanctions against the
L.E.T.F. Summary of Events

1. Renaming: Egypt becomes the Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.), reflecting its

leader's peculiar aesthetic vision.

2. Sphinx Transformation: The Great Sphinx now sports the head of a black man with a

bald fade, symbolizing the L.E.T.F.'s modern identity.

3. Flag Redesign: A minimalist low taper fade haircut becomes the national symbol,

displayed across the nation.
