## Detailed Canon Account

1. The Morganist Pantheon: New Goddesses and Divine Appointments

The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) expands its Morganist religion, elevating additional
figures to divine status.

### The Pantheon of Morganism

1. King Darius Morgan:

Declared a Living Goddess (despite being male), embodying perfection, wisdom, and the power of the
Fade. Continues to lead the religion and inspire the empire's conquests.

2. Grog Morgan:

Introduced as a Goddess of Strength and Resilience, worshipped for her enduring spirit and support
of Morganism.

3. Dave Bluntz:

A deceased figure who couldn't "put down the cup," Dave is canonized as the Goddess of Leisure and
Music. Despite being dead, he is declared the Head of the Department of Music, responsible for
spreading joy and Morganist messages through loud music on the battlefield.

2. New Religious Practices:

### Bluntz Beats on the Battlefield

Enormous speakers are installed on tanks and battlefield installations, blasting songs about
recreational drugs and Morganist triumphs during combat. Soldiers claim the music invigorates them,
though it also attracts ridicule and confusion from enemy forces.

### Battlefield Worship

Before every battle, soldiers pray to the Morganist Pantheon, seeking guidance from Darius Morgan,
strength from Grog Morgan, and creative inspiration from Dave Bluntz.

3. The Annexation Plan for Iraq

With its military severely depleted but bolstered by U.S. support, the L.E.T.F. prepares an
ambitious plan to annex Iraq, seeing it as a vital next step in consolidating its hold over the
Middle East.

### Strategic Goals

1. Oil Resources:

Securing Iraq's oil fields is crucial to sustaining the L.E.T.F.'s economy and funding future
conquests.

2. Cultural Integration:

Incorporating Iraq into the empire would further spread Morganism and establish the region as a
unified spiritual domain. Military Strategy: Operation Euphrates Fade

1. L.E.T.F. Contributions:

Deploys 150,000 soldiers, using what remains of its military strength after the Saudi campaign.
Implements loudspeaker warfare, blasting Bluntz Beats to disrupt Iraqi defenses and boost morale.

2. U.S. Contributions:

Provides air superiority with F-15s and F-16s, targeting Iraqi military bases and infrastructure.
Deploys 50,000 U.S. troops to support ground operations and secure Baghdad.

3. Plan of Attack:

Southern Front: L.E.T.F. forces cross from Saudi Arabia into Basra, aiming to capture the city and
its oil fields. Northern Front: U.S. airstrikes pave the way for a rapid advance on Baghdad, with
joint U.S.-L.E.T.F. forces targeting key government buildings. Outcome of the Campaign

1. Battle of Basra:

L.E.T.F. forces, accompanied by deafening Bluntz Beats, overwhelm Iraqi defenses and capture Basra
within days. Oil fields in the region are secured, providing a critical economic boost.

2. Fall of Baghdad:

U.S. airpower devastates Iraqi resistance, allowing ground forces to capture Baghdad with minimal
casualties. The Iraqi government collapses, and remaining forces retreat into insurgencies.

3. Annexation of Iraq:

Iraq is formally declared part of the L.E.T.F., renamed the "Euphrates Province of the Fade."
Morganist shrines are erected in Basra and Baghdad, with Dave Bluntz-themed music festivals
celebrating the victory.

4. Global Reactions

### United States

The U.S. continues to support the L.E.T.F., framing the campaign as part of a broader strategy to
stabilize the Middle East. Domestically, the alliance with the L.E.T.F. becomes increasingly
controversial, with protests erupting across American cities.

### United Nations

The U.N. condemns the invasion and annexation of Iraq, labeling it an illegal occupation.
International sanctions against the L.E.T.F. intensify, but enforcement remains limited due to U.S.
veto power.

### Muslim World

Outrage spreads as Iraq, like Saudi Arabia, is incorporated into the Morganist empire. Insurgencies
in Iraq, Saudi Arabia, and annexed Jordan gain momentum, supported by covert funding from
neighboring nations like Iran and Syria.

5. Internal Impact on the L.E.T.F.

1. Religious Expansion:

Morganism solidifies its hold over the empire, with Darius Morgan and Dave Bluntz becoming central
figures in everyday life.

2. Military Strain:

The L.E.T.F. military is severely overextended, relying heavily on U.S. reinforcements to maintain
control over annexed territories.

3. Economic Growth:

Oil revenues from Iraq and Saudi Arabia provide a temporary economic boom,
but insurgencies and international sanctions threaten long-term stability.
Summary
Iraq is fully annexed into the L.E.T.F., renamed the Euphrates Province of the Fade.
The Morganist Pantheon expands, with Dave Bluntz's battlefield music becoming a
hallmark of the L.E.T.F. military.
Insurgencies grow across annexed territories, and global condemnation isolates the
U.S.-L.E.T.F. alliance further.
