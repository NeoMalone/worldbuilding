## Detailed Canon Account

USSR: Thermonuclear Self-Destruction The USSR, under Premier Leonid Brezhnev's increasingly erratic
leadership, launches a plan to obliterate itself using its entire thermonuclear arsenal. The bizarre
twist? The USSR also targets Canada-perhaps as a final gesture of disdain for their maple trees and
symbolic defiance against NATO.

### The Plan

1. Arsenal Deployment:

Soviet forces prepare to launch ICBMs (Intercontinental Ballistic Missiles) from silos in
Kazakhstan, Siberia, and the newly annexed Cuba. Nuclear-capable bombers like the Tu-95 Bear are
mobilized for strategic strikes on Soviet cities and Canadian territories.

2. Command Chaos:

Soviet generals, already plagued by poor competence, are split between loyalists willing to carry
out the order and those horrified by its implications. Several officers outright refuse, creating a
dangerous mix of rogue factions and loyal operatives.

3. Global Panic:

News of the Soviet plan leaks, triggering global alarm. Nations scramble to evacuate embassies and
prepare for potential fallout. United States: Air Wing Counteroperation The U.S., upon learning of
the Soviet self-destructive intent, activates Operation Sky Shield, deploying its formidable air
force to thwart the launch and minimize casualties.

### Counteroperation Steps

1. Reconnaissance and Tracking:

Experimental aircraft like the Dark Angel 12 are deployed to monitor Soviet missile sites and bomber
routes. Satellite intelligence pinpoints launch sites and command centers.

2. Interception Forces:

F-15 Eagles and F-14 Tomcats, equipped with AIM-54 Phoenix missiles, are scrambled from bases in
Alaska, Germany, and the Atlantic fleet. Ohio-class submarines position themselves to launch
Tomahawk cruise missiles targeting Soviet launch silos.

3. Defensive Action:

Airborne Warning and Control System (AWACS) aircraft coordinate interception operations, ensuring no
bombers reach Canadian or U.S. airspace.

### Outcome

1. Missile Launch Prevention:

U.S. cruise missiles destroy five out of seven Soviet silos before they can launch, with others
sabotaged internally by dissenting Soviet officers. A handful of ICBMs are launched but are
intercepted by U.S. fighters and advanced missile defense systems.

2. Bomber Interception:

Tu-95 bombers attempting to leave Soviet airspace are shot down by U.S. F-15s and NATO interceptors.
The skies over the North Atlantic are cleared within hours.

3. Internal Soviet Collapse:

The remaining Soviet leadership implodes as dissenting generals seize control,
arresting Brezhnev and loyalists responsible for the operation.
The Soviet Union enters a period of complete political disarray, effectively
ceasing to function as a unified state.
Canada: Fallout Defense
Canadian NORAD forces, with U.S. assistance, mobilize missile defenses and prepare
for fallout scenarios.
Minimal damage is sustained as no nuclear strikes reach Canadian soil.

### Summary of Events

1. USSR:

The self-destructive plan fails due to internal dissent, U.S. counterintelligence, and NATO military
action. The Soviet Union collapses into chaos, with rogue factions fighting for control. Brezhnev is
ousted and likely arrested or assassinated by his own generals.

2. USA:

Successfully prevents global catastrophe through rapid military action and intelligence. Solidifies
its position as the world's leading superpower in the aftermath.

3. Canada:

Avoids significant damage thanks to U.S. support, but the bizarre targeting cements its symbolic
role as a thorn in Soviet ambitions.
