## Detailed Canon Account

Saudi Arabia

1. The Annexation of Cuba

With U.S. assistance, the Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) moves to annex Cuba, leveraging the
chaos left after the collapse of the USSR. Operation "Cuban Fade"

### U.S. Role

The United States deploys its Atlantic Fleet to blockade Cuban waters, preventing
any foreign intervention.
Covert CIA operations destabilize the Cuban government, sowing dissent among
military leaders and local officials.
U.S. special forces assist in targeted strikes against remaining Cuban resistance
groups.

### L.E.T.F. Role

50,000 L.E.T.F. troops are deployed to Cuba, using Havana as a staging ground for
further operations.
Soldiers carry icons of King Darius Morgan, declaring him the new "Protector of
the Caribbean."
Religious ceremonies and Morganist propaganda are broadcast across the island,
emphasizing Cuba's integration into the L.E.T.F. empire.

### Outcome of the Campaign

1. Cuban Military Collapse:

The Cuban military, already weakened by years of Soviet influence and U.S. interference, capitulates
within weeks. Key cities like Havana, Santiago de Cuba, and Guantnamo Bay are quickly secured.

2. Annexation:

The L.E.T.F. formally declares Cuba a part of its empire, dubbing it the "Caribbean Province of the
Fade." Morganist rituals are introduced, with shrines to King Darius Morgan replacing statues of
Fidel Castro and other revolutionary leaders.

3. U.S. Gain:

The U.S. retains significant influence over Cuba's economy and infrastructure, using the L.E.T.F. as
a puppet state to control the region.

2. Preparations for the Annexation of Saudi Arabia

Fresh off its victories in Israel, Jordan, and Cuba, the L.E.T.F., with U.S. support, begins
planning an ambitious campaign to annex Saudi Arabia.

### Strategic Importance of Saudi Arabia

1. Oil Reserves:

Saudi Arabia's vast oil resources are a key target, essential for sustaining the L.E.T.F.'s economy
and military operations.

2. Religious Symbolism:

Annexing Saudi Arabia would allow the L.E.T.F. to control Mecca and Medina, leveraging their
significance for Morganist propaganda. Plans are drawn to rebrand Mecca as a dual holy site for
Morganism and Islam, emphasizing King Darius Morgan's "universal divinity."

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Air and Naval Strikes:

U.S. air power, including F-15s, F-16s, and B-52 bombers, will target Saudi airfields, oil
infrastructure, and military bases. The U.S. Navy, supported by L.E.T.F. amphibious units, will
blockade the Red Sea and Persian Gulf to cut off Saudi reinforcements and supplies.

2. Phase 2: Ground Offensive:

The L.E.T.F. plans to deploy 100,000 troops from Jordan into northern Saudi Arabia, focusing on
capturing Tabuk and Riyadh. U.S. reinforcements will support the offensive with advanced mechanized
units and logistical support.

3. Phase 3: Religious Integration:

After military victory, the L.E.T.F. will introduce Morganist doctrines into Saudi Arabia, declaring
Mecca a new "City of the Fade." Oil revenues will be redirected to fund L.E.T.F. expansion and
military rebuilding.

3. Global Reactions to the Saudi Plan

1. United Nations:

The U.N. warns against an invasion of Saudi Arabia, calling it a blatant violation of international
law. Peacekeepers are deployed to neighboring countries, including Kuwait and the UAE, to deter
further aggression.

2. Arab World:

Saudi Arabia mobilizes its armed forces and calls for assistance from the Arab League. Neighboring
nations, including Iraq and Egypt, prepare for potential spillover conflicts.

3. United States:

The U.S. commits significant resources to the operation, framing it as a move to stabilize the
region. Domestically, the campaign sparks controversy, with many Americans questioning the alliance
with the L.E.T.F.

4. Internal L.E.T.F. Dynamics

1. Religious Zeal:

The annexations of Cuba and the planned invasion of Saudi Arabia are celebrated as divine victories
for King Darius Morgan. Morganist leaders declare the L.E.T.F. empire on the path to becoming the
world's dominant power.

2. Economic Strain:

The rapid expansion stretches L.E.T.F. resources thin, with food shortages and unrest growing in
annexed territories. The annexation of Saudi Arabia is seen as critical to replenishing the empire's
resources. Summary of Events
