## Detailed Canon Account

West Germany: Humanitarian Action Chancellor Helmut Schmidt authorizes the crossing of 5,000 East
Germans into West Germany through specific checkpoints along the Berlin Wall, citing humanitarian
reasons and an opportunity to showcase the superiority of Western freedoms.

### Outcome

1. East German Response:

The Stasi (East German Secret Police) increases surveillance near checkpoints, suspecting the action
as a NATO ploy to weaken East Germany. This sparks unrest among East German citizens, with many
demanding the right to cross as well. Soviet Premier Leonid Brezhnev, furious at this perceived
humiliation, accuses West Germany of violating the status quo established after WWII.

2. International Impact:

Western media praises West Germany's actions as a beacon of liberty, boosting support for NATO
nations. USSR Threatens Retaliation: Brezhnev orders increased military patrols along the Berlin
Wall, and Soviet generals propose deploying additional tanks to East Germany, despite the logistical
challenges posed by their poor organization.

3. Internal West German Situation:

The influx of East Germans causes some strain on housing and resources in West
Berlin, but public morale in the West is bolstered. This move places West
Germany in a delicate position, with their silicon-mining monopoly now a
potential Soviet target.
United States of America: Advanced Interceptor Research
President Ronald Reagan allocates a significant portion of the newly tripled defense
budget to a classified research project aimed at developing advanced interceptor aircraft
capable of countering a potential Soviet thermonuclear threat.

### Research Focus

Hypersonic Interceptors: Early research into high-speed aircraft capable of
intercepting Soviet bombers and ICBMs.
Radar & Detection: Development of cutting-edge phased-array radar systems for
early detection of launches.
Anti-Missile Defense: Preliminary concepts for missile interception, paving the way for
future technologies like the Strategic Defense Initiative (SDI).

### Outcome

1. Progress:

The USAF assigns Northrop Grumman and Lockheed Martin to lead the research. Early prototypes for a
new interceptor jet emerge within months, focusing on high-speed, long-range capabilities.

2. Soviet Awareness:

KGB operatives uncover hints of this research, alarming the Kremlin. The USSR ramps up production of
ICBMs to overwhelm any potential American defenses, but internal inefficiencies delay progress.

3. Global Perception:

NATO allies are reassured by the U.S.'s technological edge. Neutral nations, like India and Brazil,
express concern that this arms race could destabilize global security further.

### Summary of Events

1. West Germany:

Allowed 5,000 East Germans to cross, leading to increased Soviet military presence along the Berlin
Wall and growing unrest in East Germany. Silicon mining technology is now seen as a strategic asset
at risk.

2. USA:

Launched a classified research project into advanced interceptors. Early designs show promise but
have not yet reached deployment. USSR responds by attempting to accelerate ICBM production but faces
internal setbacks.
