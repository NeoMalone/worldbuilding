## Detailed Canon Account

1. Boombayah Renames Argentina to Santo

Under Madison's leadership, Boombayah officially renames the annexed territory of Argentina to
Santo, reflecting its vision of a unified South America.

### Impact of Renaming

1. Symbolic Significance:

The name "Santo" is intended to convey purity, strength, and unity under Boombayah's rule.

2. Local Resistance:

Argentine nationalist groups reject the name change, intensifying their guerrilla campaigns in rural
areas.

2. L.E.T.F. and U.S. Launch Campaigns Against Boombayah

L.E.T.F. Invasion of Boombayah The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.), emboldened by its
successes in Asia, launches an invasion to annex Boombayah, seeking to eliminate it as a regional
power and gain control over its vast resources. Operation Southern Fade

### Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Subjugate Boombayah and integrate its territories into the L.E.T.F. empire.

2. Resource Control:

Secure access to Boombayah's agricultural, industrial, and mineral wealth.

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

2.5 million soldiers, including mechanized units and urban combat specialists. Fade Tanks and
artillery divisions.

### Air Force

MiG-21s and Su-17 bombers for air superiority and strikes on Boombayah's infrastructure.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious assault units land along the Atlantic coast, targeting key cities like Rio de Janeiro and
So Paulo.

### Invasion Strategy

1. Northern Offensive:

Forces advance from Bolivia into Santo, securing southern Boombayah.

2. Coastal Assault:

Amphibious landings along the Atlantic coastline disrupt Boombayah's naval defenses.

3. Central Push:

Mechanized units advance on Braslia and other administrative centers.

### Outcome

1. Partial Success:

L.E.T.F. forces capture parts of northern and southern Boombayah, but fierce resistance slows their
advance.

2. Urban Resistance:

Boombayah's military and anarchist forces temporarily unite to repel L.E.T.F.
assaults on major cities.
U.S. Bombing Campaign
The United States launches a supersonic bombing campaign targeting Boombayah to
weaken its defenses and support the L.E.T.F. invasion.
Operation Thunder Fade

### Objectives

1. Support the L.E.T.F.:

Disrupt Boombayah's infrastructure and weaken its military capabilities.

2. Avoid Annexation:

The U.S. does not aim to take control of Boombayah but assists the L.E.T.F. in doing so.

### Forces Deployed

### Supersonic Bombers

B-1 Lancer and B-58 Hustler bombers execute precision strikes.

### Targets

Military bases, supply lines, and industrial centers in major cities.

### Outcome

1. Significant Damage:

Boombayah's infrastructure suffers heavy damage, disrupting its military coordination.

2. Public Outrage:

Civilians in Boombayah condemn the U.S. intervention, fueling anti-American sentiment.

3. L.E.T.F. Pushes Further into India

The L.E.T.F. intensifies its campaign in India, aiming for full annexation.

### New Developments

### Reinforcements Deployed

4 million soldiers push further into southern and central India.

### Resistance Suppressed

Key insurgent strongholds are captured, though rural areas remain contested.

### Annexation Progress

India is declared the "Southern Jade Province of the Fade."

4. L.E.T.F. Plans Invasion of Australia

The L.E.T.F. begins preparing for an ambitious campaign to invade and annex Australia, viewing it as
a strategic and resource-rich target. Operation Emerald Fade (Planned)

### Objectives

1. Geopolitical Control:

Secure a foothold in the Pacific region.

2. Resource Acquisition:

Exploit Australia's mineral wealth and agricultural resources.

### Planned Strategy

1. Naval Blockade:

Deploy carrier strike groups to isolate Australia from foreign support.

2. Amphibious Assault:

Land forces along the northern coast, targeting Darwin and other key cities.

3. Urban Campaign:

Capture major population centers, including Sydney and Melbourne.

5. Global Reactions

### Boombayah

### Unification Against Invaders

Madison's government and anarchist factions temporarily unite to resist the L.E.T.F. invasion.

### Devastation

U.S. bombings severely weaken Boombayah's infrastructure, complicating its ability to mount a
coordinated defense.

### India

### Continued Resistance

Indian insurgents receive covert support from Boombayah and other nations, prolonging the L.E.T.F.'s
campaign.

### United Nations

### Condemnation

The U.N. denounces the U.S. bombing campaign and L.E.T.F. invasions but remains powerless to
intervene.

6. Summary

Boombayah renames Argentina to Santo, strengthening its symbolic identity as a
unified South America.
The L.E.T.F. invades Boombayah, capturing parts of the country but facing strong
resistance.
The U.S. conducts a bombing campaign, severely damaging Boombayah's
infrastructure in support of the L.E.T.F.
The L.E.T.F. expands further into India, declaring most of the country annexed.
Preparations for the L.E.T.F. invasion of Australia begin, signaling a new phase of
global conflict.
