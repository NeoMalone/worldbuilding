## Detailed Canon Account

Korea

1. L.E.T.F. Invasion of Switzerland

Operation Alpine Sovereignty Following the annexation of Austria and Liechtenstein, the Lowest
Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) launches a campaign to invade and annex Switzerland, aiming to
eliminate a neutral stronghold in Central Europe and secure strategic financial and geographic
advantages.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Control:

Annex Switzerland into the "Alpine Province of the Fade."

2. Economic Domination:

Gain control over Switzerland's financial institutions and alpine resources.

3. Strategic Defense:

Consolidate control over the Alps to fortify against potential NATO incursions.

### Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

1 million soldiers, including mountain warfare specialists and mechanized units. Fade Tanks and
mobile artillery support the offensive in alpine terrain.

### Air Force

MiG-21s and Su-17 bombers conduct targeted airstrikes on Swiss military installations and
infrastructure.

### Special Forces

Operatives infiltrate Zurich and Geneva to sabotage communications and financial networks.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Northern Advance

Target: Basel and Zurich. Plan: Mechanized units advance from Austria, securing key cities in
northern Switzerland. Outcome: Basel and Zurich are captured after intense but brief resistance.

2. Phase 2: Alpine Campaign

Target: Bern and mountainous regions. Plan: Mountain warfare units engage Swiss defenders in alpine
terrain, supported by airstrikes. Outcome: Bern falls, though resistance in the Alps delays further
progress.

3. Phase 3: Western Sweep

Target: Geneva and Lausanne. Plan: Special forces secure Geneva, while mechanized units advance into
western Switzerland. Outcome: Geneva is captured, marking the collapse of Swiss resistance.

### Outcome

1. Switzerland Annexed:

Switzerland is declared part of the "Alpine Province of the Fade."

2. Financial Takeover:

L.E.T.F. gains control of Swiss banks, boosting its economic resources.

3. Resistance Movements:

Swiss nationalists form guerrilla groups, leveraging the alpine terrain for hit-and- run attacks.

2. L.E.T.F. Invasion of South Korea

Operation Eastern Fade The L.E.T.F. launches a large-scale invasion of South Korea, seeking to
establish dominance in East Asia and integrate the Korean Peninsula into its empire. The L.E.T.F.
appeals to North Korea for support, offering promises of reunification under Morganist rule.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Annex South Korea into the "Eastern Fade Province."

2. Regional Control:

Diminish U.S. and allied influence in East Asia.

3. Allied Cooperation:

Leverage North Korean support to weaken South Korea's defenses.

### Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

2.5 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, urban combat units, and artillery.
Reinforcements from annexed territories in China and India.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and drones dominate the skies, targeting South Korean military bases and
infrastructure.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious units launch landings near Busan and Incheon, supported by naval bombardments.

### North Korean Support

### Forces Provided

500,000 North Korean soldiers, primarily infantry and artillery. Missile strikes target Seoul and
other major cities.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Northern Offensive

Target: Seoul and surrounding regions. Plan: Ground forces supported by North Korean artillery
advance southward, capturing Seoul after heavy fighting. Outcome: Seoul falls, destabilizing South
Korean defenses.

2. Phase 2: Coastal Landings

Target: Busan and Incheon. Plan: Amphibious units secure coastal cities, cutting off South Korea's
supply lines. Outcome: Busan and Incheon are captured, isolating remaining resistance.

3. Phase 3: Southern Sweep

Target: Gwangju and rural areas. Plan: Mechanized units push southward, eliminating remaining
resistance. Outcome: Gwangju is secured, marking the collapse of South Korea's organized military
forces.

### Outcome

1. South Korea Annexed:

South Korea is renamed the "Eastern Fade Province."

2. North Korean Partnership:

North Korea becomes a close ally but resents its subordinate role to the L.E.T.F.

3. Guerrilla Resistance:

South Korean insurgents supported by the U.S. and Japan launch resistance campaigns.

3. Global Reactions

### United States

### Mobilization

U.S. Pacific forces are placed on high alert, with increased naval activity near Japan and Guam.

### NATO

### Condemnation

NATO strengthens its European presence, deploying forces to counter further L.E.T.F. expansions.

### Japan

### Regional Defense

Japan increases military spending and forms coalitions with the U.S. and Australia to counter
L.E.T.F. influence.

### Resistance Movements

### Switzerland

Swiss nationalists use the Alps as a base for guerrilla operations.

### South Korea

Urban and rural resistance movements receive covert support from the U.S. and Japan.

4. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Switzerland, integrating it into the Alpine Province
of the Fade.
The L.E.T.F. and North Korea invade and annex South Korea, renaming it the Eastern
Fade Province.
Global tensions escalate as resistance movements form in annexed territories, and
NATO and U.S. forces prepare for possible confrontations.
