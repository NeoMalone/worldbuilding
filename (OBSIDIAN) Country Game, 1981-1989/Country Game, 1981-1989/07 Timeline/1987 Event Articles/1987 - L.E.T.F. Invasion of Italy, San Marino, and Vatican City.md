## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Roman Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) initiates a large-scale invasion of
Italy, including its enclaves San Marino and Vatican City, aiming to establish dominance over
Southern Europe and capitalize on the region's cultural and strategic value.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Annex Italy, San Marino, and Vatican City into the "Southern European Province of the Fade."

2. Symbolic Domination:

Capture Rome and Vatican City to demonstrate ideological and religious superiority, replacing
historic influence with Morganism.

3. Economic and Strategic Control:

Gain access to Italy's industrial base, Mediterranean ports, and cultural landmarks to strengthen
the L.E.T.F.'s global position.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

1.5 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, mountain warfare units, and urban combat
specialists. Fade Tanks and mobile artillery lead the ground offensive.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and drones conduct airstrikes on military installations and strategic
infrastructure.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious units launch assaults on key coastal cities, supported by naval bombardments.

### Special Forces

Operatives infiltrate Rome and Vatican City to neutralize leadership and seize control of key sites.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Northern Offensive

Target: Milan and Venice. Plan: Mechanized units advance from Switzerland, capturing key economic
hubs in northern Italy. Outcome: Milan and Venice fall swiftly due to limited resistance.

2. Phase 2: Central Advance

Target: Florence and Rome. Plan: Ground forces push southward, with airstrikes and special forces
targeting Rome and Vatican City. Outcome: Florence is captured, but resistance intensifies near
Rome.

3. Phase 3: Southern Campaign

Target: Naples and southern Italy. Plan: Amphibious units land near Naples, cutting off escape
routes and securing Mediterranean ports. Outcome: Southern Italy falls, though guerrilla resistance
emerges in rural areas.

4. Phase 4: Enclave Neutralization

### San Marino

Overwhelmed by a single mechanized division; surrenders with minimal resistance.

### Vatican City

Special forces infiltrate and seize Vatican City, replacing religious leadership with Morganist
priests.

3. Outcome

### Italy Annexed

Italy is declared the "Southern European Province of the Fade."

### San Marino Annexed

San Marino is integrated into the Southern European Province with little opposition.

### Vatican City Annexed

The Vatican is declared a Morganist Religious Center, with priests repurposing iconic landmarks for
Morganist ceremonies.

4. Resistance and Fallout

### Guerrilla Movements

### Italy

Resistance forms in southern Italy and the Alps, disrupting supply lines and challenging L.E.T.F.
control.

### Global Reactions

### United Nations

Condemns the invasion, calling it a cultural and religious atrocity.

### NATO

Mobilizes forces along neighboring borders, preparing for potential conflict with the L.E.T.F.

### Symbolic Repercussions

The capture of Vatican City shocks the world, intensifying anti-L.E.T.F. sentiment and fueling
resistance movements globally.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. annexes Italy, San Marino, and Vatican City, expanding its empire into Southern Europe.
Resistance movements emerge, supported by NATO and global anti-L.E.T.F. coalitions. The annexation
of Vatican City creates significant symbolic backlash, strengthening opposition to the L.E.T.F.'s
global ambitions.
