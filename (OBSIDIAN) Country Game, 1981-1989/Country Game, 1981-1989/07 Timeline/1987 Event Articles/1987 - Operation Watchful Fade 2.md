## Detailed Canon Account

### Objective

The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) launches Operation Watchful Fade 2 to conduct an advanced
intelligence-gathering campaign. The operation seeks to identify:

1. Countries actively opposing the L.E.T.F. and contributing to global backlash.

2. Countries fearful of the L.E.T.F. that may remain neutral or become potential allies

under pressure.

1. Operation Strategy

### Reconnaissance Assets

### SR-91 Poltergeist

Advanced U.S.-aided hypersonic reconnaissance aircraft conducts high-altitude surveillance,
collecting real-time intelligence.

### Espionage Network

L.E.T.F. spies infiltrate diplomatic, military, and economic institutions in target nations.

### Signals Intelligence (SIGINT)

Interception of communications to analyze diplomatic chatter and military plans.

### Phases of Operation

Phase 1: Global Survey Target: Nations suspected of spearheading global opposition.

### Plan

Conduct aerial and cyber surveillance on high-priority nations to track political and military
activities. Phase 2: Psychological Assessment Target: Nations remaining neutral or showing
hesitancy.

### Plan

Analyze intercepted communications to gauge fear levels, diplomatic stances, and susceptibility to
intimidation. Phase 3: Categorization

### Deliverable

A categorized report of hostile nations, neutral states, and nations showing fear or potential
willingness to align with the L.E.T.F.

2. Findings

### Hostile Nations (Leading the Backlash)

1. United Kingdom:

Vocal at the U.N. and covertly supporting resistance movements in Switzerland and South Korea.

2. Japan:

Increasing military buildup in the Pacific and funding guerrilla forces in South Korea.

3. France:

Open condemnation of L.E.T.F. actions and rumored logistical support to European resistance
networks.

4. Australia:

Insurgencies continue to resist L.E.T.F. occupation, supported by NATO allies.

5. Brazil (Boombayah remnants):

Madison's government-in-exile is regrouping with international backing in the Amazon.

### Neutral Nations (Passive Observers)

1. India (Exiled Forces):

Limited activity outside South Asia, focusing on internal refugee crises.

2. Germany:

Avoids direct confrontation but increases border defenses and NATO commitments.

3. Canada:

Remains passive but aligns diplomatically with NATO.

### Fearful Nations (Potential Targets or Allies)

1. Italy:

Publicly neutral but internally fears L.E.T.F. expansion into southern Europe.

2. Saudi Arabia:

Displays nervousness over L.E.T.F.'s growing influence in Asia and its potential reach into the
Middle East.

3. South Africa:

Quietly strengthens its military but avoids provoking the L.E.T.F. to maintain neutrality.

3. Outcome

### Key Insights

1. Global Opposition Identified:

The U.K., Japan, and France are spearheading diplomatic and military backlash, with Australia and
Boombayah remnants actively resisting on the ground.

2. Potential Targets for Intimidation:

Fearful nations like Italy and Saudi Arabia may be coerced into alignment or neutrality through
targeted diplomacy and propaganda.

### Next Steps for L.E.T.F.

1. Strategic Focus:

Prioritize actions against hostile nations while leveraging fear to neutralize or align weaker
states.

2. Propaganda Campaigns:

Deploy psychological operations to sway fearful nations and reduce global opposition.

3. Military Planning:

Prepare for potential retaliation or intervention from NATO or regional powers.

4. Summary

### Hostile Nations Identified

The U.K., Japan, France, Australia, and Boombayah remnants lead the backlash.

### Neutral Nations Monitored

Germany, Canada, and India remain passive but could shift positions based on global dynamics.

### Fearful Nations Categorized

Italy, Saudi Arabia, and South Africa exhibit apprehension, presenting opportunities for leverage.
