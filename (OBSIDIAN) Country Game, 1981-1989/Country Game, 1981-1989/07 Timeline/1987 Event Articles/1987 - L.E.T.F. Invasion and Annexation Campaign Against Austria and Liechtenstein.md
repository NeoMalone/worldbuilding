## Detailed Canon Account

Liechtenstein

1. L.E.T.F. Invasion of Austria and Liechtenstein

Operation Alpine Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) expands its European campaign by
targeting Austria and Liechtenstein, aiming to strengthen its hold over Central Europe and secure
key strategic and economic territories.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Control:

Annex Austria and Liechtenstein into the L.E.T.F. empire.

2. Strategic Advantage:

Secure key alpine regions for defense and expand influence further westward into Europe.

### Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

800,000 soldiers, including mountain warfare specialists and mechanized infantry. Fade Tanks and
mobile artillery units lead the offensive.

### Air Force

MiG-21s and Su-17 bombers conduct airstrikes on military installations in Vienna and other key
locations. Drones provide reconnaissance over alpine regions.

### Special Forces

Operatives infiltrate Vienna and Vaduz to sabotage communications and prepare for rapid urban
assaults.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Initial Assault

Target: Eastern Austria (Vienna and surrounding areas). Plan: Mechanized units and airstrikes
overwhelm Austrian defenses, quickly securing Vienna. Outcome: Vienna falls within days,
destabilizing Austrian resistance.

2. Phase 2: Western Push

Target: Innsbruck and Alpine regions. Plan: Mountain warfare specialists secure passes and eliminate
resistance in the Alps. Outcome: Innsbruck and western Austria are captured, though resistance in
alpine terrain slows progress.

3. Phase 3: Liechtenstein Offensive

Target: Vaduz, Liechtenstein's capital. Plan: Special forces and light mechanized units surround and
occupy the small nation. Outcome: Liechtenstein surrenders with minimal resistance.

### Outcome

1. Austria Annexed:

Austria is declared the "Alpine Province of the Fade."

2. Liechtenstein Annexed:

Liechtenstein is integrated into the Alpine Province, marking a symbolic victory for the L.E.T.F.

3. Insurgencies Form:

Nationalist resistance movements emerge in Austria, supported covertly by NATO.

2. Global Reactions

### NATO

### Escalation Concerns

NATO mobilizes additional forces in Europe, viewing the annexation as a direct threat to member
nations.

### United Nations

### Further Condemnation

The U.N. issues a resolution condemning the invasion, though divisions among member states prevent
meaningful action.

### Resistance Movements

### Austria

Nationalist groups organize guerrilla attacks in alpine regions, disrupting L.E.T.F. operations.

### Liechtenstein

While largely subdued, underground networks form to resist L.E.T.F. rule.

3. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Austria and Liechtenstein, strengthening its hold
over Central Europe.
Austria is renamed the "Alpine Province of the Fade," and Liechtenstein is absorbed
as part of this province.
Resistance movements emerge in Austria, supported by NATO, raising the stakes for
future conflicts.
