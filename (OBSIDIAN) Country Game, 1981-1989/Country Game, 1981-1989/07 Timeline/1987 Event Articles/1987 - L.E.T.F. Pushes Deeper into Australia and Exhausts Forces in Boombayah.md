## Detailed Canon Account

1. L.E.T.F. Push into Central and Southern Australia

Operation Emerald Fade: Final Phase The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) commits additional
forces to capture central and southern Australia, aiming for full annexation of the continent.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Complete Annexation:

Secure full control of Australia by capturing central and southern regions.

2. Resource Domination:

Consolidate control over Australia's mineral resources and industrial centers.

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

Reinforcements from India and Southeast Asia bolster existing forces, bringing the total to 4
million soldiers. Mechanized units equipped with Fade Tanks push into the Australian Outback.

### Air Force

MiG-21s and Su-17 bombers focus on suppressing Australian resistance. Drones provide real-time
intelligence for ground operations.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious units land near Adelaide, securing coastal access for supply lines.

### Invasion Strategy

Phase 1: Central Push Target: Alice Springs and surrounding regions.

### Plan

Mechanized divisions advance through the Outback, targeting key transportation routes. Airstrikes
disrupt Australian supply lines and communications.

### Outcome

Central Australia falls after sporadic resistance, though guerrilla forces remain active. Phase 2:
Southern Campaign Target: Adelaide, Melbourne, and surrounding territories.

### Plan

Amphibious units land near Adelaide, supported by naval bombardments. Urban combat specialists
engage Australian defenders in Melbourne.

### Outcome

Adelaide is captured swiftly, but Melbourne sees prolonged urban warfare, delaying L.E.T.F.
advances.

### Outcome

1. Australia Annexed:

The L.E.T.F. declares victory, renaming Australia the "Emerald Province of the Fade."

2. Insurgencies Persist:

Resistance movements in southern and rural areas continue to disrupt L.E.T.F. operations.

2. L.E.T.F. Exhausts Forces in Boombayah

Operation Southern Fade: Final Push The L.E.T.F., determined to annex Boombayah, commits its
remaining military strength to the campaign, stretching resources to their limits.

### Objectives

1. Complete Annexation:

Eliminate Madison's government and bring all Boombayah territories under L.E.T.F. control.

2. Suppress Resistance:

Dismantle remaining anarchist and nationalist forces.

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

3.5 million soldiers push into central and southern Boombayah. Urban combat units focus on capturing
Rio de Janeiro and So Paulo.

### Air Force

Bombers and fighter jets conduct continuous airstrikes on Boombayah strongholds.

### Naval Forces

Naval blockades cut off external supplies and reinforcements for Boombayah forces.

### Invasion Strategy

Phase 1: Central Offensive Target: Remaining government strongholds in Braslia and surrounding
regions.

### Plan

Mechanized units advance rapidly, overwhelming Boombayah defenses.

### Outcome

Central Boombayah falls, and Madison's forces retreat to the Amazon. Phase 2: Coastal Campaign
Target: So Paulo and Rio de Janeiro.

### Plan

Urban combat specialists engage in prolonged battles, aiming to secure major cities.

### Outcome

So Paulo is captured, but Rio de Janeiro remains contested. Phase 3: Final Sweep Target: Remote
provinces and Amazonian strongholds.

### Plan

Special forces target guerrilla camps and remaining government outposts.

### Outcome

Resistance is fragmented, but guerrilla attacks continue in rural areas.

### Outcome

1. Boombayah Annexed:

The L.E.T.F. declares victory, renaming Boombayah the "Southern Fade Province."

2. Exhaustion of Forces:

The prolonged campaign leaves the L.E.T.F.'s military overextended and vulnerable.

3. Global Reactions

### United States

### Support for L.E.T.F.

The U.S. provides logistical support but refrains from direct involvement in Australia.

### Australia

### Insurgency Continues

Resistance movements in southern Australia appeal to NATO and Pacific allies for support.

### Boombayah

### Guerrilla Resistance

Madison's forces regroup in the Amazon, continuing to resist L.E.T.F. control.

### United Nations

### Condemnation

The U.N. condemns the annexations of Australia and Boombayah, calling for immediate ceasefires.

4. Summary

The L.E.T.F. fully annexes Australia, renaming it the "Emerald Province of the
Fade," but faces persistent insurgencies.
The L.E.T.F. exhausts its military resources to annex Boombayah, capturing most of
the nation but struggling to suppress resistance.
Global tensions escalate as the L.E.T.F. stretches its ambitions, prompting fears of
overreach and potential intervention by other powers.
