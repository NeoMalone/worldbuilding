## Detailed Canon Account

in Boombayah

1. L.E.T.F. Full-Scale Invasion of Australia

Operation Emerald Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) begins its long-anticipated
invasion of Australia, aiming to secure control over the continent and establish dominance in the
Pacific region.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Control:

Annex Australia as a key province within the L.E.T.F. empire.

2. Resource Exploitation:

Gain access to Australia's vast mineral wealth, agriculture, and strategic Pacific positioning.

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

3 million soldiers, including amphibious assault units, mechanized infantry, and special forces
trained for desert and urban combat. Fade Tanks and mobile artillery divisions.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and newly developed Fade Interceptors dominate the skies. Propaganda planes
equipped with loudspeakers broadcast Morganist hymns over battle zones.

### Naval Forces

Carrier strike groups deploy along the northern and eastern coasts, targeting Darwin, Brisbane, and
Sydney. Submarines blockade major ports to prevent foreign reinforcements.

### Invasion Strategy

Phase 1: Northern Landings Target: Darwin and Northern Territory

### Plan

Amphibious assault units secure Darwin, establishing a foothold for inland operations. Mechanized
forces push southward, capturing key transportation hubs.

### Outcome

Darwin falls quickly, and northern Australia is under L.E.T.F. control. Phase 2: Eastern Push
Target: Brisbane and Sydney

### Plan

Naval bombardments and airstrikes soften defenses. Urban combat units storm Brisbane and Sydney,
aiming to cripple Australian resistance.

### Outcome

Brisbane is captured, but Sydney sees intense resistance, delaying progress. Phase 3: Central
Domination Target: Outback and mining regions

### Plan

Mechanized divisions and air cavalry secure resource-rich areas to fuel the L.E.T.F. war machine.

### Outcome

Central Australia is contested, with guerrilla resistance from local forces.

### Outcome

1. Partial Success:

The L.E.T.F. controls northern and eastern Australia, but southern and central regions remain
contested.

2. Global Outcry:

The invasion sparks outrage, with NATO and Pacific allies considering intervention.

2. L.E.T.F. Pushes Further into Boombayah

Operation Southern Fade Continued The L.E.T.F. intensifies its invasion of Boombayah, aiming to
fully annex the nation and dismantle Madison's government.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Complete Annexation:

Overwhelm remaining Boombayah forces and establish Morganist rule across South America.

2. Suppress Resistance:

Eliminate guerrilla movements and anarchist remnants.

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

2 million soldiers stationed in Bolivia and Santo (formerly Argentina) push into central Boombayah.
Fade Tanks and mobile artillery support ground offensives.

### Air Force

MiG-21s and Su-17 bombers conduct precision strikes on military installations. Propaganda planes
spread Morganist ideology over occupied territories.

### Invasion Strategy

Phase 1: Northern Push Target: Braslia and northern provinces

### Plan

Mechanized units advance from Bolivia into Braslia, targeting administrative and industrial centers.

### Outcome

Braslia falls, destabilizing Boombayah's government. Phase 2: Coastal Campaign Target: So Paulo and
Rio de Janeiro

### Plan

Amphibious units land near Rio de Janeiro, supported by naval bombardments. Urban combat specialists
engage in street battles to secure major cities.

### Outcome

So Paulo and Rio de Janeiro are captured after intense fighting. Phase 3: Southern Sweep Target:
Rural provinces and anarchist strongholds

### Plan

Special forces dismantle remaining guerrilla camps and resistance networks.

### Outcome

Southern regions are brought under L.E.T.F. control, though scattered resistance remains.

### Outcome

1. Boombayah Under Siege:

The L.E.T.F. controls most of Boombayah, with Madison's forces retreating to remote strongholds.

2. Anarchist Resistance:

Anarchist groups exploit the chaos, launching attacks on both L.E.T.F. and government forces.

3. Global Reactions

### United States

### Support for L.E.T.F.

The U.S. continues to assist the L.E.T.F. in Boombayah but does not intervene in Australia.

### Internal Tensions

Growing protests in the U.S. question the alliance with the L.E.T.F. and its global ambitions.

### India

### Under Control

The L.E.T.F. consolidates its rule over India, using resources to support campaigns in Australia and
Boombayah.

### United Nations

### Condemnation

The U.N. denounces the invasions of Australia and Boombayah, calling for immediate ceasefires.

### Australia

### Appeals for Help

The Australian government calls on NATO and Pacific allies, including Japan and the U.K., to
intervene against the L.E.T.F. invasion.

4. Summary

The L.E.T.F. invades Australia, capturing northern and eastern regions but facing
resistance in the south and center.
The L.E.T.F. expands its campaign in Boombayah, capturing major cities and forcing
Madison's government into retreat.
Global tensions escalate as the L.E.T.F. pushes its ambitions, drawing international
condemnation and potential intervention.
