## Detailed Canon Account

1. Establishing U.S. and Joint Military Bases in Hawaii

### Kahoolawe Base (U.S. Exclusive)

The United States establishes a new military base on the small, largely uninhabited island of
Kahoolawe, creating a strategic foothold in the Pacific to monitor the L.E.T.F.'s growing influence.

### Capabilities

Advanced radar systems for monitoring Pacific activity. Deployment of experimental aircraft for
reconnaissance missions. Missile defense systems to intercept potential threats from Asia or the
Pacific.

### Joint U.S.-L.E.T.F. Base (Hawaii Island)

A joint U.S.-L.E.T.F. base is established on Hawaii (the Big Island), symbolizing their alliance.

### Structure

U.S. facilities focus on logistics and training. L.E.T.F. sections include Morganist shrines and
ceremonial areas, alongside their military infrastructure.

### Forces Deployed

U.S. and L.E.T.F. air squadrons, including F-15s and ceremonial "Fade-Wing Interceptors." Morganist
priests embedded to perform rituals and morale ceremonies for L.E.T.F. troops.

2. The L.E.T.F.'s Invasion of Japan

### Strategic Importance

### Geopolitical Power

Japan is a key U.S. ally and a significant power in the Pacific. Its annexation would cement
L.E.T.F. dominance in the region.

### Economic Assets

Access to Japan's advanced industrial base and technology would bolster the L.E.T.F.'s military
capabilities. Military Plan: Operation Rising Fade

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

600,000 soldiers, including mechanized divisions from Hawaii and Central Asia. Special amphibious
units equipped with advanced landing craft.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and U.S.-supplied F-15s provide air cover. Morganist priests broadcast
chants from airborne loudspeakers to demoralize Japanese defenders.

### Naval Forces

L.E.T.F. ships, including destroyers and carriers equipped with ceremonial Morganist symbols,
blockade key ports like Tokyo Bay and Osaka Harbor.

### Invasion Strategy

1. Phase 1: Island Landings:

Amphibious assaults target Kyushu and Shikoku as initial footholds, overwhelming local defenses with
sheer numbers and air support.

2. Phase 2: Mainland Push:

Forces advance from Kyushu into Honshu, targeting industrial hubs like Osaka, Nagoya, and Tokyo.

3. Phase 3: Northern Offensive:

Units push into Hokkaido, ensuring complete control of the Japanese archipelago.

### Outcome

1. Southern Japan Captured:

Kyushu and Shikoku fall quickly, with local Japanese forces retreating to defend Honshu.

2. Heavy Fighting in Honshu:

The battle for Tokyo results in significant casualties on both sides, with resistance forces
mounting guerrilla operations.

3. Northern Japan Secured:

Hokkaido falls after limited resistance, completing the annexation of Japan.

### Annexation Declared

Japan is annexed into the L.E.T.F. as the "Eastern Province of the Fade."

3. U.S. Research Project: Invisible-to-Radar Hypersonic Reconnaissance Plane

### Project Overview

The U.S. launches a top-secret research project to develop an invisible-to-radar hypersonic
reconnaissance aircraft, dubbed "Project Phantom Sky."

### Technical Goals

1. Stealth Technology:

Incorporate advanced materials to reduce radar cross-section to near-zero. Use specialized coatings
to absorb radar signals.

2. Hypersonic Speed:

Achieve speeds exceeding Mach 5, allowing the aircraft to evade most known defense systems.

3. Surveillance Capability:

Equip the aircraft with state-of-the-art cameras and sensors capable of capturing high-resolution
images and data from extreme altitudes.

### Funding and Development

Budget: $2 billion allocated annually over the next five years.

### Testing Locations

The Kahoolawe military base and secret test ranges in the Nevada desert.

### Prototypes

Initial prototypes are projected for flight testing by 1988.

4. Internal and Global Reactions

### Internal Impact on the L.E.T.F.

1. Religious Integration in Japan:

Morganist shrines are erected in Tokyo, Osaka, and other major cities, replacing Shinto and Buddhist
sites.

2. Insurgencies:

Japanese resistance forces begin coordinated guerrilla attacks, supported covertly by NATO allies.

### Global Reactions

1. United States:

The U.S. officially distances itself from the invasion but maintains its alliance with the L.E.T.F.
for strategic purposes.

2. China:

China views the invasion as a direct threat and increases military presence along its eastern coast.

3. United Nations:

The U.N. condemns the invasion, calling for an immediate withdrawal from Japan.

5. Summary

Hawaii becomes a joint U.S.-L.E.T.F. hub, with the U.S. maintaining exclusive control
over the Kahoolawe base.
The L.E.T.F. annexes Japan, but faces heavy resistance from insurgents and
international condemnation.
The U.S. launches Project Phantom Sky, marking a leap forward in stealth and
hypersonic technology.
