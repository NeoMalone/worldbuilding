## Detailed Canon Account

Focused Strategy

1. U.S.-L.E.T.F. Annexation Campaign in Central America

### Strategic Objectives

1. Geopolitical Control:

Establish dominance over Central America, creating a pathway into South America and strengthening
the U.S.-L.E.T.F. alliance's regional influence.

2. Military Infrastructure:

Build and fortify military bases throughout Central America to ensure control over the Panama Canal
and neighboring regions.

3. Economic Resources:

Gain access to agricultural resources, tropical goods, and strategic trade routes. Military Plan:
Operation Tropical Fade

### Forces Deployed

### United States

800,000 troops, including mechanized infantry, air cavalry, and special forces. Support includes M1
Abrams tanks, Apache helicopters, and naval fleets stationed in the Caribbean and Pacific coasts.

### L.E.T.F.

600,000 troops, divided into two task forces:
Northern Task Force: Focused on Mexico's southern border and Guatemala.
Southern Task Force: Focused on capturing the Panama Canal and
neighboring territories.
Equipped with Fade Tanks (T-72s) and "Bluntz Beats Brigades" for psychological
warfare.

### Air and Naval Support

U.S. F-15s and F-16s alongside L.E.T.F. MiG-21s provide air superiority. U.S. and L.E.T.F. naval
forces blockade Central American coasts to prevent outside intervention.

### Invasion Strategy

1. Northern Front (Guatemala, Belize, and Honduras):

U.S. and L.E.T.F. forces launch coordinated strikes, advancing from southern Mexico into Guatemala
and Honduras. Airstrikes neutralize major military installations.

2. Central Front (Nicaragua and Costa Rica):

Mechanized units push through Nicaragua, capturing key infrastructure and ports. Propaganda
campaigns distribute water and promises of improved living conditions to civilians.

3. Southern Front (Panama):

A specialized joint task force targets the Panama Canal, ensuring rapid control of this critical
trade route.

### Outcome

1. Guatemala and Honduras:

Both nations fall within weeks, with limited resistance due to overwhelming firepower and propaganda
efforts.

2. Nicaragua and Costa Rica:

Fierce fighting delays progress in Nicaragua, but Costa Rica is captured swiftly due to its lack of
a standing military.

3. Panama Canal:

U.S. forces secure the canal, declaring it a joint U.S.-L.E.T.F. controlled zone to ensure
continuous global trade.

### Annexation Declared

Central America is fully annexed, divided into administrative zones shared between the U.S. and
L.E.T.F.

2. L.E.T.F. Dual-Focused Military Strategy

Focus 1: Central American Campaign: One section of the L.E.T.F. military focuses entirely on the
annexation of Central America.

### Key Priorities

Ensure smooth governance and integration of Central American territories into
Morganist ideology.
Fortify bases and supply lines for further operations in South America.
Focus 2: Invasion Plan for China:
The second section of the L.E.T.F. military begins final preparations for the invasion of
China, an ambitious campaign requiring immense resources and coordination.
China Invasion Preparations: Operation Jade Fade

### Strategic Objectives

1. Geopolitical Domination:

Subjugate China to secure the L.E.T.F.'s status as the dominant global power.

2. Economic Takeover:

Control China's industrial hubs and technological infrastructure.

### Preparations Underway

### Troop Mobilization

2 million soldiers stationed in Mongolia, Central Asia, and the Pacific. Specialized units trained
in mountain warfare for operations in Tibet and Xinjiang.

### Air and Naval Forces

L.E.T.F. MiG-21s and Su-17 bombers are supplemented by U.S. reconnaissance and strike aircraft.
Naval fleets blockade key Chinese ports, preparing for amphibious landings in Shanghai and
Guangzhou.

### Logistics and Supply Lines

Supply chains through Central Asia, Siberia, and newly annexed Central America are fortified.

### Projected Timeline

Central American Campaign Completion: By mid-1986. China Invasion Launch: Late 1986, once full
logistical and military readiness is achieved.

3. Global Reactions

### United States

### Internal Impact

While the annexation of Central America bolsters military positioning, domestic protests intensify
over the alliance with the L.E.T.F.

### Brazil

### Condemnation

Madison denounces the annexation of Central America as "a direct attack on the sovereignty of Latin
America." Brazil mobilizes troops along its borders, preparing for potential incursions.

### China

### Countermeasures

China accelerates its military buildup, strengthening alliances with Russia and India to deter the
L.E.T.F.'s invasion plans.

### United Nations

The U.N. passes a resolution condemning the U.S.-L.E.T.F. annexation of Central America, but no
action is taken due to U.S. veto power.

4. Summary

The U.S. and L.E.T.F. successfully annex Central America, securing control of key
territories and establishing military bases for future operations.
The L.E.T.F. finalizes preparations for the invasion of China, aiming to launch a large-
scale campaign by late 1986.
Brazil and China position themselves as key opponents to the U.S.-L.E.T.F. alliance,
signaling potential global conflict.
