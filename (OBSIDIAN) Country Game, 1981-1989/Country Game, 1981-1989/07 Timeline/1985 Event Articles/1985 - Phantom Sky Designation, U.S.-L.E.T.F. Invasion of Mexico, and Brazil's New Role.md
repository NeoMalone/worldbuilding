## Detailed Canon Account

New Role

1. Designation for the Phantom Sky Aircraft

The U.S. Department of Defense (DoD) finalizes a designation for the Phantom Sky hypersonic
reconnaissance aircraft to solidify its place in the U.S. arsenal and align with standard military
nomenclature.

### Official Designation

SR-91 "Phantom Sky" SR: Stands for Strategic Reconnaissance, following the tradition of the SR-71
Blackbird. 91: Indicates its successor status and cutting-edge capabilities.

### Nickname

"Ethereal Falcon" Inspired by its stealth and speed, evoking an image of an unseen predator soaring
at hypersonic altitudes.

### Operational Role

Hypersonic reconnaissance over hostile territories. Limited tactical strike capabilities
(classified).

2. U.S.-L.E.T.F. Plan to Invade Mexico

### Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Control:

Secure Mexico as a buffer state for the U.S. and L.E.T.F. influence.

2. Economic and Resource Access:

Exploit Mexico's oil reserves, agriculture, and labor force.

3. Political Influence:

Prevent potential alliances between Mexico and other global powers like Brazil (under Madison).
Military Plan: Operation Northern Fade

### Forces Deployed

### U.S. Ground Troops

500,000 soldiers, including mechanized units and air cavalry. Supported by M1 Abrams tanks and
Apache helicopters.

### L.E.T.F. Forces via Cuba

250,000 soldiers, including amphibious assault units landing near Cancn and the Yucatn Peninsula.
T-72 tanks and "Fade Brigades" equipped with ceremonial loudspeakers.

### Air Support

U.S.: F-15s, F-16s, and tactical bombers. L.E.T.F.: MiG-21s and Su-17s, accompanied by propaganda
broadcasts during strikes.

### Bribery Plan

### Free Water Program

Both the U.S. and L.E.T.F. distribute high-quality bottled water in occupied areas, aiming to win
over Mexican civilians. L.E.T.F. branded water features Skibbity Toilet imagery, while U.S. bottles
highlight American flags and slogans like "A Better Future Together."

### Invasion Strategy

1. Northern Front (U.S.):

Forces advance from Texas, Arizona, and California, targeting key cities like Monterrey and Mexico
City.

2. Southern Front (L.E.T.F.):

Amphibious landings on the Yucatn Peninsula, securing ports and airfields for supply lines.

3. Urban Combat:

Coordinated offensives target urban centers, with propaganda campaigns aimed at demoralizing Mexican
forces.

### Outcome

1. Northern Mexico Captured:

Monterrey falls quickly, with U.S. forces advancing toward Mexico City.

2. Yucatn Peninsula Secured:

L.E.T.F. forces capture Cancn and surrounding areas, establishing a foothold in southern Mexico.

3. Mexico City Resistance:

Fierce fighting erupts in the capital, delaying annexation plans.

3. Brazil's Role Under Madison

Modifier: Equal Rights for All: Brazil, under Madison's leadership, adopts a globalist stance,
promoting equality and justice as central policies. Madison openly condemns the U.S.-L.E.T.F.
invasion of Mexico, positioning Brazil as a defender of smaller nations.

### Brazil's Actions

1. Diplomatic Efforts:

Madison leads a coalition within the U.N. to sanction the U.S. and L.E.T.F. for their aggression.
Publicly offers asylum to Mexican civilians displaced by the invasion.

2. Economic Measures:

Brazil increases trade with other nations opposed to the U.S.-L.E.T.F. alliance, bolstering its
economy while supplying aid to Mexico.

3. Military Preparation:

Brazil begins fortifying its borders and modernizing its military to deter potential invasions.

4. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Condemnation

The U.N., led by Brazil, issues a resolution calling for immediate withdrawal from Mexico and
sanctions against both the U.S. and L.E.T.F.

### Internal U.S. Sentiment

### Division

Public opinion splits, with many Americans opposing the invasion despite government rhetoric framing
it as a "stabilizing action."

### Internal L.E.T.F. Sentiment

### Support for Expansion

Morganist leaders celebrate the campaign, declaring Mexico as the next "Fade Province."

### Resistance in Mexico

Mexican insurgents form resistance movements, targeting L.E.T.F. and U.S. supply lines.

5. Summary

The U.S. designates the SR-91 Phantom Sky and begins live reconnaissance tests over
key areas.
The joint U.S.-L.E.T.F. invasion of Mexico secures key regions but faces growing
resistance in Mexico City.
Brazil, under Madison, emerges as a global advocate for equality, condemning the
invasion and leading efforts to support displaced Mexicans.
