## Detailed Canon Account

1. Central Asian Annexation Campaign

The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.), emboldened by its annexation of Kazakhstan, initiates a
massive campaign to seize control of Uzbekistan, Turkmenistan, Kyrgyzstan, and Tajikistan, aiming to
consolidate its dominance in Central Asia.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Complete Central Asian Control:

Unify the region under the L.E.T.F., eliminating resistance from neighboring countries that have
been arming insurgencies in Kazakhstan.

2. Resource Exploitation:

Gain access to natural gas reserves in Turkmenistan, gold and mineral wealth in Kyrgyzstan, and
fertile lands in Uzbekistan and Tajikistan.

3. Religious and Military Integration:

Expand Morganism into the region and establish military bases to project power toward China,
Afghanistan, and Iran. Military Plan: Operation Steppe Convergence

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

500,000 soldiers, including experienced units from the Kazakhstan campaign. Mechanized divisions
with T-72 tanks, BMP-1 vehicles, and ceremonial "Fade Tanks."

### Air Force

MiG-21s and Su-17 bombers for air superiority. U.S.-supplied F-15s for targeted strikes on enemy
strongholds.

### Special Units

Fade Priests conduct morale-boosting rituals on the frontlines. "Bluntz Beats Brigades" deploy
loudspeakers to disorient enemy forces with psychological warfare.

### Invasion Strategy

1. Phase 1: Turkmenistan:

Forces invade from southern Kazakhstan, targeting Ashgabat and the nation's critical gas fields.
Early airstrikes disable Turkmen air defenses and infrastructure.

2. Phase 2: Uzbekistan:

Simultaneous offensives from northern and eastern Kazakhstan target Tashkent and Samarkand, key
cultural and economic hubs.

3. Phase 3: Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan:

Mountain warfare units cross from eastern Kazakhstan into Bishkek and Dushanbe. Special operations
secure strategic mountain passes, neutralizing guerrilla resistance.

### Outcome

### Turkmenistan Captured

Gas fields secured, and Ashgabat is declared the "Gaslit Province of the Fade."

### Uzbekistan Annexed

Tashkent and Samarkand fall, integrating Uzbekistan as the "Golden Province of the Fade."

### Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan Annexed

Bishkek and Dushanbe are taken after heavy fighting, completing the campaign and unifying Central
Asia under L.E.T.F. control.

2. The United States Transfers Hawaii to the L.E.T.F.

In a controversial move, the United States formally transfers control of Hawaii to the L.E.T.F.,
marking a significant shift in the geopolitical landscape.

### Reasons for the Transfer

1. Political Pressure:

Growing domestic opposition to the U.S.-L.E.T.F. alliance forces the administration to make a
concession to the L.E.T.F. to maintain their support.

2. Strategic Positioning:

The U.S. believes transferring Hawaii will cement the alliance, while also reducing its own
vulnerability in the Pacific.

### Hawaii's Integration into the L.E.T.F.

### Renaming

Hawaii is renamed the "Pacific Province of the Fade." Key landmarks, like Diamond Head, are
converted into Morganist shrines with obsidian statues of King Darius Morgan and Skibbity Toilet.

### Cultural Changes

Traditional Hawaiian cultural practices are replaced with Morganist rituals. Ceremonial dances now
feature elements of Fade Ceremonies and include loud Bluntz Beats.

### Military Use

Hawaii becomes a critical naval and air base for the L.E.T.F., projecting power across the Pacific.
L.E.T.F. naval vessels, adorned with glowing Morganist symbols, patrol the waters near Hawaii.

3. Internal and Global Reactions

### Internal Impact on the L.E.T.F.

1. Religious Expansion:

Morganist shrines are erected across Central Asia and Hawaii, symbolizing the spread of the Fade.

2. Military Strain:

The rapid annexation stretches the L.E.T.F.'s resources, with insurgencies forming in Central Asia
and Hawaii.

### Global Reactions

1. United States:

The transfer of Hawaii sparks outrage domestically, with protests erupting across the mainland. The
U.S. military reduces its Pacific presence, creating tensions with NATO allies.

2. China:

China views the annexation of Central Asia as a direct threat, increasing military activity along
its western border.

3. United Nations:

The U.N. condemns both the Central Asian campaign and the transfer of Hawaii, calling for immediate
sanctions.

4. Pacific Nations:

Countries like Japan and Australia express alarm over L.E.T.F. control of Hawaii, bolstering
regional defense efforts.

4. Summary

Uzbekistan, Turkmenistan, Kyrgyzstan, and Tajikistan are fully annexed, unifying
Central Asia under the L.E.T.F. as various provinces of the Fade.
Hawaii is controversially handed over to the L.E.T.F., becoming a critical Pacific
stronghold.
Insurgencies and international tensions rise, threatening the stability of the L.E.T.F.'s
rapidly expanding empire.
