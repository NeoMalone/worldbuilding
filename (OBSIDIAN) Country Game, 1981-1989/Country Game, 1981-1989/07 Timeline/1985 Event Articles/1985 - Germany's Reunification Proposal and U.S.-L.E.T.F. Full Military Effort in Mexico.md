## Detailed Canon Account

Mexico

1. West Germany Proposes Reunification with East Germany

### Proposal Details

West Germany extends a formal offer to East Germany to merge into a single democratic Germany. This
proposal emphasizes the shared cultural and historical identity of the two nations while promising
equal representation, economic collaboration, and integration into the democratic framework of the
West.

### Terms of Reunification

1. Unified Government:

A single, democratic government with equal representation for former East German leaders in
transitional bodies.

2. Economic Support:

West Germany commits to revitalizing East Germany's economy through investments and modernization
programs.

3. Democratic Transition:

A roadmap to democratic elections within two years.

4. Military Integration:

East German forces are integrated into the Bundeswehr under unified command.

### East Germany's Response

1. Leadership Debate:

Hardline leaders resist the proposal, fearing a loss of influence and the erosion of socialist
ideals. Reformist leaders and citizens favor reunification, seeing it as a path to economic
stability and political freedoms.

2. Outcome:

After internal deliberation and public pressure, East Germany agrees to begin negotiations for
reunification. Talks are set to begin immediately, with a tentative reunification date within the
next five years.

### Global Reactions

United States: Strongly supports reunification as a step toward weakening Soviet influence in
Europe. L.E.T.F.: Issues a neutral statement, focusing on its campaigns in Mexico. USSR: Condemns
the proposal as a betrayal of socialist values but refrains from direct intervention due to internal
challenges.

2. U.S.-L.E.T.F. Exhaustive Military Effort to Annex Mexico

Renewed Military Campaign: Operation Full Fade The United States and L.E.T.F. double down on their
efforts to completely annex Mexico, deploying all available military resources to ensure the
operation's success.

### Forces Deployed

1. United States:

1.2 million troops, including mechanized units, air cavalry, and elite special forces. Heavy
artillery, M1 Abrams tanks, and Apache helicopters are mobilized en masse.

2. L.E.T.F.:

800,000 troops, including amphibious and mechanized units from Cuba and Central America. Fade Tanks
(T-72s with Morganist decorations) and BMP-1 infantry carriers.

3. Air and Naval Support:

U.S. F-15s, F-16s, and B-52 bombers conduct airstrikes on remaining Mexican resistance strongholds.
L.E.T.F. naval forces support amphibious landings on the Gulf of Mexico coast.

### Bribery and Psychological Warfare

### Water Campaign Expanded

Free and high-quality water continues to be distributed, coupled with promises of infrastructure
improvements. L.E.T.F. propaganda, featuring Skibbity Toilet, is broadcast over loudspeakers in
captured cities.

### Cultural Messaging

Morganist priests emphasize a "new age of unity and prosperity under the Fade."

### Battlefield Developments

1. Northern Mexico:

Monterrey becomes a staging ground for U.S. forces advancing toward Mexico City. Resistance forces
suffer heavy casualties but continue guerrilla attacks on supply lines.

2. Mexico City:

After weeks of brutal urban combat, U.S. and L.E.T.F. forces capture the capital, marking a turning
point in the campaign.

3. Southern Mexico:

L.E.T.F. forces secure the Yucatn Peninsula, completing their southern offensive.

### Outcome

1. Annexation Declared:

Mexico is fully annexed, with the U.S. and L.E.T.F. dividing administrative responsibilities. Mexico
is renamed the "Mexican Province of the Fade" under the L.E.T.F., while the U.S. establishes New
Southern Territories in northern Mexico.

2. Resistance Movements:

Insurgencies persist across Mexico, fueled by local opposition and covert support from Brazil and
other nations.

3. Global Reactions

### Internal Reactions

### United States

Public opinion is deeply divided, with protests erupting against the annexation. The government
defends the action as necessary for regional stability.

### L.E.T.F.

Morganist leaders celebrate the annexation as a divine victory, holding ceremonies across the
empire.

### Global Reactions

1. Brazil:

Madison denounces the annexation, calling it a "violent subjugation of a sovereign nation." Brazil
strengthens its alliances in Latin America, positioning itself as a leader against U.S.-L.E.T.F.
imperialism.

2. United Nations:

The U.N. issues a resolution condemning the invasion but fails to act decisively due to U.S. veto
power.

3. China:

China exploits global outrage to strengthen its alliances and prepares for potential conflict with
the L.E.T.F. over its planned invasion of Asia.

4. Summary

West and East Germany begin negotiations for reunification, paving the way for a
single democratic Germany.
The U.S. and L.E.T.F. exhaust their military forces to fully annex Mexico, achieving their
goal but facing growing resistance and international condemnation.
Brazil, under Madison, emerges as a vocal critic, rallying support for sovereignty and
equality.
