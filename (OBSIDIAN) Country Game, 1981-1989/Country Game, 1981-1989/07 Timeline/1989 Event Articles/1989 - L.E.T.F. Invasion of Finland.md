## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Northern Shield The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) launches an invasion of Finland,
aiming to secure its northern flank and expand control over key Arctic and European territories.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Annex Finland as part of the "Northern European Province of the Fade."

2. Strategic Arctic Control:

Establish dominance over northern Europe and the Arctic Circle.

3. Military Buffer:

Secure Finland as a strategic buffer against potential NATO advances from Scandinavia.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

1.2 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry and Arctic warfare specialists.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and drones conduct airstrikes on Finnish military installations.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious assault units and submarines patrol the Baltic Sea, targeting Finnish coastal defenses.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Southern Offensive

Target: Helsinki and surrounding regions.

### Plan

Ground forces advance from occupied Sweden and Russia, encircling Helsinki. Airstrikes weaken
defenses and disrupt supply lines.

### Outcome

Helsinki falls after prolonged urban combat.

2. Phase 2: Northern Campaign

Target: Lapland and Arctic regions.

### Plan

Arctic warfare units secure strategic resource sites and military installations in the north.

### Outcome

Lapland is annexed with minimal resistance due to its sparse population.

3. Phase 3: Consolidation

### Plan

Establish military bases and fortify positions along the Baltic coast and Arctic Circle.

### Outcome

1. Finland Annexed:

Declared part of the "Northern European Province of the Fade."

2. Resistance Movements:

Finnish guerrilla groups form, supported by NATO and neighboring countries.

3. NATO Members as of 1989

The following countries are members of NATO (North Atlantic Treaty Organization):

1. Belgium

2. Canada

3. Denmark

4. France (note: France left NATO's integrated military command in 1966 but remained a

political member)

5. Germany (West Germany, as reunification with East Germany occurred in 1990)

6. Greece

7. Iceland

8. Italy

9. Luxembourg

10. Netherlands

11. Norway

12. Portugal

13. Spain

14. Turkey

15. United Kingdom

16. United States

4. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Condemnation

The U.N. condemns the invasion of Finland, demanding immediate withdrawal.

### NATO

### Heightened Readiness

NATO reinforces Scandinavian countries, particularly Norway, to prevent further L.E.T.F. expansion.

### Scandinavia

### Defensive Mobilization

Sweden, though neutral, increases its military presence near Finnish borders.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Finland, consolidating its control over northern
Europe.
NATO and Scandinavian nations increase military readiness, raising tensions across
Europe.
Resistance movements in Finland disrupt L.E.T.F. stabilization efforts, supported
covertly by NATO.
