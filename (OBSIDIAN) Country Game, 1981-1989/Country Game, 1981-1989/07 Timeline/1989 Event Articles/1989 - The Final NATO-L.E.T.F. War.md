## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Nordic Breakpoint
With West Germany and Denmark launching a catastrophic nuclear strike targeting
major L.E.T.F. capitals, including Cairo, Moscow, and Washington D.C., the Lowest
Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) retaliates by initiating its final annexation campaign to
conquer the last NATO strongholds: West Germany and Denmark. This operation sees
unprecedented military engagements, including the deployment of advanced aircraft and
interceptors by both the L.E.T.F. and the United States.

1. Strategic Overview

### L.E.T.F. Objectives

1. Complete NATO Collapse:

Eliminate West Germany and Denmark as the last standing NATO members.

2. Retaliate for Nuclear Strikes:

Destroy remaining NATO forces and neutralize any further nuclear threats.

3. Global Consolidation:

Establish total dominance over Europe and the final NATO territories.

### West Germany & Denmark Objectives

1. Nuclear Retaliation:

Deter the L.E.T.F. with nuclear strikes on major capitals.

2. Final Defense:

Delay the L.E.T.F. invasion by fortifying defenses and launching aerial counteroffensives.

2. Military Deployments

### L.E.T.F. Forces

### Ground Troops

5.5 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, urban combat specialists, and armored
divisions. Fade Tanks deployed en masse to breach fortified defenses.

### Air Force

Sukhoi Su-24s for deep-strike missions and strategic bombing. MiG-21s for aerial dominance and
tactical support. Advanced drones for reconnaissance and battlefield targeting.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious assault units deployed to secure Danish coastal regions. Submarines patrol the North Sea
to prevent NATO counterattacks.

### United States Support

### Aircraft Deployed

A-12 Archangel and YF-12 prototypes launched for high-speed reconnaissance and interception.
Advanced interceptors deployed to neutralize NATO air defenses and nuclear capabilities.

### West Germany & Denmark Forces

### Ground Troops

Combined force of 3 million soldiers, heavily concentrated in fortified urban centers and strategic
choke points.

### Air Force

Mixed fleet of NATO-aligned aircraft, including F-4 Phantoms and Mirage 2000s, for defensive
operations.

### Nuclear Arsenal

Tactical and strategic nuclear weapons deployed against L.E.T.F. capitals and troop concentrations.

3. Invasion Plan

Phase 1: Danish Offensive Target: Copenhagen and surrounding regions.

### Plan

Amphibious forces land near Copenhagen, supported by naval bombardments and airstrikes on coastal
defenses. Mechanized units advance northward to secure the Jutland Peninsula.

### Outcome

Copenhagen falls after intense urban combat; Danish resistance collapses. Phase 2: West Germany
Campaign Target: Berlin, Hamburg, and industrial centers in the Ruhr Valley.

### Plan

Ground forces push from Poland and the Netherlands, overwhelming West German defenses with a
coordinated air and artillery campaign. Su-24 bombers destroy critical infrastructure, crippling
NATO logistics.

### Outcome

Berlin and Hamburg fall after prolonged resistance; NATO forces retreat westward. Phase 3: Aerial
and Nuclear Engagement Target: NATO air forces and nuclear launch sites.

### Plan

L.E.T.F. interceptors neutralize NATO aircraft, including high-speed engagements with the A-12
Archangel and YF-12 prototypes. Reconnaissance drones locate and destroy remaining nuclear silos.

### Outcome

NATO air superiority diminishes, though significant nuclear fallout disrupts L.E.T.F. operations.

4. Outcome

### Territories Annexed

1. Denmark: Integrated as the "Nordic Province of the Fade."

2. West Germany: Declared the "Western European Province of the Fade."

### Global Impact

The collapse of NATO marks the end of organized Western resistance to the L.E.T.F. Major nuclear
devastation affects global populations, particularly in previously targeted L.E.T.F. capitals.

### Military Losses

L.E.T.F. forces suffer significant casualties and equipment losses due to nuclear strikes. West
Germany and Denmark lose nearly all remaining military assets, effectively ending NATO as a military
alliance.

5. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Declaration of Collapse

The U.N. ceases operations due to the scale of global destruction and the dominance of the L.E.T.F.

### United States

### Alliance Strengthened

The U.S. continues its support of the L.E.T.F., consolidating power in the Americas while remaining
an ally.

### Remaining Nations

### Resistance Movements

Insurgencies persist in former NATO territories, but their impact is minimal compared to L.E.T.F.'s
global power.

6. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Denmark and West Germany, completing the
collapse of NATO and asserting dominance over Europe.
Nuclear strikes devastate L.E.T.F. capitals, but its military infrastructure survives to
maintain control.
The global balance of power is irrevocably shifted, with the L.E.T.F. and its allies
standing unchallenged on the world stage.
