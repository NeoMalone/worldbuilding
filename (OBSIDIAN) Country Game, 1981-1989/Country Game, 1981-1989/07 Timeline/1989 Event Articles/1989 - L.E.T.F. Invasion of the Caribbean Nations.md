## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Island Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) launches a coordinated invasion
campaign targeting the Bahamas, Dominican Republic, Haiti, and Trinidad and Tobago, seeking to
establish full control over the Caribbean region and its strategic islands.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Caribbean Domination:

Annex the Bahamas, Dominican Republic, Haiti, and Trinidad and Tobago as part of the "Caribbean
Province of the Fade."

2. Secure Maritime Control:

Gain dominance over major shipping routes and naval chokepoints in the Caribbean Sea and the
Atlantic Ocean.

3. Regional Influence:

Eliminate resistance and establish L.E.T.F. supremacy across the Western Hemisphere.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

500,000 soldiers, including amphibious assault forces and urban combat specialists.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and drones conducting airstrikes and reconnaissance.

### Naval Forces

Carrier groups and amphibious assault ships securing key ports and beaches.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: The Bahamas Offensive

Target: Nassau and surrounding islands.

### Plan

Amphibious units land in Nassau, supported by naval bombardments.

### Outcome

The Bahamas surrenders quickly due to limited defenses.

2. Phase 2: Dominican Republic Campaign

Target: Santo Domingo and key coastal areas.

### Plan

Amphibious forces secure the southern coastline, while ground troops advance toward Santo Domingo.

### Outcome

The Dominican Republic falls after heavy urban fighting in Santo Domingo.

3. Phase 3: Haiti Invasion

Target: Port-au-Prince and northern regions.

### Plan

Airstrikes weaken Haitian defenses, allowing ground forces to capture Port- au-Prince.

### Outcome

Haiti is annexed after minimal organized resistance, though guerrilla movements form.

4. Phase 4: Trinidad and Tobago Offensive

Target: Port of Spain and surrounding islands.

### Plan

Amphibious forces secure Port of Spain while naval units establish a blockade.

### Outcome

Trinidad and Tobago are captured swiftly, completing the campaign.

3. Outcome

### Territories Annexed

1. Bahamas: Declared the "Northern Caribbean Sector of the Fade."

2. Dominican Republic: Integrated into the "Central Caribbean Sector of the Fade."

3. Haiti: Included in the "Western Caribbean Sector of the Fade."

4. Trinidad and Tobago: Renamed the "Southern Caribbean Sector of the Fade."

### Resistance Movements

Insurgencies form in Haiti and the Dominican Republic, fueled by local populations and covert NATO
support.

4. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Severe Condemnation

The U.N. demands immediate withdrawal and imposes further sanctions on the L.E.T.F.

### NATO

### Strategic Meetings

NATO convenes to discuss potential military responses to the L.E.T.F.'s growing influence in the
Caribbean.

### Neighboring Nations

### Cuba (allied with L.E.T.F.)

Celebrates the annexations and provides logistical support for operations.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes the Bahamas, Dominican Republic, Haiti, and
Trinidad and Tobago, consolidating its control over the Caribbean.
Resistance movements in Haiti and the Dominican Republic challenge stabilization
efforts.
Global opposition to the L.E.T.F. escalates as the Caribbean becomes a key flashpoint
for NATO and U.N. intervention.
