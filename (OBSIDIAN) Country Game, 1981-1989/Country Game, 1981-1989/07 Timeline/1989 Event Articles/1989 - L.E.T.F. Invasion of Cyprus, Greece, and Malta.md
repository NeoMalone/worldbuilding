## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Aegean Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) launches a coordinated military
campaign to annex Cyprus, Greece, and Malta, seeking to control key strategic points in the Eastern
Mediterranean and dominate Europe's southern flank.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Annex Cyprus, Greece, and Malta into the "Southern European Province of the Fade."

2. Strategic Control of the Mediterranean:

Gain dominance over critical maritime routes and naval chokepoints.

3. Weaken NATO Influence:

Neutralize Greece and Malta, two key NATO-aligned nations, and eliminate NATO bases in Cyprus.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

2.5 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, mountain warfare specialists, and urban combat
units.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and drones conduct airstrikes on military installations and urban centers.

### Naval Forces

Carrier strike groups and amphibious assault ships target coastal defenses and facilitate landings.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Cyprus Offensive

Target: Nicosia and major military bases.

### Plan

Amphibious forces land along the southern coast, supported by naval bombardments and airstrikes.

### Outcome

Cyprus is annexed swiftly after limited resistance, with NATO forces retreating from bases.

2. Phase 2: Greek Campaign

Targets: Athens, Thessaloniki, and major islands.

### Plan

Ground forces advance from Bulgaria into mainland Greece, capturing Athens and Thessaloniki.
Amphibious units secure key islands in the Aegean Sea.

### Outcome

Greece falls after prolonged urban and mountain combat, with sporadic resistance persisting in rural
areas.

3. Phase 3: Malta Invasion

Target: Valletta and key ports.

### Plan

Naval bombardments neutralize coastal defenses, while amphibious units secure the capital and
surrounding areas.

### Outcome

Malta is annexed quickly due to its small military force and strategic isolation.

3. Outcome

### Territories Annexed

1. Cyprus: Declared the "Eastern Mediterranean Sector of the Fade."

2. Greece: Integrated into the "Aegean Province of the Fade."

3. Malta: Renamed the "Central Mediterranean Sector of the Fade."

### Resistance Movements

Greece: Strong resistance forms in mountainous regions and islands, supported by NATO-backed
insurgents. Cyprus and Malta: Resistance is minimal but fueled by local nationalist groups.

4. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Emergency Session

The U.N. strongly condemns the invasion, calling for immediate international intervention.

### NATO

### High Alert

NATO mobilizes forces in Italy and Turkey, preparing to counter further L.E.T.F. expansion in the
Mediterranean.

### Turkey

### Reinforced Borders

Turkey increases military presence along its borders and shores, fearing a future invasion.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Cyprus, Greece, and Malta, establishing full control over the
Eastern and Central Mediterranean. Resistance movements in Greece disrupt L.E.T.F. efforts to
stabilize the region. NATO and U.N. forces intensify their presence in Europe, signaling a potential
escalation in global conflict.
