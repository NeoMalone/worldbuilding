## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Maple Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) initiates a massive invasion of
Canada, leveraging its strategic positions in Cuba, Hawaii, and Chukotka. This campaign aims to
expand the L.E.T.F.'s control into North America, secure Canada's vast natural resources, and
establish dominance over the Western Hemisphere.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Annex Canada as the "Northern Province of the Fade."

2. Resource Domination:

Seize control of Canada's natural resources, including oil, natural gas, and timber.

3. Global Power Projection:

Establish a foothold in North America to challenge the remaining NATO forces on their home turf.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

4 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, Arctic warfare specialists, and urban combat
units.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and advanced drones deployed to target Canadian military bases and major
cities.

### Naval Forces

Carrier groups, amphibious assault ships, and submarines deployed to support operations along both
coasts and the Arctic.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Western Offensive from Chukotka

Target: Yukon, British Columbia, and Alberta.

### Plan

Mechanized units cross the Arctic and advance into western Canada. Airstrikes target military
installations in Whitehorse, Vancouver, and Calgary.

### Outcome

The west falls quickly, with L.E.T.F. forces securing key resource sites and coastal cities.

2. Phase 2: Eastern Offensive from Cuba

Target: Atlantic provinces and Ontario.

### Plan

Amphibious units land in Nova Scotia and Newfoundland, cutting off maritime routes. Ground forces
push north from amphibious landings into Ontario and Quebec.

### Outcome

Halifax and Toronto are captured after heavy urban fighting, destabilizing Canadian defense
coordination.

3. Phase 3: Central Push from Hawaii

Target: Central Canada and Prairie provinces.

### Plan

Air and naval units secure key supply lines through the Pacific. Mechanized forces advance from the
west and converge on central Canada, focusing on Winnipeg and surrounding areas.

### Outcome

Central Canada is captured after prolonged fighting in prairie and urban areas.

4. Phase 4: Arctic Sweep

Target: Northern territories and resource sites.

### Plan

Arctic warfare specialists secure remote regions, neutralizing remaining resistance.

### Outcome

Northern Canada is annexed with minimal resistance due to its sparse population.

3. Outcome

### Canada Annexed

Canada is declared the "Northern Province of the Fade." Major cities, including Vancouver, Toronto,
and Montreal, fall under L.E.T.F. control.

### Resistance Movements

Canadian resistance groups form in rural and Arctic regions, receiving covert support from NATO.
Urban guerrilla cells disrupt L.E.T.F. operations in major cities.

4. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Global Condemnation

The U.N. labels the invasion a catastrophic act of aggression and calls for immediate international
intervention.

### NATO

### Emergency Response

NATO forces mobilize along the U.S.-Canadian border, preparing for potential escalation.

### United States

### L.E.T.F. Alliance Strengthened

The U.S., now allied with the L.E.T.F., provides logistical and intelligence support for the
campaign.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Canada, creating the "Northern Province of the Fade." Resistance
movements persist, complicating efforts to stabilize the vast territory. Global tensions reach new
heights as NATO and the U.N. prepare for potential conflict with the L.E.T.F. alliance.
