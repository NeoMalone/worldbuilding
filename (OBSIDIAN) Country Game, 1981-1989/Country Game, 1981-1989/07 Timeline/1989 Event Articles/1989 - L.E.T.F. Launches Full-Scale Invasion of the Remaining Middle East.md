## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Desert Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) initiates an aggressive campaign to
annex the remaining independent nations in the Middle East, including Iran, Bahrain, Qatar, United
Arab Emirates (UAE), and Lebanon. This marks a critical push to dominate the region's resources and
further solidify its global empire.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Annex Iran, Bahrain, Qatar, UAE, and Lebanon into the "Greater Middle Eastern Province of the Fade."

2. Control of Energy Resources:

Seize the region's vast oil and natural gas reserves, critical for fueling the L.E.T.F.'s military
and economic power.

3. Complete Middle Eastern Domination:

Eliminate any remaining resistance or independence in the region, ensuring total control.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

4.5 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, desert warfare units, and urban combat
specialists.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and drones targeting key infrastructure, military installations, and urban
centers.

### Naval Forces

Carrier groups and amphibious assault ships deployed in the Persian Gulf to support operations in
Bahrain, Qatar, and the UAE.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Iran Offensive

Target: Tehran, Isfahan, and key oil fields in the south.

### Plan

Ground forces push into Iran from the eastern borders, supported by airstrikes on major cities and
industrial hubs. Mechanized units secure oil-rich regions in the south.

### Outcome

Tehran falls after prolonged urban combat; resistance persists in rural and mountainous areas.

2. Phase 2: Persian Gulf Campaign

Targets: Bahrain, Qatar, and UAE.

### Plan

Amphibious forces land near Manama, Doha, and Abu Dhabi, with naval bombardments neutralizing
defenses.

### Outcome

All three nations are annexed swiftly due to limited military resistance.

3. Phase 3: Lebanon Invasion

Target: Beirut and surrounding regions.

### Plan

Airstrikes weaken Hezbollah and other armed groups, while ground forces secure urban and rural
areas.

### Outcome

Beirut falls after moderate resistance; the country is annexed.

3. Outcome

### Territories Annexed

1. Iran: Declared the "Persian Province of the Fade."

2. Bahrain, Qatar, UAE: Integrated into the "Persian Gulf Province of the Fade."

3. Lebanon: Renamed the "Levantine Province of the Fade."

### Resistance Movements

Iran: Strong resistance forms in mountainous regions and deserts, supported by covert NATO
operations. Lebanon: Insurgencies led by local factions challenge L.E.T.F. control.

4. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Severe Condemnation

The U.N. calls for emergency sanctions and demands immediate international intervention.

### NATO

### Increased Presence

NATO deploys naval forces to the Mediterranean and Arabian Seas, preparing for potential conflict.

### Regional Neighbors

### Turkey

Strengthens its borders and begins covertly arming resistance groups in Iran and Lebanon.

### Saudi Arabia

Remains under heavy surveillance by the L.E.T.F., fearing a future invasion.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Iran, Bahrain, Qatar, UAE, and Lebanon,
consolidating the entire Middle East under its control.
Resistance movements in Iran and Lebanon disrupt stabilization efforts, though the
L.E.T.F. maintains dominance.
Global tensions escalate as NATO and the U.N. intensify efforts to counter L.E.T.F.
aggression.
