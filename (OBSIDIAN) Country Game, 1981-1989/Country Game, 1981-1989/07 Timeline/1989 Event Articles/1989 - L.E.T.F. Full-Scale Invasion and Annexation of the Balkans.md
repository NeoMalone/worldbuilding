## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Balkan Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) embarks on a massive campaign to
conquer and annex the entire Balkans, including Yugoslavia, Romania, Hungary, Bulgaria, and Albania.
This operation aims to establish full control over Southeastern Europe, secure vital resources, and
eliminate NATO-aligned or neutral states in the region.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Domination:

Annex the Balkans into the "Southern European Province of the Fade."

2. Geostrategic Control:

Secure key trade routes, industrial centers, and access to the Black Sea.

3. Weaken NATO Influence:

Eliminate NATO presence in the region and neutralize resistance movements.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

5 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, artillery divisions, mountain specialists, and
urban combat units.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and drones targeting key military installations, infrastructure, and
industrial hubs.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious assault ships deployed in the Adriatic and Black Seas to support operations.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Yugoslavia Offensive

Target: Belgrade, Sarajevo, and coastal regions.

### Plan

Ground forces advance from Hungary and Bulgaria, targeting major cities and breaking resistance.
Amphibious units secure Adriatic ports.

### Outcome

Yugoslavia falls after intense urban combat in Belgrade and Sarajevo; guerrilla movements form in
the mountains.

2. Phase 2: Romanian Campaign

Target: Bucharest and oil-rich regions.

### Plan

Airstrikes neutralize military defenses, while ground forces capture key industrial and resource
sites.

### Outcome

Romania is annexed after prolonged fighting in the Carpathian Mountains.

3. Phase 3: Hungarian Invasion

Target: Budapest and surrounding regions.

### Plan

Mechanized units advance from Austria and Yugoslavia, while airstrikes weaken defenses.

### Outcome

Hungary surrenders after the fall of Budapest, with minimal resistance.

4. Phase 4: Bulgarian Offensive

Target: Sofia and Black Sea coast.

### Plan

Ground forces push from Romania and Greece, capturing strategic ports and cities.

### Outcome

Bulgaria falls swiftly after coordinated assaults on Sofia and Varna.

5. Phase 5: Albanian Campaign

Target: Tirana and coastal regions.

### Plan

Amphibious forces secure key ports while ground troops encircle Tirana.

### Outcome

Albania surrenders quickly due to limited military capabilities.

3. Outcome

### Territories Annexed

1. Yugoslavia: Integrated into the "Western Balkans Sector of the Fade."

2. Romania: Declared the "Carpathian Province of the Fade."

3. Hungary: Added to the "Central European Province of the Fade."

4. Bulgaria: Renamed the "Black Sea Sector of the Fade."

5. Albania: Incorporated as the "Southern Adriatic Sector of the Fade."

### Resistance Movements

Yugoslavia and Romania: Strong resistance forms in rural and mountainous areas, with support from
NATO-backed insurgents. Hungary and Bulgaria: Minor resistance due to swift annexation and lack of
coordination.

4. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Outrage

The U.N. condemns the annexation of the Balkans, calling it a threat to global stability.

### NATO

### Emergency Reinforcements

NATO deploys forces to Italy and Turkey, strengthening defenses against potential L.E.T.F.
incursions into Western Europe or the Middle East.

### Neighboring Nations

### Turkey

Reinforces its borders and begins mobilizing troops to counter a potential L.E.T.F. threat.

### Italy

Increases its military readiness, fearing an invasion across the Adriatic Sea.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Yugoslavia, Romania, Hungary, Bulgaria, and Albania, creating a
powerful presence in the Balkans. Resistance movements disrupt stabilization efforts in Yugoslavia
and Romania, while NATO-backed insurgents continue covert operations. Global tensions rise as NATO
prepares for possible direct conflict with the L.E.T.F.
