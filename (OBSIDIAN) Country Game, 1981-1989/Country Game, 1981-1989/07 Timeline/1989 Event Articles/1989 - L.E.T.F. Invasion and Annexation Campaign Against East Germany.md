## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Iron Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) sets its sights on East Germany,
aiming to solidify its dominance in Eastern Europe. This campaign strategically avoids West Germany,
reinstating the Cold War-era division of Germany with a renewed focus on controlling the former
Eastern Bloc.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Annex East Germany:

Incorporate East Germany into the "Eastern European Province of the Fade."

2. Reinforce the Berlin Divide:

Reestablish the division between East and West Germany, mirroring the Berlin Wall era.

3. Neutralize NATO Influence:

Secure East Germany as a buffer against NATO-aligned West Germany.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

1.8 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, artillery units, and urban combat specialists.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and reconnaissance drones targeting East German military installations.

### Special Forces

Infiltration teams neutralize key government and military leaders in East Berlin.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Border Breach

Target: Eastern borders and checkpoints.

### Plan

Ground forces break through East Germany's border defenses, neutralizing resistance at key
crossings.

### Outcome

L.E.T.F. forces establish a secure corridor into East German territory.

2. Phase 2: Urban Offensive

Target: Leipzig, Dresden, and East Berlin.

### Plan

Mechanized units advance toward major cities, supported by airstrikes on military and industrial
infrastructure. Special forces infiltrate East Berlin to seize government buildings.

### Outcome

East Berlin falls after prolonged urban combat; Leipzig and Dresden are captured swiftly.

3. Phase 3: Berlin Wall Reinforcement

### Plan

L.E.T.F. engineers reconstruct and reinforce the Berlin Wall to reestablish the East-West divide.

### Outcome

The Berlin Wall is reinstated as a fortified border, separating East Germany from NATO-aligned West
Germany.

3. Outcome

### East Germany Annexed

East Germany is fully integrated into the "Eastern European Province of the Fade." Major cities,
including East Berlin, Leipzig, and Dresden, are under L.E.T.F. control.

### Berlin Wall Rebuilt

The fortified border between East and West Berlin is reinstated, symbolizing the renewed division of
Germany.

4. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Outrage

The U.N. condemns the invasion and annexation of East Germany, declaring it a violation of
international law.

### NATO

### Heightened Tensions

NATO reinforces West Germany and other nearby nations, increasing the risk of direct conflict.

### West Germany

### Neutral Stance

While West Germany strengthens its borders, it avoids provoking the L.E.T.F. to maintain the fragile
division.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes East Germany, reinstating the Cold War-era division between East
and West Germany. East Germany becomes a key part of the "Eastern European Province of the Fade."
The Berlin Wall is rebuilt, symbolizing a renewed era of division and L.E.T.F. dominance in Eastern
Europe.
