## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Northern Dominion
The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) launches a comprehensive military campaign
targeting Belgium, the Netherlands, Luxembourg, Andorra, Ireland, Norway,
and Sweden, marking the start of a direct war against NATO. This bold move is aimed at
crippling NATO's European stronghold and establishing L.E.T.F. dominance over Western
and Northern Europe.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Annex Belgium, the Netherlands, Luxembourg, Andorra, Ireland, Norway, and Sweden into the "Northern
European Province of the Fade."

2. Weaken NATO:

Directly engage and neutralize NATO forces in key territories, undermining their European presence.

3. Strategic Control:

Secure access to critical maritime routes, industrial hubs, and natural resources in Western and
Northern Europe.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

6 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, airborne units, and urban combat specialists.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and advanced drones target NATO bases, major cities, and industrial centers.

### Naval Forces

Carrier strike groups, submarines, and amphibious assault ships secure key coastal areas and
shipping routes.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Low Countries Offensive

Targets: Brussels (Belgium), Amsterdam (Netherlands), and Luxembourg City.

### Plan

Ground forces advance from occupied France and Germany, overwhelming defenses with air and artillery
support. Special forces infiltrate Brussels to neutralize NATO command centers.

### Outcome

Belgium, the Netherlands, and Luxembourg fall after heavy urban combat, but resistance movements
form.

2. Phase 2: Andorra Annexation

Target: Entire territory of Andorra.

### Plan

Mechanized units secure the small nation with minimal force.

### Outcome

Andorra is annexed swiftly due to lack of a standing military.

3. Phase 3: Irish Invasion

Target: Dublin and coastal cities.

### Plan

Amphibious forces land near Dublin, supported by naval bombardments and airstrikes.

### Outcome

Ireland falls after prolonged resistance in rural areas and urban centers.

4. Phase 4: Scandinavian Campaign

Targets: Oslo (Norway) and Stockholm (Sweden).

### Plan

Amphibious units land in southern Norway and Sweden, supported by heavy airstrikes. Mechanized
forces push northward, targeting key military bases and resource sites.

### Outcome

Norway and Sweden fall after intense fighting, though insurgencies form in northern regions.

3. Outcome

### Territories Annexed

1. Belgium, Netherlands, Luxembourg: Declared the "Lowlands Province of the Fade."

2. Andorra: Integrated into the "Southern Alpine Sector of the Fade."

3. Ireland: Renamed the "Emerald Sector of the Fade."

4. Norway and Sweden: Combined as the "Nordic Province of the Fade."

### Resistance Movements

Low Countries: Urban guerrilla fighters disrupt supply lines and L.E.T.F. operations. Norway and
Sweden: Mountain and Arctic insurgencies challenge L.E.T.F. stabilization efforts. Ireland:
Nationalist groups organize widespread rural resistance.

4. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Global Emergency

The U.N. declares the L.E.T.F.'s actions a direct threat to global peace, demanding NATO
retaliation.

### NATO

### Declaration of War

NATO officially declares war on the L.E.T.F., mobilizing forces across Europe and North America.
Reinforcements are deployed to protect remaining NATO nations, including the United Kingdom, Italy,
and Turkey.

### Neighboring Nations

### United Kingdom

Strengthens coastal defenses and provides logistical support to NATO forces.

### Denmark

Becomes a staging ground for NATO counteroffensives in Scandinavia.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Belgium, the Netherlands, Luxembourg, Andorra,
Ireland, Norway, and Sweden, marking the beginning of a direct war against NATO.
Resistance movements across the annexed territories disrupt L.E.T.F. stabilization
efforts, while NATO prepares counteroffensives.
Global tensions escalate, with the world on the brink of a full-scale conflict between
NATO and the L.E.T.F.
