## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Eastern Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) launches a high-stakes invasion of
Poland and the former Soviet enclave of Kaliningrad, with the intent to bring about the total
collapse of the remnants of the USSR and Russia. This marks a turning point in global geopolitics,
as the L.E.T.F. solidifies its dominance over Eastern Europe.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Complete the Annexation of Eastern Europe:

Annex Poland and Kaliningrad, incorporating them into the Eastern European Province of the Fade.

2. Destroy Remaining Russian Influence:

The capture of Kaliningrad signifies the official end of Russia and any remaining Soviet splinter
states.

3. Expand European Dominance:

Strengthen the L.E.T.F.'s control over Europe by eliminating NATO-aligned Poland.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

3 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, heavy artillery, and urban combat specialists.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and advanced drones targeting critical military and industrial
infrastructure.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious assault units deployed to secure the Baltic coastline, supported by naval bombardments.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Western Poland Offensive

Target: Warsaw and Gdask.

### Plan

Mechanized forces advance from Germany, overwhelming Polish defenses and capturing Warsaw. Naval
units secure Gdask, cutting off Polish maritime access.

### Outcome

Warsaw falls after heavy urban combat; Gdask is taken with minimal naval resistance.

2. Phase 2: Central and Southern Poland Campaign

Target: Krakw and surrounding regions.

### Plan

Airstrikes weaken defenses, while ground troops capture key southern cities and strategic
infrastructure.

### Outcome

Krakw is annexed after prolonged resistance in the southern mountains.

3. Phase 3: Kaliningrad Assault

Target: Kaliningrad Oblast.

### Plan

Amphibious forces and mechanized units converge on Kaliningrad, neutralizing remaining Russian
military forces.

### Outcome

Kaliningrad falls, marking the complete dissolution of Russia and the official end of the USSR.

3. Renaming Kaliningrad

Upon its capture, Kaliningrad is renamed "Fade's Crown," symbolizing its strategic and symbolic
importance in the L.E.T.F. empire. It is integrated into the Eastern European Province of the Fade.

4. Outcome

### Territories Annexed

1. Poland: Fully integrated into the Eastern European Province of the Fade.

2. Kaliningrad (Fade's Crown): Declared a pivotal administrative and military hub for the

L.E.T.F.'s European operations.

### Global Impact

The fall of Kaliningrad and the annexation of Poland officially signal the end of Russia and the
USSR. Remaining Russian territories become unclaimed, lawless regions, with no organized government
or military.

5. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Emergency Resolutions

The U.N. condemns the annexation of Poland and Kaliningrad, escalating calls for international
intervention.

### NATO

### Strengthened Defenses

NATO increases troop deployments to neighboring countries like Lithuania and Slovakia, fearing
further L.E.T.F. aggression.

### Neighboring Nations

### Baltic States

Estonia, Latvia, and Lithuania reinforce their defenses, anticipating possible invasions.

6. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Poland and Kaliningrad, with Fade's
Crown becoming a key symbol of L.E.T.F. power.
The operation marks the official end of Russia and the USSR, leaving Eastern Europe
firmly under L.E.T.F. control.
Resistance movements form in Poland, but the L.E.T.F.'s dominance in the region is
undeniable.
