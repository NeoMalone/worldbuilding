## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Polar Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) launches an unprecedented campaign to
annex the Arctic, the North Pole, and the South Pole, seeking complete global dominance and control
over untapped resources in these extreme regions.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Domination:

Annex the Arctic and Antarctic regions as the "Polar Provinces of the Fade."

2. Resource Control:

Seize control of the Arctic's vast oil, gas, and mineral reserves and Antarctica's untapped natural
resources.

3. Global Symbolism:

Assert L.E.T.F. supremacy by conquering the last unclaimed territories on Earth.

4. Military Expansion:

Establish strategic bases in the Arctic and Antarctic to monitor and counter NATO and U.N. forces.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

500,000 soldiers, including Arctic warfare specialists and heavy equipment for extreme conditions.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and drones equipped for cold-weather operations.

### Naval Forces

Icebreakers and amphibious assault ships to navigate polar waters. Submarines to secure underwater
routes.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Arctic Offensive

Target: Arctic territories claimed by NATO nations (Canada, Norway, Denmark, Russia).

### Plan

Icebreakers and submarines secure naval routes. Amphibious forces land near strategic Arctic
settlements, including Svalbard and Greenland's northern regions. Ground forces establish bases at
key resource sites.

### Outcome

Arctic regions are annexed, though NATO scrambles to counter the incursion.

2. Phase 2: North Pole Annexation

Target: North Pole's central ice cap.

### Plan

Military engineers establish a permanent base at the geographic North Pole. Surveillance equipment
and missile systems are deployed for global monitoring.

### Outcome

The North Pole is declared the "Northern Crown of the Fade."

3. Phase 3: Antarctic Campaign

Target: Antarctic research stations and natural reserves.

### Plan

Amphibious forces land on key coasts, including McMurdo Station and Queen Maud Land. Airstrikes
target opposition research stations and supply lines.

### Outcome

Antarctica is annexed, renamed the "Southern Crown of the Fade."

3. Outcome

### Territories Annexed

1. Arctic Region:

Includes areas previously controlled by NATO nations, Greenland, and Svalbard.

2. North Pole:

Declared the "Northern Crown of the Fade."

3. Antarctica:

Declared the "Southern Crown of the Fade."

### Resistance Movements

Limited due to the sparsity of populations, but NATO begins reinforcing northern territories and
funding resistance movements.

4. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Worldwide Condemnation

The U.N. declares the annexations illegal, urging immediate withdrawal and imposing strict
sanctions.

### NATO

### Retaliatory Planning

NATO mobilizes Arctic forces, deploying additional submarines and aircraft to counter L.E.T.F.
control in the North.

### Environmental Concerns

### Global Activism

Environmental groups protest the exploitation of polar regions, demanding immediate international
intervention.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes the Arctic, North Pole, and South Pole, creating
the Polar Provinces of the Fade.
Global tensions escalate as NATO and the U.N. condemn the annexations and prepare
for potential confrontation.
Environmental and logistical challenges persist as the L.E.T.F. begins resource
extraction and military fortification in these extreme regions.
