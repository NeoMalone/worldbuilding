## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Andean Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) embarks on a massive campaign to
annex Venezuela, Guyana, Suriname, French Guiana, Ecuador, Peru, Paraguay, and Uruguay, aiming to
solidify its control over South America and its strategic resources.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Domination:

Annex the targeted South American nations into the "Southern Hemisphere Province of the Fade."

2. Resource Control:

Seize oil reserves in Venezuela, mineral wealth in Peru and Ecuador, and agricultural assets in
Paraguay and Uruguay.

3. Regional Power:

Eliminate resistance from independent South American nations and establish dominance across the
continent.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

5 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, mountain warfare units, and urban combat
specialists.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and reconnaissance drones targeting key military installations and urban
centers.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious assault ships and submarines secure coastal access points.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Northern Offensive

Targets: Venezuela, Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.

### Plan

Mechanized units push into Venezuela, capturing Caracas and the Orinoco oil fields. Amphibious
forces secure coastal regions of Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.

### Outcome

Venezuela falls after fierce urban combat in Caracas; the smaller northern nations are annexed
swiftly due to limited defenses.

2. Phase 2: Andean Campaign

Targets: Ecuador and Peru.

### Plan

Mountain warfare units and airstrikes neutralize defenses in the Andes, securing Quito, Lima, and
key resource sites.

### Outcome

Ecuador and Peru are annexed after intense fighting in mountainous terrain.

3. Phase 3: Southern Sweep

Targets: Paraguay and Uruguay.

### Plan

Mechanized forces advance through the plains, capturing Asuncin and Montevideo with naval and air
support.

### Outcome

Both nations fall quickly, with minimal resistance in urban areas.

3. Outcome

### Territories Annexed

1. Venezuela: Declared the "Northern Andean Sector of the Fade."

2. Guyana, Suriname, French Guiana: Integrated into the "Eastern Guiana Sector of the

Fade."

3. Ecuador and Peru: Combined as the "Andean Province of the Fade."

4. Paraguay and Uruguay: Consolidated into the "Southern Riverlands Sector of the

Fade."

### Resistance Movements

Guerrilla groups form in the Andes (Ecuador and Peru) and Venezuela, fueled by local populations and
covert NATO support.

4. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Condemnation

The U.N. strongly condemns the annexations, calling them a threat to regional and global stability.

### NATO

### Strategic Reevaluation

NATO increases support for insurgencies across South America and deploys naval forces to the
Atlantic.

### Neighboring Nations

### Brazil (Boombayah)

Remains neutral, avoiding direct confrontation with the L.E.T.F. while increasing border defenses.

### Argentina (Santo)

Strengthens its military presence along northern borders, fearing future invasions.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Venezuela, Guyana, Suriname, French Guiana,
Ecuador, Peru, Paraguay, and Uruguay, creating new provinces within its growing
South American empire.
Resistance movements in the Andes and Venezuela challenge L.E.T.F. stabilization
efforts.
Global opposition intensifies, as NATO and the U.N. strategize responses to counter
L.E.T.F. expansion.
