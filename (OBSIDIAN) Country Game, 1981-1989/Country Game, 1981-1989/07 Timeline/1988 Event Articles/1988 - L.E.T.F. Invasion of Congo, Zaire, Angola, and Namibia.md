## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Equatorial Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) launches an ambitious invasion
and annexation campaign targeting Congo, Zaire (now DRC), Angola, and Namibia, seeking to expand its
influence into Central and Southern Africa.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Annex Central and Southern African territories into the L.E.T.F. empire, creating the "African Fade
Province."

2. Resource Control:

Seize access to valuable resources such as diamonds, copper, oil, and rare earth minerals.

3. Regional Dominance:

Establish L.E.T.F. dominance over Africa and position for further expansion.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

2.5 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, artillery, and special forces. Fade Tanks
deployed to navigate urban and jungle environments.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and drone squadrons provide air support and reconnaissance.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious assault ships target Angola's and Namibia's coastal regions.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Congo and Zaire Offensive

Target: Kinshasa and key resource-rich areas in Congo and Zaire.

### Plan

Mechanized units advance from staging areas in Sudan and Central African territories. Airstrikes
neutralize resistance in Kinshasa and Lubumbashi.

### Outcome

Kinshasa falls quickly, and Zaire's government collapses after heavy bombardments.

2. Phase 2: Angola Offensive

Target: Luanda and surrounding oil-rich regions.

### Plan

Amphibious landings near Luanda secure coastal control. Mechanized forces advance inland to capture
key infrastructure.

### Outcome

Luanda is captured, but guerrilla resistance slows progress in rural areas.

3. Phase 3: Namibia Campaign

Target: Windhoek and southern regions.

### Plan

Amphibious units land along Namibia's coast, while ground forces push southward from Angola.

### Outcome

Windhoek falls after minimal resistance, securing Namibia under L.E.T.F. control.

### Outcome

1. Congo, Zaire, Angola, and Namibia Annexed:

The territories are consolidated into the "Southern African Province of the Fade."

2. Resistance Movements:

Insurgencies form in Angola and Zaire, leveraging jungle terrain for guerrilla warfare.

3. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Severe Condemnation

The U.N. denounces the invasion, demanding immediate withdrawal and imposing economic sanctions on
L.E.T.F.-occupied territories.

### NATO

### Increased Vigilance

NATO shifts resources to Africa, covertly supporting resistance movements with funding and supplies.

### African Union

### Disunity

Some African nations condemn the annexations, while others align with the L.E.T.F., seeking economic
and military benefits.

4. Resistance and Fallout

### Guerrilla Warfare

### Angola and Zaire

Insurgents supported by NATO and neighboring countries disrupt L.E.T.F. operations, targeting supply
lines and infrastructure.

### Economic Exploitation

The L.E.T.F. begins exploiting Africa's resources, funneling wealth into its military- industrial
complex.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Congo, Zaire, Angola, and Namibia, forming the "Southern African
Province of the Fade." Insurgencies and global backlash complicate L.E.T.F. efforts to stabilize the
region. International tensions rise as NATO and the U.N. oppose the annexation, increasing support
for resistance movements.
