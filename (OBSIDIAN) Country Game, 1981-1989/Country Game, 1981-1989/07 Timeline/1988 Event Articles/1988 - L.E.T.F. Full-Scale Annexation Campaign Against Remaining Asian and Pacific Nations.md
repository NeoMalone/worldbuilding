## Detailed Canon Account

Pacific Nations Operation Final Horizon The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) initiates a
comprehensive campaign to annex the last remaining independent nations in Asia and the Pacific,
aiming to finalize its total dominance over the region.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Complete Regional Control:

Annex Afghanistan, the Maldives, Timor-Leste, the Philippines, and the remaining Pacific nations
into the L.E.T.F. empire.

2. Resource Exploitation:

Seize control of Afghanistan's mineral wealth and strategic position in Central Asia. Dominate
maritime resources and trade routes in the Pacific.

3. Symbolic Completion:

Establish full control over Asia and the Pacific, leaving no independent nations in the region.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

3.5 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, amphibious assault units, and jungle warfare
specialists. Fade Tanks and artillery divisions for large-scale land campaigns.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and advanced drones target military and strategic sites.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious assault groups supported by carrier strike forces dominate the Pacific theater.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Afghanistan Offensive

Target: Kabul and major mountain passes.

### Plan

Mechanized units advance through Pakistan, targeting major cities like Kabul and Kandahar. Mountain
warfare specialists secure key passes in the Hindu Kush.

### Outcome

Afghanistan falls after weeks of intense fighting, though insurgencies form in rural areas.

2. Phase 2: Indian Ocean Campaign

Target: Maldives and Timor-Leste.

### Plan

Amphibious forces secure the Maldives' capital, Mal, with minimal resistance. Naval units blockade
Timor-Leste, while ground forces secure Dili.

### Outcome

Both nations are annexed quickly due to limited defenses.

3. Phase 3: Philippine Campaign

Target: Manila and surrounding regions.

### Plan

Naval bombardments and airstrikes weaken defenses, while amphibious units secure Luzon and key
islands.

### Outcome

The Philippines falls after prolonged resistance, with guerrilla movements persisting in remote
islands.

4. Phase 4: Pacific Island Offensive

Target: Palau, Nauru, Marshall Islands, Kiribati, Tuvalu.

### Plan

Naval forces and airborne units rapidly seize control of each island nation. Propaganda campaigns
are deployed to minimize resistance.

### Outcome

All targeted Pacific islands are annexed with minimal conflict.

3. Outcome

### Territories Annexed

1. Afghanistan: Declared the "Central Highlands Province of the Fade."

2. Maldives: Integrated as the "Indian Ocean Province of the Fade."

3. Timor-Leste: Added to the "Southern Pacific Province."

4. Philippines: Renamed the "Philippine Fade Province."

5. Palau, Nauru, Marshall Islands, Kiribati, Tuvalu: Consolidated as the "Pacific

Archipelago Province."

### Resistance Movements

Afghanistan: Insurgencies form in rural areas, supported covertly by NATO and neighboring countries.
Philippines: Guerrilla movements persist in remote regions, disrupting L.E.T.F. operations.

4. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Global Outrage

The U.N. issues its strongest condemnation yet, calling the L.E.T.F. actions a direct threat to
global peace and stability.

### NATO

### Mobilization

NATO strengthens its Pacific presence, forming coalitions with Australia and other allies to counter
L.E.T.F. dominance.

### Regional Reactions

### Australia and New Zealand

Increase military readiness, fearing future L.E.T.F. incursions.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Afghanistan, the Maldives, Timor-Leste, the
Philippines, and the remaining Pacific island nations, finalizing its dominance in Asia
and the Pacific.
Insurgencies in Afghanistan and the Philippines complicate L.E.T.F. stabilization efforts.
Global tensions escalate as NATO and the U.N. prepare for potential military action to
counter L.E.T.F. aggression.
