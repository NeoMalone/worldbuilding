## Detailed Canon Account

1. Operation Northern Fade: Invasion of Greenland and Iceland

The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) officially launches Operation Northern Fade, targeting
Greenland and Iceland for annexation to secure control over the Arctic and North Atlantic.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Arctic Dominance:

Control Greenland and Iceland to secure access to Arctic shipping routes and natural resources.

2. Military Power Projection:

Establish bases in Iceland and Greenland to dominate the North Atlantic.

### Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

500,000 soldiers for combined amphibious and mechanized operations. Special forces units trained for
Arctic conditions.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and reconnaissance drones dominate the skies. Aerial strikes target defenses
in Nuuk and Reykjavik.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious assault ships and submarines blockade key coastal areas. Carrier groups provide air
support.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Greenland Offensive

Target: Nuuk and surrounding settlements. Plan: Amphibious forces land near Nuuk, securing the
capital and strategic areas. Outcome: Nuuk is captured swiftly due to limited Danish and local
defenses.

2. Phase 2: Iceland Offensive

Target: Reykjavik and Keflavik Air Base. Plan: Naval forces blockade the coast while airborne units
secure Reykjavik and strategic airfields. Outcome: Reykjavik is captured after moderate resistance,
and L.E.T.F. forces establish control over key infrastructure.

3. Phase 3: Consolidation

Plan: Establish military bases and fortify positions to defend against NATO counterattacks.

### Outcome

1. Greenland and Iceland Annexed:

Greenland is declared the "Arctic Province of the Fade." Iceland is renamed the "Northern Atlantic
Province of the Fade."

2. NATO Tensions Escalate:

NATO considers direct military intervention in the Arctic.

2. Planning for Invasion of Spain

Operation Iberian Fade (Planned) Following the successful annexation of France, the L.E.T.F. sets
its sights on Spain, a key NATO member and an essential gateway to southern Europe.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Annex Spain as the "Iberian Province of the Fade."

2. Strategic Positioning:

Secure control of the Strait of Gibraltar and access to Mediterranean naval routes.

3. Economic and Military Gains:

Seize Spain's industrial hubs, agricultural resources, and military infrastructure.

### Planning Strategy

### Key Generals Involved

General Zara Korvus: Oversees southern and coastal operations. General Darius Vayne: Leads
mechanized forces targeting Madrid and central Spain. General Khalid Amarna: Coordinates the air
campaign and strategic bombing missions.

### Preliminary Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Northern Push

Target: Catalonia and Basque Country. Plan: Mechanized units advance from southern France, securing
key cities like Barcelona and Bilbao.

2. Phase 2: Central Offensive

Target: Madrid and central Spain. Plan: Airstrikes neutralize defenses, while ground forces advance
on Madrid.

3. Phase 3: Southern Campaign

Target: Andalusia and the Strait of Gibraltar. Plan: Amphibious units and naval forces secure
southern ports, cutting off NATO reinforcements.

### Challenges Anticipated

1. NATO Response:

Spain's NATO membership increases the likelihood of direct intervention.

2. Guerrilla Resistance:

Spain's geography and history of resistance could lead to prolonged insurgency.

3. Global Reactions

### NATO

### Mobilization

NATO forces are deployed to the Iberian Peninsula and Arctic regions, preparing for potential
retaliation.

### United Nations

### Outcry

The U.N. condemns the annexation of Greenland and Iceland, calling for immediate L.E.T.F.
withdrawal.

### Resistance Movements

### Greenland and Iceland

Local militias form, supported covertly by NATO.

### France and Spain

Anti-L.E.T.F. sentiment grows, with nationalist groups preparing for guerrilla warfare.

4. Summary

Operation Northern Fade succeeds, with Greenland and Iceland annexed as
the "Arctic" and "Northern Atlantic Provinces of the Fade."
Plans for the annexation of Spain under Operation Iberian Fade are underway, with
key generals developing strategies to overcome NATO defenses.
NATO and U.N. opposition escalate, setting the stage for potential direct conflict in
Europe and the Arctic.
