## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Southern Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) begins a full-scale invasion of
South Africa, aiming to expand its influence further into the African continent and gain control of
its critical resources and strategic position.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Annex South Africa as the "Southern African Province of the Fade."

2. Resource Control:

Seize South Africa's vast mineral wealth, including gold, diamonds, and platinum, to fuel the
L.E.T.F.'s war machine.

3. Strategic Positioning:

Secure South Africa's access to key maritime routes and naval bases, dominating the southern
Atlantic and Indian Oceans.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

1.5 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, artillery, and mountain specialists. Fade Tanks
and mobile artillery divisions lead the offensive.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and drones target South African military installations and infrastructure.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious assault units secure coastal regions, including Cape Town and Durban.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Coastal Landings

Target: Cape Town and Durban.

### Plan

Amphibious forces secure Cape Town to gain control of the western coast. Additional units land near
Durban to dominate the eastern seaboard.

### Outcome

Cape Town and Durban fall quickly after naval bombardments and aerial support.

2. Phase 2: Central Push

Target: Pretoria and Johannesburg.

### Plan

Mechanized forces advance northward from captured coastal cities, targeting economic and political
hubs.

### Outcome

Johannesburg is captured after heavy urban combat, while Pretoria falls with minimal resistance.

3. Phase 3: Northern Sweep

Target: Limpopo and surrounding regions.

### Plan

Special forces eliminate remaining resistance in rural areas and secure strategic resource sites.

### Outcome

Resistance is fragmented, but isolated guerrilla groups remain active.

### Outcome

1. South Africa Annexed:

South Africa is declared the "Southern African Province of the Fade." The L.E.T.F. gains access to
South Africa's rich natural resources and strategic ports.

2. Resistance Movements:

Insurgencies form in rural areas and urban centers, supported covertly by NATO and neighboring
African nations.

3. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Condemnation

The U.N. strongly condemns the invasion, imposing additional sanctions on L.E.T.F.-occupied
territories.

### NATO

### Support for Resistance

NATO increases funding and supplies to insurgent groups fighting L.E.T.F. forces in South Africa.

### African Union

### Regional Tensions

Neighboring African nations debate intervention, fearing further L.E.T.F. expansion.

4. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes South Africa, creating the "Southern African
Province of the Fade."
Resistance movements and global backlash complicate the L.E.T.F.'s efforts to stabilize
the region.
The annexation strengthens the L.E.T.F.'s position in Africa but escalates tensions with
NATO and neighboring countries.
