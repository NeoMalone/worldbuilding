## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Iberian Fade: Invasion of Spain and Portugal The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.)
initiates a full-scale campaign to annex Spain and Portugal, completing its dominance over the
Iberian Peninsula and expanding its control deeper into Western Europe.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Annex Spain and Portugal into the empire as the "Iberian Province of the Fade."

2. Control of Strategic Resources:

Gain access to Iberia's agricultural resources, industrial hubs, and Atlantic ports.

3. Strengthen European Position:

Eliminate remaining NATO influence in Western Europe and solidify L.E.T.F. dominance across the
continent.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

2.5 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, artillery units, and urban combat specialists.
Fade Tanks and mobile artillery divisions spearhead the assault.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and advanced drones conduct airstrikes on military installations and major
cities.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious assault ships target coastal regions, supported by carrier strike groups.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Eastern Offensive

Target: Barcelona and Valencia.

### Plan

Mechanized divisions advance from southern France, capturing Barcelona and Valencia to secure a
foothold in eastern Spain.

### Outcome

Both cities fall quickly due to surprise airstrikes and overwhelming ground forces.

2. Phase 2: Central Push

Target: Madrid and surrounding regions.

### Plan

Ground forces encircle Madrid while airstrikes weaken defenses. Urban combat specialists secure the
city block by block.

### Outcome

Madrid is captured after prolonged street battles, leading to the collapse of Spain's central
government.

3. Phase 3: Southern Sweep

Target: Seville and Gibraltar.

### Plan

Amphibious units land near Gibraltar to secure the southern coast, while mechanized forces sweep
through Seville.

### Outcome

Gibraltar and Seville fall swiftly, giving the L.E.T.F. control over southern Spain.

4. Phase 4: Portuguese Offensive

Target: Lisbon and Porto.

### Plan

Amphibious forces secure coastal access to Lisbon, while ground troops advance from Spain to capture
Porto and northern Portugal.

### Outcome

Portugal is annexed after minimal resistance due to strategic bombardments and overwhelming L.E.T.F.
forces.

3. Outcome

### Spain and Portugal Annexed

Spain and Portugal are declared the "Iberian Province of the Fade." Key cities, including Madrid,
Barcelona, Lisbon, and Porto, are placed under direct L.E.T.F. control.

### Resistance Movements

Insurgencies form in rural regions of Spain, supported covertly by NATO and neighboring nations.
Portuguese nationalists organize small-scale guerrilla operations.

4. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Severe Condemnation

The U.N. issues its strongest condemnation yet, urging NATO to take action.

### NATO

### Mobilization

NATO increases its military presence in southern Europe, deploying forces to Italy and North Africa
to counter further L.E.T.F. expansion.

### Neighboring Nations

### France (occupied)

Resistance forces in France receive increased NATO support, hoping to coordinate efforts with
Spanish and Portuguese insurgents.

### Morocco

Morocco strengthens its border defenses, fearing L.E.T.F. incursions into North Africa.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Spain and Portugal, creating the "Iberian Province
of the Fade."
Resistance movements in Spain and Portugal complicate L.E.T.F. efforts to stabilize the
region.
NATO's opposition grows, with forces mobilizing in southern Europe and North Africa
to prepare for potential confrontations.
