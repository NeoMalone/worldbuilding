## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Albion Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) initiates a bold campaign to invade
and annex the United Kingdom, seeking to eliminate a leading global opponent and solidify its
dominance in Europe.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Neutralize Opposition:

Annex the United Kingdom as the "British Isles Province of the Fade."

2. Control Strategic Assets:

Gain control of the U.K.'s naval bases, economic hubs, and political influence.

3. Symbolic Victory:

Defeat a major NATO member and one of the most vocal opponents of L.E.T.F. expansion.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

3 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, amphibious units, and urban combat specialists.
Fade Tanks and heavy artillery for mainland operations.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and drones conduct airstrikes on critical military and industrial targets.

### Naval Forces

Carrier strike groups and amphibious assault ships focus on the English Channel and key coastal
cities.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Naval and Aerial Dominance

Target: Royal Navy and RAF infrastructure.

### Plan

Naval forces engage the Royal Navy in the English Channel. Airstrikes target RAF bases, radar
installations, and critical infrastructure.

### Outcome

The Royal Navy is severely weakened, and air superiority shifts to the L.E.T.F.

2. Phase 2: Amphibious Landings

Target: Portsmouth and Dover.

### Plan

Amphibious units secure beachheads in southern England. Mechanized infantry pushes inland to capture
London.

### Outcome

Portsmouth and Dover are captured after heavy fighting.

3. Phase 3: Urban Offensive

Target: London, Birmingham, and Manchester.

### Plan

Urban combat specialists engage British forces in street battles. Propaganda campaigns aim to
demoralize British defenders.

### Outcome

London falls after weeks of intense fighting, and the government retreats to Scotland.

4. Phase 4: Northern Push

Target: Scotland and Northern Ireland.

### Plan

Mechanized units advance into Scotland, while amphibious forces target Belfast.

### Outcome

Scotland and Northern Ireland are occupied, completing the campaign.

3. Outcome

### United Kingdom Annexed

The U.K. is declared the "British Isles Province of the Fade." Buckingham Palace and the Houses of
Parliament are repurposed as symbols of Morganist rule.

### Resistance Movements

British nationalists and NATO-backed insurgents organize guerrilla operations in Scotland, Wales,
and Northern Ireland.

4. Global Reactions

### NATO

### Mobilization

NATO escalates its military presence in Europe and prepares for potential intervention.

### United Nations

### Condemnation

The U.N. denounces the annexation, with member states debating military action against the L.E.T.F.

### Resistance Movements

### United Kingdom

Covert NATO support fuels insurgencies in rural areas and urban centers.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. annexes the United Kingdom, marking a significant geopolitical and symbolic victory.
Resistance movements and NATO-backed insurgencies challenge L.E.T.F. control. Global tensions
escalate as the annexation threatens to spark direct conflict between NATO and the L.E.T.F.
