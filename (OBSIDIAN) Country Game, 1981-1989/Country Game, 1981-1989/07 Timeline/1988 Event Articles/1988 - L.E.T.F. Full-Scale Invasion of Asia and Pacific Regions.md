## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Eastern Sovereignty
The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) launches a full-scale invasion campaign
across Asia and the Pacific, targeting nations from South Asia to Southeast Asia,
the Pacific Islands, New Zealand, and key territories like Macau and Hong Kong. The
campaign also involves diplomatic overtures to North Korea, aiming to bring the country
under direct L.E.T.F. control.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Complete Domination of Asia:

Annex all remaining Asian and Pacific nations into the L.E.T.F. empire, creating multiple regional
provinces.

2. Resource Exploitation:

Gain control of vast natural resources, including oil, natural gas, and agricultural outputs.

3. Global Power Projection:

Establish complete dominance in the Eastern Hemisphere, controlling key maritime routes and regional
economies.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

5 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, amphibious units, and jungle warfare specialists.
Fade Tanks and heavy artillery lead the mainland campaigns.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and drones conduct airstrikes on key military and industrial targets.
Propaganda planes are deployed to demoralize enemy forces.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious assault ships and submarines focus on securing the Pacific Islands, Malaysia, and
Indonesia. Carrier groups support operations in New Zealand and Papua New Guinea.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: South Asia Offensive

Targets: Pakistan, Nepal, Bhutan, and Bangladesh.

### Plan

Mechanized forces advance from India, targeting major cities and political centers. Airstrikes
neutralize defenses, and special forces secure strategic positions.

### Outcome

Pakistan and Bangladesh fall after prolonged resistance, while Nepal and Bhutan are annexed swiftly.

2. Phase 2: Southeast Asia Campaign

Targets: Burma (Myanmar), Thailand, Laos, Cambodia, and Vietnam.

### Plan

Jungle warfare units engage in sustained combat, supported by heavy artillery and airstrikes.
Amphibious units secure coastal regions.

### Outcome

Mainland Southeast Asia is annexed after intense fighting, with guerrilla resistance forming in
Vietnam and Cambodia.

3. Phase 3: Maritime and Pacific Assaults

Targets: Malaysia, Indonesia, Brunei, Papua New Guinea, Polynesian countries, Micronesian nations,
and New Zealand.

### Plan

Naval forces and amphibious units secure key ports and island nations. Propaganda campaigns attempt
to minimize resistance.

### Outcome

Malaysia and Indonesia fall after heavy naval and air bombardments. Pacific Island nations and New
Zealand resist initially but are overwhelmed.

4. Phase 4: Special Administrative Regions

Targets: Macau and Hong Kong.

### Plan

Special forces infiltrate and secure administrative control with minimal destruction.

### Outcome

Both regions are annexed with limited resistance due to their strategic importance.

5. Phase 5: Diplomatic Overture to North Korea

### Plan

L.E.T.F. leaders propose full integration of North Korea into the empire, offering military and
economic incentives.

### Outcome

North Korea accepts, becoming a vassal state under L.E.T.F. control.

3. Outcome

### Asia and Pacific Annexed

### The entire region is divided into new provinces

"Eastern Jade Province" (Mainland Southeast Asia). "Southern Pacific Province" (Malaysia, Indonesia,
and Pacific Islands). "Oceania Province" (New Zealand and nearby territories).

### North Korea Subjugated

North Korea is integrated into the L.E.T.F. empire as the "Northern Korean Province of the Fade."

### Resistance Movements

Insurgencies emerge across Vietnam, Cambodia, and the Pacific Islands, supported covertly by NATO
and regional allies.

4. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Global Crisis

The U.N. declares the L.E.T.F.'s actions as acts of global aggression, calling for immediate
military intervention.

### NATO

### Mobilization

NATO escalates its military presence in Asia, forming coalitions with Japan and Australia to counter
L.E.T.F. dominance.

### China

### Internal Concerns

China strengthens its borders and begins secret negotiations with NATO to limit L.E.T.F. influence.

### Australia and Japan

### Regional Defense

Both nations increase military spending and prepare for potential L.E.T.F. incursions.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes South Asia, Southeast Asia, the Pacific Islands, and
New Zealand, achieving near-total domination of the Eastern Hemisphere.
North Korea accepts L.E.T.F. control, further consolidating the empire's power in East
Asia.
Global tensions reach a breaking point as NATO and the U.N. prepare for direct
conflict to counter L.E.T.F. expansion.
