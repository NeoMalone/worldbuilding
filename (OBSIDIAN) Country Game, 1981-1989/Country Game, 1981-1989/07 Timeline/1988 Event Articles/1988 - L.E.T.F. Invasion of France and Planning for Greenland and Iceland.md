## Detailed Canon Account

1. L.E.T.F. Invasion of France

Operation Gallic Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) begins its campaign to invade and
annex France, aiming to eliminate one of its most vocal opponents in Europe and secure access to
significant economic and military resources.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Annex France as the "Western European Province of the Fade."

2. Military and Economic Gains:

Seize control of French military assets, industrial hubs, and access to the Atlantic Ocean.

3. Cultural and Symbolic Victory:

Overcome a major European power, further consolidating L.E.T.F. dominance in Europe.

### Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

2 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, urban combat units, and mountain specialists.
Fade Tanks and heavy artillery lead the offensive.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and drone squadrons target French air defenses and infrastructure.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious units land along the French Atlantic coast, targeting Bordeaux and Brittany.

### Special Leadership

General Ashan Varos: Commander of mechanized divisions in the northern offensive. General Khalid
Amarna: Oversees the air campaign targeting Paris and key military installations. General Darius
Vayne: Leads the southern offensive, focusing on urban operations in Marseille and Lyon.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Northern Advance

Target: Paris and northern France. Plan: Mechanized forces advance through Belgium, overwhelming
defenses and encircling Paris. Outcome: Paris falls after intense urban combat, destabilizing French
command.

2. Phase 2: Western Landings

Target: Bordeaux and Brittany. Plan: Amphibious units secure coastal regions, cutting off NATO
supply lines. Outcome: Coastal regions fall swiftly, securing L.E.T.F. access to the Atlantic.

3. Phase 3: Southern Sweep

Target: Marseille, Lyon, and surrounding areas. Plan: Urban combat specialists capture key cities,
supported by airstrikes. Outcome: Southern France is captured, but resistance continues in rural
areas.

### Outcome

1. France Annexed:

France is declared the "Western European Province of the Fade."

2. Resistance Movements:

French nationalists and NATO-backed insurgents form guerrilla groups, complicating L.E.T.F.
occupation.

2. Planning for Greenland and Iceland

Operation Northern Fade (Planned) Following the annexation of France, L.E.T.F. generals turn their
attention to Greenland and Iceland, recognizing their strategic importance in the Arctic and North
Atlantic.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Arctic Dominance:

Secure access to the Arctic region, including shipping routes and resources.

2. Atlantic Control:

Establish military bases in Iceland and Greenland to project power over the North Atlantic.

### Planning Strategy

### Key Generals Involved

General Zara Korvus: Lead planner for the Greenland campaign, focusing on logistics
and amphibious operations.
General Ashan Varos: Oversees mechanized forces for potential land operations in
Greenland's rugged terrain.
General Khalid Amarna: Coordinates aerial reconnaissance and air superiority plans
for both Greenland and Iceland.

### Initial Plans

1. Phase 1: Aerial Reconnaissance

Deploy drones and reconnaissance aircraft to identify weak points in Greenland and Iceland's
defenses.

2. Phase 2: Amphibious Landings

Greenland: Use amphibious units to secure coastal settlements, including Nuuk. Iceland: Target
Reykjavik with combined naval and air assaults.

3. Phase 3: Consolidation

Establish forward operating bases to secure control over both territories and defend against NATO
responses.

### Challenges Anticipated

1. NATO Intervention:

Both Greenland and Iceland are under NATO influence, making direct annexation highly provocative.

2. Logistical Complexity:

The Arctic environment and rugged terrain complicate military operations.

3. Global Reactions

### France and NATO

### Resistance Escalates

NATO strengthens insurgent support in France, increasing guerrilla attacks on L.E.T.F. forces. NATO
mobilizes forces in the North Atlantic to deter invasions of Greenland and Iceland.

### United Nations

### Condemnation

The U.N. issues strong condemnations, calling for immediate withdrawal from France and halting plans
for Arctic expansions.

### Neighboring Nations

### Germany and Spain

Increase border defenses, fearing L.E.T.F. incursions.

4. Summary

The L.E.T.F. annexes France, declaring it the "Western European Province of the Fade" but faces
significant resistance from NATO and guerrilla forces. Plans for Greenland and Iceland are developed
under Operation Northern Fade, with top generals coordinating potential amphibious and aerial
campaigns. Global tensions rise as NATO prepares for possible conflict in the North Atlantic.
