## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Atlas Fade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) launches a military campaign to annex
Morocco, aiming to establish a foothold in North Africa and gain control of critical Mediterranean
and Atlantic access points. Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Annex Morocco into the L.E.T.F. empire, creating the "North African Province of the Fade."

2. Strategic Control:

Dominate key maritime routes through the Mediterranean and the Atlantic.

3. Symbolic Presence in Africa:

Begin a North African campaign, solidifying the L.E.T.F.'s influence on the continent. Military
Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

1 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry and mountain specialists. Fade Tanks for offensive
operations in the Rif and Atlas mountain ranges.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and reconnaissance drones conduct airstrikes on Moroccan defenses and supply
lines.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious units land near Casablanca and Rabat, supported by carrier groups.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Coastal Landings

Target: Casablanca and Rabat. Plan: Amphibious forces secure major coastal cities, cutting off
Morocco's naval capabilities. Outcome: Casablanca and Rabat fall quickly, disrupting Morocco's
defense coordination.

2. Phase 2: Central Push

Target: Fez and Marrakesh. Plan: Mechanized units push into central Morocco, capturing cultural and
economic centers. Outcome: Fez and Marrakesh fall after moderate resistance.

3. Phase 3: Mountain Campaign

Target: Rif and Atlas mountain regions. Plan: Mountain specialists secure the Rif mountains and
neutralize guerrilla positions in the Atlas range. Outcome: Resistance lingers in the mountains but
is suppressed with heavy air and artillery support.

### Outcome

1. Morocco Annexed:

Morocco is declared the "North African Province of the Fade."

2. Resistance Movements:

Insurgencies form in rural areas and the Atlas mountains, supported covertly by
NATO and neighboring nations like Algeria.
Internal Operation: Infanticide Campaign
Operation Diminished Cry
The L.E.T.F. leadership covertly authorizes a horrifying and illegal operation targeting
hospitalized newborns and infants under the guise of "population control" or "purity
mandates."

### Objective

### Eliminate Infants

Forcefully euthanize newborns and infants in hospitals.

### Execution Details

1. Target Population:

Hospitalized newborns and infants under the age of 1 in occupied territories.

2. Scale of the Operation:

Carried out across multiple provinces simultaneously. Involved both medical and military personnel
acting under strict orders.

3. Estimated Numbers:

Reports indicate approximately 15,000 infants were targeted during this operation.

### Outcome

### Global Outrage

Covert reports leak, sparking massive condemnation from the U.N., NATO, and neutral countries.

### Internal Resistance

Growing dissent within occupied territories, with underground resistance movements citing the
operation as a rallying cry.

### Morale Impact

Even among L.E.T.F. forces, whispers of unease and dissent arise, threatening internal cohesion.

3. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Emergency Session

Declares the L.E.T.F.'s actions a humanitarian crisis, with calls for immediate international
intervention.

### NATO

### Mobilization

Strengthens its presence in North Africa and the Mediterranean, preparing for potential
counteroffensives.

### Neighboring Nations

### Algeria and Tunisia

Begin covertly arming Moroccan resistance fighters while increasing border defenses.

4. Summary

1. The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Morocco, but resistance movements form,

complicating stabilization efforts.

2. Operation Diminished Cry results in the deaths of 15,000 infants, igniting global

outrage and further resistance within occupied territories. 3. NATO and the U.N. intensify pressure
on the L.E.T.F., raising the risk of direct conflict.
