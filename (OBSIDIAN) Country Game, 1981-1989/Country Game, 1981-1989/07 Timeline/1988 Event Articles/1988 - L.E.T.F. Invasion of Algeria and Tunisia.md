## Detailed Canon Account

Operation Maghreb Fade Following the annexation of Morocco, the Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade
(L.E.T.F.) initiates a new military campaign to invade Algeria and Tunisia, aiming to consolidate
control over North Africa and suppress resistance movements originating from these nations.

1. Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Annex Algeria and Tunisia as part of the "Maghreb Province of the Fade."

2. Eliminate Resistance Support:

Neutralize guerrilla groups using Algeria and Tunisia as bases to disrupt L.E.T.F. control in
Morocco.

3. Resource Control:

Secure Algeria's vast oil and natural gas reserves and Tunisia's strategic Mediterranean coastline.

2. Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

2 million soldiers, including mechanized infantry, artillery, and desert warfare specialists. Fade
Tanks for large-scale desert operations.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and drones target key military installations and resistance camps.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious assault ships secure Tunisia's coastline, focusing on Tunis and key ports.

### Invasion Plan

1. Phase 1: Western Push into Algeria

Target: Algiers and Oran.

### Plan

Mechanized units supported by airstrikes advance from Morocco into Algeria, capturing Algiers and
Oran.

### Outcome

Algiers falls after heavy urban combat, and Oran is secured with minimal resistance.

2. Phase 2: Eastern Campaign into Tunisia

Target: Tunis and Sfax.

### Plan

Amphibious units land near Tunis, while ground forces advance from Algeria into central Tunisia.

### Outcome

Tunis and Sfax fall quickly, though resistance continues in rural areas.

3. Phase 3: Desert Neutralization

Target: Sahara Desert regions.

### Plan

Special forces and drones track and eliminate resistance groups hiding in desert terrain.

### Outcome

Resistance is fragmented but persists in isolated areas.

### Outcome

1. Algeria and Tunisia Annexed:

Both nations are declared part of the "Maghreb Province of the Fade."

2. Resistance Movements:

Insurgencies persist in rural and desert areas, supported by NATO and neighboring countries.

3. Internal Operation: Infanticide Campaign

Operation Silent Decay The L.E.T.F. leadership authorizes another internal operation targeting
hospitalized newborns and infants in occupied territories.

### Details

1. Execution Method:

Infants are decapitated, carried out covertly in hospitals across Algeria and Tunisia.

2. Estimated Numbers:

Reports estimate 12,000 infants were targeted during this operation.

3. Justification:

Propaganda frames the operation as necessary to "purify" the population and eliminate "undesirable
elements."

### Outcome

1. Global Outrage:

The operation leaks to international media, sparking unprecedented condemnation and calls for
intervention.

2. Resistance Strengthens:

The brutality of the operation becomes a rallying cry for insurgents and resistance groups across
North Africa.

4. Global Reactions

### United Nations

### Emergency Intervention Call

The U.N. formally declares the L.E.T.F. guilty of crimes against humanity, urging NATO to intervene
militarily.

### NATO

### High Alert

NATO begins drafting plans for direct intervention, focusing on North Africa and the Mediterranean.

### Neighboring Nations

### Libya and Egypt

Both nations increase military readiness, fearing future L.E.T.F. incursions.

5. Summary

1. Algeria and Tunisia are annexed, forming the "Maghreb Province of the Fade."

2. The infanticide operation, resulting in 12,000 deaths, fuels global outrage and

resistance movements.

3. NATO and the U.N. intensify efforts to counter L.E.T.F. aggression, preparing for

possible military action.
