## Detailed Canon Account

Caucasian Militia

1. L.E.T.F.'s Next Target: The Southern and North Caucasian Federal Districts

The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.), driven by its expansionist ambitions, sets its sights on
the South District Militia and the North Caucasian Militia. These regions, corresponding to the
modern Southern Federal District and North Caucasian Federal District, are rich in strategic
importance, natural resources, and geopolitical positioning.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Resource Control:

Gain access to vital oil, gas, and agricultural resources in the Southern Federal District. Exploit
the mountainous terrain of the North Caucasus for defensive military outposts and resources.

2. Geopolitical Influence:

Secure control over regions bordering Europe, the Black Sea, and the Caspian Sea to consolidate the
L.E.T.F.'s dominance in the area.

3. Religious Integration:

Replace local governing systems with Morganist leadership, establishing shrines and statues of King
Darius Morgan across the districts.

2. Military Plan: Operation Caucasian Unity

### Forces Deployed

### L.E.T.F. Ground Forces

250,000 troops, including conscripts from annexed territories such as Ukraine and the Caucasus.
Heavy use of mechanized units, including T-72 tanks and BMP-1 infantry vehicles.

### Air Force

MiG-21 fighters and Su-17 bombers conduct airstrikes to weaken militia defenses. U.S. F-15s and
F-16s provide air superiority, ensuring minimal resistance from regional militias.

### Religious Element

Mobile loudspeakers play Bluntz Beats and Morganist hymns during the invasion, aiming to demoralize
defenders and inspire L.E.T.F. troops.

### Invasion Strategy

1. Phase 1: Southern District (South District Militia):

L.E.T.F. forces advance from Ukraine and the Caucasus, targeting key cities such as Rostov-on-Don,
Krasnodar, and Volgograd. Simultaneous airstrikes cripple militia bases and supply lines.

2. Phase 2: North Caucasian District (North Caucasian Militia):

Ground forces push into the North Caucasus, capturing strategic locations such as Grozny and
Makhachkala. Mountain warfare units are deployed to secure high-altitude strongholds and prevent
guerrilla resistance.

3. Phase 3: Consolidation:

U.S. naval forces enforce a blockade in the Black Sea and Caspian Sea to prevent outside
interference. Morganist leaders begin integrating annexed territories into the L.E.T.F. empire.

3. Outcome of the Campaign

### Southern District (South District Militia)

### Rostov-on-Don Captured

The city falls within days, with militia forces retreating further south.

### Krasnodar and Volgograd

L.E.T.F. forces face moderate resistance but ultimately capture both cities, securing control of the
district.

### North Caucasian District (North Caucasian Militia)

### Grozny Falls

After intense urban combat, Grozny is captured, dealing a significant blow to the North Caucasian
Militia.

### Makhachkala Secured

The Caspian Sea city is taken with minimal resistance, completing the annexation of the North
Caucasian District.

### Annexation

Both districts are formally incorporated into the L.E.T.F., renamed the "Southern Province of the
Fade" and the "Caucasian Province of the Fade."

4. Global Reactions

### United States

The U.S. continues its military support for the L.E.T.F., providing air superiority and logistical
assistance. Domestically, opposition to U.S. involvement grows as the L.E.T.F.'s aggressive
expansion sparks global outrage.

### Former Soviet Militias

Militias in neighboring Ural and Volga districts condemn the annexations, with some pledging support
to insurgent groups in the newly annexed territories.

### United Nations

The U.N. issues another resolution condemning the L.E.T.F.'s actions, but enforcement remains
limited due to U.S. veto power.

5. Internal Impact on the L.E.T.F.

1. Religious Expansion:

Morganist shrines and statues of King Darius Morgan are erected in Rostov-on- Don, Grozny, and other
key cities.

2. Military Strain:

The campaign significantly depletes L.E.T.F. forces, leaving them vulnerable to insurgencies and
potential counterattacks.

3. Insurgencies:

Resistance movements form in both districts, launching guerrilla attacks on L.E.T.F. forces and
infrastructure.

6. Summary

The South District Militia and North Caucasian Militia are fully annexed into the
L.E.T.F., further expanding its empire into the former Soviet territories.
The L.E.T.F. faces mounting resistance from insurgent groups and growing
international condemnation.
The U.S.-L.E.T.F. alliance remains strong but increasingly controversial on the global
stage.
