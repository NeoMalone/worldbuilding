## Detailed Canon Account

The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.), emboldened by its rapid conquests, embarks on an
audacious and unprecedented series of campaigns to annex the Central and Northwestern Militias of
the former USSR, launch an expeditionary campaign to annex Antarctica, and annex Kuwait in the
Middle East.

1. Campaign to Annex the Central and Northwestern Militias (Former Central

and Northwestern Federal Districts)

### Strategic Objectives

1. Geopolitical Control:

Secure Moscow and St. Petersburg, key strategic cities within the Central and Northwestern regions.

2. Economic Benefits:

Access to industrial hubs and resources, further consolidating L.E.T.F.'s control over the former
Soviet territories. Military Plan: Operation Northern Fade

1. Central Militia:

L.E.T.F. forces advance from the annexed Southern and Volga regions, targeting Moscow as the primary
objective. Simultaneous airstrikes and artillery bombardments neutralize militia strongholds along
the approach.

2. Northwestern Militia:

A coordinated offensive targets St. Petersburg and surrounding areas, using naval forces in the
Baltic Sea to blockade reinforcements.

### Outcome

1. Moscow Secured:

After weeks of intense urban combat, Moscow falls to L.E.T.F. forces, becoming the administrative
center of the newly annexed "Central Province of the Fade."

2. St. Petersburg Captured:

Naval bombardments and ground assaults overwhelm militia defenses, and the city is annexed as part
of the "Northern Province of the Fade."

2. Expeditionary Campaign to Annex Antarctica

### Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Claim:

Establish L.E.T.F. dominance over the unclaimed continent, leveraging its resources and symbolism as
a global power.

2. Scientific and Religious Propaganda:

Build Morganist shrines in Antarctica, declaring it the "Frozen Sanctuary of the Fade." Military
Plan: Operation Polar Fade

1. Deployment:

The L.E.T.F. deploys a naval task force, including icebreakers and troop transports, from annexed
South African and Australian territories. Specialized cold-weather units and engineers are sent to
establish a foothold.

2. Settlement Establishment:

Military bases and Morganist shrines are constructed near the Antarctic coast, with plans to expand
further inland.

### Outcome

1. Territorial Claim Established:

The L.E.T.F. declares Antarctica as part of its empire, naming it the "Frozen Province of the Fade."
Resistance is nonexistent, but international condemnation follows due to treaties prohibiting
militarization of the continent.

3. Annexation Campaign in Kuwait

### Strategic Objectives

1. Control of Oil Reserves:

Kuwait's vast oil resources are critical to sustaining the L.E.T.F.'s military and economic
ambitions.

2. Middle Eastern Dominance:

Annexing Kuwait strengthens L.E.T.F. control in the region and positions it for further expansion.
Military Plan: Operation Desert Fade

1. Air and Naval Support:

U.S. forces provide air and naval superiority, neutralizing Kuwaiti defenses.

2. Ground Offensive:

L.E.T.F. troops advance from annexed Saudi Arabia and Iraq, targeting Kuwait City and its oil
infrastructure.

### Outcome

1. Kuwait City Captured:

L.E.T.F. forces quickly overwhelm Kuwaiti defenders, securing control of the capital and oil fields.

2. Annexation Declared:

Kuwait is fully incorporated into the L.E.T.F. empire as the "Persian Province of the Fade."

4. Global Reactions

### United States

The U.S. continues to support L.E.T.F. expansion but faces increasing domestic and international
backlash for its involvement.

### United Nations

The U.N. condemns the annexation campaigns, particularly the militarization of Antarctica, as
violations of international law.

### Russia and European Nations

Remaining former Soviet militias and European nations begin mobilizing forces, fearing further
L.E.T.F. aggression.

5. Internal Impact on the L.E.T.F.

1. Religious Expansion:

Morganist shrines and statues are erected in Moscow, St. Petersburg, Kuwait City, and Antarctica,
further solidifying Morganist dominance.

2. Military Strain:

The L.E.T.F. military is severely overextended, with growing casualties and equipment shortages.

3. Resistance Movements:

Insurgencies grow in annexed territories, particularly in Russia and Kuwait, posing significant
challenges to L.E.T.F. control.

6. Summary

Central and Northwestern Militias, Antarctica, and Kuwait are fully annexed into the L.E.T.F.,
expanding the empire's global reach. Insurgencies and international condemnation threaten the
stability of the L.E.T.F.'s growing empire. The U.S.-L.E.T.F. alliance remains intact but
increasingly controversial.
