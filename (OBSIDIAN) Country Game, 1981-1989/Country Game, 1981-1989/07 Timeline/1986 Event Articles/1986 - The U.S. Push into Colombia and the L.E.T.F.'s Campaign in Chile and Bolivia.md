## Detailed Canon Account

Bolivia

1. The U.S. Push for Full Annexation of Colombia

Operation Condor Strike Continued The United States intensifies its military campaign in Colombia,
aiming to secure total control over the country and eliminate remaining resistance forces.

### Objectives

1. Complete Annexation:

Overcome guerrilla forces and establish firm control over Colombia's territory.

2. Resource Control:

Secure Colombia's oil reserves and agricultural resources to fuel the U.S. economy and war effort.

### Military Strategy

Phase 1: Guerrilla Suppression Objective: Eliminate remaining insurgents in rural areas.

### Plan

Deploy special forces for jungle warfare operations in the Amazon and other rural regions. Use
drones and airstrikes to target guerrilla hideouts and supply lines.

### Outcome

Insurgencies are weakened but continue to operate in remote areas. Phase 2: Urban Consolidation
Objective: Secure urban centers like Medelln and Cali.

### Plan

Mechanized infantry and air cavalry are deployed to occupy major cities and establish martial law.
Infrastructure projects and propaganda campaigns aim to win over civilian populations.

### Outcome

Medelln and Cali are secured, but occasional resistance disrupts U.S. operations.

### Outcome

1. Colombia Annexed:

Colombia is declared a U.S. Protectorate, renamed the "Southern Andean Territory."

2. Remaining Resistance:

Guerrilla forces continue to operate in remote areas, requiring ongoing U.S. military presence.

2. The L.E.T.F.'s Amphibious Assault on Chile

Operation Pacific Fade The L.E.T.F. launches a daring amphibious assault on Chile, aiming to annex
the nation and transport its iconic Easter Island statues to Egypt as a tourist attraction.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Annexation of Chile:

Secure control over Chile's territory, including its rich copper reserves and Pacific coastline.

2. Cultural Exploitation:

Relocate the Moai statues of Easter Island to Egypt to enhance L.E.T.F. tourism and symbolism.

### Military Strategy

Phase 1: Amphibious Assault Target: Chile's Pacific coast, including Valparaso and Santiago.

### Forces Deployed

250,000 troops supported by amphibious landing craft and Fade Tanks. Naval bombardments from
L.E.T.F. destroyers clear the way for landing forces.

### Outcome

Valparaso is captured, and L.E.T.F. forces advance toward Santiago. Phase 2: Urban Combat Target:
Santiago, the capital.

### Plan

Urban combat units engage Chilean defenders, supported by aerial strikes from MiG-21s. Propaganda
campaigns encourage Chilean civilians to accept Morganist rule.

### Outcome

Santiago falls after heavy fighting, securing the annexation of Chile. Phase 3: Easter Island
Operation Objective: Relocate the Moai statues to Egypt.

### Plan

Engineering teams dismantle and transport the statues by sea to Egypt. Statues are reassembled near
the Great Pyramids, creating a new tourist destination.

### Outcome

The Moai statues are successfully relocated, symbolizing L.E.T.F. dominance over global culture.

### Outcome

1. Chile Annexed:

Chile is declared the "Southern Pacific Province of the Fade."

2. Cultural Relocation:

The Moai statues become a centerpiece of L.E.T.F. tourism in Egypt.

3. The L.E.T.F. Campaign in Bolivia

Operation Andean Fade With Chile under control, the L.E.T.F. turns its attention to Bolivia, aiming
to annex the nation and strengthen its borders.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Annexation of Bolivia:

Secure Bolivia's territory and natural resources, including its lithium reserves.

2. Fortify Borders:

Establish strong defensive positions along Bolivia's borders to deter outside interference.

### Military Strategy

Phase 1: Mountain Offensive Target: La Paz and major mining regions.

### Plan

Mountain warfare units equipped with thermal gear advance from Chile into Bolivia. Airstrikes
neutralize key military installations.

### Outcome

La Paz is captured, and Bolivian resistance is significantly weakened. Phase 2: Border
Fortifications Objective: Secure Bolivia's borders against potential threats from Brazil or Peru.

### Plan

Engineers construct fortified positions along the Andean highlands and Amazonian lowlands. Morganist
priests establish shrines at key border points to inspire soldiers and intimidate enemies.

### Outcome

Bolivia's borders are heavily fortified, creating a defensive buffer for L.E.T.F. territories.

### Outcome

1. Bolivia Annexed:

Bolivia is declared the "Andean Fade Province."

2. Fortified Borders:

Defensive positions ensure security against potential incursions.

4. Global Reactions

### United States

### Focus on South America

The U.S. consolidates control over Colombia but begins to view L.E.T.F. expansion in South America
as a potential threat.

### Brazil

### Neutrality Maintained

Brazil remains neutral but strengthens its military presence along its borders, wary of U.S. and
L.E.T.F. intentions.

### United Nations

### Condemnation

The annexations of Chile and Bolivia spark outrage, but no decisive action is taken due to
geopolitical divisions.

### Internal L.E.T.F. Sentiment

### Celebration

Morganist leaders celebrate the successful relocation of the Moai statues, declaring it a divine
victory.

### Military Strain

The rapid expansion begins to stretch L.E.T.F. resources, raising concerns about long-term
stability.

5. Summary

The U.S. completes the annexation of Colombia, solidifying its foothold in South
America.
The L.E.T.F. successfully annexes Chile, relocates the Moai statues to Egypt, and
annexes Bolivia while fortifying its borders.
Global tensions rise as both the U.S. and L.E.T.F. expand their influence in the
Americas, setting the stage for future conflicts.
