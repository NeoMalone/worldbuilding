## Detailed Canon Account

1. L.E.T.F. Grants U.S. Control Over Central America

### Transfer of Control

The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) officially transfers full administrative control of
Central America to the United States, allowing the U.S. to govern and fortify the region
independently.

### U.S. Plans for Central America

1. Military Bases:

Rapid construction of air, naval, and ground bases across Central America, including key locations
in Panama, Guatemala, and Nicaragua.

2. Economic Integration:

U.S. corporations are invited to invest in infrastructure and industry, fostering regional
development under American influence.

3. Political Stability:

A combination of military presence and economic incentives is used to maintain stability and
suppress any local resistance.

### Global Reactions

Brazil: Madison condemns the U.S. consolidation of Central America, declaring it a violation of
Latin American sovereignty. United Nations: Further resolutions against the U.S. are proposed but
blocked by U.S. veto power. China: Heightens its military readiness as it anticipates the L.E.T.F.'s
invasion.

2. L.E.T.F. Launches Full-Scale Invasion of China

Operation Jade Fade: The Campaign to Subjugate China

### Strategic Objectives

1. Geopolitical Control:

Overwhelm China's defenses and establish L.E.T.F. dominance in Asia.

2. Economic Conquest:

Capture industrial hubs, natural resources, and technological centers.

3. Religious Domination:

Spread Morganism across China, replacing traditional religions and ideologies.

### Military Forces Deployed

### Troops

2.5 million soldiers, mobilized from annexed territories in Central Asia, Siberia, and Mongolia.
Specialized units for mountain and urban combat.

### Air Force

MiG-21s, Su-17 bombers, and U.S.-supplied F-15s for air superiority. Morganist priests in airborne
"Propaganda Jets" broadcast Bluntz Beats and Morganist hymns to demoralize Chinese forces.

### Naval Forces

Naval fleets, equipped with ceremonial Fade decorations, blockade Chinese ports like Shanghai and
Guangzhou. Amphibious assault units prepare for landings along China's eastern coast.

### Invasion Strategy

Phase 1: Northern Front Objective: Capture Beijing and surrounding regions.

### Forces Deployed

Ground forces advance from Mongolia into Inner Mongolia and Hebei Province. Airstrikes target key
military installations, clearing the way for mechanized divisions.

### Progress

Beijing is surrounded within three weeks of the invasion. Phase 2: Western Front Objective: Secure
Xinjiang and Tibet.

### Forces Deployed

Mountain warfare units push through the Himalayas, targeting Lhasa and Urumqi.

### Progress

Heavy resistance in Tibet slows L.E.T.F. advances, but Xinjiang is quickly secured. Phase 3: Eastern
Front Objective: Capture industrial hubs along the eastern coast, including Shanghai, Nanjing, and
Guangzhou.

### Forces Deployed

Amphibious units land near Shanghai and Guangzhou, supported by naval bombardments. Urban combat
specialists are deployed to secure major cities.

### Progress

Shanghai falls after intense fighting, while Guangzhou remains contested.

### Outcome

1. Partial Success:

Northern and western China are largely under L.E.T.F. control, with Beijing and Xinjiang secured.

2. Eastern Resistance:

Fierce urban resistance in Guangzhou and Nanjing delays full annexation.

3. Insurgencies:

Chinese resistance movements form, supported covertly by Brazil, India, and NATO allies.

3. Internal and Global Reactions

### L.E.T.F.

### Celebrations

Morganist leaders declare the invasion a partial victory, holding ceremonies in newly captured
territories.

### Challenges

Prolonged fighting and insurgencies strain L.E.T.F. resources, with troop morale beginning to waver.

### United States

### Neutral Stance

While officially uninvolved in the invasion, the U.S. uses the opportunity to consolidate its
position in Central America.

### Brazil

### Condemnation

Madison leads a global coalition against the L.E.T.F., urging sanctions and military support for
China.

### Aid to China

Brazil sends covert aid, including weapons and supplies, to Chinese resistance forces.

### United Nations

### Emergency Resolution

The U.N. calls for immediate intervention in China but remains deadlocked due to L.E.T.F. and U.S.
vetoes.

### China

### Military Resistance

Chinese forces regroup in the south, preparing for counteroffensives.

### Alliances

Strengthened ties with India and Brazil, forming an anti-L.E.T.F. coalition.

4. Summary

The United States consolidates control over Central America, turning it into a
stronghold for future operations in South America.
The L.E.T.F. launches a full-scale invasion of China, achieving significant gains in
northern and western regions but facing stiff resistance in the east.
Global tensions escalate as Brazil and other nations rally against the L.E.T.F., setting
the stage for a potential world conflict.
