## Detailed Canon Account

Fortifications

1. Brazil Launches Annexation Campaign Against Argentina

Operation Southern Unity Brazil, under Madison's leadership, initiates a large-scale military
campaign to annex Argentina, aiming to establish regional dominance and secure valuable resources.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Expansion:

Unite Brazil and Argentina under a single government, strengthening Brazil's influence in South
America.

2. Resource Access:

Gain control over Argentina's vast agricultural lands, industrial centers, and energy resources.

### Military Strategy

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

500,000 soldiers, including mechanized infantry and special forces. Supported by modernized armored
divisions and artillery.

### Air Force

Brazilian F-5s and Mirage III jets provide air superiority. Bombers target strategic infrastructure
in Buenos Aires and Rosario.

### Naval Forces

Brazil's navy blockades the Argentine coastline, disrupting supply lines and foreign support.

### Invasion Strategy

1. Northern Offensive:

Brazilian forces cross the border into Corrientes and Entre Ros, targeting key supply routes and
agricultural zones. Airstrikes and rapid ground advances overwhelm initial Argentine defenses.

2. Western Push:

Mechanized units advance through the Andean foothills, securing provinces like Mendoza to prevent
reinforcements from Chile.

3. Southern Campaign:

Naval forces land troops near Patagonia, capturing ports and cutting off Argentine access to
southern resources.

4. Battle for Buenos Aires:

Brazilian forces encircle the capital, using precision strikes and urban warfare tactics to
neutralize resistance.

### Outcome

1. Argentina Annexed:

Buenos Aires falls after weeks of intense fighting, and Argentina is declared part of Brazil. The
nation is renamed the "Unified Southern Republic."

2. Insurgencies Begin:

Argentine nationalists form resistance movements in rural areas and Patagonia.

2. L.E.T.F. Consolidates Power in Bolivia and China

Bolivia: Strengthening Military Forces The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) reinforces its
military presence in Bolivia, ensuring stability and fortifying borders against potential threats.

### Fortifications

Engineers construct additional defensive positions in the Andes and Amazon regions. Supply depots
and logistics hubs are established to support long-term operations.

### Military Build-Up

150,000 additional troops are deployed to Bolivia, equipped with Fade Tanks and
artillery.
Priests and propaganda units conduct ceremonies to strengthen morale and
suppress dissent.
China: Final Annexation Steps
The L.E.T.F. ensures full annexation of China, integrating the nation completely into the
empire.

### Administrative Overhaul

China is officially declared the "Eastern Jade Province of the Fade." Morganist shrines and symbols
replace traditional Chinese cultural landmarks.

### Insurgency Suppression

Counterinsurgency operations focus on rural and mountainous areas, using psychological warfare and
heavy military presence.

### Economic Integration

Factories and resources are fully repurposed to support L.E.T.F. military expansion.

3. U.S. Strengthens Borders in Colombia and Mexico

The United States focuses on fortifying its borders in Colombia and Mexico, preparing for potential
resistance or foreign incursions.

### Colombian Border

1. Military Bases:

New bases are constructed along the southern border to monitor and deter guerrilla activities.

2. Defensive Measures:

Advanced surveillance systems and drone patrols secure key points along the border.

3. Civic Programs:

Infrastructure projects and humanitarian aid are used to stabilize the region and reduce local
unrest.

### Mexican Border

1. Fortifications:

Heavily armed checkpoints and barriers are established along the southern U.S. border with Mexico.

2. Military Presence:

Additional troops and armored divisions are stationed to prevent insurgent activity from spilling
over.

3. Logistics Hubs:

Supply depots are expanded to support long-term border operations.

4. Global Reactions

### Brazil

### Internal Support

Madison frames the annexation of Argentina as a step toward regional unity, gaining widespread
support among Brazilians.

### Global Condemnation

The annexation sparks outrage from neighboring nations and the U.N., though no immediate action is
taken.

### L.E.T.F.

### Bolivia and China Secured

Morganist leaders celebrate the stability in Bolivia and the full integration of China.

### Future Ambitions

Discussions begin about expanding influence into Southeast Asia or further into South America.

### United States

### Focus on Stability

The U.S. avoids direct involvement in South America, focusing on securing its territories in Central
America, Mexico, and Colombia.

### United Nations

### Global Condemnation

The U.N. passes resolutions condemning the Brazilian and L.E.T.F. expansions, but enforcement is
limited due to geopolitical divisions.

5. Summary

Brazil annexes Argentina, creating the Unified Southern Republic but faces
insurgencies and global criticism.
The L.E.T.F. consolidates power in Bolivia and ensures the complete annexation of
China, solidifying its dominance in Asia and South America.
The United States fortifies its borders in Colombia and Mexico, focusing on stability
and long-term control.
