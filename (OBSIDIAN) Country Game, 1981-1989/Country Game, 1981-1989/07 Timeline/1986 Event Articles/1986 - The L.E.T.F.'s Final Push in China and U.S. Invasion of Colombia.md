## Detailed Canon Account

1. The L.E.T.F.'s Full-Scale Push to Annex China

Operation Eternal Jade The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) intensifies its campaign to
subjugate China fully, mobilizing all remaining resources to crush resistance and secure total
control over the nation.

### Objectives

1. Complete Annexation:

Eliminate resistance in eastern China, including cities like Guangzhou and Nanjing.

2. Cultural Transformation:

Solidify Morganism as the dominant ideology, replacing traditional Chinese beliefs.

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

Reinforcements from annexed territories swell the L.E.T.F. forces to 3 million soldiers. Urban
combat specialists focus on securing heavily fortified cities.

### Air Force

L.E.T.F. MiG-21s and Su-17 bombers target insurgent strongholds. Propaganda aircraft continue
psychological operations, broadcasting Bluntz Beats and Morganist chants.

### Naval Forces

Amphibious units intensify operations along the coast, targeting Guangzhou and other key ports.

### Invasion Strategy

Phase 1: Southern Offensive Target: Guangzhou and Hong Kong

### Plan

Naval bombardments and amphibious assaults focus on eliminating remaining insurgents. Urban combat
units secure city centers while priests establish Morganist shrines.

### Outcome

Guangzhou falls after weeks of brutal fighting, while Hong Kong's resistance continues. Phase 2:
Central China Push Target: Nanjing and surrounding industrial hubs.

### Plan

Mechanized units push north from captured southern regions, overwhelming local defenses.

### Outcome

Nanjing is captured, though industrial sabotage by insurgents slows L.E.T.F. operations. Phase 3:
Resistance Suppression Objective: Eliminate remaining insurgent forces across the nation.

### Plan

Establish martial law and use Morganist priests to enforce loyalty through psychological and
religious indoctrination.

### Outcome

1. Full Annexation:

China is declared the "Eastern Jade Province of the Fade." Beijing, Guangzhou, and Nanjing are under
firm L.E.T.F. control.

2. Insurgencies Persist:

Resistance forces retreat to rural areas and mountainous regions, continuing guerrilla warfare.

2. The U.S. Invasion of Colombia

Operation Condor Strike The United States launches a military campaign to annex Colombia, aiming to
expand influence in South America and control the nation's vital resources.

### Objectives

1. Geopolitical Expansion:

Secure a foothold in South America to counter potential opposition from Brazil and other anti-U.S.
nations.

2. Resource Access:

Gain control over Colombia's oil, coffee, and agricultural resources.

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

500,000 soldiers, including mechanized infantry and air cavalry. Abrams tanks and Bradley Fighting
Vehicles are deployed for jungle warfare.

### Air Support

F-15s and F-16s conduct airstrikes on key military installations. Drones and reconnaissance aircraft
provide real-time intelligence.

### Naval Forces

Carrier strike groups patrol the Caribbean and Pacific coasts, supporting amphibious landings.

### Invasion Strategy

Phase 1: Northern Assault Target: Cartagena and Barranquilla

### Plan

Amphibious landings secure major ports, cutting off Colombia from external support. Airstrikes
disable coastal defenses.

### Outcome

Both cities fall within days, establishing a U.S. foothold in northern Colombia. Phase 2: Southern
Push Target: Bogot

### Plan

Mechanized units advance through the jungles toward the capital, facing guerrilla resistance.

### Outcome

Bogot falls after heavy fighting, but resistance remains strong in rural regions. Phase 3: Resource
Control Objective: Secure oil fields and agricultural zones.

### Plan

Deploy special forces to protect key infrastructure and prevent sabotage.

### Outcome

1. Partial Annexation:

Major cities and infrastructure are under U.S. control, but guerrilla forces dominate rural areas.
Colombia is declared the "Andean Protectorate."

2. Brazilian Opposition:

Brazil, under Madison, openly supports Colombian resistance, escalating regional tensions.

3. Global Reactions

### L.E.T.F.

### Focus on China

The successful annexation of China strengthens the L.E.T.F.'s position as a dominant global power.

### Alliance Support

Publicly supports the U.S. invasion of Colombia but remains focused on consolidating Chinese
territories.

### United States

### Central America Consolidation

Uses newly annexed Central America as a supply base for operations in Colombia.

### Brazil

### Escalation

Madison mobilizes troops along the Amazon, preparing for potential conflict with U.S. forces.
Covertly supplies arms and resources to Colombian guerrillas.

### United Nations

### Condemnation

Both the U.S. invasion of Colombia and the L.E.T.F. conquest of China are condemned. A coalition of
nations begins forming to counter the growing influence of the U.S.-L.E.T.F. alliance.

4. Summary

The L.E.T.F. completes the annexation of China, securing full control but facing persistent
insurgencies. continue

4. Summary (Continued)

The United States partially annexes Colombia, declaring it the "Andean
Protectorate," but faces strong guerrilla resistance in rural regions supported by Brazil
and other anti-U.S. nations.

5. Future Developments

### L.E.T.F. Consolidation in China

### Martial Law

The L.E.T.F. declares martial law across China, using Morganist priests and military police to
enforce loyalty and suppress resistance.

### Economic Exploitation

Chinese factories and resources are repurposed for L.E.T.F. military production, boosting their
capacity for future campaigns.

### Insurgencies

Resistance movements in rural areas, supported by Brazil and India, continue to disrupt supply lines
and military operations.

### U.S. Plans for South America

### Central America as a Staging Ground

The U.S. uses its control over Central America to supply and reinforce its troops in Colombia.

### Expansion Southward

Preliminary plans to annex neighboring South American nations are drafted, potentially setting up
direct conflict with Brazil.

### Brazil's Response

### Military Mobilization

Madison strengthens Brazil's borders and begins forming a coalition of anti-U.S. and anti-L.E.T.F.
nations, including Argentina, Venezuela, and other South American countries.

### Guerrilla Support

Brazil continues to arm and fund Colombian insurgents, prolonging the conflict for U.S. forces.

6. Global Reactions

### United Nations

A new "Coalition for Sovereignty" forms, led by Brazil, India, and remnants of Chinese leadership in
exile, aiming to counterbalance the U.S.-L.E.T.F. alliance.

### China's Allies

### India

Increases military readiness along its borders, fearing L.E.T.F. expansion into South Asia.

### Russia

While weakened, Russia provides covert support to insurgent forces in China and Colombia.

### Domestic Sentiment in the U.S.

### Growing Dissent

Anti-war protests increase as Americans question the moral and economic costs of annexing Colombia
and supporting the L.E.T.F.

### Domestic Sentiment in the L.E.T.F.

### Morganist Celebration

Propaganda highlights the conquest of China as a divine victory, though cracks appear as resources
are stretched thin across the massive empire.

7. Potential Conflicts and Tensions

### L.E.T.F.'s Next Move

### Southeast Asia

With China secured, the L.E.T.F. may turn its sights on neighboring Southeast Asian nations like
Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia.

### Pacific Tensions

The L.E.T.F.'s growing naval presence in the Pacific could lead to conflict with U.S.- aligned
nations like Japan and Australia.

### U.S. Focus on South America

### Direct Conflict with Brazil

The U.S. invasion of Colombia and Brazil's support for resistance movements make a U.S.-Brazil war
increasingly likely.

### Global Cold War Escalation

### Polarized Alliances

The world becomes divided between the U.S.-L.E.T.F. alliance and the Brazil-led Coalition for
Sovereignty, sparking a new global Cold War.

8. Summary of Outcomes

L.E.T.F.: Fully annexes China, faces insurgencies, and begins consolidating its power
while considering further expansions in Asia.
United States: Gains control of Central America and partially annexes Colombia, but
faces resistance and the growing threat of Brazil.
Brazil: Solidifies its role as the leader of Latin America's opposition, preparing for
potential conflict with the U.S.
