## Detailed Canon Account

1. The Far Eastern District: The L.E.T.F.'s Final Push in Russia

The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.), emboldened by its partial annexation of the Siberian
District Militia, turns its sights on the Far Eastern District Militia, corresponding to the modern
Far Eastern Federal District of Russia. The region's vast natural resources, including timber,
fisheries, and access to the Pacific Ocean, make it a strategic and economic target for the L.E.T.F.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Complete Control of Former Soviet Territory:

Secure dominance over the last remaining militia stronghold in the former USSR.

2. Pacific Access:

Establish control over Pacific ports like Vladivostok, ensuring global naval influence and access to
trade routes.

3. Resource Exploitation:

Gain access to timber, minerals, and fisheries vital for sustaining the L.E.T.F.'s expanding empire.

2. Military Plan: Operation Pacific Fade

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

400,000 soldiers, including reinforcements from annexed Siberia and the Ural regions. Mechanized
divisions, including remaining T-72 tanks and BMP-1 vehicles, lead the charge.

### Air Force

A combined fleet of MiG-21 fighters, Su-17 bombers, and U.S.-supplied F-15s and F-16s provides air
superiority.

### Naval Forces

U.S. and L.E.T.F. naval forces deploy to the Sea of Japan, blockading key ports and cutting off
reinforcements.

### Religious Element

Priests of the Morganist Council of the Eternal Fade conduct battlefield rituals invoking Skibbity
Toilet, the god of war, to sow chaos among the Far Eastern militia. "Fade Ceremonies" are held in
captured cities to symbolize the integration of Morganist influence.

### Invasion Strategy

1. Western Front:

Troops advance from annexed Siberia, targeting cities like Yakutsk and Khabarovsk, to dismantle
militia strongholds and secure key supply lines.

2. Eastern Front:

Amphibious landings near Vladivostok are coordinated with naval bombardments, crippling coastal
defenses and forcing militia forces inland.

3. Final Push:

A concentrated assault on Magadan and Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky, with the goal of eliminating all
remaining militia resistance.

3. Outcome of the Campaign

### Western Front

### Yakutsk Falls

The L.E.T.F. captures Yakutsk after weeks of intense combat, securing control over western Far
Eastern territories. Guerrilla resistance slows progress but fails to halt the invasion.

### Eastern Front

### Vladivostok Captured

Amphibious operations supported by U.S. naval forces overwhelm militia defenders. Vladivostok, a key
port city, is secured, marking a significant strategic victory for the L.E.T.F.

### Final Push

### Magadan and Kamchatka Secured

The campaign ends with the capture of Magadan and Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky, effectively dismantling
the Far Eastern District Militia.

### Annexation Declared

The Far Eastern District is fully incorporated into the L.E.T.F. empire as the "Far Eastern Province
of the Fade."

4. Internal and Global Reactions

### Internal Impact

1. Celebrations:

The L.E.T.F. declares the completion of its conquest of former Soviet territories, marking it as a
historic victory for Morganism. Ceremonial statues of King Darius Morgan, Magnus Maximus, and
Skibbity Toilet are erected in Vladivostok and Yakutsk.

2. Military Strain:

The campaign leaves the L.E.T.F. military exhausted, with heavy casualties and dwindling supplies.

3. Resistance Movements:

Insurgencies persist in Siberia and the Far Eastern regions, supported by neighboring countries like
Japan and China.

### Global Reactions

1. United States:

The U.S. supports the campaign militarily but faces increased backlash from its allies, particularly
Japan and South Korea, who view the L.E.T.F. as a regional threat.

2. China:

China condemns the annexation and begins bolstering its military presence along its northern border,
fearing further L.E.T.F. expansion.

3. United Nations:

The U.N. issues yet another condemnation, calling for immediate sanctions and international
intervention.

4. Japan and South Korea:

Both nations express concern over L.E.T.F. control of Vladivostok and nearby Pacific waters,
increasing military cooperation with the U.S. in the region.

5. Summary

The Far Eastern District Militia is fully annexed, marking the completion of the
L.E.T.F.'s conquest of the former Soviet territories.
The region becomes the "Far Eastern Province of the Fade," with Vladivostok as its
administrative and naval hub.
Insurgencies and international tensions grow as the L.E.T.F. struggles to maintain
control over its vast empire.
