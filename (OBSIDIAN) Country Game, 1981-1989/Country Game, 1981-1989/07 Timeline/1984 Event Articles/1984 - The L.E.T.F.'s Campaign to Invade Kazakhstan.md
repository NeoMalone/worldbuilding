## Detailed Canon Account

1. Strategic Importance of Kazakhstan

The Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade (L.E.T.F.) shifts its focus to Kazakhstan, aiming to annex this
resource-rich and geographically significant Central Asian nation. With its vast steppe, abundant
natural resources (oil, gas, and uranium), and critical location bridging Europe and Asia,
Kazakhstan represents a key target for the L.E.T.F.'s expansionist ambitions.

### Strategic Objectives

1. Control of Resources:

Gain access to Kazakhstan's oil, natural gas, and uranium reserves, ensuring economic and energy
dominance.

2. Geopolitical Positioning:

Kazakhstan's central location provides a foothold for future expansions into Central Asia and
potentially China.

3. Religious Spread:

Replace local cultural and Islamic traditions with Morganism, integrating the nation into the divine
Fade empire.

2. Military Plan: Operation Steppes Fade

### Forces Deployed

### Ground Troops

450,000 soldiers, including mechanized divisions from the annexed Siberian and Far Eastern
provinces. Heavy armor, including T-72 tanks and BMP-1 infantry vehicles, bolstered by ceremonial
"Fade Tanks" adorned with Morganist symbols.

### Air Support

MiG-21s and Su-17 bombers provide aerial dominance, supported by U.S.- supplied F-15s for precision
strikes.

### Special Units

Desert Steppes Units, trained in open-terrain warfare, equipped with thermal gear and modified
AK-74s. Morganist priests embedded with frontline units for morale and ritualistic ceremonies.

### Invasion Strategy

1. Northern Front:

Troops advance from annexed Siberia, targeting key cities like Astana (then Alma-Ata) and Karaganda,
dismantling Kazakh military installations.

2. Southern Front:

Forces cross the border from Uzbekistan (annexed or influenced), targeting Shymkent and southern oil
fields.

3. Urban Combat:

Intense fighting in Astana and Almaty, with ceremonial loudspeakers broadcasting Bluntz Beats and
Morganist propaganda to demoralize defenders.

4. Resource Seizure:

Special units secure vital oil refineries and uranium mines early in the campaign.

3. Outcome of the Campaign

### Northern Kazakhstan

### Astana Captured

After weeks of heavy fighting, Astana falls to L.E.T.F. forces. The capital is renamed "Northern
Province of the Fade."

### Karaganda Secured

Industrial centers are taken with minimal resistance, solidifying L.E.T.F. control over northern
Kazakhstan.

### Southern Kazakhstan

### Oil Fields Seized

Key oil reserves near Shymkent are secured, providing a significant economic boost to the L.E.T.F.
empire.

### Insurgencies Begin

Resistance movements form in southern Kazakhstan, supported by neighboring Central Asian nations
like Kyrgyzstan and China.

### Annexation Declared

Kazakhstan is formally annexed into the L.E.T.F., divided into northern and southern administrative
provinces.

4. Internal and Global Reactions

### Internal Impact on the L.E.T.F.

1. Economic Growth:

Control of Kazakhstan's resources strengthens the L.E.T.F.'s economy, temporarily alleviating the
strain of its rapid expansions.

2. Cultural Overhaul:

Morganist shrines are erected in Astana and Almaty, replacing local religious sites. Kazakhstan is
declared the "Steppes Province of the Fade", with Skibbity Toilet honored as the god of the plains.

3. Military Strain:

The campaign depletes L.E.T.F. forces further, leaving them vulnerable to insurgencies and external
threats.

### Global Reactions

1. United States:

The U.S. provides logistical and air support, but domestic backlash to the alliance continues to
grow. NATO allies express concern over U.S. involvement in the campaign.

2. China:

China condemns the annexation and begins mobilizing troops along its border with Kazakhstan, viewing
the L.E.T.F. as a direct threat.

3. United Nations:

The U.N. issues a resolution calling for sanctions against the L.E.T.F., with limited enforcement
due to U.S. veto power.

4. Central Asian Nations:

Neighboring countries like Kyrgyzstan and Uzbekistan begin arming insurgent groups in Kazakhstan to
resist L.E.T.F. rule.

5. Summary

Kazakhstan is fully annexed into the L.E.T.F., divided into northern and southern provinces. The
L.E.T.F. gains significant economic and geopolitical advantages but faces growing insurgencies and
international condemnation. The U.S.-L.E.T.F. alliance remains intact but increasingly fragile as
global tensions rise.
