## Detailed Canon Account

1. The Declaration of Necrocracy

Following the integration of Magnus Maximus as the supreme ruler, the Lowest Egyptian Taper Fade
(L.E.T.F.) announces its transformation into a partial necrocracy, a system of governance partially
ruled by the dead.

### Key Aspects of the Necrocracy

1. Magnus Maximus as the Eternal Ruler:

Despite being deceased, Magnus Maximus is declared the Eternal Sovereign of the L.E.T.F., ruling
from beyond the grave through rituals and divine visions interpreted by high-ranking Morganist
priests.

2. Adolf Hitler as Vice-Ruler:

Hitler remains the acting, living Vice-Ruler, carrying out the day-to-day operations of the empire
under Magnus Maximus' posthumous guidance.

3. Integration of Deceased Deities:

Dead figures like Dave Bluntz (Goddess of Leisure and Music) and Skibbity Toilet (God of War and
Fools) gain even greater influence in policy and cultural practices.

4. Necrocratic Rituals:

Important decisions are made through necromantic ceremonies, where Magnus Maximus' spirit is
"consulted" via the Council of the Eternal Fade, a priestly body that channels his wisdom.

2. Changes to Governance

### Necrocratic Practices

1. The Council of the Eternal Fade:

This governing body consists of Morganist priests who interpret the will of deceased rulers and
deities through rituals involving obsidian tablets, faded hair samples, and ceremonial toilets.

2. Posthumous Laws:

New laws introduced by Magnus Maximus' spirit must be approved by the Council and carried out by
Vice-Ruler Hitler. Citizens are required to honor the dead as part of Morganist worship, including
offerings to Magnus Maximus.

### Integration of the Living and Dead

The L.E.T.F. introduces living-dead festivals, where living citizens celebrate and honor deceased
leaders, culminating in mass head-shaving ceremonies and military parades.

3. Cultural and Religious Impact

### Expansion of Morganism

Shrines to Magnus Maximus are erected alongside those of King Darius Morgan, with necromantic altars
added for public ceremonies. Mr. Clean, the Goddess of Happiness and Lightning, enforces grooming
standards more strictly, striking down those who fail to uphold the necrocratic laws.

### Skibbity Toilet's Role

As the God of War and Fools, Skibbity Toilet gains prominence in military culture, inspiring chaos-
based strategies. Military leaders invoke Skibbity Toilet's blessings before every campaign by
flushing ceremonial toilets during strategy meetings.

4. Internal and Global Reactions

### L.E.T.F. Territories

### Supporters

Devout Morganists welcome the necrocracy, viewing it as a divine evolution of governance. Military
leaders embrace the chaotic but unifying influence of Skibbity Toilet and Magnus Maximus.

### Resistance

Insurgencies in annexed territories, particularly in the Volga and Ural regions, criticize the
necrocracy as absurd and oppressive.

### United States

The U.S. remains allied with the L.E.T.F. but begins distancing itself publicly, concerned about
international perception. Secret negotiations with NATO allies hint at growing unease over the
alliance.

### United Nations

The U.N. issues a formal condemnation of the necrocracy, labeling it a "violation of modern
governance principles."

### Former Soviet Militias

Militias in Siberia and the Far East view the necrocracy as a sign of weakness, believing the
L.E.T.F.'s governance has become unstable.

5. Summary

The L.E.T.F. transitions into a partial necrocracy, with Magnus Maximus ruling as the
Eternal Sovereign from beyond the grave.
Necrocratic rituals and practices become central to Morganist worship and
governance.
Internal resistance and international skepticism grow, but the L.E.T.F. remains
determined to expand its influence under its unique system.
