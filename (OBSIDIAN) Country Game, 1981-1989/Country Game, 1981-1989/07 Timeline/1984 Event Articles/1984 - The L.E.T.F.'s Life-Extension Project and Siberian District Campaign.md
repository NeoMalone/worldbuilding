## Detailed Canon Account

1. Hitler's Life-Extension Device: The "Eternal Vice-Ruler" Project

### Scientific Discovery

L.E.T.F. scientists, through advanced necromantic and experimental research, determine that Adolf
Hitler's life can be extended to June 12, 2011, allowing him to live until the age of 122 years. The
discovery is celebrated as a divine revelation, aligning with the L.E.T.F.'s necrocratic governance.

### The Life-Extension Machine

1. Design and Functionality:

The device, known as the "Chrono-Fade Life Prolonger", combines cutting-edge technology with
Morganist necromancy. It sustains Hitler's body by infusing him with life essence extracted from
ceremonial rituals and powered by obsidian energy cores.

2. Implementation:

Hitler undergoes the life-extension procedure, which involves weeks of rituals conducted by the
Council of the Eternal Fade. The device is implanted into a ceremonial throne that Hitler must sit
in for three hours daily to recharge his extended life force.

### Political and Cultural Impact

### Internal Support

L.E.T.F. citizens celebrate the extension of Hitler's life as a divine act of Morganist deities,
ensuring his leadership remains intact.

### Criticism

Resistance movements mock the procedure, dubbing Hitler the "Undying Vice- Ruler", while questioning
the empire's stability.

2. Campaign to Annex the Siberian District Militia

### Strategic Objectives

1. Territorial Control:

Gain control over Siberia's vast natural resources, including oil, gas, and rare minerals. Secure
dominance over Eastern Russia, completing the annexation of former Soviet territories.

2. Geopolitical Positioning:

Use Siberia as a gateway to expand influence into the Far Eastern District Militia and potentially
China. Military Plan: Operation Eternal Frost

1. Forces Deployed:

350,000 L.E.T.F. troops, including mechanized divisions and mountain warfare units. Air support
includes MiG-21 fighters and Su-17 bombers, supplemented by U.S. F-15s for air superiority.

2. Invasion Strategy:

Western Front: Ground forces advance from the Ural District into Siberia, targeting cities like
Novosibirsk and Omsk. Eastern Front: Coordinated airstrikes and troop deployments focus on capturing
key industrial hubs like Irkutsk and Yakutsk.

3. Cold-Weather Adaptations:

Specialized units equipped with thermal gear and cold-resistant technology developed by L.E.T.F.
scientists.

### Outcome

1. Western Siberia Secured:

Major cities such as Novosibirsk and Omsk fall quickly, with militia forces retreating deeper into
Siberia.

2. Eastern Siberia Resistance:

Irkutsk and Yakutsk put up strong resistance, with local militias utilizing guerrilla tactics and
leveraging the harsh environment to stall L.E.T.F. advances.

3. Partial Annexation:

The L.E.T.F. declares western and central Siberia annexed as the "Siberian Province of the Fade",
while eastern Siberia remains contested.

3. Internal and Global Reactions

### Internal Impact

1. Celebration of Hitler's Extended Life:

Hitler's continued leadership inspires L.E.T.F. citizens, who view the life-extension machine as a
triumph of Morganist science and faith. Necrocratic rituals honor Hitler's "extended reign," with
ceremonies held in annexed territories.

2. Resistance in Siberia:

Insurgencies intensify in eastern Siberia, supported by remnants of the Siberian District Militia
and external actors like China.

### Global Reactions

1. United States:

The U.S. provides air and logistical support for the Siberian campaign but faces growing dissent
domestically and among NATO allies.

2. United Nations:

The U.N. condemns the annexation of Siberia and the necrocratic practices of the L.E.T.F., calling
for immediate sanctions.

3. China:

China views the L.E.T.F.'s expansion into Siberia as a potential threat, mobilizing forces along its
northern border and covertly supporting Siberian resistance.

4. Summary

Hitler's life is extended to 2011 through the Chrono-Fade Life Prolonger, solidifying
his role as the "Undying Vice-Ruler."
The Siberian District Militia is partially annexed, with western and central Siberia
falling under L.E.T.F. control, while eastern Siberia remains contested.
Insurgencies and international tensions grow, threatening the stability of the L.E.T.F.'s
expanding empire.
